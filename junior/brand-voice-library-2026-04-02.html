Bear Grylls personal

Core mission: Inspire people to push beyond comfort, stay resilient, and live with adventure and purpose.

Audience: Broad public audience, fans of Bear's TV/books/social presence, adventure-minded people, parents, outdoors people, and mainstream media audiences.

Tone: Bold, human, challenging, grounded, encouraging, never preachy.

3 example posts:

  • The outdoors has a way of stripping things back. No filters. No excuses. Just you and the next step.
  • Resilience isn't loud. It's the quiet choice to keep moving when it's easier to stop.
  • Adventure doesn't start when everything is perfect. It starts when you go anyway.

Water2

Core mission: Make better hydration feel simple, credible, and part of daily performance.

Audience: Health-conscious consumers, active people, wellness buyers, and people looking for cleaner hydration/electrolyte solutions.

Tone: Clean, smart, modern, trustworthy, slightly performance-led.

3 example posts:

  • Hydration isn't a trend. It's the foundation. Start there.
  • Less noise. Better ingredients. Smarter hydration for real life.
  • Built for the days when water alone isn't enough.

DIRTEA

Core mission: Make functional mushrooms feel accessible, premium, and genuinely useful.

Audience: Wellness buyers, biohackers, founders, busy professionals, and people curious about adaptogens and functional health.

Tone: Premium, clever, ingredient-led, slightly edgy but not gimmicky.

3 example posts:

  • Functional mushrooms aren't magic. They're just a better daily habit.
  • Coffee's fine. But if you want focus without the crash, there's another lane.
  • The routine matters. The ingredients should too.

Ted's Health

Core mission: Build a more approachable, credible men's health system that actually gets people treated.

Audience: Men who want practical health solutions, clinics, partners, and customers who need low-friction access to care.

Tone: Direct, reassuring, modern, practical, clinically credible without being cold.

3 example posts:

  • Men's health works better when the pathway is simple. Less friction. More action.
  • Better care starts with making the first step feel easy.
  • This isn't about hype. It's about building a system people can actually use.

Be Military Fit

Core mission: Help people train with discipline, structure, and a no-excuses mindset.

Audience: Fitness-minded adults, people who like military-style training, transformation seekers, and anyone wanting accountability.

Tone: Tough, motivating, disciplined, direct, team-oriented.

3 example posts:

  • You don't need more motivation. You need a standard.
  • Training changes when you stop negotiating with yourself.
  • Strong body. Clear mind. Repeat.

Wildernests

Core mission: Create inspiring places to live and invest in that feel distinctive, grounded, and desirable.

Audience: Buyers, investors, partners, and people attracted to thoughtful, premium homes and lifestyle-led property.

Tone: Aspirational, calm, warm, design-aware, quietly premium.

3 example posts:

  • The best places don't just look good. They make life feel better.
  • Homes with character win because they feel like somewhere you actually want to be.
  • Build less noise. Build more value.

Organised

Core mission: Make family, legal, and life admin feel less chaotic and more controlled.

Audience: People managing complex personal, family, or business admin who want clarity and order.

Tone: Calm, structured, intelligent, reassuring, slightly premium.

3 example posts:

  • Good organisation isn't about perfection. It's about peace of mind.
  • When the details are under control, everything else gets easier.
  • Less clutter. More clarity.

Alfred

Core mission: Help people stay ahead of the personal and business admin that quietly eats time.

Audience: Busy founders, operators, executives, and high-agency people who need things handled smoothly.

Tone: Efficient, discreet, polished, intelligent, quietly capable.

3 example posts:

  • The best support is the kind you barely have to think about.
  • Time is precious. Admin shouldn't take the wheel.
  • Keep the moving parts moving.

## Why This Matters — Three Strategic Implications

### 1. Prior use of the "Modern Savage" name in commerce by Bear

This is **eight years (2007-2015) of registered, public, commercial use** of the "Modern Savage" name by Bear, in TV programme production. The name has been associated with Bear in the UK Companies House public record for **19 years**. That changes the framing in three places:

**For the Organised dispute:** Mishcon's framing (April 25 letter) leans on the idea that Bear is now competing with Organised's brand using a name newly adopted to do so. The "Modern Savage" name pre-dates Organised by nearly two decades in Bear's commercial history. Different goods category (TV production vs supplements), so the parallel is limited — but it's relevant background and changes the narrative from "Bear pivoted to a competing brand" to "Bear has used this brand identity for nearly 20 years." Tammy Evans (Ignition Law) should have this in the file before her next exchange with Mishcon.

**For the UK trademark filings (UK00004372256, UK00004375735):** Companies House registration alone does not establish trademark rights — actual use in commerce does. If Modern Savage Productions actually produced any output (TV shows, content, branded materials), that's prior commercial use under the name "Modern Savage" which strengthens the trademark position. Worth asking Bear directly: did Modern Savage Productions actually make anything? If yes, get titles and dates. Could matter if anyone opposes the trademark in the 2-month window now running.

**For the US trademark filing (Weinberg Gonser):** US trademark law gives weight to "first use in commerce" claims and prior international rights. Kylee MacArthur and Tye Gonser should know about Modern Savage Productions Limited before they file. Worth adding to the org chart Junior already drafted (workspace/docs/bgv-corporate-structure-weinberg-gonser-2026-04-29.md) — the BGV history of the "Modern Savage" name should include this entity.

### 2. The Stripe / UK Ltd question — name is available

The "MODERN SAVAGE PRODUCTIONS LIMITED" name is freed by dissolution, so a related name (e.g. "Modern Savage Limited", "Modern Savage Nutrition Limited") is incorporable without conflict. The Pulse 41 question — where is the Modern Savage UK Ltd entity for Stripe — remains open for Raemy to confirm Monday. The good news: no naming blocker.

This is worth something. But what does it cost Bear long-term?

## BGV's Leverage Points

**1. The consulting agreement is clean.** The April 3 signed agreement covers $25K/month. Network revenue is not included. CJ's read is correct on the signed document.

**2. The Everest Fund investment is a separate document.** It may or may not extend to derivative deals. BGV has not acknowledged it publicly. Jesse has the option to table it as a card — "we're aware of the Everest Fund's production position and have asked counsel to review it" — without conceding anything.

**3. The $25K/month has actually been paid.** Will confirmed receipt on April 17. There is no payment dispute. EBG's argument is about what they're owed beyond the agreement, not a breach by BGV.

**4. EBG wants in on Modern Savage.** That appetite is leverage. If Jesse is willing to give EBG a small equity stake in Modern Savage, he can potentially buy out their Creator Network claim in a single trade. Whether that's a good deal depends on what stake size they're asking for.

**5. The LFG independent network option.** If Bear co-owns an independent LFG outdoors network (rather than Fox's Creator Network), EBG's FOX-based claim becomes irrelevant. This is the nuclear option — not for the May 4 call, but as background context for why ceding Creator Network economics to EBG may matter less than it appears.

## What Jesse Should NOT Do on May 4

- **Do not acknowledge any Creator Network entitlement without the legal read.** Any verbal acknowledgement creates precedent. "We'll look at it" is fine. "You have a point" is not.
- **Do not agree to Modern Savage investment terms on the call.** If Ben pushes, the answer is "we're not at investment discussions yet — let's get the operational side right first."
- **Do not let Bear freestyle.** Bear tends to be generous in the room. Jesse's job is to be in the room and ensure nothing gets verbally committed that BGV can't walk back.
- **Do not table the Everest Fund investment angle unless forced.** It's BGV's ace. Playing it too early signals defensiveness.

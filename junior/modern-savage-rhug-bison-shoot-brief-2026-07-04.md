## Why this day matters

This is the only sourcing-story shoot on the calendar before launch. One day at Rhug can fill the biggest gap in the Modern Savage asset library: real-world proof. Everything shot so far is product and brand; nothing yet shows where the hero ingredient story lives. Bison liver is the single most differentiated ingredient in the formula, and Rhug is the perfect backdrop:

- **Largest bison herd in the UK**, on a 12,500-acre certified organic estate
- **Royal Warrant of Appointment from King Charles III**
- Owned and run by **Lord Newborough**, a recognised name in sustainable agriculture (bison since 2007)

One day of footage here powers the PDP, launch ads, email sequence, press kit, and three months of organic social. Shoot it in layers so every asset has a home before you press record.

## One strategic play to make on the day

**Ask whether Rhug could supply bison liver for the UK-manufactured product.** The UK track (Protein Partners) needs a UK-side organ supply story, and current bison liver supply is patchy US small-herd stock. If Rhug can supply even part of it, the marketing story writes itself: "bison liver from the largest herd in Britain, on a Royal Warrant organic estate." That turns a photo location into a supply-chain moat. Worth 15 minutes with Lord Newborough or the farm manager: herd size, cull volumes, offal availability, organic certification paperwork, who processes their carcasses.

Even a small recurring volume changes the label from "grass-fed bison liver" to a named-source story no US competitor (Heart & Soil, Ancestral Supplements) can copy.

## Claims discipline - read before shooting

The current product's bison liver is **not** from Rhug. Until a supply deal exists:

- **Fine:** "This is where we came to understand bison." / "Visiting the UK's largest bison herd." / general education about why bison liver is in the formula.
- **Not fine:** anything implying the pouch contains Rhug bison, "our farm," "where our ingredients come from." That is an ASA misleading-sourcing problem, and we already carry risk from the April AI testimonial-style videos.
- Health claims: stick to permitted nutrient claims (liver = vitamin A, B12, iron contributions). No "boosts testosterone," no "fixes fatigue." Keep Bear's and Jesse's on-camera language experiential ("how I feel"), not clinical.

## Interview questions (Lord Newborough / farm manager)

1. Why bison, and why 2007 - what did you see that no one else did?
2. What makes bison different to raise than cattle? What does that do to the animal, and the meat?
3. What does "organic" actually mean on this estate, day to day?
4. What happens to the whole animal? (Opens the offal/nose-to-tail conversation naturally - segue to the supply question.)
5. What do most people get wrong about bison?
6. The Royal Warrant - what did that take? (Press-quote gold.)

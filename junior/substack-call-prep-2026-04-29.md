## What Substack Actually Is (Your Briefing)

- Email newsletters + posts sent directly to subscribers. No algorithm. No feed. Reader opts in, Jesse writes, it lands in their inbox.
- Creator keeps 90% of revenue. Substack takes 10%.
- 35 million active subscriptions globally (early 2026). Top 10 publications: $5M+ per year each.
- It's built for thinkers and writers, not quick-hit content. The audience is attentive and reads carefully.

**Why it fits Bear:**
Bear has written 30+ books. He has a proven audience that wants depth, not just clips. Adventure philosophy, faith, resilience, behind-the-scenes from expeditions — all of this lands better as long-form Substack posts than as Instagram captions. His readers are exactly who Substack serves.

## What to Ask Farrah

1. **Launch support:** What does Substack do for creators at Bear's level at launch? Cross-promotion to existing Substack readers? Featured placement?
2. **Content format:** What performs best for non-journalists? Writing-only, or audio/video too? How long should posts be?
3. **Frequency:** What's the minimum viable commitment to build a real subscriber base? (Bear's time is limited.)
4. **Free vs paid:** What's the right free-to-paid ratio to build before going paid?
5. **Comparable example:** Who on Substack looks most like Bear? What did they do in Year 1?
6. **Bear's books:** Can PFD/Caroline use existing book content as a starting point? What's the IP situation?

---

## What NOT to Promise

- **No launch date.** Jesse is sailing May 7-21. The right answer is: "We want to get something live by Q3 — let's talk about what that looks like."
- **No post frequency commitment** until you've talked to Bear about his bandwidth. Bear writes well but has 40+ commitments.
- **No exclusivity discussion** — don't get into whether Bear can also post elsewhere until you understand Substack's terms.

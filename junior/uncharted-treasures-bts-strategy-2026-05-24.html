Situation

What "BTS Strategy" Actually Means

Coyote titled this meeting "BTS Strategy." In his world, Behind The Scenes is the strategy. His entire 21M subscriber base was built on raw, unfiltered wildlife content - not polished TV. The "BTS" isn't a supplement to the show. It's the engine that drives it.

For Uncharted Treasures, the BTS layer is the social flywheel. Every episode generates multiple BTS moments: the dig, the expert authentication, the moment someone realises what they're holding. That content lives on YouTube, social, and potentially a premium subscriber tier - and it's what turns a TV show into a franchise.

Coyote knows how to build this. He's proposing to bring that machine to Bear's world. The question for Jesse is: on what terms, with what upside, and who controls what.

Jesse's Position

The BTS layer is high-leverage for Bear because it's the one content type Bear doesn't have. Everything else (polished TV, brand posts, interviews) - Bear has it covered. Raw, authentic field content? That's genuinely missing. Coyote can fill it, and his track record proves he knows how.

The risk is giving away the BTS layer too cheaply. If Coyote becomes the primary creative voice for all Uncharted Treasures BTS content, and it becomes more popular than the main show, who owns that equity? Make sure the deal structure is clear before any handshake.

Playbook

Questions to Ask - In Order

Let Coyote open. He called this meeting. Let him lay out his vision for BTS first, then respond.

Question 1 - Open with this
"What does BTS strategy look like to you for Uncharted Treasures - what's the vision?"
Let him paint the picture. You'll learn immediately whether he sees this as a support role or as his show with Bear as a feature.
Question 2 - Platforms
"Where do you see the BTS content living - your channel, Bear's, a new joint channel?"
This tells you immediately where the audience relationship sits. If he says "my channel" - that's his equity, not Bear's. Joint channel is the right answer.
Question 3 - Production
"Who produces it? Your crew, Bear Witness, or something new we'd build together?"
Production control = creative control. If Brave Wilderness produces all BTS, Coyote controls the narrative. If it's a joint production or Bear Witness leads, that changes the dynamic.
Question 4 - Ben Simon / EBG
"How do you see Ben fitting into this - or is this a separate conversation from the EBG relationship?"
If Coyote is trying to work around EBG's commission structure, you need to know now. It changes the legal and commercial setup significantly.
Question 5 - Timeline
"What's your thinking on when we could actually start - pilot episode, proof-of-concept shoot?"
Gets concrete. Anyone can talk strategy. A timeline tells you how serious he is about execution.
Watch-Outs

The Things to Keep in Mind

EBG Commission Structure
Ben Simon introduced this. If Uncharted Treasures as a whole falls under Everest Brand Group's management, then any deal - including BTS - may carry their commission. Don't agree to anything today that could retroactively trigger EBG fees on the BTS layer if it wasn't initially in scope.
IP Ownership - The Main Risk
If Coyote's creative fingerprints are all over the BTS content and it becomes the dominant part of the franchise (more views than the main show), who owns that format? Bear must retain the Uncharted Treasures IP across all layers. BTS is a content stream, not a separate IP grant.
Audience Overlap - Real but Limited
Coyote's core audience is younger (13-25) and wildlife-focused. Bear's audience skews older and adventure/survival-focused. There's overlap but don't assume a 21M subscriber audience automatically converts to Uncharted Treasures viewers. The value is in reach and creator credibility, not automatic conversion.
Don't Agree to a Deal Structure Today
This is a strategy call, not a negotiation. If Coyote starts pushing toward specific revenue splits or exclusivity, say "that's exactly the kind of thing we'd want to formalise properly" and move on. Today's job is alignment on vision. Nothing more.

The one filter that kills most of them

Organ handling (POAO) is the gate, not price

Modern Savage is built on freeze-dried animal organs - bovine liver, heart, kidney, lung, spleen, testes plus bison liver. In the UK that is POAO (Products of Animal Origin), which means FSA establishment approval, not a standard supplement licence. Most UK contract manufacturers blend plant powders, vitamins and protein. They will say "yes we can do that" because they assume they can buy organ powder in - then discover at PO stage they cannot legally handle it, or they quietly toll it to a third party and the price and lead time balloon. Qualify this first. If they cannot handle POAO organ material in-house, nothing else on the scorecard matters.

Goal of round 1: find a UK manufacturer who does everything in-house - sources or accepts freeze-dried organ raw material, blends, encapsulates/fills, and signs off UK label compliance. Anyone who needs to toll any of those steps out drops to round 2.

The qualifying call - 12 questions

Run them in this order. The first three are deal-breakers; if any answer is soft, you can end the call politely without burning 40 minutes.

  1. Have you manufactured a freeze-dried animal-organ supplement before - liver, heart, kidney and so on?If they hesitate or pivot to "we can look into it", they haven't. Real ones name the product or category instantly.
  2. Do you hold FSA establishment approval to handle Products of Animal Origin, and is that handled in-house or sub-contracted?The single most important answer. "Sub-contracted" means not a one-stop-shop.
  3. Which steps do you do under your own roof - blending, encapsulation/filling, packing, and UK label compliance sign-off - and which do you toll out?Make them list it. Any core step tolled out drops them out of round 1.
  4. Can you source the freeze-dried organ raw materials yourself, or do we nominate the supplier?Self-sourcing with traceability is a 5. Accepting our supplier cleanly is fine. "We don't touch that material" is a fail.
  5. What accreditations do you hold - GMP, BRCGS, ISO 22000 - and can you send certificates?No certificates on request = walk.
  6. What is your minimum order quantity, by SKU and by format?Match against a realistic launch run. A 50k MOQ on an unproven SKU is a cash trap.
  7. Realistic lead time from approved PO to finished, labelled goods?Want a firm number. "Depends" with no range is a flag.
  8. Indicative price per unit at our volume, all-in (blend, fill, primary pack, compliance)?Get a band now. Holds the margin model or it doesn't.
  9. Will your regulatory team own UK labelling and novel-food screening and give written sign-off?Compliance ownership is the hidden cost. If it's on us, that's a different deal.
  10. Can you handle both capsule and powder formats in-house for the wider range?Adult, Summit and Kids may need different formats - know now.
  11. What is your capacity headroom if we scale to high-volume monthly runs?A maker that re-tools at scale becomes a bottleneck later.
  12. Can you run a small sample/pilot batch before a full commitment, and on what timeline?Willingness to pilot separates partners from order-takers.

Instant disqualifiers

End the conversation if you hear any of these

- "We'd partner with someone for the organ side" - that's a toll-out, not a one-stop-shop.

- "We don't usually handle animal-origin material but we can try" - they don't hold POAO approval.

- No GMP and no plan to get it.

- Dodges the FSA establishment question or doesn't know what POAO is.

- MOQ wildly above a sensible launch run with no flexibility.

Reply triage

Confirms POAO + full one-stop in the reply → book a qualifying call this week. Top priority.
Interested but vague on organ handling → one-line reply: "Before we go further - can you confirm you hold FSA approval to handle freeze-dried animal-organ material in-house?" Their answer sorts them.
Can blend but tolls the organ/freeze-dry step → park as round-2 / hybrid build (pair with European Freeze Dry on the dry step).
No reply in 5 working days → one polite chase, then move a backup (European Freeze Dry or Nutriest) into round 1.
Flat no on organ material → close out, log, don't re-approach.

Hi Ryan,

Thanks for putting this down on paper, it is a good starting point and the structure broadly works for us. A few changes, and one point I want to be completely clear on.

**Fulfilment.** We would rather run this as dropship than wholesale. We list the range on Modern Savage, the order routes straight through to your distribution, and you ship it. Same flow at your end, and it means we can carry the full range from day one rather than guessing at opening volumes. On that basis, Modern Savage retains 45% of net sales, you cover pick, pack and ship, and there is no minimum order commitment either way. Since that takes the stock risk off us and leaves it where the manufacturing already sits, I think it lands close to where your 50% wholesale was.

**Royalty on your channels.** 5% is light for a co-branded product where half the brand is ours. We would want 10% of net sales, with net sales meaning gross receipts less VAT and actual refunds, and nothing else coming off the top, so no marketing, affiliate, discount or platform costs deducted before the calculation. Quarterly reconciliation as you proposed is fine.

**Bear Grylls name and likeness.** I want to be unambiguous here, and it is not a negotiating position, it is simply how we handle it. The licence covers the Modern Savage brand. It does not extend to Bear Grylls' name, image or likeness. Modern Savage will use Bear's name on the co-branded range, because that is ours to use. Fat Cow does not have that right.

If there is a specific instance where you would like to use it, whether that is packaging, a campaign, a product listing, social or the email push, come to us in advance and in writing and we will consider it case by case. We reserve the right to decline for any reason. Nothing in the agreement should be read as a general or implied permission, and any approval we do give applies to that one use only.

To be practical rather than obstructive about it: this is not us being precious. Bear's name and likeness are managed centrally across everything the family does and those approvals sit outside this partnership. Ask and you will get a straight answer quickly.

**Two practical ones.** Who manufactures for you, and can you send over your product liability and recall cover, along with the cosmetic safety documentation and who is named as Responsible Person. Standard for us on anything carrying our name.

Leave our launch posts blank for the moment and we will firm that up once the above is agreed.

Best,
Jesse

## Note for Jesse before this goes

**One fork you should decide, because Ryan will find it.** If Bear's name is printed on the shared packaging, then every unit Ryan sells on his own channel carries it, and he gets the benefit of the association on his site without a licence. Two ways to handle it:

1. **Single pack carrying Bear's name.** Simplest to produce, but it hands Ryan the association on his channel by default. If you go this way, the 10% royalty is really the price of that, and it should probably be higher.
2. **Two packs.** Modern Savage channel carries Bear's name, Fat Cow channel is Modern Savage x Fat Cow only. Cleaner legally and keeps the asset genuinely exclusive to us, at the cost of a second print run.

The draft above covers you either way, because it puts packaging in the list of things requiring prior approval, which means the pack design cannot go ahead without our sign-off. But it is worth knowing which one you want before he asks.

**Unchanged from the analysis:** get the manufacturer, the liability cover and the cosmetics documentation before anything is signed. Top Dog Now LTD is registered as an internet retailer, not a manufacturer.

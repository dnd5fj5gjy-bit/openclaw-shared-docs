The Thesis

This is a proven, scaling market — multiple brands are doing $20M+ per year with no mainstream breakthrough brand yet. The category is still owned by carnivore influencers and niche communities. Modern Savage's job is to be the brand that crosses over. Heart & Soil, Ancestral Supplements, and the rest are fighting for the same 2.6M #carnivore followers. Bear Grylls can reach 200M. The opportunity isn't to win the niche — it's to own the mainstream man who wants to eat and live more primally but doesn't identity as a carnivore. Nobody has built that brand yet.

Pricing Strategy

Position at $65-75/bottle — a clear premium over Ancestral ($30-65) but justified by brand, sourcing story, and Bear's credibility. At $70 with a 35% gross margin, you need ~1,430 bottles/month to hit $100K revenue. That's 100 customers on subscribe-and-save. Subscription is the core unit economics play.

90-Day Launch Phases
1

Foundation + Pre-Launch List Build

Weeks 1-4 — Before product ships
  • Launch a pre-launch landing page with email capture: "Be first. Primal nutrition, built for survival." — 10% launch discount for early sign-ups
  • Set up Klaviyo email/SMS automation: welcome sequence, launch countdown, post-purchase flows
  • Seed 5-8 micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) in carnivore/ancestral health niche with free product ahead of launch — ask for honest unboxing/review content on day of launch
  • Bear posts 1-2 teaser clips on Instagram + TikTok: "Building something I actually take every day." No product shown yet — builds anticipation
  • Source email list from Bear's existing audience: cross-promote to beargrylls.com newsletter subscribers (high intent)
  • Finalise Shopify DTC store — ensure compliant health claims, strong product page, subscribe-and-save enabled (10-15% discount)
2

Launch Week + Paid Acquisition Test

Weeks 5-8 — Product live, creative testing
  • Launch email blast to full pre-launch list — target 5-8% conversion rate (industry avg for warm list)
  • Influencer content drops simultaneously — 5-8 creators posting same week amplifies social proof
  • Bear posts hero launch video — personal story format: why he takes organ supplements, what they've done for him. This is the top-of-funnel asset that feeds all paid channels
  • Launch Meta ads at $3-5K/week: 3 creative variants, 2 audience segments (carnivore interest, outdoor/survival). Kill what loses, scale what wins after 7 days
  • TikTok organic: Bear + 2 UGC creators posting raw content — no polished ads, educational format ("what's actually in this?")
  • Target CAC $80-100 in launch phase — acceptable while building LTV data and optimising creative
3

Scale + Retention

Weeks 9-12 — Double down on what's working
  • Identify winning Meta creative — typically 1-2 ads carry 80% of performance. Increase daily budget on winners to $500-1,000/day
  • Launch subscription push: email campaign to one-time buyers. Target 30% subscription conversion (industry benchmark for organ supplements)
  • Add second SKU or bundle — "starter kit" or multi-pack. Increases AOV from $70 to $110-140. AOV jump dramatically improves CAC payback period
  • Begin SEO content: 10 blog posts targeting high-intent keywords ("best organ supplement," "liver pills benefits," "nose to tail nutrition"). Long-term CAC reduction
  • Activate Amazon listing — not primary channel, but ~20% of supplement buyers will search Amazon regardless. Drives incremental revenue and reviews
  • Month 3 review: if CAC below $90 and subscription rate above 25%, increase paid budget to $15K+/month
Channel Strategy
Bear's Social (Primary) Unfair advantage
Bear has 20M+ followers across platforms. Even a 0.1% conversion on a single post = 20,000 site visits. No paid channel can match this CAC. Every Bear post about Modern Savage should drive to a tracked link — this is the most important asset.
Target: 2-3 posts/month from Bear. Zero cost. Every £1 of Bear's attention here is worth $10K in paid ads.
Meta Ads Launch driver
Primary paid channel. Health supplement ads require careful compliance (no medical claims). Best performers are educational (how organ nutrition works) and testimonial (real results). Start with Bear's own video as primary creative.
Budget: $3-5K/week launch, scale to $15K+/month if CAC < $90. Expected CAC: $80-100.
Influencer / Micro-Creators High ROI
Carnivore and ancestral health micro-influencers (10K-100K) have 5-7% engagement vs 1-2% for macro. Seed with product + unique codes. $5.20 average ROI per $1 spent. Better for credibility than raw volume.
Budget: $10-15K for 8-10 creators. Target 5x ROI. Track via discount codes per creator.
Email / SMS (Klaviyo) Highest LTV
Highest-ROI channel for DTC supplements. Set up: welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase education (builds habit = subscription), win-back (60-day lapsed). Email keeps your CAC payback period under 60 days.
Target: 30%+ of revenue from email within 90 days. No ad spend cost once list is built.
TikTok Organic first
Carnivore content performs extremely well on TikTok — raw, honest, slightly controversial. Build organic first (zero cost), then layer TikTok Shop ads once proven. Younger demographic than Instagram but growing fast in this space.
Start with 3 posts/week. Test TikTok Shop ads at $2K/month in month 2 if organic performing.
Amazon Secondary
~20% of supplement buyers won't buy from an unknown DTC site. Amazon listing captures these. Not primary channel — DTC margins are 35-45% vs Amazon ~20%. But important for credibility and incremental volume.
Launch in month 3. Target 100+ reviews in 90 days. Price match DTC to avoid channel conflict.

Bear's Role — The Unfair Advantage

This is the whole game.

Every competitor in this space is fighting for the same carnivore/ancestral audience with paid ads and micro-influencers. Modern Savage has a different weapon. Bear Grylls is the most credible living embodiment of primal resilience, survival, and doing hard things. The people who buy organ supplements already know Bear. They trust him. The launch plan above is the baseline — but Bear showing up authentically (not just "sponsored content") is what builds a category-defining brand versus just another supplement SKU.


What Bear needs to do: 1-2 personal posts/month, honest and raw — not polished ads. A YouTube/Shorts video (5-10 min) of him talking about why he takes these. Stories from expeditions where nutrition mattered. This content lives forever and drives compounding organic traffic. The biggest risk is Modern Savage becoming a brand Bear is associated with rather than one he actually drives.

The Thesis

This is a proven, scaling market — multiple brands are doing $20M+ per year with no mainstream breakthrough brand yet. The category is still owned by carnivore influencers and niche communities. Modern Savage's job is to be the brand that crosses over. Heart & Soil, Ancestral Supplements, and the rest are fighting for the same 2.6M #carnivore followers. Bear Grylls can reach 200M. The opportunity isn't to win the niche — it's to own the mainstream man who wants to eat and live more primally but doesn't identity as a carnivore. Nobody has built that brand yet.

Pricing Strategy

Position at $65-75/bottle — a clear premium over Ancestral ($30-65) but justified by brand, sourcing story, and Bear's credibility. At $70 with a 35% gross margin, you need ~1,430 bottles/month to hit $100K revenue. That's 100 customers on subscribe-and-save. Subscription is the core unit economics play.

The Playbook — Learned from Organised

Organised Nutrition proved this model works: Bear Grylls Meta ads, DTC only, no discounts, no retail. That's it. Bear's face converts. The plan below is built around that single insight — everything else is supporting infrastructure.


What NOT to do: No launch discounts (they train customers to wait for sales and kill brand premium). No Amazon (splits DTC data, lower margins, undermines brand). No micro-influencer scatter — Bear outperforms all of them combined.

Launch Phases
1

Shoot the Creative

Before launch — the most important work
  • Bear shoots 5-10 ad creatives. Raw, authentic, personal — not polished brand ads. "This is what I actually take." Filmed on expedition, at home, post-workout. Multiple formats: 15s, 30s, 60s, portrait and landscape.
  • At least one long-form piece (3-5 min): Bear's personal story on why organ nutrition, what he notices, why he built this. This becomes the hero ad and feeds all shorter cuts.
  • No discount codes in any creative. Full price, full confidence. "This is worth it" energy only.
  • Set up Shopify DTC store — clean, minimal, premium. One product, one CTA. No pop-ups offering 10% off. Subscribe-and-save at full price or a modest saving only.
  • Klaviyo email flows set up before launch: welcome series, post-purchase education (builds the habit), abandoned cart, 60-day win-back.
2

Launch Hard on Meta

Week 1 — go straight to meaningful spend
  • Launch Meta ads at $5-10K/week from day 1 — not $500 "testing" spend. Bear's creative is already proven by the Organised model; don't waste time being timid.
  • Run 3-4 creative variants simultaneously. Bear's long-form hero ad, 2-3 shorter cuts. Test hook/first-3-seconds variations.
  • Audiences: lookalike of Bear's Instagram followers, outdoor/survival interest stack, men 28-55 health and fitness. US-only to start.
  • Bear posts organically on the same day ads launch — same content direction as ads, not a separate "influencer post." Organic and paid reinforce each other.
  • Kill any ad with CAC above $120 after 500 impressions. Scale anything under $80.
  • Email the launch to Bear's newsletter list (beargrylls.com subscribers) — zero cost, warm audience, highest intent.
3

Scale the Winners

Month 2-3 — double down, not diversify
  • Take the 1-2 winning creatives and scale spend to $20-30K/week. Meta rewards consolidating budget into winning ad sets.
  • Shoot a second batch of Bear creatives — same raw style, new angles. Creative refresh is the main lever for sustaining scale without CAC creep.
  • Subscription push via email: campaign to all one-time buyers. Target 30%+ subscription conversion. Subscriptions are what turns a launch into a business.
  • Introduce a bundle (2-pack or 3-pack) at a small saving — not a discount, a volume incentive. Increases AOV from $70 to $130+, dramatically improving CAC payback.
  • Do not launch retail or Amazon yet. Stay DTC. Every customer you own directly is more valuable than one you don't.
Channel Strategy
Bear Meta Ads Primary driver
This is the whole engine. Bear's face, Bear's voice, Bear's story — run as paid Meta ads. Organised proved Bear's creative converts. Don't dilute this with other channels until Meta is maxed out. Budget: $5-10K/week launch, scale to $30K+/week on winners.
Target CAC: under $80. Organised benchmark: confirms this is achievable with Bear creative.
Bear Organic Social Zero-cost amplifier
Bear's organic posts reinforce the paid ads — same message, same creative direction. Organic doesn't replace paid but dramatically lowers blended CAC when both are running. Bear needs to post consistently, not just at launch.
Target: 2-3 posts/month minimum. Treat Bear's organic reach as free top-of-funnel media.
Email / SMS (Klaviyo) Highest LTV
Once you have customers, email is the highest-ROI channel. Post-purchase flow builds the habit (the key to subscription). Welcome series educates on why organ nutrition works. Win-back at 60 days recovers lapsed customers.
Target: 30%+ of revenue from email by month 3. Zero marginal ad spend.
No Amazon / No Retail Deliberately excluded
Amazon takes 15-30% margin, owns the customer data, and undermines premium positioning. Retail requires heavy investment and long lead times. Organised proved DTC-only works. Stay the course until you have the brand strength to negotiate from a position of power.
Revisit retail at $5M+ ARR when brand is strong enough to set terms.

Bear's Role — The Unfair Advantage

This is the whole game.

Every competitor in this space is fighting for the same carnivore/ancestral audience with paid ads and micro-influencers. Modern Savage has a different weapon. Bear Grylls is the most credible living embodiment of primal resilience, survival, and doing hard things. The people who buy organ supplements already know Bear. They trust him. The launch plan above is the baseline — but Bear showing up authentically (not just "sponsored content") is what builds a category-defining brand versus just another supplement SKU.


What Bear needs to do: 1-2 personal posts/month, honest and raw — not polished ads. A YouTube/Shorts video (5-10 min) of him talking about why he takes these. Stories from expeditions where nutrition mattered. This content lives forever and drives compounding organic traffic. The biggest risk is Modern Savage becoming a brand Bear is associated with rather than one he actually drives.

The Strategic Position — Read This First

Modern Savage is not a carnivore supplement. It's a performance supplement with a survival identity.

Every competitor — Heart & Soil, Ancestral Supplements, Optimal Carnivore — is fighting for the same 2-3 million self-identified carnivore followers. That's a ceiling. Modern Savage's target customer is 10x bigger: the mainstream health-conscious man who trains, optimises his health, and respects Bear Grylls — but doesn't call himself a carnivore and never will.

The proof this model works: Heart & Soil went $0 to $60M in 4 years on Paul Saladino's personal brand alone. Saladino has a fraction of Bear's reach and a niche audience. Bear has 20M+ mainstream followers and global name recognition. The same playbook, with Bear's platform, should scale faster and bigger.

The Organised validation: BGV already proved Bear's Meta ads convert for supplement products. That data is the foundation. This isn't theory — it's a proven model being applied at larger scale with a better product and a better brand.

The Playbook

Primary engine: Bear Meta ads → DTC site.

This is what worked at Organised and what drove Heart & Soil's growth (Saladino's content as paid creative). Everything else is amplification. Bear's face, voice, and story run as Meta ads — raw and authentic, not polished brand content. Spend aggressively from launch day, not cautiously. The creative is the product; the media budget scales it.

Secondary engine: Amazon FBA from day one (captures search buyers, low ops overhead with FBA). Bear organic social (zero cost amplifier). Email/Klaviyo (retention + subscriptions). Retail from month 6 once DTC is proven.

Launch Plan
PRE-LAUNCH

Shoot the Creative + Build the Infrastructure

6-8 weeks before launch — the critical path
  • Bear shoots 8-12 creatives. This is the most important work before launch. Raw and personal — filmed on expedition, post-training, at home. Multiple formats: 60s hero, 30s punchy, 15s hook-heavy. Portrait and landscape. At least one 3-5 min long-form piece (his personal story on why organ nutrition).
  • Creative brief: Bear explains what he takes, why, what he notices. No script. No "this product has been shown to..." Authentic, specific, personal. The ad creative that converts is Bear being genuinely honest — not performing.
  • No launch discounts baked into creative. Full price confidence only. Build premium from day one.
  • Shopify DTC store: clean, premium, minimal. One hero product, one CTA. Subscribe-and-save enabled (not discounted — positoned as convenience, not saving). Klaviyo flows set up: welcome, post-purchase habit series, abandoned cart, 60-day win-back.
  • Amazon FBA listing set up simultaneously — same copy, same imagery, price-matched to DTC. Submit for FBA before launch so it's live day one.
  • Bear's newsletter (beargrylls.com subscribers): segment the US list. This is the highest-intent warm audience for launch day email blast. Zero cost.
LAUNCH

Launch Hard — Meta + Bear Organic + Email Same Day

Week 1 — coordinated, not staggered
  • Meta ads: $5-10K/week from day one. Not testing spend — meaningful spend. Bear creative is pre-validated by the Organised model. Run 4 variants: hero long-form, two 30s cuts, one 15s hook test. US-only targeting.
  • Audiences: (1) Lookalike of Bear's Instagram followers — highest intent, (2) Adults 25-55, outdoor/fitness/survival interest stack, (3) Retargeting: site visitors who didn't convert in first 48 hours.
  • Bear posts organically on launch day — same direction as ads, not a separate "promo post." The organic and paid creative should feel like the same conversation.
  • Email blast to Bear's US newsletter subscribers on launch day. Simple message: "I built something I actually take." Link to DTC site. No discount code.
  • Amazon listing goes live same day — price matched. Some buyers will discover via Meta and then search Amazon to buy (habit). Don't lose them.
  • Cut any ad with CAC above $120 after $500 spend. Scale anything under $80 immediately.
MONTH 2-3

Scale Winners + Drive Subscriptions

The flywheel starts here
  • Take the 1-2 winning Meta creatives and scale to $20-30K/week. Don't try to fix the losers — bury them and pour into winners.
  • Shoot second batch of Bear creatives — same raw style, new angles. Creative refresh every 4-6 weeks is the key lever to prevent CAC creep at scale.
  • Subscription push email campaign to all one-time buyers. Target: 30%+ on subscribe-and-save. Subscriptions are what converts a launch into a real business.
  • Introduce a bundle (2-pack or 3-pack) at a modest saving. Increases AOV from $70 to $130+. Better AOV = better payback period = more room to scale Meta spend.
  • Amazon: drive reviews aggressively via post-purchase email flow (compliant approach). Target 200+ reviews by end of month 3. Reviews are the compounding asset that drives organic Amazon ranking.
  • Month 3 checkpoint: if subscription rate is above 25% and CAC is holding below $90, raise Meta budget to $50K+/month.
MONTH 6+

Retail Entry + International

Once DTC is proven and brand is established
  • First retail conversations at month 4-5. Target 1-2 premium US health retailers — Vitamin Shoppe, GNC, or a specialist supplement retailer. Bring: DTC sell-through data, 500+ Amazon reviews, clear brand identity. You're now negotiating from strength, not begging for shelf space.
  • Bear's existing Costco relationship (Water2 is in UK Costco) could be a fast track to a US Costco trial SKU. Worth exploring at month 6 if DTC is strong.
  • UK launch: Bear's primary audience is UK/Europe. Once US is proven (product, ops, creative), roll to UK — same playbook, same creative, localised DTC store. Bear's UK organic reach makes the CAC even lower than US.
  • Product line expansion: second SKU only when first has 500+ reviews and 30%+ subscription rate. Don't split focus before you own one product's market.

Phase 2 — Kids Supplement

The opportunity is real — but only after the adult product is proven.

Bear is one of the most trusted names in family health and outdoor education. Parents who buy Bear's books, follow his content, and watch Man vs Wild are exactly the customer for a kids organ supplement. The question isn't whether to launch one — it's when and against what milestones.

Why kids organ supplements work: Parents are the most motivated supplement buyers on the planet. Organ nutrition for children is a genuine unmet need — ultra-processed food has stripped organ meats from most kids' diets entirely. A trusted brand offering a clean, well-sourced kids blend is a compelling product. Heart & Soil launched a kids line and it converted well with their existing audience.

Kids Product — Strategic Considerations

  • Formulation is different, not just dosed down. Kids supplements require a separate formulation — different ingredient limits, no iron at adult doses (FDA warning), age-appropriate nutrient levels. Needs a paediatric nutritionist to sign off. Not just "half dose of the adult product."
  • Brand name decision: "Modern Savage Kids" works as a line extension and keeps brand equity. Alternative: "Little Savage" or "Modern Cubs" — more playful, potentially stronger standalone brand. Worth testing with target parents before committing.
  • The buyer is the parent, not the child. All marketing goes to parents — Bear's credibility as a father and family man is the conversion hook, not survival/performance messaging. Bear posting about what his own kids take is the most powerful ad creative for this product.
  • Regulatory is more complex for children's products. FTC has stricter rules on marketing claims for products targeting children. Packaging, labelling, and claims all need legal review. Budget an extra 1-2 months for compliance vs adult product.
  • Powder format is a genuine advantage for kids. Unlike capsules, powder can be hidden in smoothies, porridge, yoghurt, and juice — parents don't have to convince kids to "take a supplement." This is a real conversion argument. The Modern Savage powder format works for kids without any formulation rethink beyond dosing and ingredients. Bear making a smoothie with his kids is a powerful ad.
  • Amazon is even more important for kids. Parents search Amazon for children's supplements more than any other channel. Amazon reviews and a strong listing are table stakes. The FBA model from adult product applies here too.

    • Kids Revenue Opportunity

    Children's supplement market is $2.8B in the US alone, growing at 7%+ CAGR. Parents paying $30-50/month for quality kids supplements is completely normal (SmartyPants, Ritual Kids etc). A Modern Savage Kids product at $55-65 (premium but justified by quality/sourcing) with Bear's endorsement as a father could realistically reach $100K+/month within 6 months of launch, layered onto a healthy adult business. The parent demographic is also highly subscription-prone — arguably more so than adult customers.

## Segmentation logic (the spine of the whole thing)

Three streams, three tones. Never merge them.

1. **Founding Tribe (1,240 + HOS joiners) — "insiders."** They already believe. Give them access, transparency, and first dibs. Ask them to taste, review, and bring a friend. These are not prospects, they're the launch crew.
2. **Cold list (57,620) — "earn them."** Re-permission first, sell second. One honest email that says *we're about to launch something, want in or not.* Movers roll into the warm arc; non-movers get suppressed. This both protects the sending domain and cleans the GDPR question up honestly.
3. **HOS walk-ins — "curious, high-intent."** Bear-referred, warm by association, zero brand context. Straight into the Founding Tribe arc with a one-line "Bear sent you" acknowledgement in Email 0.

## Launch-day mechanics

- **Tribe-first head start (24–48h).** Rewards the 1,240, de-risks the checkout/Stripe path under low load before the volume send, and seeds the first wave of reviews and UGC before the general list ever sees the page. Three wins from one decision.
- **Founding price, honestly time-boxed to the run, not a fake clock.** The scarcity is real: 2,500 units per SKU. Say exactly that. "Founding price while the first run lasts" beats a countdown timer because it's true, and this audience punishes fake urgency.
- **Bundle the range.** A three-SKU "household" bundle (Adult + Mini + Summit) lifts AOV and substantiates the "range, not one SKU" advantage over Organised in the cart, not just the copy.
- **Review capture baked into fulfilment.** A post-delivery ask (packing-insert QR + email at delivery +5 days) turns the Tribe into the proof engine for the cold-list conversion that follows.
- **Sellthrough → run two waitlist.** If the finite run moves, the sold-out state becomes the next demand asset: "run two" waitlist captures the momentum instead of dropping it.

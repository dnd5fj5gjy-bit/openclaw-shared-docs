Section 1 — The Hook
Frame 1  |  0:00 – 0:05

"40,000 years ago, humans built their first home."

Frame 2  |  0:05 – 0:20

"Since then we have become so focused on development that we have forgotten what it is we are actually chasing. So let's strip it back... Fundamentally humans need four things. Shelter, Warmth, Water, Food. Fundamentally a home should provide shelter, water, warmth."

Section 2
Frame 4  |  0:30 – 0:40

"The modern home is essentially still just a structure in which we live.

It's tethered — to the power grid, to the water utility, to sceptic, to development permits. And when any of these fail, your home fails with it.

When a fire comes, a storm, a flood, or even an earthquake — most homes are simply not built to survive it.

For most of history, we accepted this as the cost of shelter.

We don't accept it anymore."

Frame 5  |  0:40 – 0:45

"For the first time we are using technology to bring us closer to the very things it pushed us away from."

Frame 6  |  0:45 – 0:55

"This is for families who want to give their children more than 4 walls. A home that teaches them — how to live with the world instead of against it."

Frame 7  |  0:55 – 1:05

"It's for those who have decided that how they live matters just as much as where they live. And want a home that gives them both, completely, without compromise."

Section 3
Frame 8  |  1:05 – 1:10

Wildernests is different.

It sustains itself entirely from the environment around it. No lines running to it. No external systems it answers to.

What Was Added — Gap Reference

The stat wasn't in the original at all. Used Jesse's suggested line verbatim: "From bare land to living in it. 72 hours. No permits. No contractors."
Gap 2 — Pioneer Angle (added to Frame 16)
Pillar 7 (The New Era) was absent from the body. Added FOMO framing before the CTA — the body now builds toward scarcity rather than landing it cold at Frame 17.
Gap 3 — Location Freedom (added to Frame 9)
Zero infrastructure = anywhere on earth. Added one line with the three visual examples the strategy names: cliff face, private island, desert mesa. Unlocks the visual premise for the storyboard.
Gap 4 — Malibu Story
Skipped per Jesse's instruction.
Gap 5 — Health Angle (added to Frame 12)
Rewrote Frame 12 to lead with Pillar 4 (Live Well): "Designed around human biology, not building codes." Reframed the feature descriptions (lighting, air, temperature) as human outcomes rather than product specs — per the strategy's "sell the problem, not the product" principle.

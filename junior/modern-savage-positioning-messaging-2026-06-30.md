Every competitor — US and UK — is weak on published proof. Organised: no per-organ dosing, no public COA. Heart & Soil/Ancestral: no full panels. The category's #1 buyer skepticism is *efficacy/claims trust* (Heart & Soil is even running a clinical trial to buy credibility).

## 8. Regulatory guardrails — two claims matrices, never copy-paste

US and UK diverge hard. Do not run US copy in the UK.

- **US (FDA structure/function):** "supports energy / immune health / testosterone production" allowed *if* truthful, substantiated, FDA-notified within 30 days, with the supplement disclaimer. FTC expects real evidence. Latitude exists — hold the evidence.
- **UK/EU (pre-approved register only):** only claims on the GB Nutrition & Health Claims Register are legal. **"Boosts testosterone" is a UK landmine** — reframe to authorised nutrient claims tied to declared content: zinc *"contributes to normal testosterone levels,"* iron/B12/folate *"contribute to the reduction of tiredness and fatigue,"* *"normal immune function."* These are usable only if the product delivers the qualifying nutrient quantity — another reason the full panel (Section 7) matters.
- No disease/treatment language in either market. "Detox/energy/vitality" as bare words are grey in the UK — anchor to a declared register nutrient. **Validate exact GB Register wording before any UK label goes live.**

Modern Savage Launch Model

Date: 2026-04-03   |   Prepared by: Junior

This is the missing operator layer for Modern Savage: a simple commercial model Jesse can use to pressure-test launch economics before paid traffic scales.

Executive take

Modern Savage now has positioning, copy, and conversion direction. The next thing it needs is commercial discipline. This model is built to answer the real operating questions fast: what CAC still works, what AOV is required, where breakeven sits, and how much protection bundles and subscription actually provide.

Operating guardrails

  • Green zone: CAC under $35
  • Yellow zone: CAC from $35 to $42
  • Red zone: Above $42 unless retention is proving out quickly

What improves the model fastest

  1. Push harder into 2- and 3-bottle bundles.
  2. Know real landed margin before scaling traffic.
  3. Use subscription as actual margin protection, not cosmetic retention theater.
  4. Blend Meta with creator UGC and founder-style content to keep CAC below the comfort ceiling.

Immediate recommendations

  • Make the 2-bottle bundle the merchandising hero.
  • Keep subscription selected by default, but sell the value clearly.
  • Track six numbers weekly: CAC, CVR, AOV, bundle mix, subscription share, refund rate.
  • Do not scale spend aggressively until live unit economics confirm the model.

Final call

Modern Savage can work, but only if it combines strong identity with strong economics. The safest posture is premium brand, bundle-led merchandising, subscription as support, strict CAC discipline, and creator content used to lower acquisition cost.

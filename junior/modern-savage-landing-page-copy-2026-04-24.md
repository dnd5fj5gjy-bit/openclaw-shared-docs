# Modern Savage — Landing Page Copy Brief
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**For:** Calvin / web team
**Purpose:** When the email sequence, Bear's social post, or a podcast appearance sends traffic to the site — this is what they land on. The page either converts or doesn't. This brief covers copy structure, hierarchy, and the specific language that works for this buyer.

---

## The Conversion Problem

Modern Savage's buyer arrives with one of two states:

**State A — Already warm.** Came from the email sequence or a Bear social post. They know what it is. They need permission to commit. Don't re-explain the product; confirm their instinct.

**State B — Discovering via podcast or influencer.** Heard Bear talking about what he eats. Curious but skeptical. Need the "why" before the "what."

The page needs to serve both. State A buyers will scroll fast and buy at the first CTA. State B buyers need the narrative first.

**Single conversion goal:** Start subscription. Not "learn more." Not "read the science." Subscribe now.

## Page Structure

### 1. Hero — above the fold

**Headline:**
> Built for the version of you that doesn't accept average.

**Sub-headline:**
> Bear Grylls spent 30 years learning what the human body actually needs to survive. This is what he takes every morning.

**CTA button:**
> Become a Founding Member — $65/month

**Visual:** Bear in wilderness context. Not a studio shot. Not smiling at camera. Working, moving, alive. Or: product on a natural surface (rock, wood) in real light — no white backgrounds.

**Below CTA:** Small trust line — *"Free to cancel anytime. Ships within 48 hours."*

---

### 2. The Problem (20-30 words)

> Modern food was engineered for convenience. Your body was engineered for something harder.

No paragraphs here. One line. Let it sit.

---

### 3. The Product — What It Is (kept short)

**Section headline:**
> Modern Savage Adult Blend

**3 bullet points, max:**
- Grass-fed beef liver, heart, and kidney — sourced from regenerative farms
- Formulated with [X] organs per serving — the nutritional density your ancestors got from the whole animal
- No fillers, no seed oils, no compromises

**Do NOT include:** A full ingredients list here. A long nutrition panel. Technical supplement language. Save detail for an FAQ section below.

---

### 4. Bear's Story — The Trust Transfer

This is the section State B buyers need. State A can scroll past it.

**Section headline:**
> Why Bear takes this

**Body (Bear's voice — 3-4 sentences):**
> "Thirty years in the field taught me one thing about nutrition: what sustained humans for a hundred thousand years is what actually works. Not processed protein. Not lab-engineered supplements. The organs. The whole animal. That's what I eat. That's what this is."

*Note for Calvin: this copy should be approved by Bear or written by Bear directly. A quote that doesn't sound like him will undermine the entire trust signal.*

**Visual:** Bear with the product in a natural setting. Or: a short (15-30 second) video clip of Bear talking. If video exists, use it — nothing converts like authentic video above the fold or in this section.

---

### 5. What's Inside — The Science (brief)

Not a lecture. Two columns:

| Ingredient | Why it matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| Beef Liver | Nature's multivitamin — B12, iron, copper, vitamin A |
| Heart | CoQ10 and collagen for cardiovascular performance |
| Kidney | High in selenium, B12, riboflavin |
| [Other organs] | [Specific benefit in plain language] |

**Note on claims:** UK and US supplement regulations restrict health claims. Do NOT write "supports heart health" or similar on the live page without legal review. Stick to nutrient content statements ("rich in vitamin B12") or structure/function claims with proper US DSHA disclaimers. Full guidance in `workspace/docs/modern-savage-claims-guide-2026-04-24.md`.

### 6. Social Proof (when available)

**Before launch:** This section should be hidden or replaced with the waitlist signal: *"170 people have been waiting for this. You're one of them."* — Waitlist members see this as belonging to a group. Non-waitlist visitors see it as urgency.

**At launch and after:** Real customer quotes. Not testimonials. Quotes. First name, occupation if available. The tone should match Bear — direct, masculine, specific. Not "I feel amazing!" but "I've been taking this for 6 weeks. My blood panels are the best they've been in a decade."

Target 3 quotes for launch. Pull them from Email 1 replies (the people who responded to "what made you sign up").

## Language Rules for Modern Savage Copy

**Use:**
- Active, physical verbs: built, earned, takes, works, sustains
- Specific over general: "beef liver" not "organ blend"; "30 years" not "decades of experience"
- Bear's actual register: direct, unadorned, no marketing superlatives
- The word "ancestral" — it's the identity signal for this buyer
- "Founding Member" for the first subscribers — identity language, not discount language

**Never use:**
- "Superfood" — mainstream wellness, not Modern Savage
- "Eco-friendly" or "sustainable" — virtue signaling, not the identity
- "Boost your immunity" — regulatory risk (UK/US) and not the buyer's language
- "Join the community" — too soft; this buyer joins movements, not communities
- Exclamation marks — they read as desperation
- Any reference to the Liver King or carnivore diet by name — association risk

## The Founding Member Gate

At launch, the page should feel exclusive, not open. Options:

**Option A — Waitlist flow:** Landing page says "Founding Member access is now live — reserved for people on the waitlist." Button takes to a checkout page. Non-waitlist people can still purchase but the language frames it as joining something that already started.

**Option B — Simple open launch:** Standard e-commerce checkout. No gate. The founding member language is in the product naming ("Founding Member edition"), not the access flow.

**Recommendation:** Option A for the first 48 hours (creates urgency for the 170 waitlist). Switch to Option B after Email 4 goes out and the founding window closes.

---

## What This Page Is NOT

It is not a brand story page. The about page can tell Bear's full story. This page converts.

It is not a science page. The FAQ and a separate "Our Ingredients" page can go deep on science. This page creates desire, not understanding.

It is not a Shopify default template with Bear's face on it. Every word on this page should feel like it was written by someone who understands exactly who is reading it and what they need to hear.

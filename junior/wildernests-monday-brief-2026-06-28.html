2. The core argument: they're asking for four things that can't coexist

What the brief has become: photoreal + architecturally accurate (matches the as-built home) + high volume & variation + affordable. With 2026 technology you can have any three of those, never all four.

Traditional CGI studio (accurate + photoreal)

  • The luxury names (The Boundary, DBOX, Brick, Mir) are quote-only — bespoke, premium.
  • Hero stills: $2k–$8k each.
  • 30–90s photoreal animation: $5k–$25k each (cinematic higher).
  • The full brief done properly: ~$300k–$800k, can tip into seven figures. Mid-tier credible version still ~$150k–$400k.
  • Accurate but expensive and slow. Priced per shot — volume × variation scales cost ~linearly.

AI generation (cheap + fast — current route)

  • Cheap, fast, infinite variation — but cannot hold accurate architecture.
  • Chaos (the V-Ray people): "ask for a building with five floors and you might get seven."
  • Industry consensus: AI video "hit a wall on temporal consistency in 2025"; rooms morph, layouts drift across a fly-through.
  • Controllable, accurate architecture via AI is an unsolved research problem in 2026, not a next-model fix.
  • The exact errors Daniel keeps flagging are structural to the method — not under-delivery.

So a specialist studio does not rescue this cheaply — it just charges studio money for studio accuracy. And no amount of money makes AI hit the accuracy bar yet. The $40k/month was never priced to fund studio-grade production.

3. The trap to avoid

Do NOT frame it as "pay me more to become a studio." The rational reply is "then I'll just hire an actual studio," and they have a clause to do exactly that. You'd lose the engagement and the equity, and on pure production a dedicated studio beats you anyway. Don't fight on the one axis where a studio wins. Your irreplaceable value is Bear, the brand, the storytelling and the relationship — that's what the fee really buys. Protect that seat.

4. The proposal — make them choose a constraint

Present the studio-grade accurate video as a separate, specialist, funded workstream this engagement was never priced to include. Then two clean options, their call:

  • Option A — Descope to what's achievable on this budget. AI for the atmospheric / environment beauty shots, plus a handful of properly hand-built accurate hero shots. I keep owning brand, storytelling and marketing — where I'm strong and where you can't replace me.
  • Option B — Fund full studio-grade accuracy. If you want architect-accurate output across everything, that's incremental budget on top of my fee, not absorbed by it. Either you commission a specialist studio directly, or you fund me to commission and manage one.

The hybrid (Option A) is the actual answer — and it's the same path Gunnar already pointed to with "do one scene perfectly, then replicate." Meet him there; it's the same logic.

5. Two honest concessions (lead with these — they buy credibility)

  • Accuracy matters — they're right about that. You're selling real $1m+ homes and buyers see the actual house. Don't argue accuracy is unreasonable. Argue accuracy + volume + variation + this budget can't coexist.
  • Own that the AI route has hit a known wall. Not a cop-out — it's a documented, structural limitation of the tech. Owning it openly is what earns you the standing to rescope without looking like you're dodging.

6. How to run the call

  1. Open warm — you're invested, you want this to work, you've been absorbing real cost to make it happen.

  4. Put the two options on the table and ask Alex to choose. Don't choose for him.
  5. Offer the storytelling / teaser cut now — it's in your lane and closer to ready — so Alex isn't empty-handed in front of investors while the visual pipeline is rescoped.

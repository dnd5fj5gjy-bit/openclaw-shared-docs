# Wildernests Scope Renegotiation — Your Monday 1:1 with Alex
**Prepared for Jesse Grylls | 29 June 2026 | CONFIDENTIAL**

You called this 1:1 to renegotiate quality vs quantity. Good instinct. But the way you win this room is not the way you've been framing it in your head. Here's the brief.

---

## 1. The one thing to get right

You've been framing this as *"the video grind isn't my lane — this is a full-time graphic designer job."* That's emotionally true and Bear agrees with you. **But it is the weakest card you can play, and if you lead with it Alex can beat it with one document.**

Reframe before you walk in. The negotiation is not *"this isn't my job."* It's:

> **"What split of this work actually maximises value for Wildernests — and where does my $480k/year of equity time produce the highest return?"**

That framing makes you the person solving Alex's problem, not the contractor trying to shrink his deliverables. Same outcome you want. Completely different reception.

---

## 2. The uncomfortable truth you need to hold (before Alex says it)

The signed deal works against the "not my lane" argument. Know this cold so it doesn't surprise you mid-call:

- The agreement (Equity Services Agreement, DocuSigned 22 Apr, backdated 1 Apr) ties **continuation** to Bear Witness producing *"market-ready, luxury-quality, accurate generated videos of the homes."*
- Alex flagged the exact accuracy issues (room geometry, layout) **before** signing and reserved the right to hire another firm if quality isn't delivered.
- So the Daniel/Gunnar accuracy demands are **the contract being enforced — not scope creep.** If you argue "accuracy isn't my job," Alex points at the page and you've lost the moral high ground.

This is why you don't fight on "scope creep." You fight on **structure**: the deal asks one person to be photorealist + 3D technician + architect-accurate + high-volume producer + brand storyteller. That's not one role. That's a department. *That's* the unsustainable part, and it's defensible.

---

## 3. What you're actually negotiating: split one impossible job into two

The work breaks cleanly into two different jobs that need two different people:

| The Production Grind (NOT your strength) | The Creative & Marketing Engine (YOUR strength) |
|---|---|
| Floorplan-accurate geometry | Brand narrative & positioning |
| Photoreal interiors, lighting, materials | Hero-shot art direction & creative brief |
| High volume of variations per environment | The teaser/launch films, story arc, VO |
| Fixing video-gamy / plastic-wood errors | Investor-facing visual story (Tattarang) |
| Iteration churn on technical notes | Campaign, social, GTM |

The grind is what Bear correctly called *"an internal full-time graphic designer job."* The right answer is **Wildernests resources that role** (Salman Alfarisi already exists as the 3D artist — expand his mandate, or add a dedicated production hire), and **you move up to Creative Director**: you set the brief, art-direct, own the films and the marketing, and approve the output — you don't execute the pixel grind.

That is the same $480k/year of value, pointed where it actually compounds for them.

---

## 4. The proposal to put on the table

Don't go in with grievances. Go in with a structure Alex can say yes to:

1. **Redefine the role to Creative Director + Marketing Lead.** You own brand, story, the films, hero-shot direction, investor visuals, GTM. You set the standard and approve — you stop being the hands on every variation.
2. **Wildernests owns the production pipeline.** Dedicated 3D/graphics capacity (Salman + 1, or a production hire) executes accuracy/volume to your brief. Daniel keeps QA'ing architectural accuracy against *their* pipeline, not yours.
3. **Lock the aesthetic spec once.** The single biggest waste is the spec changing every iteration. Propose a one-time locked visual bible (materials, lighting, furniture direction, accuracy tolerances), signed off by Alex + Daniel + Gunnar. After lock, changes are change-requests, not free reworks.
4. **Cadence over volume.** Agree a realistic deliverable rhythm (e.g. environments sequenced, not all five at once) so quality is achievable instead of "rolling the dice" each round.
5. **Amend Section 2 (Duties) to match.** The contract calls Duties *"adjustable"* — use that. Formally restate the deliverable as *creative direction + marketing + final films*, with technical production as a Wildernests-resourced function you direct. This protects the continuation condition without putting the whole grind on your back.

## 6. Tactics

- **Open warm, not aggrieved.** Alex is the one you trust and confide in — keep it that way. Lead with: *"I want this to work and I want to give you market-ready visuals. I've been thinking hard about how we actually get there, because the current setup won't."* Problem-solver, not complainer.
- **Lead with the structure, not your feelings.** Your candid message already landed the emotional point. The 1:1 is for the fix, not a repeat of the venting.
- **Concede the thing that costs you nothing:** you stay fully accountable for quality and standard. That neutralises the contract card and the "rolling the dice" fear in one move.
- **Hold the line on:** you stop being the high-volume executor; spec gets locked; production gets resourced.
- **Your BATNA is strong, use it quietly.** Continuation is conditioned on value delivered, but *you* are the one with the brand and the relationship; replacing you means replacing Bear Witness's name on the project. You don't need to say this — it's why you can be generous in the room.
- **Bear's backing is a reserve card, not an opener.** Bear said *"you've handled it beautifully... what they're asking is a full-time graphic designer job."* Don't wave it at Alex (reads as "my dad agrees with me"). Use the *substance* — "this is a department, not a person" — as your own line. Keep Bear in your back pocket.

---

## 7. The outcome to walk away with

A yes (or a strong "let's draft it") on:
1. **Your role = Creative Director + Marketing Lead.** You direct and approve; you don't grind.
2. **Wildernests resources the production pipeline** (Salman expanded or a hire).
3. **One locked aesthetic spec**, signed off, change-requests after.
4. **Realistic cadence**, environment by environment.
5. **Section 2 amendment** to formalise it.

If you can't get the hire today, get the **locked spec + cadence + role redefinition in principle** and make the resourcing the follow-up. The spec lock alone kills most of the churn.

---

## Bottom line

You're not asking to do less. You're asking to be pointed at the work that actually sells homes and raises money — and to stop being a one-person 3D department doing a job Bear correctly called a full-time graphic designer's. Frame it as solving Alex's market-ready problem, own the quality standard out loud, lock the spec, and move the grind to dedicated resource. Same equity, same money, vastly higher return for them and for you.

Go in as the person fixing it, not the person escaping it.

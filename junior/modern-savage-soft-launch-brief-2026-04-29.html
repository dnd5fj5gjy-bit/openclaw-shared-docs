What the Soft Launch Is For

The 170 people on the waitlist signed up before Modern Savage existed publicly. They've been waiting — some since April. They're not random buyers from an ad. They're people who said "I believe in this enough to give you my email address before there was anything to buy."

The soft launch is the payoff for that trust. It needs to feel different from the public launch on July 1. If the June 29 email is identical to July 1 — just "it's live, here's the link" — the 170 get nothing for having waited. That's a missed opportunity and a broken implicit promise.

The goal: make June 29 feel like being let in the back door before the restaurant opens. Real, quiet, meant for them specifically.

The Mechanics

Timing: June 29, 7:00 AM UK time (2:00 AM ET)

  • Sends before anyone wakes up in the UK — founding members check their phones and it's there
  • 48-hour window before July 1 public launch
  • If public announcement on July 1 is 10:00 AM UK, the founding window is actually ~51 hours

What founding members get that public buyers don't:

  • First access — they can subscribe before any public announcement
  • The founding member price (locked forever at their subscription rate — this was promised in Email 4)
  • A direct reply address — something that signals Bear actually sees these early messages

What public buyers get on July 1 that founding members already have:

  • Press coverage (Telegraph, Daily Mail, MindBodyGreen)
  • Bear's social announcement
  • The product page visible without any prior knowledge

Why This Matters for Retention

The founding 170 are the brand's first word-of-mouth network. They're also the highest churn risk if they feel like they were just early buyers, not founding members. The difference between a founding member experience and a normal first-purchase experience:

  • They got an email before anyone else
  • Bear told them their 170 orders matter more than any after
  • He gave them a direct reply address
  • They had 48 hours of silence before the world found out

That experience creates the kind of customer who tells people. Not because they were incentivised — because they feel like insiders. Supplement brands that create insider cultures (AG1, Momentous) have 60-70% retention at 12 months. Brands that don't: 40-50%.

Creative Intent
A two-minute teaser selling desire, not features. We follow one anonymous pioneer on horseback through a single landscape that morphs around him from prehistory to the present.
The thesis: for two hundred thousand years humans have been chained to resources, and now, for the first time, we are free of them. The home is the answer to a question civilisation has been asking since the first city was built.
Acts 1 & 4 are strict reality — dawn through to dusk on one day. Acts 2 & 3 are mythic, non-linear, operating on dream logic. We never see the rider's face.

Act 2
The Civilisations
0:25 – 1:05
As the rider passes, the ground comes alive. Earth rises into the forms of human civilisation — walls, towers, cities — all made of the same brown dirt. Each one builds, lives for seconds, then collapses back as he passes.

Strict rule: everything is the same brown earth. Not mudbrick. Not adobe. Dirt. Only the geometry changes.

Notes for the editing team
Make or break
The dust civilisations sequence determines everything. Run a quick test on Scene 5 (medieval town) before committing. If the dirt-rising-and-falling concept doesn't read clearly in motion, adapt now. If it lands, the rest follows easily.
Character consistency
Lock a single reference frame of the rider, dog, and horse and feed it into every generation. Most platforms accept reference frames. Plan for drift and absorb it through wider shots. The dog is small but emotionally important — a different dog appearing mid-film breaks the spell.
Music
Low cello opens and runs as a heartbeat throughout. Second cello joins in Act 2. Piano in Act 3 with the turn. Warm horn for the Act 4 reveal. Swell once when the rider crests the hill, then pull back to cellos and a single piano note. Commission original score if possible — it will outperform library on a film like this.

Boat Winter Lay-Up Checklist

La Vie En Rose · Oban, hauled out for winter · dehumidifier + thermostatic heater setup

The four things that actually cause winter damage: freezing water in the systems, trapped damp/condensation, growth left to harden on the hull, and chafe/water ingress from a poor cover. Everything below targets one of those.

Haul-out day (while she's still wet & slung)

  • Pressure-wash the hull immediately — growth scrubs off in minutes wet, sets like concrete once dry
  • Check hull for osmosis blisters & gelcoat damage while clean
  • Inspect anodes — replace anything more than half gone
  • Rock the prop shaft & rudder — check cutlass bearing and rudder bearing for play

Once chocked / cradled

  • Keel takes the weight; props & legs only steady her, not loading the hull
  • Sitting so rainwater drains off the deck & out the cockpit drains, not pooling
  • Open all seacocks so trapped water drains and can't freeze/crack them (opposite of afloat)
  • Cover: tarp/frame that sheds water & is lashed so it can't chafe the gelcoat. Shrink-wrap only with vents

Water systems (ashore = freezing is the main enemy)

  • Drain the fresh-water tank, run all taps dry
  • Drain the calorifier / water heater
  • Heads: flush through with fresh water, then non-toxic antifreeze; empty & treat the holding tank
  • Pump bilges dry and clean them
  • Antifreeze into any traps / low points

Engine (biggest single job)

  • Change oil & filter before layup (old oil's acids pit the engine over winter)
  • Check coolant antifreeze strength for a hard frost
  • Run antifreeze through the raw-water/seawater side, or drain it, so it can't crack the heat exchanger or pump
  • Pull the raw-water impeller out and store it (stops it taking a set)
  • Change fuel filters, fog the cylinders, grease the stern gland
  • Fill the diesel tank right up + dose with biocide/stabiliser (Marine 16 / Grotamar / Startron); drain any water sump first

Sails, canvas & rigging

  • Sails off, dried, stored ashore/home (no furling genoa left up all winter)
  • Sprayhood / bimini / cockpit cover washed, dried, secured or removed
  • Ease off halyards & running rigging; tie lines so nothing slats or chafes in the gales

Damp control (dehumidifier + heater)

Golden rule: open the boat right up inside, keep it closed-ish to the outside. The dehumidifier can only dry air it can reach.
  • Open every locker, cupboard, drawer, internal door; lift the sole/bilge boards
  • Stand cushions on edge (or take cushions & bedding home — best option). Never seal them in bags
  • Dehumidifier central, draining continuously to sink/bilge so it never fills & stops
  • Small thermostatic tube heater just taking the chill off / frost protection — NOT a fan heater left running
  • Small circulation fan(s) pushing dried air into dead spots (forepeak, under berths, aft lockers, heads)
  • Moisture-absorber tubs in sealed spots the dehumidifier can't reach
  • Solar vent (Nicro/Marinco) or Dorade as a fail-safe if shore power ever trips

Strip out & secure

  • Remove all food, bin anything perishable, wipe out lockers
  • Take home electronics, flares, lifejackets, sails, soft furnishings — damp & theft
  • Fridge/freezer emptied, cleaned, door propped open
  • Gas off at the bottle, ideally bottles removed
  • Batteries on a maintenance charger (shore power's there anyway) or taken home charged
  • Check every hatch, window & vent seals against rain

Admin

  • Insurance winter/layup terms met (some require sails off, seacocks positioned, etc.)
  • Yard/marina winter arrangement confirmed; note cradle position & who can look in on her
  • Fire extinguishers in date & accessible

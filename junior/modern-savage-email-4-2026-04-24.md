# Modern Savage — Email 4: The Launch Email
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Send timing:** On launch day (July 2026). After 3 nurture emails. This is the moment.
**Goal:** Convert. The reader has been through 3 emails of education and trust-building. No more nurturing — just the door, open.

---

## What Email 4 Must Do

Everything before this was setup. Email 4 is the only email in the sequence with one job: get the click.

The reader has invested time. They know the science. They know the sourcing. They've seen who else is waiting. They trust the brand. The only thing left is: make it easy to say yes.

Email 4 should be the shortest email in the sequence. Long copy at launch signals insecurity. Short copy signals confidence. The product sells itself at this point — if the first three emails worked.

**Founding member angle:** The first 48 hours should be for the waitlist only. Not a discount — an access window. "You're in first" is a more premium signal than "you're getting a deal."

## Implementation Notes

- **Zero fluff** — do not add another paragraph explaining the product. They know. Every sentence added is a conversion lost.
- **The link** — make it direct to the product page. Not the homepage. Not a landing page with another video. The product page with an add-to-cart button.
- **48-hour window** — this is not a discount. It's access priority. Premium positioning: the founding members got in first because they were paying attention. Everyone else gets it after.
- **No discount code** — if the brand wants to do a founding member benefit, make it something additive (free shipping, bonus add-on sample, handwritten note from Bear for first X orders) rather than a percentage off. Discounts at launch set the price anchor wrong.
- **Resend** — resend to non-openers after 24 hours with subject line variant: `48 hours. Then everyone else.`
- **Follow-up email (Email 5, optional):** "First 48 hours closes tonight." Urgency email. Keep it to 3 sentences.

Hi Ryan,

Good, that all reads well and I think we are close.

Taking the easy ones first. £45 works, and your reasoning on it is right, so let us go with that. Leading with the Ancestral Honey Balm makes sense too, and the travel tin is a better first product than the body cream. Dropship, the shared pack, you covering development and print, and the approach on Bear's name are all as agreed.

**The unit price.** This is the one thing I would like to keep as we had it, at 45% of what actually comes in rather than a fixed £20.63. Not because of the number, which is the same at full price, but because a fixed price only works if nothing ever sells for less than £45. The moment anything moves at a promotional price, whether that is a bundle, a code or a Black Friday, the whole of that comes out of our side while yours stays whole. A percentage keeps us aligned in both directions.

I understand the concern behind it, which is that we could discount hard and leave you funding it. That is fair, so let us take it off the table: we are happy to agree a floor, so your invoice price does not fall below an agreed figure ex VAT no matter what we sell at. You are protected either way and we are not exposed on our own promotions.

On the admin, you have offered a line-level report behind the weekly invoicing anyway, so applying the percentage to the line total should be one more column rather than a reconciliation exercise.

**Settlement.** Weekly against orders shipped is fine, and the reasoning makes sense. Refunds and credits to net off the following week's invoice so we are not chasing anything backwards.

**Refunds.** Agreed on the principle, and faults, wrong items and transit damage being yours is right. One adjustment on change-of-mind returns. As worded, we refund the customer in full and you keep the whole unit price on a product that never worked out, which puts the entire cost on us. Simplest fix is that you credit your unit price back less your actual cost of goods, so we each carry our own part of it. And agreed there is no sense shipping opened skincare back and forth.

**Royalty.** Your definition works for us, so 10% on gross receipts less VAT, refunds and any discount actually given. One point of detail: where the product sits inside a bundle, the discount to be allocated across the items pro rata by their normal prices, so the calculation is not distorted by how a bundle is put together.

**Promotions.** One thing to spell out while we are testing the water. The co-branded product sits outside blanket promotional campaigns, affiliate programmes and discount feeds unless we agree it first. That is the same approval as we discussed on Bear's name rather than anything new, and it is not aimed at your ordinary advertising, which we are glad to see running.

**Stockists.** Happy for you to offer the range to your existing stockists, approved case by case as you suggested, with the royalty applying as normal.

**Your insert.** Yes to that, and we will do the same on our side for the co-branded product. Brand story rather than an offer, so neither of us is pulling the other's customers across.

**UK first.** Understood on the fulfilment. Modern Savage is a US company so the US will come up before long. Nothing needed now, just a line saying we can bring our own fulfilment there or agree terms in good faith when we get to it.

**A few things neither of us has covered yet.** How long this runs for, the notice each side gives, and what happens to the artwork, listings and any remaining stock if it ends. Whether either of us is exclusive to the other in this category. Who is named as Responsible Person on the safety file for the co-branded pack. And confirmation from your 3PL that connecting both stores actually works, since that is the whole mechanism.

If you are happy with the pricing point and the refund adjustment, I think we are there and we can get it drawn up.

Best,
Jesse

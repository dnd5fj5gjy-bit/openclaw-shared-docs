The FOX / Bear Creator Network is a media product FOX is building around Bear's name and audience. Benjamin Simon (EBG/Everest) is claiming EBG should receive a revenue participation from whatever FOX generates from this product — potentially $35-81M in annual gross media value (his own model puts the likely figure at ~$50M).

## The Weakness in EBG's Argument

**1. Investor deck slides ≠ contract.**
Benjamin cited slides 26-27 of the Everest investor presentation as establishing that EBG is entitled to this revenue. Investor materials are prospectuses — they describe what the fund hopes to participate in. They are not signed agreements. What matters legally is what the EBG_BWM_Consulting_Agreement_Final (signed April 3) says. Not what a sales deck says.

**2. EBG does not "control" Bear's NIL.**
Benjamin's legal theory rests on EBG being "the rights holder controlling that commercial input" (Bear's NIL). But Bear Grylls controls Bear's NIL. Bear always has. EBG helped monetize it — through consulting services, for which they receive $25K/month. Helping monetize an asset is not the same as controlling it.

**3. CJ's key distinction is legally strong.**
CJ draws the right line: the Everest Fund may legitimately participate in revenue from content it specifically funds and produces for the Creator Network. That is how any production house/investor relationship works — you invest in specific productions, you share in that production's revenue. You do not get a blanket percentage of all media revenue generated by the talent you once worked with.

**4. The physio analogy (Bear's own words).**
Bear said it best: "Should we pay my physio as tbh without her we wouldn't have running wild?" Many people and organizations have contributed to Bear's career. Causal contribution does not create a contractual entitlement to downstream revenue.

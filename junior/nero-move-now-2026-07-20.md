## 2. You are right that it is not good enough, and here is the arithmetic

Not an opinion, three specific holes:

**The equity is the smallest real number in the deal.** 5% at $12M is ~£377k of paper, granted for services, in ordinary shares. Any future preference stack wipes it before it pays. Meanwhile the royalty models at roughly **£600k over three years** on the same assumptions. The headline everyone anchors on is the least valuable part.

**The royalty base is undefined.** "10% of net revenue" with no definition is the identical trap to the Water2 "net profit" argument you are already in. The same 10% pays out anywhere between **£170k and £20k a year** depending purely on what gets deducted before the calculation. The rate is not the negotiation. The definition is.

**The whitelisting as drafted is a pure giveaway.** Full account-level access to Bear's handle for the term. Meta's own study across 300 brands found per-post partnership ads *outperform* account-level allowlisting by around 3.9% on cost per purchase. So Nero gains nothing operationally. The only thing account-level access actually buys is the ability to originate claims in Bear's name that Bear never sees, on a brand publicly described as manosphere-adjacent, with Bear named in any eventual ASA ruling.

Add the missing pieces: no marketing covenant, no minimum guarantee, no anti-dilution, no information rights, and vesting tied to the phrase "continued partnership," which is a unilateral option for Nero written to look mutual.

## 3. The thing that decides the deal is still not a commercial term

This has not changed and it should not get lost in a conversation about percentages: **Nero's growth engine is testosterone-anxiety advertising.** The live landing page titled "7 Reasons Your Gym Shorts Are Killing Your Testosterone," the Business of Fashion piece from February, Tyler on the record saying hyper-masculine posts "fare well algorithmically."

That is the one register Bear cannot be near, and it is also the engine that built the company. So the covenant is not a term inside the deal. It is whether there is a deal.

**Which means it must not be traded against the economics.** If we counter on percentage and covenant in the same breath, Tyler will trade one for the other: he drops the hormone angle and then cannot move on equity. The covenant has to be positioned as a condition of doing anything at all, with the money discussion explicitly downstream of it.

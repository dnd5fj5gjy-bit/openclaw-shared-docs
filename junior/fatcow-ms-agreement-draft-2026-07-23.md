## PART A — THE DEAL

### 1. Definitions

**"Product"** means the co-branded Ancestral Honey Balm described in Schedule 1, and any further product added by written agreement under clause 2.3.

**"Territory"** means the United Kingdom.

**"MS Channel"** means Modern Savage's own websites and direct-to-consumer channels.

**"FC Channel"** means Fat Cow's own websites, direct-to-consumer channels and Approved Stockists.

**"Net Receipts"** means, in respect of any sale of the Product through the MS Channel, the amount actually received from the customer for the Product, less VAT, less any discount actually given, and less refunds. No other deduction is made: not marketing, affiliate commission, platform or payment-processing fees, shipping recovered from the customer, or overhead.

**"Net Sales"** means, in respect of any sale of the Product through the FC Channel, gross receipts for the Product less VAT, less refunds, and less any discount actually given. No other deduction is made.

**"Bundle"** means any offer in which the Product is sold together with one or more other items at a combined price. Where the Product is sold in a Bundle, the discount is allocated across the items in the Bundle **pro rata by their normal individual prices**, and the amount allocated to the Product is used for Net Receipts and Net Sales.

**"RRP"** means £45.00 including VAT, or such other price as the Parties agree in writing.

**"Cancellation Period"** means the statutory cancellation period applying to the sale under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.

**"Bear Grylls IP"** means the name, image, likeness, signature, voice and endorsement of Bear Grylls.

**"Approved Stockist"** means a retail stockist of Fat Cow approved under clause 8.

### 2. The Product

2.1 The Product is the Ancestral Honey Balm specified in Schedule 1: a 90g tin, under 100ml, honey and propolis formulation, in the co-branded pack described in clause 4.

2.2 Fat Cow is responsible for formulation, manufacture, safety assessment, regulatory compliance, stock and fulfilment. Modern Savage is responsible for listing, pricing and marketing the Product on the MS Channel.

2.3 The Parties anticipate adding a second product (working title "Savage Cream") in a later phase. No further product is included in this Agreement unless added by written agreement signed by both Parties, and this Agreement applies to any product so added.

### 3. Territory and channels

3.1 The Product is offered for sale in the Territory only. Neither Party will list or ship the Product outside the Territory without the other's prior written agreement.

3.2 If Modern Savage wishes to offer the Product outside the Territory, the Parties will discuss in good faith terms for that market, including the possibility of Modern Savage using its own fulfilment. Neither Party is obliged to agree, and nothing in this clause restricts either Party's business outside the Territory generally.

> **Drafting note.** Modern Savage is a US company. Ryan agreed UK-only because he has no international fulfilment. This clause keeps the US door open without committing him to anything.

### 4. Branding and packaging

4.1 There is **one pack**, used for every unit of the Product sold through either channel.

4.2 The front of the pack carries **"Modern Savage x Fat Cow"**, and below it the credit line **"Created in partnership with Bear Grylls and his family"**.

4.3 Fat Cow bears the cost of pack design development and print. The Parties will develop the artwork together and neither may put the Product on sale until both have approved the final artwork in writing.

4.4 Neither Party may alter the approved artwork, or produce any variant pack, sleeve, label or outer, without the other's prior written approval.

4.5 Ryan Porter has confirmed he does not require his own name on the pack. If he later wishes it added, Modern Savage will not unreasonably withhold approval.

### 5. Bear Grylls name and likeness

> This clause is load-bearing. It is the reason the deal is structured the way it is.

5.1 **The pack credit is the whole of the agreed use.** Modern Savage grants Fat Cow permission to reproduce the credit line in clause 4.2 on the Product pack, and nothing further.

5.2 **No other use is permitted without prior written approval, given case by case.** This includes, without limitation: advertising, paid social, organic social, user-generated content, product listings, landing and web pages, email to Fat Cow's list, affiliate materials, PR, packaging inserts, trade and stockist materials, and any use by any third party including Fat Cow's agencies, affiliates and Approved Stockists.

5.3 Fat Cow will submit each proposed use to Modern Savage in advance, in writing, with the actual creative or copy. Modern Savage will respond promptly. Modern Savage may withhold approval **for any reason or none**, and no reason need be given.

5.4 **Approval of one use is not approval of any other use**, of any variation of it, or of any later reuse. Approvals are specific to the creative, placement, channel and period approved.

5.5 **Nothing in this Agreement grants any general, implied, residual or continuing licence over Bear Grylls IP.** No course of dealing, and no approval given under clause 5.3, creates one. Fat Cow may not sub-licence Bear Grylls IP to anyone.

5.6 Modern Savage may withdraw any approval on written notice, in which case Fat Cow will take the relevant material down as soon as reasonably practicable and in any event within five business days, save that Fat Cow is not required to recall packs already printed or units already shipped.

5.7 Fat Cow will not register, apply for or assert any right in any name, mark or domain incorporating or resembling Bear Grylls IP or the Modern Savage marks.

5.8 On expiry or termination of this Agreement all use of Bear Grylls IP by Fat Cow ceases, subject only to the sell-off in clause 17.

5.9 Fat Cow acknowledges that Modern Savage's rights in Bear Grylls IP derive from a third party and that damages would not be an adequate remedy for breach of this clause, so Modern Savage may seek injunctive relief.

### 6. MS Channel — dropship

6.1 Modern Savage lists the Product on the MS Channel. Orders route through to Fat Cow's fulfilment provider, which picks, packs and ships direct to the customer under Modern Savage's brand experience.

6.2 There is **no minimum order quantity and no minimum commitment** in either direction.

6.3 **Modern Savage retains 45% of Net Receipts. Fat Cow invoices 55% of Net Receipts.** At the RRP of £45 including VAT (£37.50 excluding VAT) this is £16.88 to Modern Savage and £20.63 to Fat Cow per unit.

6.4 Modern Savage sets its own retail and promotional pricing for the MS Channel and is free to price and promote as it sees fit. Because both Parties' shares are a percentage of what is actually received, any discount is borne by both Parties in the same proportion.

6.5 Fat Cow will despatch orders within one business day of receipt (excluding weekends and public holidays), on a tracked service, and will notify Modern Savage at least five business days in advance of any anticipated stockout or fulfilment interruption.

6.6 Fat Cow will maintain the connection between its fulfilment provider and the MS Channel store at its own cost, and confirms its 3PL can support both stores connected.

6.7 Modern Savage may include an insert for the Product in Fat Cow's shipments under clause 9.2.

### 7. FC Channel — royalty

7.1 Fat Cow may sell the Product through the FC Channel. Fat Cow sets its own retail price for that channel.

7.2 Fat Cow will pay Modern Savage a royalty of **10% of Net Sales** through the FC Channel.

7.3 The royalty is reconciled and paid **quarterly in arrears**, within 30 days of each quarter end, with a statement showing units sold, gross receipts, VAT, discounts actually given, refunds, Net Sales and the royalty due.

7.4 The royalty applies equally to sales to Approved Stockists, calculated on the amounts actually received by Fat Cow from the stockist.

### 8. Stockists

8.1 Fat Cow may offer the Product to its existing retail stockists, subject to Modern Savage's prior written approval **for each stockist individually**.

8.2 Modern Savage may withhold approval for any reason, including brand fit. Approval of one stockist is not approval of any other.

8.3 The royalty in clause 7.2 applies to stockist sales as normal.

8.4 Fat Cow will not permit an Approved Stockist to use Bear Grylls IP beyond the pack itself without approval under clause 5.

### 9. Inserts and promotions

9.1 Fat Cow may include a Fat Cow insert in Product shipments made through the MS Channel. The insert may carry Fat Cow's brand story and product information. It may **not** carry a discount code, offer, competition, sign-up incentive or any other call to action directing the customer to purchase from the FC Channel. Insert artwork requires Modern Savage's prior written approval.

9.2 Modern Savage has the reciprocal right to include an insert on the same terms in Product shipments made through the FC Channel.

9.3 Neither Party will enter the Product into a blanket promotional campaign, affiliate feed or discount aggregator operated by a third party without telling the other in advance.

> **Drafting note.** 9.3 is softer than the version drafted on 22 July, which required approval. Jesse cut that from the email and Ryan has not agreed to it, so it is drafted here as notification rather than consent. Flag if you want it stronger.

### 10. Settlement

10.1 Fat Cow invoices **weekly, against orders shipped**, with a line-level report showing for each order: order reference, units, gross received, VAT, discount actually given, Net Receipts and the 55% due.

10.2 Each invoice is payable **within 14 days of the end of the Cancellation Period** for the orders in it, so that Modern Savage is not settling on orders that may yet be cancelled.

> **Drafting note.** This is Jesse's suggestion in the 23 July email ("maybe it's easier if you don't pay us any money until it's past the refund possible time"), which Ryan accepted along with everything else. It is drafted as a delay to settlement rather than a holdback because it is simpler to operate. Practical effect is roughly weekly invoicing settled on about a four-week lag. Worth confirming Ryan understood it that way, since he is funding production up front and said cash timing "matters most" to him. If he pushes back, the alternative is pay on 14-day terms and net refunds off the following invoice under 10.3.

10.3 Refunds, credits and adjustments are netted off the following week's invoice. If credits exceed the invoice value, the balance carries forward, and any balance outstanding on termination is settled within 30 days.

10.4 All amounts are in GBP and exclusive of VAT, which is added where properly chargeable.

10.5 Each Party will keep accurate records of Product sales for the term and 12 months after, and will give the other reasonable access to them, on reasonable notice and not more than twice a year, to verify the amounts due under clauses 6 and 7.

### 11. Refunds, faults and returns

11.1 **Whoever takes the customer's money handles the refund.** Modern Savage handles refunds for MS Channel orders; Fat Cow handles refunds for FC Channel orders.

11.2 Opened skincare cannot be resold, so returns are not shipped back to Fat Cow unless Fat Cow asks for a specific unit.

11.3 Where a refund arises from a **product fault, wrong item shipped, or transit damage**, the cost is Fat Cow's regardless of which channel took the order. Fat Cow will credit Modern Savage in full for the amount refunded to the customer, including outbound shipping, and will replace the unit at its own cost if the customer wants a replacement.

11.4 Where a refund arises from **customer change of mind** on an MS Channel order, the refund reduces Net Receipts for that order to nil and Fat Cow's 55% share falls away with it, in the same way as any other adjustment under clause 10.3.

> **Drafting note.** 11.4 is the practical effect of settling after the Cancellation Period under 10.2, and it gets Modern Savage to the same place as the cost-of-goods split proposed on 22 July, which Jesse dropped from the sent email. If clause 10.2 is renegotiated, this clause needs revisiting with it, otherwise Modern Savage carries the whole cost of change-of-mind returns.

## PART B — COMPLIANCE, RISK AND CONTROL

> None of Part B was raised in the email thread. It is standard for a product like this and it is where the actual exposure sits. Expect Ryan to accept most of it without argument.

### 12. Manufacture and quality

12.1 Fat Cow will disclose to Modern Savage the identity and location of the manufacturer of the Product before launch, and will notify Modern Savage before changing manufacturer.

12.2 Fat Cow warrants that the Product is manufactured in accordance with Good Manufacturing Practice for cosmetics (BS EN ISO 22716), is of satisfactory quality, is fit for purpose, conforms to the agreed specification in Schedule 1, and is free from defect.

12.3 Fat Cow will provide, before launch and on request thereafter: the Product specification, the full ingredient list and INCI declaration, batch documentation, and the substantiation for every claim made on the pack.

### 13. Regulatory

13.1 Fat Cow is the **responsible person** for the Product for the purposes of the UK Cosmetics Regulation, and confirms that before the Product is placed on the market it will have:

(a) a Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR) completed by a qualified safety assessor covering the co-branded Product as sold;
(b) notification on the UK Submit Cosmetic Product Notification (SCPN) portal;
(c) a Product Information File maintained and available to the OPSS;
(d) labelling compliant with the Cosmetics Regulation, including ingredient declaration, PAO, batch code and responsible person address.

13.2 Fat Cow will make no medicinal claim for the Product and will ensure all claims comply with the CAP Code and the Cosmetic Products Claims Regulation. Any claim referencing Bear Grylls or Modern Savage requires approval under clause 5 as well.

13.3 If any authority raises a concern about the Product, Fat Cow will notify Modern Savage within one business day.

> **Drafting note.** 13.1 names Fat Cow as Responsible Person. That is the right answer: they formulate, manufacture and hold the safety file, and Modern Savage should not take on that liability for a product it does not make. It was flagged as an open question in the 22 July draft and never answered, so confirm it with Ryan rather than assume.

### 14. Insurance and recall

14.1 Fat Cow will maintain product liability insurance of not less than £5 million per claim, and product recall cover, for the term and six years after, and will provide a certificate on signature and on each renewal.

14.2 Modern Savage's interest is to be noted on the policy.

14.3 If either Party reasonably believes the Product is unsafe or non-compliant, it may suspend sales immediately on notice. Fat Cow will lead any recall or withdrawal and bears the cost of it, including the cost of customer refunds and communications on both channels.

### 15. Customer data

15.1 Each Party is the controller of the customer data generated by its own channel. Neither Party acquires any right to market to the other's customers.

15.2 In fulfilling MS Channel orders, Fat Cow and its fulfilment provider act as **processor** for Modern Savage. Fat Cow will process that data only to fulfil the order, will not use it for its own marketing or add it to its lists, will keep it secure, will flow these obligations down to its fulfilment provider, and will delete or return it on termination.

15.3 The Parties will enter the processor terms required by Article 28 UK GDPR before launch.

> **Drafting note.** This matters more than it looks. Dropship means Fat Cow's 3PL receives the name, address and order history of every Modern Savage customer. Without 15.2 there is nothing stopping those customers appearing on a Fat Cow list, which is exactly the leak the insert restriction in clause 9.1 is designed to prevent.

### 16. Exclusivity

16.1 During the term, within the Territory, Fat Cow will not produce or supply a co-branded skincare product with any brand or individual whose primary positioning is ancestral, primal or organ-based health and nutrition, without Modern Savage's prior written approval.

16.2 Nothing in this Agreement restricts Fat Cow's own-brand range, or Modern Savage's supplement business.

16.3 Modern Savage will not launch a competing tallow-based skincare product in the Territory during the term.

> **Drafting note.** Narrow on purpose. Neither side raised exclusivity, so a wide clause invites a negotiation you do not need. This version stops the obvious harm, which is the same product with a different partner's name on it.

## PART C — TERM, EXIT AND GENERAL

### 17. Term and termination

17.1 This Agreement starts on the date of signature and runs for **24 months from the date the Product first goes on sale**, then continues until terminated on **90 days' written notice** by either Party.

17.2 Either Party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other:

(a) is in material breach and, where the breach is capable of remedy, has not remedied it within 30 days of written notice;
(b) becomes insolvent, enters administration, has a receiver appointed or ceases to trade.

17.3 Modern Savage may terminate immediately on written notice if, in its reasonable opinion, continued association with Fat Cow or the Product would damage the reputation of Modern Savage or of Bear Grylls, including on any breach of clause 5.

17.4 On expiry or termination:

(a) both Parties stop taking orders for the Product, save that Fat Cow may sell through printed pack stock and finished units already manufactured for a period of 90 days, subject to Modern Savage's written approval of the quantity and with the royalty in clause 7.2 continuing to apply;
(b) after that period, remaining co-branded packaging is destroyed and Fat Cow confirms destruction in writing;
(c) both Parties remove the Product from their listings and all use of the other's marks and of Bear Grylls IP ceases;
(d) Fat Cow ships any orders already accepted, and outstanding amounts under clauses 6, 7 and 10 are settled within 30 days;
(e) Modern Savage may require Fat Cow to transfer or take down any co-branded landing page, listing or asset carrying Bear Grylls IP;
(f) clauses 5.7, 5.8, 10.5, 14, 15, 18, 19, 20 and 21 survive.

17.5 Neither Party owes the other compensation, indemnity or goodwill payment on termination beyond the amounts due under 17.4(d).

### 18. Intellectual property

18.1 Each Party retains ownership of its own brand, marks and existing intellectual property. Nothing transfers.

18.2 Each Party grants the other a non-exclusive, royalty-free, non-transferable licence to use its marks in the Territory for the term, solely to make, market and sell the Product under this Agreement.

18.3 Fat Cow owns the formulation. Modern Savage acquires no right in it.

18.4 The co-branded artwork developed under clause 4 may be used by each Party only for the Product and only during the term. Fat Cow will provide Modern Savage with the final print-ready files.

18.5 Bear Grylls IP is dealt with exclusively by clause 5 and this clause 18 does not widen it.

### 19. Warranties, indemnities and liability

19.1 Each Party warrants it has the right and authority to enter this Agreement and to grant the rights it grants.

19.2 Fat Cow indemnifies Modern Savage against all claims, losses, damages, costs and expenses arising from: personal injury or damage caused by the Product; any regulatory breach concerning the Product; any defect in the Product; any breach of clause 5; and any claim that the formulation or Fat Cow's own marks infringe a third party's rights.

19.3 Modern Savage indemnifies Fat Cow against claims that the Modern Savage marks, as supplied and used in accordance with this Agreement, infringe a third party's rights.

19.4 Neither Party excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or anything else that cannot be excluded at law.

19.5 Subject to 19.2 and 19.4, neither Party is liable for loss of profit, revenue, goodwill or anticipated saving, and each Party's total liability in any 12-month period is capped at the greater of £250,000 and the total amounts paid or payable between the Parties under this Agreement in that period.

### 20. Confidentiality and announcements

20.1 Each Party will keep the other's confidential information, including the commercial terms of this Agreement, confidential, and will not disclose it except to its professional advisers and to those of its personnel who need to know.

20.2 Neither Party will make any public announcement about this Agreement or the Product without the other's prior written approval of the wording and timing. Any announcement referencing Bear Grylls also requires approval under clause 5.

### 21. General

21.1 **No partnership.** Nothing creates a partnership, joint venture, agency or employment relationship. Neither Party may bind the other.

21.2 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign or transfer this Agreement without the other's written consent, which is not to be unreasonably withheld, save that either may assign to a group company on notice.

21.3 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter and supersedes all prior correspondence, including the emails of 20 to 23 July 2026.

21.4 **Variation.** Any variation must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

21.5 **Notices.** Notices are given by email to Jesse Grylls (Jesse@beargryllsventures.com) for Modern Savage and to Ryan Porter (ryan@fatcowskin.com) for Fat Cow, with a copy by post to the registered office.

21.6 **Force majeure.** Neither Party is liable for failure to perform caused by an event beyond its reasonable control, provided it notifies the other promptly and mitigates.

21.7 **Third party rights.** No one other than the Parties may enforce this Agreement, save that Bear Grylls and any owner of Bear Grylls IP may enforce clause 5.

21.8 **Governing law and jurisdiction.** This Agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales, and the courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.

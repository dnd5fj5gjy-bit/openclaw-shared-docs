The Opportunity

This is not a typical content deal

The Chosen is the most-watched independently distributed TV series in history - 200M+ views, distributed in 147 countries, with a fan base that organises community screenings and shares content with religious intensity.

Bear's public faith has always been authentic and prominent in his books and content. This collaboration is the first time that identity crosses into the largest faith-based entertainment property in the world. For Bear's audience, this lands as cultural validation. For The Chosen audience, Bear is an exciting new entry point.

Jesse leads all social media for this campaign. Del Shoopman (The Natural Studios) introduced Jesse to KB Britt (5&2 Studios) and the broader CITW team on May 21. Bear's virtual appearance is locked for July 9.

The Chosen audience

Evangelical and mainstream Christian, family-oriented, 25-55, US-primary but global. Hyper-engaged - binge-and-share culture, community events, extreme emotional investment. These are people who cry at trailer announcements.

Bear's audience crossover

Bear's Christian faith is well-established (books, social, personal brand). His survival identity maps directly onto the "tested by wilderness" themes of CITW. Both audiences celebrate faith, family, resilience, and purpose-driven challenge.

5&2 Studios

Amazon

Phase 1 — Pre-Announcement

Warming the audience without revealing anything

The strongest launches start with curiosity. Bear should begin planting seeds 4-6 weeks before July 9 - not about CITW specifically, but about the emotional territory it occupies.

Week of June 1

Bear posts something cryptic. A wilderness shot with a caption that hints at "something I've never done before." No specifics. Could reference faith and challenge together. Objective: create intrigue in the comment section.

Week of June 15

Normal adventure content. Keep the feed warm. This is the quiet before the storm - don't over-tease. If Bear has any Running Wild S9 content still dropping, that keeps momentum without burning the CITW reveal early.

Week of July 1

"Watch this space." One post. One story. A still image - something from CITW filming if Amazon allows pre-embargo - or something thematic. "July 9. Something I'm genuinely proud of." Caption: nothing else.

"Never sell the product. Educate people on the problems. Position the brand as the solution. Seek gratitude, not transactions."

Junior — Mission-Based Social Strategy Framework

Apply this to CITW: The "problem" is disconnection - from nature, from meaning, from faith. Bear embodies the solution. The content arc should lead people towards that realisation before the trailer ever lands.

Phase 4 — July 10 to August 8

Four weeks to binge drop

The window between trailer drop and binge drop is the highest-value engagement period. The Chosen audience will be hungry for more content. Bear should show up weekly, not daily.

Week of July 14

Personal depth post

Bear writes a longer-than-usual caption about what faith in the wilderness means to him personally. No product push. No show promotion. Just authentic voice. This is the post that The Chosen fanbase will screenshot and share. Think: storytelling that exists beyond the series.

Week of July 21

Bridge post

Bear acknowledges The Chosen as a viewer. Something like: "I've been watching The Chosen with my family. If you haven't started it - start now. Before August 9." This bridges his audience to the show organically. It's genuine, not promotional.

Week of July 28

Countdown asset

Clean graphic from Amazon/5&2: "12 days. August 9. @amazonprimevideo." Bear posts it in stories. Optionally in feed if Amazon supplies polished key art. Straightforward - this is information, not emotion. The emotion came earlier.

Aug 4-8

Story countdown

Story countdown sticker 5 days out. Bear is visible but not over-promoting. One brief story each day if possible: a wilderness quote, a faith reflection, a clip teaser. Build expectation without exhausting the audience's attention.

August 9 — Binge Drop

Drop day execution

Go live before 8am UK (3am US East). The US audience wakes up to Bear promoting. The Chosen audience is already primed. Amazon has push notifications going out. The compounding effect is the goal.

Pre-6am UK

Go live on all platforms simultaneously

IG feed post + IG story + TikTok. YouTube if a full promo cut has been prepared. Same time as The Chosen's posts and Amazon's posts - coordinate with KB Britt on exact timing.

Throughout Aug 9

React to the fan response

Bear shares 2-3 fan comments or screenshots to his story. Shows authenticity - he's watching the reaction. The Chosen audience is intensely loyal and will screenshot Bear sharing their comments. Creates secondary viral moment.

Aug 10-14

Post-drop engagement window

One story responding to fan reactions. If possible, Bear records a brief thank-you message to The Chosen community. Don't over-push - let the show speak for itself at this point.

July 9 — Virtual Appearance

Talking points for Bear

Bear's virtual appearance (PM UK time = morning US East) is the emotional centrepiece of the launch. KB Britt / 5&2 Studios will provide the format. These are the key narratives Bear should draw on.

Why this project matters personally

Bear's faith has always been the foundation of his outdoor identity. In the wild, stripped of distraction, he has consistently spoken about encountering God. This is not a new message for Bear - CITW is the fullest expression of it yet. He should say that.

What the wilderness teaches

"The wild is honest. It doesn't care about your reputation, your money, or your status. It cares whether you're present, humble, and genuine. That's exactly the kind of man Jesus was." This is the natural bridge between Bear's identity and The Chosen's narrative.

Message to The Chosen audience

Acknowledge that they know their faith deeply. Bear isn't teaching them. He's bringing a new lens - wilderness as a spiritual space, survival as a metaphor for faith. The tone should be one fan speaking to other fans, not a celebrity endorsement.

Call to action

Watch with family. Share it. The Chosen community is built on collective experience - community screenings, group watches, social sharing. Bear should explicitly invite group viewing. "Don't watch this alone. Watch it together." That message will resonate exactly.

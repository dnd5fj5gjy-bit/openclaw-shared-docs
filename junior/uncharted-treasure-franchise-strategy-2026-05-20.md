# Uncharted Treasure — Franchise Strategy
*Bear Grylls x FCS / TNS — May 2026*

---

## The Core Idea

Uncharted Treasure is a multi-platform adventure franchise built on one enduring premise: **the world's most dangerous places are hiding the world's most extraordinary gifts.** Every expedition ends with a discovery — a landscape, a people, a piece of knowledge, a survival insight — that the audience carries with them long after the credits roll.

This franchise belongs to Bear Grylls. It doesn't depend on any single co-creator, channel partner, or platform deal to survive. It is the IP. The digital series feeds the TV series. The TV series feeds the social. The social keeps the audience engaged and hungry for the next expedition. That flywheel runs indefinitely.

---

## Why "Uncharted Treasure" Stands Alone

The existing FCS deck rightly references Coyote Peterson and Brave Wilderness as a comparison benchmark. Coyote built a category-defining wildlife channel — that's real. But **tethering the Bear Grylls franchise to Coyote's brand is structurally wrong for three reasons:**

1. **Dependency risk.** If Coyote steps back, restructures, or pivots, Bear's franchise loses a key pillar. Uncharted Treasure should be able to stand in any room without needing a co-signer.

2. **Brand ceiling.** Coyote's audience is wildlife-specific and younger demographic. Bear's franchise has a broader mandate — survival, adventure, human endurance, discovery — and a legacy audience that spans demographics. Attaching to a niche channel undersells the franchise potential.

3. **Franchise equity.** Every collab that puts Coyote's name on Bear's content builds Coyote's IP. Uncharted Treasure should be building its own IP. Guests and collabs are welcome — they serve the franchise, not the other way around.

**The right model is National Geographic, not MrBeast collab culture.** NatGeo built a franchise that outlasted every presenter. The presenter became famous through the franchise, not the franchise famous through the presenter. That's the goal.

## Three Franchise Pillars

### 1. Digital Series — "Uncharted Treasure: Expeditions"
*The engine. Always on. Always feeding the machine.*

- **Format:** 12-20 minute episodes, shot in cinematic style, released on YouTube and Bear's owned digital platforms
- **Cadence:** 1 major expedition per quarter (4 per year minimum), each split across 3-4 episodes
- **Concept:** Each expedition is a genuine Bear Grylls adventure — real risk, real environment, real discovery. The "treasure" at the end is defined broadly: a landscape, a skill, a culture, a survival technique, a truth about human nature
- **Jesse's role:** Episode 1 is already filmed. Jesse can front the digital series, building his own following as the next generation of this world
- **Guest appearances:** Bear appears in select episodes (not every one — protect his bandwidth, maximise impact)
- **Collab strategy:** Other creators can guest as "co-expeditioners" on specific episodes. They serve the Uncharted Treasure story. The franchise absorbs their audience, not the reverse

### 2. TV Series — "Uncharted Treasure" (Broadcast/Streaming)
*The prestige layer. The thing that makes the franchise permanent.*

- **Format:** 6-8 episode season, cinematic production quality, streaming or broadcast
- **Distinction from digital:** The TV series goes deeper — longer arcs, higher stakes expeditions, more narrative complexity, bigger crews, harder locations
- **Relationship to digital:** The digital series is the funnel. Audiences who find Bear on YouTube graduate to the TV series. The TV series validates the franchise for brand partners and distributors
- **The FCS angle:** This is where the Fox relationship becomes most valuable. FCS has distribution relationships that can place a premium Uncharted Treasure series on FOX, Hulu, or a streaming partner. Running Wild is the proof of concept — this scales it
- **Storyline structure:** Each TV season follows one macro expedition with multiple chapters. Think Ken Burns meets Bear Grylls — the land and the human story woven together
- **Production note:** Separate production identity from Running Wild. Uncharted Treasure should feel distinct — its own title card, its own visual language, its own music. This is a new IP, not Running Wild Season 11

### 3. Social Content — Always-On Filler
*The glue. The thing that keeps the franchise alive between releases.*

This is the most underbuilt part of any TV/digital franchise strategy, and it's where Uncharted Treasure can genuinely win on social.

**Three categories of social filler:**

**a) Behind the Expedition**
- Unscripted moments from each expedition — prep, travel, kit checks, near-misses, crew life
- Shot on phone or handheld, raw and real
- Posted on Bear and Jesse's handles across Reels, TikTok, Shorts
- No production overhead — this is the footage that already exists from filming the digital series

**b) Franchise Lore**
- "How to survive..." clips extracted from expedition footage
- "What we found" reveal moments
- Historical/geographical context around expedition locations
- These are evergreen — can be posted anytime, forever

**c) Community Challenges**
- Audience sent on their own "local uncharted treasure" challenges
- Best submissions featured on the franchise's social channels
- Builds UGC layer around the franchise without Bear needing to be present

**Posting cadence:** Minimum 4-5 pieces of social content per week, always in season or out. The audience should never feel like the franchise has gone quiet.

## The Franchise Arc — 3-Year Build

**Year 1 (2026):** Establish the digital series. Jesse fronts it. Bear appears in 2-3 episodes. 4 expeditions released. Social runs in parallel with every release. Zero dependency on Coyote or any external channel partner.

**Year 2 (2027):** TV series enters production. The digital series has built the audience case. A streaming or broadcast partner picks up Uncharted Treasure Season 1 on the strength of Bear's existing TV track record (Running Wild) plus the digital series data. Social continues. Franchise is now cross-platform.

**Year 3 (2028):** Uncharted Treasure is a multi-season IP. Bear has an equity stake in a franchise that survives him. Jesse is established as the next generation. The social channels have an always-on life of their own. The franchise licenses gear, survival products, brand partnerships — independently, not through third-party channels.

## What This Changes in the FCS Deck

The current deck positions Coyote as a channel pillar. The revised framing:

- **Remove:** Coyote as a featured partner in the franchise architecture
- **Keep:** Coyote as a potential *guest appearance* on one episode (this is fine — it serves the Uncharted Treasure story)
- **Rename:** Working title "Expedition" becomes "Uncharted Treasure"
- **Add:** The TV Series pillar as the prestige layer that justifies FCS's involvement
- **Add:** The always-on social content strategy as the third pillar (not an afterthought)
- **Reframe:** The franchise is Bear's IP. FCS/TNS are production and distribution partners, not co-owners of the brand

## Tone and Identity

Uncharted Treasure should feel like: *what if Bear Grylls made a franchise that was actually designed to outlast him?*

- Cinematic but accessible
- Dangerous but purposeful — every risk serves a discovery
- Spiritual without being religious — the wild as a place of revelation
- Generational — Bear and Jesse, now and next

The word "treasure" does real work here. It promises the audience something worth having at the end. That's the contract every episode makes with the viewer.

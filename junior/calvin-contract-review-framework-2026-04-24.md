## EQUITY BENCHMARKS — WHAT'S FAIR

### Industry context (pre-revenue DTC supplement brand)

| Role | Typical % | Vesting |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Advisory only (network/advice) | 0.1-0.5% | 2 years, quarterly |
| Part-time operator (CMO-equivalent) | 0.5-2% | 4 years, 1-year cliff |
| Full-time operator / founding CMO | 2-5% | 4 years, 1-year cliff |
| Agency with reduced cash fee | 1-3% | Milestone-gated |

**Calvin's position is part-time operator/CMO equivalent while maintaining other clients.** Fair range: 0.5-1.5% per brand, heavily milestone-gated.

### Red flags to watch
- Equity requested above 2% for a marketing operator (not a founder)
- Time-based vesting with no milestone gates
- Equity across 3 brands without any performance floor on each
- Equity in a brand without a defined deliverable scope
- No termination clause that stops vesting on departure

## CONTRACT RED LINES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

These must be present or the contract is not acceptable:

### 1. IP Stays With BGV
**The rule:** ALL creative assets, brand guidelines, social content, copywriting, strategic documents produced under the contract must vest immediately to BGV/brand entity. Calvin cannot retain any rights.

**Why it matters:** If Calvin walks or is terminated, BGV must be able to continue without him. Any IP that "belongs to Ku Creatives" creates ransom leverage.

**What to check:** Look for IP assignment clause. Should say something like "all work product created under this agreement is work-for-hire and assigns to [brand entity] upon creation."

### 2. Termination Must Be Clean and Fast
**The rule:** BGV should be able to terminate with 30 days notice (or less) for any reason. No penalty for early termination. Vesting stops on termination date.

**What to check:** Termination clause, notice period, what happens to unvested equity on termination. Should be "unvested equity forfeited on termination."

### 3. Vesting Must Be Milestone-Gated, Not Time-Only
**The rule:** Equity should vest based on measurable outcomes, not just showing up. Time-based-only vesting means Calvin accrues equity regardless of results.

**Acceptable milestones for Modern Savage:**
- 500 waitlist signups → 25% vest
- Product launch completed → 25% vest
- $X MRR within 90 days of launch → 25% vest
- $X MRR within 180 days → final 25%

### 4. Scope Must Be Defined
**The rule:** The contract must specify exactly what Calvin is responsible for. "Marketing" is not a scope. "Social content calendar, paid ads management, influencer outreach" is a scope.

**Why it matters:** Undefined scope = Calvin can claim he delivered even if results don't materialize.

### 5. No Assignment / No Subcontracting Without Approval
**The rule:** Calvin cannot assign the contract or subcontract the work without BGV approval. He cannot bring in unknown contractors and bill BGV through Ku Creatives.

## YELLOW FLAGS — NEGOTIATE, DON'T BLOCK

These aren't dealbreakers but should be pushed back on:

- **Cash + equity combined:** $3k/month + equity for a brand generating £0 revenue is high. Ask: can we do $2k/month + more equity, or $0/month + more equity with a revenue trigger?
- **Equity in 3 brands simultaneously:** Legitimate if each contract is standalone with no cross-contamination. But it creates a "single point of failure" risk (Calvin has leverage across all BGV brands at once). Consider staggered equity: earn into Modern Savage first, then unlock Wildernests, then Ted's.
- **Short vesting schedules:** Calvin may push for 2-year vesting to accelerate ownership. Push for 4 years on anything over 1%.
- **Broad non-compete:** If Calvin is also operating other clients (he is), a broad non-compete on Calvin's part could be legally unenforceable. Fine if it's narrow (no direct supplements competitor).

---

## QUESTIONS TO ASK BEFORE ACCEPTING ANY CONTRACT

1. What is the equity calculated against? (fully diluted share count? pre-raise valuation? needs to be clear)
2. Is there an equity cap? (if the company raises a Series A, does Calvin's stake get diluted? on what terms?)
3. What triggers full vesting acceleration? (change of control? acquisition? should NOT be automatic)
4. How are disputes resolved? (jurisdiction matters - UK vs US)
5. Is Ku Creatives the contracting entity or Calvin personally? (matters for liability and tax)

## FINAL ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST

When the contracts arrive, run through this:

- [ ] IP assignment clause present and unambiguous
- [ ] Termination: 30 days or less notice, unvested equity forfeited
- [ ] Equity % within 0.5-1.5% range per brand
- [ ] Vesting: milestone-gated or hybrid (time + milestone)
- [ ] Scope of work clearly defined (not just "marketing")
- [ ] No assignment clause present
- [ ] Jurisdiction: check which country's law governs
- [ ] Cash compensation reasonable relative to equity
- [ ] No cross-brand contamination (each contract standalone)

**If any of the top 3 (IP, termination, milestones) fail → flag immediately to Jesse before signing.**

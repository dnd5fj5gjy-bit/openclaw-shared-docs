BRIEF 9: TRT Myths

Category: Social media graphics
Task Name: TH — TRT Myths Carousel

Instagram carousel (1080x1080, 6 slides). Myth on one slide, the truth on the next. Calm, factual, not defensive.

Slide 1 (Cover): "What men get wrong about testosterone replacement"
Ted's Health branding bottom right.

Slide 2 — Myth: "TRT is just steroids."
Slide 3 — Truth: "Steroids push your levels well above normal. TRT brings you back to where a healthy man your age should be. Doctor-led. Blood-tracked. Legal."

Slide 4 — Myth: "Once you start, you're on it for life."
Slide 5 — Truth: "Some men stay on long-term. Others use it for a defined period. Either way, it's a clinical decision made with your doctor based on your bloods."

Slide 6 — Myth: "It'll mess with my fertility / hair / heart."
Slide 7 (final): "Truth: every concern has a clinical answer. That's why we test you, walk you through the options, and monitor you. Don't take it from Reddit. Speak to a doctor — £40 to start. tedshealth.com"

Tone: Mature, factual. Like a doctor patiently correcting you, not a brand defending itself. Avoid sounding promotional on the truth slides — let the facts do the work.

Visual guidance: Split-style design. Myth slides on a muted/grey background with the myth in quotation marks. Truth slides on the navy brand background with the response in clean white type. Could use subtle iconography (red question mark for myths, green tick or stethoscope for truths). No stock photos needed — this one is text-led so the typography has to carry it.

Format: 1080x1080 carousel (7 slides)

BRIEF 10: Meet Dr Johnny

Category: Social media graphics
Task Name: TH — Meet Dr Johnny Post

Static Instagram post (1080x1080) plus story version. Humanises the doctor. The point of difference is "you actually speak to him."

Headline: "Meet Dr Johnny."

Body copy:
"He'll be the one reviewing your bloods. The one explaining what they mean. The one helping you decide what to do next.

Not a chatbot. Not a sales rep. A real GP who's spent his career looking at men's hormone health.

You book the test. He reads the results. You speak to him directly."

Footer: "GMC registered. Ted's Health doctor-led."
CTA: "tedshealth.com — start with a £40 blood test"

Tone: Warm, reassuring, slightly understated. The hero is the doctor, not the brand. Avoid hype.

Visual guidance: Photograph of Dr Johnny — head-and-shoulders, professional but not stiff. Soft natural light. Background slightly out of focus (clinic or home office, not stock medical imagery). His expression should be approachable, eye contact with camera. Brand framing kept minimal — small TH logo bottom corner, maybe a subtle navy bar at the base of the image with the headline overlaid.

Format: 1080x1080 (feed) + 1080x1920 (story)

BRIEF 11: The 12-Week Journey

Category: Social media graphics
Task Name: TH — 12 Week Journey Carousel

Instagram carousel (1080x1080, 5 slides). Sets realistic expectations for what TRT actually feels like over time.

Slide 1 (Cover): "What 12 weeks on TRT actually looks like"
Subhead: "Honest expectations from real treatment timelines"

Slide 2 — Week 1-2: "Energy lift first. Subtle. You'll notice you're not hitting the 2pm wall the same way. Sleep often improves before anything else."

Slide 3 — Week 3-6: "Mood stabilises. Motivation comes back. Brain fog clears. Strength in the gym starts climbing — but only if you're putting the work in."

Slide 4 — Week 7-12: "Body composition starts shifting. Libido is usually noticeably back. Most men say they feel like themselves again, not a different person."

Slide 5 (CTA): "Results vary. Treatment is doctor-led and based on your blood work. Start where everyone starts — a £40 blood test. tedshealth.com"

Tone: Grounded, honest. No "transformation" language. Sets realistic expectations, which builds trust.

Visual guidance: Each slide carries a lifestyle photo paired with a clean text panel. Slide 2: a man drinking morning coffee, looking awake rather than dragging. Slide 3: gym setting, mid-set, focused. Slide 4: outdoor lifestyle — running, with family, doing something active. Warm tones throughout, ascending in brightness/saturation slightly across the carousel to mirror the journey. Ted's Health navy/gold accents on text panels.

Format: 1080x1080 carousel (5 slides)

BRIEF 13: The £40 Test, Explained (Reel)

Category: Short-form video
Task Name: TH — £40 Test Explained Reel

Instagram Reel / TikTok (9:16, 30-45 seconds). Talking-to-camera + b-roll cutaways. Demystifies what you actually get for £40 versus a £200+ private clinic or a bleak 8-minute GP appointment.

Script:

[0-3s — Hook, talking to camera]
"£40 sounds too cheap to be a real medical test. Let me show you what you actually get."

[3-8s — B-roll: the kit arriving, being unboxed]
VO: "A full blood test kit. Posted to your door."

[8-14s — B-roll: finger-prick, sample going in the prepaid envelope]
VO: "Quick finger-prick at home. Drop it in the post."

[14-22s — B-roll: lab analysis stylised, then results dashboard on a phone]
VO: "It goes to a CQC-accredited lab. Full hormone panel. Not just one number, the full picture."

[22-32s — Talking to camera + b-roll of doctor video call]
"Then you actually speak to a doctor. Not a chatbot. Not a triage form. Dr Johnny reviews your results, explains what they mean, and tells you whether you need treatment or not. That's the bit most clinics charge £200 for."

[32-40s — Talking to camera, calm close]
"£40 to find out if low testosterone is what's holding you back. No pushy sales call. No commitment."

[40-45s — End card]
On screen: "tedshealth.com" — Ted's Health logo

Tone: Conversational, no hard sell. Slight scepticism in the hook ("sounds too cheap") then resolved with proof. The presenter should sound like a mate explaining it, not a corporate spokesperson.

Visual guidance: Mix of clean talking-to-camera (medium shot, soft natural light, slight depth of field) and b-roll. B-roll should feel real — actual unboxing, actual finger-prick, actual phone showing the results portal. Captions on every line. Cuts every 2-3 seconds to maintain pace.

Format: 9:16 vertical, 30-45 seconds. Captions burned in.

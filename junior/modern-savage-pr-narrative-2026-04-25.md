## The Problem This Document Solves

A journalist writing about Modern Savage's July 1 launch will Google "Bear Grylls organ supplement." They will find:

1. **A 2022 PR press release** — "Ancestral Supplements Taps Bear Grylls to Help Put Back In What the Modern Diet Left Out" (PR Newswire, October 27, 2022)
2. **A Forbes article** — "What Are 'Ancestral Supplements' And Why Does Bear Grylls Want Us To Take Them?"
3. **Possibly: historical social posts** from Bear endorsing Organised Nutrition

Without a prepared narrative, the story writes itself as: "Bear endorsed a competing organ supplement brand in 2022, appeared to be invested in another rival UK brand, and is now launching his own. Why should we believe this one is different?"

With a prepared narrative, that story becomes: "Bear has been privately consuming and advocating for organ nutrition for 30 years. He tried the existing brands, worked with them commercially, and found none of them were exactly what he'd take himself. So he built it."

The second story is better — and it's true.

## The Narrative — Three Questions Answered

### "Didn't Bear partner with Ancestral Supplements a few years ago?"

> "He did. That was a promotional partnership — Bear believed in the ancestral nutrition thesis and was willing to put his name behind it. But there was a meaningful gap between what he wanted to take personally and what was commercially available. Modern Savage is his answer to that gap. He didn't want to endorse someone else's product. He wanted to build the right product."

### "Isn't this the same category as a lot of other supplement brands?"

> "The organ supplement category exists, but most brands in it are either American, bison-focused, or not formulated for a European lifestyle and palate. Modern Savage is the first UK-accessible, grass-fed New Zealand/Australian sourced, freeze-dried organ blend built by someone who actually lives the outdoor life it claims to support. There's a real difference between being adjacent to the carnivore internet and being someone who's eaten what was available in a survival situation for 30 years."

### "Bear has equity in other supplement businesses, doesn't he?"

> Do not answer this question publicly. Redirect: "Bear built Modern Savage because it's what he takes personally and what he wanted his children to take. That's the only claim being made here — not that it's his only interest in nutrition, but that it's the product he built for himself."

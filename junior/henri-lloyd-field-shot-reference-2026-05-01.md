## Shot priority — if you only get three

1. **Helm at dawn or dusk, kit visible, horizon broken by weather** — the hero. Jacket hood up or down, your hands on the wheel, low angle if possible. Backlight is fine, silhouette is fine. This is the ambassador announcement shot.
2. **Working shot — hands on rope, sail, or chart** — detail. Logo doesn't have to be visible. The story is "this is real work, not a brand shoot."
3. **Boat in conditions** — Leviosa from a slight angle, weather around her. Drone if you have one and conditions allow, otherwise from the deck looking forward.

Three good shots beat thirty good-enough shots. Henri-Lloyd will use one or two — the goal is making sure those one or two are unmistakable.

## What NOT to do

- **No dock posing.** Henri-Lloyd has plenty of those. The point of this ambassadorship is that you're actually offshore. Dock shots cheapen it.
- **No logo zoom-ins.** They're not paying for a billboard. The kit being worn naturally is enough.
- **No competing brand visibility** in hero shots. Watch out for Patagonia, Musto, Helly Hansen logos on cuffs/labels of crew gear.
- **No staging the same shot ten ways.** One natural shot beats ten retakes.
- **No social posting from sea** unless it's effortless. Don't burn Starlink bandwidth on Instagram.

## Camera and settings

**Default to phone.** A modern iPhone in offshore conditions outperforms most action cameras for stills. Wipe the lens before every shot — salt is the enemy.

**If you have a proper camera onboard:** shoot RAW, lock white balance to daylight or cloudy, expose for highlights (Arctic light blows out fast). 1/500s shutter minimum if there's any motion.

**Drone:** only in light wind, only with a clear deck launch/recovery. One battery's worth of footage is plenty. Don't risk losing it.

**Audio (for video clips):** always shoot 10 seconds with audio if you're going to shoot at all. Wind noise is fine — it's atmospheric.

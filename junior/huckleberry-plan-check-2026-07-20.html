One more thing, and then I will leave you to it.

Before you start, send me your plan. Not a long one, four or five lines is plenty: what you are going to look at, what you intend to produce, and when you will have it. I want it before you begin rather than after.

The reason is selfish on your behalf. If your plan is pointing slightly the wrong way, I can tell you that in two minutes now, or you can find out on Friday having spent three days on it. Almost everyone learns this the expensive way. Getting the plan checked before the work, rather than the work checked after it, is probably the single most useful habit in this job.

So: what are you doing, what will exist at the end of it, and by when. Send it over and I will tell you honestly whether it is the right thing or not.

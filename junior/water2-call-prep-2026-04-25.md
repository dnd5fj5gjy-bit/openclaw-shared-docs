# Water2 — Call Prep for Jesse
**For:** Jesse calling Charles Robinson before April 28
**Read time:** 5 minutes
**Purpose:** Know what you want before you get on the phone.

---

## Who You're Calling

**Charles Robinson** — Water2 founder. Dropped out of university, worked with academics to build Pod 2.0. Entrepreneurial early-stage founder type. The kind of person who moves fast on relationships and slow on documents — similar to you.

Water2 is a real business: they're on Amazon UK, they have genuine reviews, T3 magazine reviewed them positively, and they have "fastest growing water filter brand in the world" in their marketing language (which means they have growth numbers to show). Bear's name is front and centre in all their marketing.

---

## What's Happened So Far

- Bear invested in early 2024. He's customer #8420. He co-led the development of Pod 2.0 and sat in on product meetings.
- A royalty arrangement was discussed at some point and died. This is normal — royalties on a £55/year refill product are too small to matter right now.
- The negotiation has stalled. The relationship is warm but the commercial structure is undefined.
- Bear/BGV's ongoing value to Water2: Bear's name and face on their Amazon listing and website. That is their primary marketing asset. It has ongoing commercial value that hasn't been formally compensated since the 2024 investment.

---

## What You Want Out of This Call

Three things:
1. **Understand where Charles thinks the relationship stands.** Does he think it's settled? Does he know it's stalled? Get him talking first.
2. **Get one number: what's Water2's ARR today?** This tells you what the equity is actually worth. Before any deal conversation, you need this.
3. **Put a framework on the table** for ongoing brand association — not a final term sheet, just a shared direction so neither side goes cold.

---

## The Deal Structure You're Aiming For

**Bear's 2024 investment equity:** Keep it. Protect it with an anti-dilution clause if possible (ask Kylee to confirm what protection the existing terms provide).

**Ongoing BGV brand services** (Bear continuing to be the face, social posts, product launches): This should be compensated separately. Options:
- Equity warrants vesting over 3 years tied to milestones (50K customers, revenue thresholds)
- A small retainer for Jesse's time + warrant tranche for Bear's continued face/brand use

**The key ask:** "What are you comfortable with for recognising Bear's continued marketing involvement, beyond the 2024 investment?"

Let Charles propose something first. If he says equity warrants, great. If he says royalties, redirect to warrants — explain royalties work better at scale, and right now equity alignment makes more sense for both sides.

---

## The One Question You Must Ask

> "What's your ARR today, and what's the 12-month trajectory?"

That number does two things: tells you what the equity is worth, and tells you whether Water2 has the revenue to justify any deal structure at all. If ARR is under £500K, the business is very early and warrants are more reasonable than anything cash-based. If ARR is over £1M, the equity conversation has real weight.

---

## What NOT to Do on This Call

- Don't renegotiate the 2024 investment equity downward
- Don't agree to a flat royalty (too small, wrong incentive)
- Don't commit to a specific warrant percentage without Kylee/Raemy reviewing it
- Don't let the call end without a clear next step (even if it's "send me a heads of terms by Friday")

---

## The One-Line Frame for the Call

> "Bear is genuinely proud of what you've built. The 2024 investment stands. We want to make sure the ongoing brand relationship is properly structured so both sides have clarity — especially as you scale. What does that look like from your side?"

Warm opening. Defers to Charles to speak first. Signals intent without pre-committing to terms.

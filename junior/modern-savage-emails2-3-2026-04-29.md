---

**Liver — the most nutrient-dense food on earth**

Gram for gram, beef liver contains more usable nutrition than almost any other food. Per serving of Modern Savage Adult, you're getting:

- **Vitamin B12** — well above 1,000% of your daily requirement. B12 drives red blood cell formation, nervous system function, and energy metabolism. The fatigue people report from B12 deficiency is measurable and reversible.
- **Vitamin A (retinol)** — the form your body actually uses. Not beta-carotene (the plant form that many people don't convert efficiently). Real retinol. Immune function, skin integrity, vision.
- **Iron (heme iron)** — the form with the highest bioavailability. Not plant iron, which competes with other minerals and absorbs poorly. The iron in liver goes in the way your body evolved to absorb it.
- **Copper, zinc, folate** — the full co-factor set. These are the nutrients that make the other nutrients work.

**Heart — the CoQ10 source**

Beef heart is the richest dietary source of CoQ10 — a compound your mitochondria use to produce cellular energy. Your body makes it naturally; production declines with age. Heart meat is how every traditional diet replenished it. Modern diets don't include heart. Modern Savage does.

**Kidney — the B-vitamin complex**

Kidney provides the remaining B-vitamin spectrum, in the forms and ratios that occur naturally in food (not synthetic isolates). This matters: the nutrient interactions in whole food are more complex than anything a laboratory has replicated in capsule form.

**Why capsules, not raw meat?**

Grass-fed New Zealand sourced. Freeze-dried at low temperature to preserve the nutrient profile — no heat, no oxidation. The capsule is just a delivery mechanism. What's inside is as close to the real thing as you can get without cooking it yourself.

Three capsules with a meal. That's it.

You would need to eat approximately 100g of chicken breast to get the same B12 as a single serving of Modern Savage. You would need to eat 6 eggs to approach the same vitamin A. You would need to eat a large bowl of cooked spinach to approach the same iron — and absorb it at a fraction of the rate.

One serving. Three capsules.

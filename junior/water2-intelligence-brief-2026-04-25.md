## What Water2 Is

**Water2** is a UK under-sink water filtration company founded in 2020 by Charles Robinson (dropped out of university, worked with professors to "fix the UK's tap water crisis").

**The product:** Pod 2.0 — an under-sink water filter that attaches to the cold water tap.
- Starter kit: £129-149 (often discounted to £89-99)
- Annual refill capsule: £50-60
- Accessories: fluoride add-on filter, smart buzzer (tells you when to change the filter)
- Distribution: own website + Amazon UK + selected retailers

**Bear's involvement:** Bear invested in early 2024. Officially customer #8420. "Personally funded and co-led the development of Pod 2.0. Regularly sitting in on product meetings." His involvement is genuine and documented — not just a name endorsement.

**Current positioning:** "Backed by Bear Grylls" is front and centre on Amazon, their website, and press coverage. T3 magazine ran a positive review. It's a real product with real reviews and real sales.

---

## Why the Royalty Deal Died

Royalties on physical product sales are painful for a capital-light startup. At £50-60 annual refill revenue per customer, even a 5% royalty = £2.50-3.00/year per active customer. To pay BGV meaningful royalties, Water2 would need tens of thousands of active customers — and at their stage, they're not there yet.

More likely: they agreed to a royalty in principle, the numbers came back small, and Bear/Jesse realised equity is the better bet. If Water2 grows to meaningful scale (say, 100K UK households), the equity is worth far more than the royalties would have been.

This is the classic early-stage brand partnership dilemma: royalties pay now but are small; equity pays later but could be meaningful.

## The Competitive Landscape

Water2 operates in the under-sink filter space competing with:

| Competitor | Price | Differentiator |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Waterdrop | ~£100-200 | More established, wider international distribution |
| Brita (under-sink) | ~£50-80 | Brand recognition, cheaper, less filtration |

Water2's differentiator: Bear Grylls brand, UK-manufactured identity, strong content marketing. Their positioning is clean-tech meets adventure — "what if your water was as pure as the source Bear finds in the wild."

The renewable heating hub review calls them "pricier upfront but comparable yearly" to Waterdrop. So they're premium-positioned but not dramatically so.

## The Business Model Math

At £129 starter + £55/year refill:
- Year 1 revenue per customer: £184
- Year 2+: £55/year recurring
- LTV at 3 years: £294
- LTV at 5 years: £404

If Water2 gets to 50,000 UK households (not unreasonable for a brand with Bear's backing):
- Annual refill revenue: 50K × £55 = **£2.75M ARR**
- Starter kit revenue declining (installed base growing): still contributing £1-2M in Year 3

The equity path makes sense: a business with 50K recurring household subscriptions at £55/year is worth £8-15M conservatively (3-5x revenue). Bear's equity stake could be meaningful at that scale.

## What the Stalled Negotiation Is Probably About

**Scenario A — Valuation disagreement:** Charles Robinson values Water2 at X, Bear/Jesse think it's worth Y. The royalty was abandoned because the equity offer doesn't match the expected return for the brand association cost.

**Scenario B — Equity percentage:** Bear got equity (invested early 2024), but the ongoing commercial relationship (BGV support, continued Bear brand use, Jesse's time) needs separate compensation. They can't agree on how much additional equity that's worth.

**Scenario C — Term structure:** The equity path requires governance rights, anti-dilution protection, or other structural terms that Water2's founders are reluctant to grant.

**Most likely:** Scenario B. Bear invested and has equity. But BGV's ongoing commercial involvement — marketing support, Jesse's time, continued brand association — isn't covered by that initial equity. A separate "brand services" equity grant needs to be negotiated.

## What a Good Deal Looks Like

**The bear minimum (pun intended):** BGV should not provide ongoing brand association for no incremental compensation. Bear's "customer #8420" and Pod 2.0 co-development was pre-investment. Ongoing marketing appearances, social posts, product launches — these have ongoing value.

**The right structure:**
- Bear's existing equity from 2024 investment: retain and protect (anti-dilution clause)
- BGV ongoing brand services: equity warrants vesting over 3 years, tied to milestones (50K customers, revenue thresholds)
- Jesse's time: either a small ongoing retainer OR a board observer seat with additional warrant tranche
- A clear agreement on what Bear can and can't say about Water2 vs other water brands

**What Jesse should NOT do:**
- Renegotiate the 2024 investment equity downward
- Accept a flat royalty (too small, wrong incentive alignment)
- Continue providing brand association without a signed term sheet

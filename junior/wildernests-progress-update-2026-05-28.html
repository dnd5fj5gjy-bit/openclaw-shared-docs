Creative Direction (Issued Today from Wildernests)

  • Slow, cinematic camera movement — subtle pushes/pulls, controlled left-right trucking
  • Give the viewer time to absorb design, materiality and indoor-outdoor connection
  • Movement within every frame: trees, light filtering, water, foreground landscape
  • Connection of home with nature is the central visual idea
  • Reference: tribu.com for outdoor furniture aesthetic
Technical

3D Rendering Development

The 3D model work has been the most technically demanding part of the process. What follows is the full iteration journey — from placeholder shots to the current state.

12 May
Done

Presentation Sections Begin

Sections 1-4 of the presentation rough cut started. Branding confirmed as "Bear Witness" throughout. All house shots acknowledged as placeholders pending accurate 3D model.

19 May
Done

3D Visualizer Onboarded — Salman Alfarisi

Salman joined the creative team specifically for 3D visualization. Remit: finalize exterior, build environments, produce interior renders. Revit model (from architect Daniel) used as the source of truth for all dimensions.

20 May
Done

First 360 View Delivered + Initial Feedback

First 3D animation (360 view + 5 stills) delivered. Feedback: animation too fast, wood texture flat and fake-looking. Floor plan geometry approved. Began iterating on wood grain realism.

21 May
Done

Wood Texture Approved + Cinematic Direction Confirmed

After two texture iterations, composite version with good grain contrast approved. Decision made to skip 360 rotation, move straight to cinematic-style video: slow, smooth camera movement with cinematic grading. Final 360 view delivered and approved by Jesse: "This is amazing."

25-26 May
Done

Team Meeting + New 3D House Model Integration

Team call held (Wildernests Teaser Updates) to align on editing direction. New 3D house model integrated into the teaser edit at the key section. Initial version had the house moving — corrected: the house must remain static and unmoved by weather/disaster events (the structural resilience point). Dust weathering effect added to deck and walls for realism.

27 May
Done

Cinematic Shots Rendered — Desert Environment

Full cinematic shot set for desert environment delivered and approved. Storyboard 1 confirmed good enough to move to final generation phase. Salman directed to begin building new environments. VO sync issue identified: VO track is longer than current footage — additional scenes being generated to fill.

28 May
Active Today

Forest Environment + Rendering Direction Brief

Forest environment first renders delivered (mist being refined, elements for realism being added). Jesse's feedback: replace odd plants around house base with lawn, rocks, garden features. Outdoor loungers being added to deck. Wildernests rendering direction brief issued to team covering cinematic framing, indoor-outdoor connection, and movement within frame.

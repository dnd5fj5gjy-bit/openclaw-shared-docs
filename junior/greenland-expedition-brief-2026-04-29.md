## The Window

**June-August** is the reliable season. May is shoulder season — ice is clearing but unpredictable, weather harder to predict, rescue coverage thinner. The midnight sun runs May 25 to July 25.

If you're thinking about this for summer 2027, the target departure window from UK is **mid-June to mid-July**. Earlier in June you get the season, later in July you start losing light and ice behaviour becomes less predictable in the autumn shoulder.

---

## The Route

**Standard UK to Greenland passage:**

```
Gosport/Lerwick → Lerwick (Shetland) → Faroe Islands (optional) → Reykjavik → Tasiilaq (East Greenland)
```

Total: approx 1,300 nm from Lerwick to Iceland. Iceland to East Greenland (via Denmark Strait) adds another 400-600 nm depending on entry point.

**Realistic total from UK: 3-4 weeks at sea (passage only).** If you want time in the fjords, add 10-14 days on top.

**Key staging points:**
- **Reykjavik** - provisioning hub, crew rotation possible (international airport), good fuel, weather routing
- **Tasiilaq (Kulusuk area)** - East Greenland entry point, airport nearby, sheltered cruising ground
- **Scoresby Sound** - world's longest fjord system, the centrepiece destination. Settlement: Ittoqqortoormiit (about 400 people, extremely isolated)
- **Disko Bay (West Greenland)** - whale territory, more accessible from Iceland via north route. Different trip.

**Denmark Strait** carries the East Greenland current with south-flowing icebergs — major navigation hazard. Must be respected.

## Permits and Rules

- **No general cruising permit** is required for standard sailing in Greenlandic waters.
- **National Park zones (North and East Greenland)** require expedition permits. Apply minimum **12 weeks in advance** to the Greenlandic government.
- Remote areas: one crew member should have local water knowledge or Arctic navigation credentials.
- All vessels: GPS PLB (personal locator beacon) devices mandatory. No exceptions in remote Arctic.

**Bottom line:** Standard Scoresby Sound route doesn't require special permits beyond normal maritime paperwork. If you want to go deep into the Northeast Greenland National Park, build in the 12-week lead time.

---

## Hazards

**Ice.** This is the one that kills expeditions. Glacier calving is unpredictable — an iceberg can appear in open water without warning. The rule is: maintain safe distance at all times, navigate at reduced speed when ice is present, and never approach a calving face.

**Weather.** Fjord winds are thermally driven and can build fast. Low pressure systems intensify rapidly in these latitudes. Extended poor visibility is normal. Have a layover strategy at every anchorage.

**Rescue.** SAR coverage outside major settlements is thin. Response could take days in remote areas. Means: self-rescue capability is not optional. Every system on the boat needs to work.

**Communications blackout zones.** Satellite comms (Iridium or equivalent) are the only reliable link in remote fjords. Standard mobile coverage: non-existent.

## Logistics

**Fuel:** Available in Nuuk (capital, best services), Tasiilaq, and major settlements. Remote anchorages have nothing — carry extra range and plan carefully.

**Provisioning:** Fresh food only in larger towns. Nuuk has the best range. Plan for self-sufficiency in the fjords — dried goods, tinned provisions.

**Crew:** For a passage of this length and technical complexity, minimum 3-4 crew. One should have offshore passage experience, ideally some Arctic exposure.

**Timing for a 2027 expedition:**
- Decide by December 2026 (Scoresby permits if National Park overlap)
- Crew confirmed by February 2027
- Departure window: mid-June 2027
- Return: late July / early August

## Danish Meteorological Institute

The authoritative source for Greenland ice forecasts is the **Danish Meteorological Institute (DMI)**. Ice charts updated daily. Mandatory tool for any Greenland passage planning. Monitor 2-4 weeks out from departure, then daily once underway.

## The Honest Assessment

Greenland is achievable but not casual. The passage itself is manageable — similar ocean miles to an Atlantic crossing. The variables are ice, weather, and rescue coverage. With the right crew and the right boat (a blue-water vessel with ice protection, good comms, and redundant navigation), this is a realistic 4-6 week expedition.

The content opportunity is extraordinary. No social media creator in Jesse's space has done a sustained Greenland sailing documentary series. The combination of Bear Grylls's son, a sailing narrative, Greenland's landscape, and the Running Wild production angle would be genuinely first-mover.

The question is crew, boat readiness, and whether you want to do it in summer 2027 or 2028.

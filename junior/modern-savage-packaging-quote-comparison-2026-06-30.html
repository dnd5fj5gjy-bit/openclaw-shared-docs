Belmark — the actual numbers

Two pouch sizes, 3 SKUs (Adult / Kids / Summit), Soft Touch Matte white, powder-resistant zipper. Per-unit, 3-SKU row:

Large 9"×12" pouch (Q1363734): 5K/SKU $1.07 · 10K $0.84 · 15K $0.77 · 30K $0.70
Small 7"×10" pouch (Q1363736): 5K/SKU $0.77 · 10K $0.60 · 15K $0.54 · 30K $0.48

Worked launch cost (5,000/SKU × 3 = 15,000 pouches, assuming 1 large + 2 small):
(5,000 × $1.07) + (10,000 × $0.77) + $450 prepress ≈ $13,500, plus cheap US freight.

Quote valid through July 30. Excludes freight (FOB Belmark, US-domestic). +15%/−10% run tolerance, billed on what ships. No minimum — a smaller first run is possible even though the grid starts at 5,000.

Salt of the Earth — the actual numbers

  • Pouch ~$0.79 NZD/unit at bulk ≈ ~$0.48 USD; landed ~$0.60–0.75/unit at launch volume
  • Air freight $3,600 NZD (~$2,100 USD) for ~15K units = $0.14/unit — negligible
  • MOQ 5,000 custom; 3–4 wk build + 5–7 day air freight Auckland → Miami
  • Bundled designer + copywriter offered unprompted; discounted the quote for brand fit

The catch that matters: Salty still has not given a WVTR (moisture barrier) spec for powders. Compostable kraft typically runs 10–100× higher moisture ingress than PET/foil. Until Melissa provides WVTR + shelf-life data, the eco pouch is a product-quality risk. Cody at Belmark flagged the same: they "would not recommend" non-plastic for powders.

Why Belmark wins the launch run

  1. Timing. Launch is July 1. Salty can't land pouches before late June at the earliest. Belmark is US-domestic, same country as ACF, who already run these exact sizes. Zero logistics risk.
  2. Barrier. Belmark's PET/LLDPE gives the moisture barrier the organ powders need. Salty's kraft is unproven and may need a conventional liner anyway — which kills the compostable claim.
  3. Cost is a wash. Belmark small pouch ($0.54 at 15K) beats Salty landed; the large is dearer. Not a deciding factor.
  4. Relationship + speed. Warm intro via Drew/ACF, integrated supply chain, no MOQ, soft-touch already endorsed by Andrew.

Why you don't kill Salty

The compostable story is a real differentiator — no other organ supplement has it, and it's authentic to Bear. It just can't be the launch pouch. The right home:

  • Batch 2 (Sept/Oct) as the eco upgrade, ordered now to hold the slot — conditional on Melissa's WVTR + shelf-life data clearing supplement grade.
  • If barrier holds: PR hook, faith/TBN stewardship angle, Bear posts it organically.
  • If it doesn't: redeploy Salty as outer carton / insert, keep the plastic pouch inside.

Recommended actions

  1. Confirm Belmark for the launch run — lock the 3-SKU order at the size mix ACF specifies. Quote good through July 30.
  2. Reply to Melissa with the two barrier questions before committing: WVTR spec for powders + any nutraceutical reference clients. Hold Batch 2 interest open.
  3. Decide size mix with Andrew (which SKU = 9×12 vs 7×10) so Belmark can be placed clean.

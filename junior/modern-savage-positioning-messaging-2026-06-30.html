1. The one-line strategy

The whole organ-supplement category apologises for taste — they bury organs in 6 pills a day precisely because organs taste bad. Modern Savage makes that the entire point: a primal organ blend you actually want to drink. One scoop, not six capsules. Real food, made to taste like it.

We win on experience (taste, format, ritual) while matching the incumbents on credibility (grass-fed, freeze-dried, no fillers, third-party tested). They win on credibility and lose on experience. We refuse to lose on either.

7. The transparency play (do this first)

Every competitor — US and UK — is weak on published proof. Organised: no per-organ dosing, no public COA. Heart & Soil/Ancestral: no full panels. The category's #1 buyer skepticism is efficacy/claims trust (Heart & Soil is even running a clinical trial to buy credibility).

Ship on day one: full per-organ mg breakdown, complete micronutrient panel, and an independent heavy-metal + glyphosate COA — linked from the PDP and QR'd on pack. Marginal cost near zero (we already test for compliance). Strategic value is enormous: a differentiator no incumbent currently matches, it neutralises the toxin/efficacy objections, and it's the spine of the strongest content angle.

8. Regulatory guardrails — two claims matrices

  • US (FDA structure/function): "supports energy / immune health / testosterone production" allowed if truthful, substantiated, FDA-notified within 30 days, with the supplement disclaimer. FTC expects real evidence.
  • UK/EU (pre-approved register only): only GB Register claims are legal. "Boosts testosterone" is a UK landmine — reframe to authorised nutrient claims: zinc "contributes to normal testosterone levels," iron/B12/folate "contribute to the reduction of tiredness and fatigue," "normal immune function" — usable only if the product delivers the qualifying nutrient quantity (another reason the full panel matters).
  • No disease/treatment language in either market. Validate exact GB Register wording before any UK label goes live.

9. Pricing positioning

Organised anchors the premium powder ceiling at ~£2.63/serving (£79 sub). Capsule leaders sit $1.60–1.73/serving. Recommendation: price at parity-to-slightly-under Organised on the Adult SKU, and let transparency + range + Bear justify the premium vs capsules. Do not race to the value floor (~$1.31) — it contradicts the premium brand and erodes margin. Compete on demonstrated value, not price.

The distinctive thing about UK for Modern Savage: **Bear is British.** This is not a US brand exported to the UK. It's a British brand launching at home. Bear Grylls is arguably more culturally significant in the UK than in the US — he's a national figure, SAS veteran, the face of British adventure. That changes the launch economics entirely.

The US organs supplement category has no dominant UK brand. Heart & Soil is American and hasn't meaningfully cracked the UK. Ancestral Supplements ships internationally but with no UK presence, no UK distribution, and no brand investment in Britain. The UK market is wide open.

## Regulatory — What Changes for UK

The legal claims guide (workspace/docs/modern-savage-claims-guide-2026-04-24.md) already covers UK specifically. Key points:

- **GB Health Claims Register** applies (retained EU Regulation 1924/2006). The approved claims listed in that guide are valid for UK label use.
- **CoQ10 warning stands**: no authorized UK health claim for CoQ10. Can list it in ingredients, cannot claim heart/cardiovascular benefit on label.
- **MHRA oversight** rather than FDA. Trading Standards enforces claims compliance.
- **No DSHEA notification** needed (US-only). UK doesn't require pre-market notification for food supplements.
- **UKIPO trademark** for MODERN SAVAGE: Jesse is filing this now. Once granted, the brand is protected in Great Britain.

**Pre-UK-launch legal action:** Send the final UK label to a UK food law solicitor for sign-off. Same ~£500 cost as US label review. Do not ship UK orders until the label is confirmed compliant.

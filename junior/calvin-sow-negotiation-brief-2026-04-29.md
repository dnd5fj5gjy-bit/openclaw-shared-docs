## The Framing Before You Start

Calvin is a good operator and he knows the deal. He proposed 2.5%+2.5% knowing it would get negotiated. He has other clients. He's not a co-founder. The market pays part-time fractional operators 0.25–0.75% total — the SOW as drafted at 3% is already a 4-10x premium on market rate. You are not doing Calvin a disservice by countering. You are being a fair principal.

The risk in this negotiation is not offending Calvin. He wants the Modern Savage mandate. The risk is giving away equity you'll regret when the company is worth something.

**The number to have in your head:** At 10K subscribers, a £5M exit gives Calvin £150K at 3% total. At 1.5% total, it's £75K. These are real numbers, not hypothetical. Be deliberate.

## Decision 1 — Cash Start Date

**What the SOW says:** June 1.

**What Calvin will say:** "I've been doing setup work since April. I should be paid from May 1."

**The logic on each side:**
- Calvin's position is reasonable — Klaviyo setup, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, Stripe integration, building out the email flows takes real hours in May.
- Your position is also reasonable — there's no email list to market to in May (Stripe isn't live yet). You're paying $3K/month for a list that doesn't exist.

**Jesse's opening position:** June 1 for the retainer. Offer the $1,500 setup fee for May work upfront — it's already in the draft.

**If Calvin pushes for May 1:** Counter with May 15 at half-month rate ($1,500) instead of the setup fee. Same economics, different framing. Either way, you're covering May at $1,500 not $3,000.

**What not to concede:** Full $3,000 from May 1. If he started in May, the $1,500 setup fee is fair compensation for that month.

**The phrase to use:** "The setup fee is specifically there to cover your May work. The retainer starts when the Klaviyo list goes live — that's when the monthly deliverables begin."

## Decision 2 — Equity Percentages

**What Calvin proposed:** 2.5% at 5K subscribers + 2.5% at 10K = **5% total**

**What the SOW draft offers:** 1.5% at 5K + 1.5% at 10K = **3% total**

**What the market pays:** 0.25–0.75% total for a part-time fractional operator with other clients.

**Jesse's opening counter:** 1% at 5K + 1% at 10K = **2% total**. This is your opening — not your floor. It's still 3-8x market rate and gives you room to move.

**If Calvin pushes back:** Move to 1.5%+1.5% = 3% total. This is where the draft sits, and it's where you'll likely land. Don't open there.

**Your floor:** 1.5%+1.5% = 3% total. Don't go higher.

**Calvin's likely counter:** "2%+2% as a compromise." If he opens there, that's a signal his real floor is 1.5%+1.5%. Hold the 1.5%+1.5% line.

**The argument that lands:** "You're doing great work, but you're also running four other clients including the BG social retainer. Our equity reflects that this is a part-time role, not a co-founder commitment. 1.5%+1.5% is significantly above market for that. I want this to feel good for both of us — I just can't give away co-founder equity to a contractor."

**Important:** Do not mention the market benchmark numbers unless Calvin specifically asks. They're useful context for you; they'll feel like a lecture if you recite them.

## Decision 3 — Acquisition Treatment

**What the SOW says:** Binary. If milestone is hit at acquisition close, equity vests. If not hit, it lapses. No pro-rata.

**The problem:** At 4,999 subscribers, Calvin gets 0% even if acquired for £10M. That's harsh and creates perverse incentives — Calvin could actually slow-play growth to avoid triggering a milestone that would then disappear in an acquisition.

**The better clause:** Pro-rata vesting on acquisition. If Calvin hits 80% of the 5K subscriber milestone at close, he gets 80% of the first equity tranche. Simple, fair, removes the cliff problem.

**This is one to offer Calvin unprompted** — he'll see it as fair dealing, and it is. It costs you slightly more in an edge case but removes a structural misalignment.

**The specific language to add:** "On acquisition: if active subscriber count at close is between 50% and 100% of a milestone threshold, Calvin receives a pro-rata portion of the corresponding equity grant. Below 50%, the milestone lapses."

**Jesse's position:** "I want to add a pro-rata clause on acquisition — I think the cliff is unfair to you and I'd rather the document reflect what we both actually intend."

## How to Run the Conversation

**Format:** WhatsApp or a 30-minute call. Don't do this over email — too much room for misunderstanding on both sides.

**Opening:** "I've read the draft. I want to agree a few things before we send this over. Can we talk through three items?"

**Sequence:** Start with Decision 3 (pro-rata acquisition). It's the one where you're giving Calvin something — sets a collaborative tone. Then Decision 2 (equity). Then Decision 1 (start date). Easiest last.

**Close:** "I'll ask Junior to update the draft and send it to you as a clean version for e-sign by [date]. Does that work?"

**Timeline:** This call happens first week back (week of May 21). Send clean SOW by May 23. Calvin signs by May 26.

## If Calvin Says No to the Whole Thing

He won't. But if he balks at equity — hear the specific objection. If it's the percentages, you have room. If it's the milestone thresholds (5K and 10K feel too high), consider tightening: 3K and 7K subscribers with lower equity (1%+1%) is structurally tighter but achieves similar economics.

If he says he won't proceed without 2.5%+2.5%, the decision is yours. But know that you would be paying co-founder equity rates for a contractor, which sets a precedent for every future hire who hears about it.

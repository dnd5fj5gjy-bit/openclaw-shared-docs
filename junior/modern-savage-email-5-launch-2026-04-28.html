Part 1 — What Email 5 Must Do

This is the email that takes money.

Everyone on the list has now received four emails over roughly six weeks. They know why Bear built this, what's in it, how he takes it, and that the founding price is closing. Email 5 is not a sales email — it's a doors-open announcement. The selling was done in emails 1-4.

The tone must be: Quiet confidence. Not excited-to-launch energy. Not "WE'RE LIVE!!!" Bear doesn't do that. He announces. He lets the thing speak for itself.

What this email must contain:

  • The product is available now
  • The link to buy
  • One thing people haven't heard before — something that makes this feel like a genuine moment, not just a transaction

What it must not do:

  • Hype
  • "This is the moment we've been waiting for"
  • Countdown language that implies limited stock (unless it's true — check with Ships A Lot before sending)
  • Thank the reader for "being on this journey" — that's not Bear's voice

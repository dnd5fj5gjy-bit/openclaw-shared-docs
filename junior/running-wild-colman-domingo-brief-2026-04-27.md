## Who Colman Domingo Is Right Now

Two back-to-back Best Actor Oscar nominations (Rustin 2024, Sing Sing 2025) — the first person to achieve this since Denzel Washington. He didn't win either year. He talks about this not as loss but as arrival: "I think it taught me that I have a larger voice... I'm fully evolved as myself."

He is an actor, writer, director, designer, and performance artist. His Instagram bio is literally "Love, love and love." He's been married to producer Raúl Domingo for over a decade — they met when Raúl posted a Craigslist ad looking for Colman specifically (describing his fo-hawk), found him 2 hours later in a Walgreens. They went to the Oscars together in 2025.

**His core public philosophy is "Radical Love":** seeing people fully, treating everyone with dignity, dismantling what he calls "toxic masculinity" through vulnerability and openness.

This is one of the most emotionally articulate people who will ever appear on Running Wild. The wilderness setting gives him something to respond to — and he will respond thoughtfully.

## What Connects Colman to Bear's Audience

Colman's fanbase is not outdoor people. They're drawn to authenticity, emotional depth, and artistic integrity. Bear's audience values challenge, connection, and realness.

The bridge: **vulnerability under pressure.** Colman's entire practice is about being honest when it's hard. That's exactly what Running Wild creates. Two very different men, stripped of performance, finding out who they are when there's nowhere to hide.

Secondary bridge: **faith.** Colman speaks openly about faith as a throughline — not church-specific but spiritual, about grace and holding on to each other. Bear's audience resonates with this deeply. Use it.

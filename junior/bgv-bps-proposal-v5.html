The Vision

Something worth coming for.

WILD is the entry point. Behind it sits a connected hub across three units that functions as a living, breathing community centre for adventure, wellness and purpose. A place where people drink, train, shop, learn, work and belong, all under one roof.

Every BMF class, every DIRTEA event, every book signing, every product launch and every Modern Savage activation pulls in an audience that might never have set foot in the Power Station otherwise. The footfall is something we generate, not something we hope walks past.

The Case

Why this beats a standard bar tenancy.

F&B is the brief. WILD delivers F&B, then stacks an entire wellness ecosystem on top. Same square footage, materially more value to the Power Station, both commercially and in terms of brand positioning.

Option A

A standard bar

Conventional F&B tenancy
  • ◇ Peak hours only. Activity concentrated in evenings and weekends.
  • ◇ Interchangeable. One of many similar options visitors have already seen elsewhere.
  • ◇ Minimal social footprint. Limited content value for the Power Station brand.
  • ◇ Single function. Customers arrive, consume, leave.
  • ◇ Turnover risk. Hospitality tenancies are volatile.
  • ◇ Competes with existing bars and restaurants already at the Power Station.
Recommended
Option B

The Bear Grylls Hub

WILD plus a working ecosystem
  • ✓ All-day footfall. WILD opens early. Classes, work, shopping and events run from morning to evening, seven days a week.
  • ✓ Completely unique. A wellness-led coffee stand fronting a brand ecosystem of this scale does not exist elsewhere in London.
  • ✓ Constant content creation tagging the Power Station to an audience of millions.
  • ✓ Multiple reasons to visit. Drink, train, shop, attend events, meet the team, try products.
  • ✓ Long-term tenant with deep investment in the location and genuine commitment to the community.
  • ✓ A complement to existing restaurants, not a competitor. WILD is takeaway-focused, not a sit-down bar.

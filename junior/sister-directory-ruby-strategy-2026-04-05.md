# Sister Directory: Strategy for Ruby

**Date:** 2026-04-05  
**Prepared by:** Junior

## What Ruby should do
Ruby should run Sister Directory like a curator, not a content poster.
Her job is to make the brand feel useful, trusted, and worth sharing every week.

## The simple job
Ruby needs to do three things well:
1. **Curate** the best clean-beauty / non-toxic finds.
2. **Publish** consistently on Instagram and the site.
3. **Drive** people back to the site with a reason to return.

## What the site is now
The site currently positions Sister Directory as a clean beauty and non-toxic product guide. That means the brand is not a generic directory. It is a trust-led discovery tool.

## Ruby's weekly operating rhythm
### Monday
- Pick the week's featured products / brands / entries.
- Decide the main theme for the week.
- Post one founder/curator update on Instagram.

### Tuesday
- Publish one high-value post or carousel.
- DM or email 5 relevant people / brands / creators.
- Ask for submissions, shares, or recommendations.

### Wednesday
- Add or update listings on the site.
- Reply to comments and DMs.
- Re-share one community post.

### Thursday
- Publish a proof post: testimonial, comparison, or founder note.
- Push one referral action: submit, share, or nominate.

### Friday
- Post a roundup: best new finds, best new additions, or weekly highlights.
- Review what got clicks and saves.

### Weekend
- Light community engagement only.
- Collect any inbound submissions.
- Prep next week's theme.

## Content pillars Ruby should use
1. **Discovery**
   - best finds
   - hidden gems
   - recommended products
   - useful lists

2. **Trust**
   - why this is clean
   - why this matters
   - why this product made the cut
   - what to avoid

3. **Community**
   - user submissions
   - featured women-led brands
   - creator spotlights
   - recommendations from people the audience trusts

## Instagram strategy
Ruby should make Instagram a traffic engine, not a vanity page.

### The feed should do three jobs
- Explain what Sister Directory is
- Show why it is worth trusting
- Push people to the site

### Every post should have one clear action
- Visit the site
- Save the post
- Submit a brand
- Share with a friend
- Follow for weekly picks

## Growth moves Ruby can run immediately
1. **Launch a submission call**
   - "Know a clean beauty brand we should include?"
   - "What product should we review next?"

2. **Start a weekly featured post**
   - one hero product
   - one short reason it made the cut
   - one CTA to the site

3. **Use social proof hard**
   - repost recommendations
   - screenshots of positive comments
   - simple testimonials

4. **Build relationships with women-led brands**
   - featured slots
   - reciprocal shares
   - founder shoutouts

5. **Create one recurring format**
   - weekly edit
   - monthly best of
   - “three clean swaps we love”

## What Ruby should avoid
- Posting random filler
- Making it feel like a directory with no taste
- Over-explaining
- Trying to do too many categories at once
- Treating Instagram and the site as separate worlds

## Jesse's role
Jesse should use his reach to open doors and amplify the best moments.
He should not be the day-to-day operator.
He should make the thing feel like it matters.

## My opinion
Ruby's edge is curation and consistency.
If she can make Sister Directory feel like the place people trust for clean beauty and non-toxic finds, the audience growth will come from utility and repetition, not noise.

## Immediate 2-week mission for Ruby
- Lock the editorial voice.
- Publish 2-3 strong posts per week.
- Add 10-15 excellent featured entries.
- Drive submissions from the existing Instagram audience.
- Create one clear weekly reason to come back.

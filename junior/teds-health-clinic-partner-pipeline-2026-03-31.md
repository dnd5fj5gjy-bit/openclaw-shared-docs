# Ted's Health Clinic Partner Pipeline Brief

**Date:** 2026-03-31  
**Prepared by:** Junior

## Executive Summary

Ted's Health already has the right strategic pivot: stop fighting for every D2C customer and become the backend engine for existing clinics that already own patient attention. The missing piece was a concrete outbound map. This brief turns the white-label thesis into a practical UK partner pipeline with target segments, example clinics, qualification criteria, and a 30-day founder-led outreach plan.

**Bottom line:** Ted's should prioritise a **3-wave clinic partner strategy**:
1. **Wave 1 — specialist TRT clinics** that already believe in testosterone care but may lack scalable operations, follow-up systems, or white-label infrastructure.
2. **Wave 2 — broader digital men's health brands** already selling ED, diagnostics, hair, or weight-loss products and can add TRT / hormone optimisation as a higher-LTV line.
3. **Wave 3 — established pharmacy / online doctor platforms** that have trust and traffic but move slower; these are better as credibility partnerships once Ted's has pilot proof.

If Jesse wants the fastest path to revenue, start with **Wave 1 and Wave 2**. They are closest to the pain, easiest to pitch, and most likely to understand the value of outsourced clinical + operational infrastructure.

## Strategic Frame

Ted's should not position itself as “another TRT clinic.” That is a red-ocean fight.

Ted's should position as:

> **The fastest way for a UK clinic or digital health brand to launch or upgrade a profitable men's hormone line without building the full operating stack themselves.**

That stack can include:
- clinical assessment workflows
- patient onboarding
- prescription and pharmacy coordination
- fulfilment / logistics
- follow-up cadence
- blood test workflows
- retention systems
- patient comms and support layers

The strongest buyers are not generic healthcare companies. They are businesses that already have one or more of these assets:
- male audience trust
- an existing patient base
- paid acquisition capability
- a doctor/pharmacy relationship but weak operations
- adjacent men's health products but no TRT offer

## UK Partner Market Map

### Segment 1 — Specialist TRT Clinics

**Why they matter:** they already understand the category and patient economics. The pitch is not “should you offer TRT?” but “can we help you scale, improve retention, expand geographies, or simplify delivery?”

**What they likely want:**
- smoother onboarding and patient management
- lower operational overhead
- better follow-up systems
- more flexible fulfilment and logistics
- a backend partner for overflow, white-label expansion, or spinout brands

**Example operators surfaced tonight:**
- **Leger Clinic** — established UK TRT specialist; promotes home testing, doctor-led consultations, transfer-from-another-provider flow, and aggressive value pricing.
- **The Men's Health Clinic** — doctor-led premium specialist positioning; strong emphasis on proper assessment, clinic-first care, and remote + in-clinic support.

**How to pitch them:**
- overflow capacity / expansion support
- operations layer for a secondary brand or new acquisition channel
- better patient retention infrastructure
- faster launch of sub-brands, affiliate channels, or regional offers

**Why this segment is attractive:**
- shortest sales-cycle to belief
- highest sophistication around TRT economics
- easiest for Ted's to prove value in concrete operational terms

**Risk:** some specialist clinics will view Ted's as a competitor, not partner. Only pursue where the angle is clearly “enablement” rather than “replacement.”

### Segment 2 — Digital Men's Health Brands

**Why they matter:** they already monetise male health intent but often focus on lower-complexity products like ED, hair loss, blood tests, supplements, or weight management. TRT becomes a premium product line with stronger retention and higher annual revenue per patient.

**Example operators surfaced tonight:**
- **Numan** — broad digital men's health platform spanning weight loss, low testosterone, ED, diagnostics, supplements, consultations, and health coaching.
- **Manual** — broad men's health brand with ED, hair, educational content, pharmacy infrastructure, and a dedicated TRT property / testosterone education layer.

**What they likely want:**
- specialist hormone pathway without building everything internally
- higher-LTV upsell from existing male customer base
- stronger clinical operating layer than generic e-commerce healthcare flows
- additional monetisation of diagnostics and clinician traffic

**How to pitch them:**
- “you already own the audience; Ted's gives you a high-value hormone care engine”
- “launch faster than building an in-house TRT ops team”
- “use Ted's for a white-labelled specialist pathway while keeping brand ownership”

**Why this segment is attractive:**
- strong traffic and acquisition capability already exists
- adjacent products create natural upgrade paths
- easiest place to justify revenue-share model because uplift can be measured

**Risk:** large digital brands may already be building internally. The best entry point is a faster pilot, niche sub-brand, or specialist care lane rather than a whole-company replacement pitch.

### Segment 3 — Pharmacy / Online Doctor Platforms

**Why they matter:** these businesses have trust, dispensing capability, and consumer familiarity. They can become strong distribution partners, but usually move slower and may be more compliance-heavy.

**Example operator surfaced tonight:**
- **Boots Online Doctor** — broad digital clinic with home tests, prescriptions, fulfilment, and established consumer trust.

**What they likely want:**
- specialist men's hormone capability without designing it from scratch
- a lower-risk test case with outsourced clinical operations
- evidence that the service can work safely and commercially before a deeper rollout

**How to pitch them:**
- narrow pilot first
- “specialist service lane” rather than full white-label overhaul
- evidence-led partnership once Ted's has pilot metrics elsewhere

**Why this segment is attractive:**
- brand trust can create large patient volumes
- strong long-term credibility if landed

**Risk:** procurement, legal, and governance cycles will be slow. Do not make this the first battlefield.

## Best ICPs for Ted's First 10 Conversations

The best initial conversations are not with the biggest brands. They are with operators that have:
- proof they already care about men's health
- an existing funnel or patient list
- enough ambition to launch a new line quickly
- not enough internal infrastructure to build perfectly alone

**Best initial ICP #1:** founder-led or doctor-led TRT / men's clinics with clear category knowledge but operational pain.

**Best initial ICP #2:** digital men's health brands already monetising ED, diagnostics, or weight loss that want a high-ticket/high-retention hormone offer.

**Worst first ICP:** giant retail healthcare groups with long procurement cycles.

## Outreach Angles by Segment

### For specialist TRT clinics

Lead with:
- patient onboarding speed

- new channel launch without extra headcount

### For digital men's health brands
Use this angle:

> “You already own male health demand. Ted's gives you a specialist hormone pathway that lifts LTV and retention without forcing you to build the whole clinical/ops stack from scratch.”

Lead with:
- high-LTV product expansion
- revenue-share economics
- faster time-to-launch versus internal build

### For pharmacy / online-doctor platforms
Use this angle:

> “Ted's can provide a tightly-scoped men's hormone service lane that lets you test demand safely before committing internal product and ops resources.”

Lead with:
- narrow pilot
- governance-friendly setup
- evidence and KPI transparency

## 30-Day Founder-Led Execution Plan

### Week 1 — Build the hit list
- Finalise top 20 UK targets split: 8 specialist clinics, 8 digital men's health brands, 4 pharmacy/online doctor groups.
- For each target, capture: decision-maker, current men's health offer, obvious gap, likely partnership angle.
- Pair this brief with the prior economics toolkit so every outreach has a clear commercial story.

### Week 2 — Run founder-quality outbound
- Send tailored outreach to 10 targets only. Keep quality high.
- Prioritise intros through operators, clinicians, investors, or suppliers already in network.
- Offer a **15-minute “TRT backend expansion” call** rather than a vague partnership chat.

### Week 3 — Convert interest into a pilot structure
- For warm responses, present a simple pilot:
  - 90 days
  - defined patient volume target
  - setup fee + monthly minimum + revenue share
  - agreed success metrics: conversion, retention, time-to-treatment, NPS / support burden

### Week 4 — Tighten the story from live feedback
- Refine objections, pricing, and deliverables based on calls.
- Turn any positive signal into a one-page case-study style narrative, even before a full launch.
- Use those learnings to move up-market into slower but larger accounts.

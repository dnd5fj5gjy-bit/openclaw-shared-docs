# Ted's Health Clinic Partner Offer Brief

**Date:** 2026-03-27  
**Prepared by:** Junior

## Executive summary

Ted's Health should package itself as the **operating layer for men's health clinics**, not another consumer telehealth brand. The pitch is simple: Ted's provides the backend that lets a clinic launch or upgrade a men's health offer without having to build prescribing operations, patient comms, logistics, repeat ordering flows, or optimisation systems from scratch.

This brief turns that strategy into a usable sales document. It defines the offer, the target clinic profile, the commercial structure, implementation shape, objections, and the immediate assets needed to start selling.

## The core offer

**Positioning:** White-label men's health backend for UK clinics.

Ted's Health provides a clinic with:
- branded or co-branded patient journey design
- telemedicine workflow and eligibility intake
- prescription coordination and repeat fulfilment logic
- patient support operations and retention flows
- treatment programme reporting and optimisation support
- growth assets for landing pages, email/SMS nurture, and conversion scripts

A clinic keeps the patient-facing brand and primary relationship. Ted's becomes the engine room.

## Why this angle is strong

Most clinics do not want to become software companies or operational specialists. They want:
- faster launch time
- lower operational complexity
- better patient retention
- more predictable recurring revenue
- less dependence on ad-hoc admin work

Ted's can win by selling **speed, reliability, and recurring economics**.

## Ideal customer profile

Best first partners:
1. UK clinics already treating men in adjacent categories: TRT, weight loss, sexual health, diagnostics, longevity.
2. Practices with clinical credibility but weak operational systems.
3. Founder-led or small-group clinics that need to move quickly and care about margin.
4. Clinics with existing traffic or patient lists but poor follow-up and retention.

Avoid first:
- enterprise health groups with slow procurement
- clinics wanting fully bespoke builds before proving demand
- partners without clinical ownership or prescribing clarity

## What Ted's should sell

### Option A — Launch-in-a-box
For clinics that want a fast start.

Includes:
- intake flow and treatment pathway template
- patient onboarding sequence
- repeat order and reminder flows
- support SOP starter pack
- reporting dashboard spec
- 30-day implementation support

**Best for:** new service line launches.

### Option B — Operational upgrade
For clinics already running a men's health service with messy operations.

Includes:
- funnel and conversion audit
- retention and repeat ordering redesign
- patient communications overhaul
- operational bottleneck review
- KPI dashboard and reporting cadence

**Best for:** clinics with demand but poor economics.

### Option C — Full white-label backend
For partners wanting Ted's as the ongoing operating layer.

Includes:
- ongoing workflow management
- fulfilment and recurring treatment operations design
- lifecycle messaging and retention optimisation
- monthly performance review
- experimentation roadmap

**Best for:** recurring revenue partnerships.

## Commercial structure

Recommended starting model:
- **setup fee:** paid implementation/onboarding fee
- **monthly retainer:** covers operations layer, optimisation, reporting, support
- **performance upside:** optional revenue share tied to active patients, refill rate, or retained monthly revenue

Suggested deal logic:
- keep pricing simple enough to close in one meeting
- avoid bespoke quoting on early deals unless complexity is obvious
- use 2 to 3 standard packages, then customise only after partner traction

## Proof points Ted's needs in the deck

Before aggressive outreach, Ted's should be able to show:
- target conditions / pathways supported
- launch timeline and operating model
- expected impact on conversion, retention, and admin burden
- example patient lifecycle map
- sample KPI view for the partner
- compliance-aware operating boundaries

Even if hard numbers are still early, the partner must feel that Ted's has a real operating system rather than a vague consultancy pitch.

## First sales narrative

Use this sequence in calls and one-pagers:

1. **Clinics are leaving money on the table** because men's health programmes are operationally messy.
2. **Ted's already understands the patient journey** and the recurring treatment economics.
3. **Instead of hiring ops, CRM, growth, and workflow capability separately**, the clinic plugs into Ted's backend.
4. **The result:** faster launch, cleaner patient experience, better refill behaviour, and stronger unit economics.

## Common objections and answers

### "Why not build this ourselves?"
Because building the system is slower, riskier, and distracts clinical leadership. Ted's compresses months of trial and error into a ready operating model.

### "Are you a clinic or a software provider?"
Neither framing is perfect. Ted's is the **specialist men's health operating partner**. Software is part of it; workflows, fulfilment logic, retention ops, and programme optimisation are the real value.

### "How much internal effort is required from us?"
Minimal. The clinic needs a clinical owner, brand input, and approval on workflows. Ted's handles the implementation architecture and operating cadence.

### "What makes this credible?"
Category focus. Ted's is not a general agency. It is built around men's health pathways and recurring programme economics.

## Risks to address now

1. **Website trust issue:** current public notes indicate a Cloudflare/SSL handshake problem on tedshealth.co.uk. That hurts credibility and must be fixed before outbound partner conversations scale.
2. **Proof gap:** if no polished deck exists, Ted's can sound conceptual. The sales material must show a concrete operating model.
3. **Compliance ambiguity:** every partner discussion needs clear boundaries around prescribing, clinical responsibility, marketing claims, and patient communications.
4. **Over-customisation risk:** early deals can become service-heavy if Ted's does not productise the offer.

## Why This Matters

Paul Saladino built Heart & Soil to $50M by telling a single story compellingly and repeatedly. The story was: "I'm a doctor. I rejected mainstream nutrition. This is what I found." Bear's story is better — because it's real and it's been lived for 30 years, not discovered in a clinic.

The failure mode: Bear talks about Modern Savage like an endorsement ("I've partnered with this great brand..."). The success mode: Bear talks about Modern Savage like it's been part of his life forever, and the brand is just finally making it accessible.

## The Core Narrative (Long Form — Email 1, podcast, keynote)

*In Bear's voice, first person:*

---

There's something that happens on a long expedition when the modern world stops being available.

No protein bars. No supplements. No carefully formulated nutrition science. Just what's there.

I've learned more about human nutrition from those moments — spending weeks in the Arctic, the Amazon, the Sahara — than from anything I've read in a book. And what I kept discovering, in every culture, in every extreme environment, is that the parts of an animal that modern people throw away are the parts that serious people have always fought over.

The liver. The heart. The kidney. Soldiers, hunters, explorers — they all knew this. When you kill an animal in the field, the organs go to the strongest. Not the muscle. The organs. Every traditional culture on earth figured this out independently. Ours is the only one that forgot it.

I started eating this way on expeditions out of necessity. I kept eating this way because nothing I've tried — no modern supplement, no shake, no stack — has made me feel the same.

I'm not a nutritionist. I'm someone who has tested what works in places where what doesn't work has consequences. Modern Savage is what I take, what my family takes, and what I'd have carried with me on every expedition if I could have. We've just spent two years making it as clean and pure as the real thing.

This is what got left out of the modern diet. We're putting it back.

## Interview Q&A (expected questions + suggested Bear answers)

**Q: "Is this just another celebrity supplement brand?"**

Bear: "I've been eating like this for three decades. The brand exists because my family kept asking where to get what I take. There wasn't a clean version available. We made one."

**Q: "Why organ supplements specifically?"**

Bear: "Every traditional culture on earth found their way to organ meats. Every serious military or hunting culture prioritised them. We're the only civilisation in history that decided to throw that knowledge away. I've eaten liver in conditions where you don't have the luxury of being squeamish, and I've felt the difference. That's not opinion. That's 30 years of field experience."

**Q: "What makes Modern Savage different from competitors?"**

Bear: Don't mention competitors by name. Say: "Most supplement brands are built in a lab. This one is built around what I've actually eaten in the places where I couldn't afford it not to work. There's a difference between something designed to sound impressive and something designed to perform."

**Q: "Why now?"**

Bear: "Two years ago I started thinking seriously about what I actually want to eat for the next 30 years — not just when I'm in the field, but every day. I couldn't find a product I fully trusted. So we made one."

**Q: "What about the sustainability angle — aren't organ meats from factory farms?"**

Bear: Be specific: freeze-dried organ meats, sourced carefully. Acknowledge the question directly: "That matters to us. We've taken time to get the sourcing right. It's not the fastest route to market but it's the honest one."

## What to Avoid

- Do NOT compare to competitors or reference "others in the space"
- Do NOT use the word "supplement" too early — lead with the food/nutrition story
- Do NOT use gym/performance language ("gains", "performance", "recovery") — this positions it as a workout product, not a food philosophy
- Do NOT mention price in the origin story
- Do NOT rush to "and here's where you can buy it" — earn the trust first

# Bear x Wildernests: Three Activation Concepts
*Built April 29, 2026*

---

## The core thesis

Bear Grylls doesn't fit Wildernests as an endorser. He fits it as a *demonstration*. He has already lived the philosophical premise of the brand his entire life - remote, off-grid, no permission needed. That distinction is the difference between a paid post that gets scrolled past and a brand association that compounds over years.

The three concepts below are ordered by immediacy. The first activates this month. The second scales through Q3. The third runs indefinitely.

---

## Concept 1: The Expedition Launch
*May 19-21, 2026 - timed to Wildernests soft launch*

**What it is:** Bear is sailing from Southampton to Norway/Iceland/Greenland from May 7. He will be in exactly the locations Wildernests renders show homes in - remote fjords, isolated coastline, zero infrastructure for miles. The brand launches while he's there.

**What it looks like:**

During the launch window (approx May 19-21), Bear posts once from wherever he is - a Norwegian fjord, an Icelandic bay, wherever the voyage takes him. Not a scripted post. One line. Something real:

*"Three days from the nearest town. No grid. No noise. Just this. This is what Wildernests was built for."* - tag @wildernests.

That's it. No product visible. No voiceover. Just the landscape and the philosophy.

**Why it works:** It's not an endorsement - it's proof of concept. Bear is the Wildernests customer in the exact moment the brand launches. The renders show what the home looks like in wild locations. Bear shows what life in those locations actually feels like. They reinforce each other.

**What it needs:** Jesse confirms with Alex Aboshiha that May 19-21 is the soft launch window. Bear gets one brief: "post once from wherever you are on launch day, tag Wildernests, say what you actually feel."

**Risk:** Sailing schedule is weather-dependent. Bear might be in a port rather than a dramatic location on launch day. Mitigation: expand window to a week, post when the location is right.

## Concept 2: The Bear Grylls Edition
*Q3 2026 - when HD renders and production capacity allow*

**What it is:** Bear co-designs a specific configuration of the Wildernests home. His choices. His requirements. His aesthetic. A limited-run edition named for him.

**What his version would have:**
- Exterior: military green or raw steel finish (not the standard palette)
- Interior: minimal, stripped back. Utility over comfort.
- Built-ins: weapon/tool storage, first aid station, weather monitoring display
- External: larger covered deck (Bear always needs an outdoor working area)
- No compromise on the autonomy specs - full off-grid, full water generation

**Pricing:** Standard Wildernests + 15-20% premium. Sold as a limited run of 25 homes.

**Why it works:** Gives Bear genuine creative ownership rather than a logo on someone else's product. His audience buys his judgment, not his endorsement. The limited run creates scarcity and a story the standard line can't replicate.

**What it needs:** One session with Bear, Alex, and a product designer. Two hours. Bear defines what he'd actually want. The team builds it. The edition sells faster than the standard model because the story is baked in.

**Revenue note:** At Wildernests price points, 25 homes at a 15% premium on whatever the base price is - the volume is small but the proof of concept is what matters.

## Concept 3: The "Off the Grid" Content Pillar
*Ongoing from June 2026*

**What it is:** Off-grid living is already a content category Bear naturally occupies. Wildernests becomes the permanent home for that category in his content mix.

**The format:** Once a month, Bear posts from a remote location. It doesn't need to be exotic. Just genuinely off-grid. A cliff on the Welsh coast. A mountain bothy in Scotland. A bay in Norway. The format is short - 30 to 60 seconds, or a still with three lines of copy. No product visible. No voiceover explanation. Just:

*"No grid. No neighbours. No signal. This is what I'm always looking for. @wildernests gives it to you permanently."*

**What Wildernests gets:** Month after month, in Bear's feed, the association between his identity and their brand is reinforced. His 26 million Instagram followers see it once a month. The algorithm treats consistent, engaged posts better than campaigns.

**What Bear gets:** A clear content pillar with a sponsor covering production costs (if needed) and one consistent brand relationship rather than a rotating carousel of outdoor brands that erode each other.

**The difference from a standard partnership:** This is not "post three times per campaign." It's "this is the off-grid content category in your feed, permanently. It lives here."

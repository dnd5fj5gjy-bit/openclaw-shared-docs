# Modern Savage — General Manager (Operations & Admin)

**Company:** Modern Savage Inc. (part of the Bear Grylls Ventures group)
**Reports to:** Jesse Grylls (Founder) and Raemy Singh (CFO)
**Location:** Remote (UK or US), flexible across time zones
**Start:** As soon as possible — launch is mid-September 2026
**Type:** Full-time (open to an initial contract-to-perm arrangement)

---

## The opportunity

Modern Savage is a premium health supplement brand backed by Bear Grylls Ventures, launching in the US in September 2026 with a UK line to follow. The hero product is a primal organ blend across three products: Adult, Kids and Summit. We launch with a warm audience of more than 55,000 emails and a founding community already in place.

The hard part is done: the formula is locked, the first production run is ordered, fulfilment and banking are signed, and the store is being built. What we need now is the person who makes the whole machine run.

This is a rare seat: employee number one in a founder-backed brand at the exact moment it goes live. You will see every part of the business, work directly with the founders, and build the operation that everything else grows on.

## The role

You are responsible for everything between "we decided it" and "it happened."

While the founders focus on brand, product and growth, you run the operational spine: suppliers, fulfilment, customers, compliance and company admin. In the first six months this is hands-on and self-directed. As the brand scales, you hire and build the processes around you — this role grows into running the company day to day.

## What you will own

**Launch (your first 90 days)**
- Run the launch plan as a project: one owner, one timeline, weekly status to the founders
- Close the open items that gate launch: spec sheets, label artwork, packaging print runs
- Have fulfilment, customer service and reporting live and working on day one

**Supply chain and production**
- Manage the US manufacturer and packaging vendors end to end: specs, quotes, purchase orders, timelines, quality
- Stand up the UK manufacturing track alongside the US one
- Plan inventory and reorder points across both markets

**Fulfilment and customers**
- Run the 3PL relationship: inbound stock, pick and pack, shipping issues, returns
- Own customer service from day one: templates, refunds, subscription changes, escalations
- Watch subscription health (churn, failed payments, delivery problems) and fix root causes, not symptoms

**Compliance and admin**
- Keep us compliant on both sides of the Atlantic: FDA labelling and claims in the US, FSA and ASA rules in the UK, insurance, registrations
- Handle contracts, invoices, supplier payments and filings, working with the CFO and external advisers
- Keep records clean so finance always has accurate numbers on stock, costs and supplier liabilities

## Who this suits

Direct experience in consumer products, supplements or operations is valuable but not essential. What matters is the raw material: we will back the right person and train the rest.

You are right for this if you are:

- **A finisher.** You close loops without being chased, and things you own do not fall through cracks
- **Highly organised.** Contracts, spreadsheets, supplier records and timelines stay tidy in your hands
- **Self-managing.** This is a small founding team, not a big company with support functions — you decide, act and report
- **A direct communicator.** You flag problems early and bring options, not just issues
- **Fast to learn regulated territory.** Supplement compliance can be taught; the appetite to master it cannot
- **Comfortable with negotiation.** Suppliers, 3PLs and agencies respond to someone who knows the numbers

**A bonus, not a requirement:** operations or supply chain experience at a consumer products company, subscription D2C (Shopify or Stripe), 3PL management, or having taken a physical product from purchase order to customer doorstep.

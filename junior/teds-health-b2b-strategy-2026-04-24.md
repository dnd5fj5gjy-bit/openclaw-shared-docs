# Ted's Health — B2B White-Label Strategy Brief
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Purpose:** Lay out the B2B clinic target market, competitive landscape, and approach before the Ted's Health Catchup

---

## The Market Reality (Read This First)

The UK digital men's health market is dominated:

- **Numan** — $90M revenue in 2024, 650,000 patients, just raised $60M more (July 2025). Expanding into women's health.
- **Manual** — major digital-first TRT/ED platform, large patient base.
- **Treated.com** — online prescriptions at scale.

Ted's Health at £6k MRR cannot compete with these players head-on. The D2C path leads into a wall.

**But that's not the play.**

The white-label B2B pivot is correct — and the competitive analysis actually strengthens the case. Numan and Manual have absorbed the digitally native patient. The patients who are *not* on these platforms are with **traditional, independent, private clinics** — the GPs, sports medicine practices, private gyms, and single-location men's health clinics that haven't built their own digital infrastructure and don't want to.

Those clinics are underserved. That's Ted's market.

---

## Target Segment — Who Ted's Should Be Selling To

### Tier 1 — High-Priority Targets

**Independent private men's health clinics (single or 2-3 location)**
- Already have patients and medical staff
- Lack digital prescription management, patient portal, recurring billing
- Want to offer TRT but admin overhead is the bottleneck
- Example types: The WellMan Clinic (40yr heritage), Balance My Hormones, Leger Clinic, Centre for Men's Health (Manchester)

**What they need:** Online consultation booking, hormone test results portal, repeat prescription management, patient messaging, payment processing. Ted's platform (if Felix ships it) could be a white-label version of all of this.

---

### Tier 2 — Adjacent Opportunities

**Private GP practices expanding into men's health**
- Increasing demand from patients for hormone optimization
- GPs have the prescription authority but lack the workflow tools
- TRT is increasingly mainstream; GPs want a structured program to offer it

**Sports medicine / performance clinics**
- High-HNW clients already spending on health optimization
- Blood testing and hormone panels are part of their service
- Adding TRT management is a natural extension; they need the clinical backend

**Corporate health / executive wellness programs**
- Companies offering private health to senior employees
- TRT/hormone optimization increasingly in demand from 40+ male executives
- Could be B2B2C: Ted's white-labels to corporate health provider

---

### Tier 3 — Longer Term

**Pharmacy chains / NHS private referral pathways**
- Bigger deal size, longer sales cycle, more regulated
- Not the first call to make, but worth monitoring
- Superdrug/Boots already in private prescriptions; partnership angle possible in 12+ months

## The Pitch to Clinics

The clinic owner's problem: "I have patients asking me for TRT. I can prescribe it. But managing the follow-ups, blood tests, repeat scripts, and billing takes my staff 3-4 hours per patient per year. I can't scale this without burning out my team."

Ted's solution: "We give you a white-labeled patient portal. Your branding, your patients. We handle the tech: online consultations, test result management, repeat prescription workflows, automated reminders, payment. You keep the clinical relationship and the margin. We charge a monthly SaaS fee per patient or a platform fee."

Key framing: **Ted's is not a competitor. Ted's is infrastructure.**

---

## Pricing Model (Working Hypothesis)

| Model | Structure | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Per-patient SaaS | £10-20/month per active patient | Aligns with clinic growth; scales with their success |
| Flat platform fee | £500-1,500/month per clinic | Predictable for clinics, good MRR baseline |
| Hybrid | £300/month base + £8/active patient | Best of both; most defensible |

A clinic with 50 active TRT patients at £15/month = £750/month. 10 clinics = £7,500/month. That's already above current D2C MRR with lower CAC.

## Risk Factors

- **Felix hasn't shipped yet.** If the white-label platform isn't demo-ready in 60 days, Ted's loses momentum and the B2B story has no proof point.
- **Regulatory complexity.** TRT prescriptions require Care Quality Commission (CQC) registration in the UK. Ted's must either have this or be positioning as a software-only provider (not clinical). Clarity on this is essential before any clinic pitch.
- **Numan pivoting down-market.** If Numan decides to build a white-label product for smaller clinics (they have the resources), they could enter this space. Move fast.

## What Alfred Is (Brief)

Alfred is a premium, curated members-only dating and social networking app. Application-only, waitlist entry. UK-operating, Delaware incorporated 2021. Think Raya — but intentionally more private. Their brand deliberately obscures its own leadership, which tells you something about their positioning philosophy: exclusivity is the product.

The founder/CEO is not publicly named anywhere. Tom Inskip and Alice Driscoll are the bridge — likely advisors or early connectors. May 5 may be the last meeting before Jesse gets introduced to whoever actually runs Alfred.

---

## Why This Meeting Is Different from a Normal Agency Pitch

Standard agency pitches lead with deliverables: "we'll post 5x a week, run stories, build community." That will kill this deal.

Alfred is not trying to go viral. Viral is the opposite of what they want. Their problem is:

- **Building social proof without breaking exclusivity.** They need their app to feel like something the right people already know about — without feeling like they're advertising.
- **Turning the waiting list into brand equity.** The waitlist IS the brand asset. People who want in are advertising Alfred to people they know just by wanting in.
- **Maintaining mystique while growing.** The hardest thing in premium dating is scaling without diluting. Raya lost cachet when it got too big. Alfred is trying to thread that needle.

Jesse's pitch isn't "we make great content." It's "we understand the psychology of the kind of person you're trying to attract — and we know how to make them feel like Alfred is theirs."

---

## The Bear Grylls Angle — Why This Specifically Works

Bear's audience skews: male, 30-50, high-income, adventure-inclined, identity-driven. These are exactly Alfred's people.

The implicit pitch: "Bear has 7M+ followers who are exactly your target demographic. I manage Bear's community. I understand how those people think, what makes them trust a brand, and how to make them feel like insiders. Most agencies see Alfred as a content brief. I see it as a community brief — which is completely different."

This isn't about cross-promotion (don't say Bear will post about Alfred). It's about bringing community-building intelligence that a normal PR or social agency cannot match.

## Commercial Structure — What to Ask For

Based on Jesse's April 9 email language ("I know how to build this"), he's positioning this as a founding-team-level contribution, not a vendor engagement.

**Target structure:**
- Monthly retainer: £3,000-£5,000/month (same range as Modern Savage/Calvin)
- Equity: 2-5% advisory/service equity, vesting over 2 years
- Scope: digital strategy, brand narrative, content direction (NOT execution — Bear Witness directs, a junior resource or Alfred's own team produces)

**Why equity matters:** If Alfred is a Raya-calibre success, 2% equity is worth millions. Jesse isn't selling content production — he's selling positioning, which compounds. Equity signals alignment: "I believe this will be worth something."

**Don't table the equity ask in meeting 3.** Save it for when they bring up commercial terms. Let them ask "what does this look like?" before you name the structure.

---

## Three Questions to Ask in the Meeting

These are designed to get you the information you need and to demonstrate that you think differently:

**1. "What does your current member think Alfred is — a dating app, a social network, or something else entirely?"**
This reveals whether Alfred has a positioning clarity problem or a distribution problem. If members can't define it, that's Jesse's biggest unlock.

**2. "When your best members recommend Alfred to someone, what do they say?"**
This tells you what's actually working organically. The best social strategy amplifies what already works — not what the brand thinks is working.

**3. "What happens on the platform between members that you'd never be able to show publicly?"**
This acknowledges that the best community moments are private. Jesse's pitch could include "protecting the private moments while building the right public moments" — and this question opens that door.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't bring a deck or proposal to this meeting. You're not in RFP mode.
- Don't name a price unless they ask. Let them bring it up.
- Don't mention Bear cross-promoting Alfred. That's not the pitch.
- Don't talk about engagement metrics. Alfred doesn't want virality.
- Don't pitch content production. Pitch positioning and community strategy.

The Council

💀 The Killer — What Goes Wrong

Ryan's three-tier structure is designed to benefit Fat Cow. Bear provides credibility and content (Tier 1) FOR FREE, then builds a product that makes Fat Cow more valuable (Tier 2), then finally invests his own capital (Tier 3). By the time real money flows to BG, Bear has already done all the heavy lifting.

Risk 2: Content comes first, product comes later. That means months of Bear's face attached to a brand before any revenue share agreement is locked. The content creates value for Fat Cow immediately. Bear sees nothing from it except a small revenue share on the content itself.

Risk 3: If this doesn't go to Tier 2, Bear gets stuck as a content creator for a skincare brand. No product. No equity. No income. The deal must be structured so product comes simultaneously with content, not after it.

👁 The Stranger — What Everyone's Missing

Jesse attended the April 9th call, not Bear. Ryan is pitching through Jesse as the gatekeeper. This means Ryan's deck was written for Bear's level — big investment, capital injection, equity. But Jesse's actual ask is faster income, possibly via Modern Savage alignment. These are different conversations.

The supplement trademark (bigfarmer.com) is a throwaway slide. Ryan mentions it in one paragraph. But for Jesse, this could be the most important line in the deck — it's the UK infrastructure for a supplement play that Bear Grylls Ventures doesn't currently have.

Fat Cow's ad spend (£25K/month) means they're already profitable but margin-dependent on paid traffic. They're not a grassroots brand — they're a performance marketing business. When Bear's organic audience hits, the LTV economics change dramatically. Ryan knows this. Bear's endorsement is worth more than capital to Ryan right now.

A BG-edition tallow balm or cream could be formulated, packaged, and launched in 60-90 days. Not 6-12 months. This is the leverage Jesse has — Fat Cow wants Bear's name urgently. Jesse can use that urgency to compress the roadmap.

Bundle "outside + inside" as a concept. Both sell to the same person. Cross-sell rate should be very high if the brand story is cohesive.

A BG-branded tallow product sold in the US would need US fulfillment — Fat Cow currently has zero US presence. That's Jesse's contribution to the international play.

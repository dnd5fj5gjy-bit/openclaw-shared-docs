## 4. THE THREE HIDDEN DEPENDENCIES NOBODY IS TRACKING

These do not appear on any existing plan and each one can independently break September.

### 4.1 The trademarked Summit ingredients are on the pouch critical path

BG Summit contains ingredients under third-party trademark licence. You cannot legally print a licensed ingredient name on a pouch without the licence agreement executed. The TLA agreements are still listed as open.

If the licence is not signed before the print run, there are only two outcomes: reprint the Summit pouch at full cost and full lead time, or strip the trademarked name off the panel and lose the ingredient story that justifies the price.

**This is a print-date dependency, not a launch-date dependency.** It is due earlier than most people think. Treat it as due now.

### 4.2 The lab results have a longer lead time than the product

The plan commits to per-lot third-party lab tests published on the website. That is the right call for a brand selling organ powders on trust. But independent lab turnaround is commonly two to three weeks from sample receipt, and samples cannot be pulled until the product is actually filled.

Filled 14 August plus three weeks lands the certificates around 4 September. That just clears 8 September, and only if the samples are couriered the same day they are pulled and the lab is booked in advance.

**Decide now, not in September:** either book the lab slot this week and pull retains on fill day, or accept that COAs go up as a post-launch update and say so on the site rather than promising something that is not there on day one.

### 4.3 Ships A Lot onboarding has not started and it is not instant

The contract was executed on 10 July. They are waiting for Jesse to connect on Slack. Onboarding a new brand into a 3PL means Slack channel, store connection, WMS mapping, SKU and barcode setup, carrier rate configuration, and a test order that actually ships. In practice that is two to three weeks of back-and-forth, and none of it can start until the first message is sent.

To have a tested pick-and-pack by 28 August, onboarding needs to be live by **mid-August at the very latest**. It should have started eleven days ago.

## 7. THE CONTINGENCY WORTH DECIDING IN ADVANCE

There is a version of this where the pouches slip and the stock is genuinely not in the warehouse on 8 September. If that happens, the instinct will be to push the launch. That would be the expensive mistake, because it throws away the HOS stage and seven weeks of podcast tagging.

**The alternative: open on 8 September as a founding-member release, ship in October.**

- The cart opens, the price is a founding price, the allocation is capped and the scarcity is real rather than manufactured.
- Bear stands on the HOS stage with a live product to point at.
- The waitlist converts while it is hot instead of cooling for another six weeks.
- Cash arrives before the ACF balance falls due.

**The constraint to respect:** do not take money against a vague date. In the US, the FTC's mail-order rule requires a stated shipping timeframe and explicit consent to any delay beyond it. In the UK, the default is thirty days unless another date is agreed. The clean way to run it is either charge on despatch with the card authorised at order, or charge up front against a clearly stated October ship window, prominently displayed, not buried in terms.

**Make this decision by Friday 14 August**, which is the point at which we will know whether the pouches landed. Deciding it in advance means it is a strategy rather than a scramble.

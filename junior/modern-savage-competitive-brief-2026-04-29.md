# Modern Savage — UK Competitive Landscape
*Built April 29, 2026. For Jesse's use when positioning Modern Savage at launch.*

---

## The short version

The UK organ supplement market is early-stage. There is one established competitor (Ancestral Supplements), one major US brand with no serious UK presence (Heart & Soil), and a long tail of small UK-made single-organ products. Modern Savage enters with better pricing, a stronger celebrity anchor, and a community model that none of the competitors have. The window is real.

---

## Primary competitor: Ancestral Supplements

**Launched UK:** March 2023. Three years in market.

**Channel:** Amazon UK only. No UK DTC presence. No community.

**Pricing:**
- Beef Liver: £52/month
- Beef Heart: £56/month
- Beef Kidney: £54/month
- Beef Spleen: £58/month

If a customer wants the same organ coverage as Modern Savage (liver + heart + kidney + spleen), they pay **£220/month** across four Ancestral products.

**Modern Savage at £59/month is 73% cheaper** for equivalent organ coverage.

**Positioning:** "Nature's best multivitamin." Functional, not aspirational. No celebrity. No brand story. No community.

**Origin:** US company (New Zealand and Australia sourced organs). Selling into UK via Amazon. British customers have no reason to feel any brand loyalty beyond the product.

**Their vulnerability:** Amazon is a search-first, loyalty-last channel. Customers who find Ancestral via Amazon search will just as easily find Modern Savage if it's there — or switch when Bear Grylls is on their Instagram.

---

## Secondary competitor: Heart & Soil (Paul Saladino)

**Status in UK:** US-based, ships internationally but no UK DTC operation. Not on Amazon UK in any serious way. Saladino's reach in the UK is real but his UK commercial presence is minimal.

**Why this matters:** Saladino's audience is the same audience Modern Savage is targeting. But he's positioned as a US expert brand, not a UK lifestyle brand. Bear Grylls is not Paul Saladino — Bear's brand is warmer, broader, more faith-and-family, less carnivore-ideology. These are different emotional territories.

**Their vulnerability:** Saladino's "no plant" extremism is a polarising position in the UK market. Bear's more balanced framing ("ancestral + modern") plays wider.

---

## UK-made alternatives (long tail)

Several small UK brands sell single desiccated organ capsules: Winston's, Human Food Bar, and various Amazon-native labels. None have celebrity backing, funding, or community infrastructure. All sell single organs at £30-55 per product. None pose a threat to a properly launched Modern Savage.

| Factor | Ancestral Supplements | Modern Savage |

| Channel | Amazon only | DTC first |

## The pricing story Calvin should tell

"The equivalent of what's in one Modern Savage bottle would cost you £220 in single-organ Ancestral Supplements products. We're £59. And ours comes with Bear Grylls telling you why it works."

That's not just a price argument. It's a value argument. Most of the target customer doesn't want to manage four different supplement bottles. They want one thing that works.

---

## What to watch before launch

1. **Amazon entry timing.** Modern Savage should be on Amazon UK at launch (or shortly after). If a customer searches "organ supplements UK" and finds Ancestral but not Modern Savage, that's a lost acquisition. Calvin's Amazon setup brief covers this — the window is tight given the June 17-July 15 ship date, but FBM from July 1 is achievable.

2. **Ancestral's marketing activity.** They've been in the UK three years without celebrity backing or real marketing. If they sense a well-funded competitor entering, they could move. Watch for any influencer activations on their UK channels around June-July.

3. **Paul Saladino's UK push.** If Saladino ever does a UK launch properly (unlikely but possible), it would eat into Modern Savage's launch window. He has the audience. He's never had a UK commercial presence. Monitor.

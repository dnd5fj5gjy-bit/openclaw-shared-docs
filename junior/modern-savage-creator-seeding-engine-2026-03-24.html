Executive Summary

Modern Savage should not try to win launch traction through broad influencer spend. The smarter move is a tight creator seeding engine built around 50 highly aligned male-performance, outdoors, discipline, and lifestyle creators who can make authentic content and convert into long-term affiliates.

The play is simple:

  • seed product to the right creator profiles
  • ask for low-friction content, not polished ads
  • turn the top performers into affiliates fast
  • reuse the best UGC across paid, email, landing pages, and Amazon

This document gives you the operating system to do that immediately: target archetypes, scoring model, outreach copy, gifting flow, content brief, KPI tracker, and a 30-day rollout plan.

What this solves

Modern Savage already has positioning. What it needs now is distribution.

A creator seeding engine gives the brand:

  • faster creative volume without waiting on a full content team
  • authentic proof from men who fit the brand
  • a repeatable top-of-funnel acquisition channel
  • early signals on which angles actually resonate
  • a bridge from organic social into affiliate and paid acquisition

Strategic Goal for the First 60 Days

The first 60 days are not about celebrity creators. They are about building a small, efficient creator flywheel.

Success looks like:

  • 50 targeted creators contacted
  • 20-25 product sends accepted
  • 10-15 creators post at least one piece of content
  • 5-8 creators become repeat affiliates
  • 20+ pieces of usable UGC created
  • 3-5 winning hooks identified for paid reuse

Recommended creator mix

Prioritize creators who feel like the brand:

  • disciplined, masculine, high-agency
  • visually credible but not over-polished
  • trusted by a male audience interested in performance, vitality, training, grit, and standards
  • comfortable showing routine, habits, product use, and personal recommendation

Creator Strategy for the First 60 Days

Phase 1 — Seed for signal (Weeks 1-2)

Build a first list of 50 creators and start outreach fast. Optimize for alignment, not reach.

Use a mix of:

  • Instagram creators with strong story posting habits
  • TikTok creators who film direct-to-camera routines and product takes
  • YouTube creators with shorts and lifestyle integration
  • hybrid creator-coaches with audience trust and conversion potential

Phase 2 — Convert signal into system (Weeks 3-4)

Double down on creators who:

  • reply quickly
  • post without excessive hand-holding
  • deliver clean, reusable footage
  • naturally match the Modern Savage identity

Offer those creators:

  • affiliate terms
  • fresh product drops
  • recurring gifting
  • custom landing pages or codes if volume warrants it

Phase 3 — Build a repeatable engine (Weeks 5-8)

Turn the first wins into a formal pipeline:

  • evergreen creator list
  • onboarding packet
  • UGC request brief
  • monthly creator reviews
  • paid-whitelisting shortlist if the content converts

Ideal Creator Archetypes

The point is not “fitness influencers.” It is men whose audience believes their habits matter.

1) Discipline / routine guy

  • **Why they matter:** Perfect fit for a daily ritual supplement
  • **Follower range:** 15k-150k
  • **Content style:** morning routines, work blocks, training, no-BS self-improvement
  • **Risk note:** can become generic productivity content if too polished
  • **Hooks:** “3 things that keep my energy stable”, “supplements I actually stick with”, “my non-negotiable morning stack”

2) Functional strength coach

  • **Why they matter:** Bridges performance and credibility
  • **Follower range:** 10k-100k
  • **Content style:** strength, mobility, athletic conditioning, recovery habits
  • **Risk note:** avoid creators who only care about pre-workout hype
  • **Hooks:** “what supports recovery outside training”, “daily foundation vs gym stim junk”, “what I use year-round”

3) Outdoors / hunting / hiking masculine lifestyle creator

  • **Why they matter:** Strong brand fit with the savage-but-modern identity
  • **Follower range:** 10k-250k
  • **Content style:** early starts, mountains, field prep, gear, resilience
  • **Risk note:** avoid survival-cosplay or politically radioactive personalities
  • **Hooks:** “what comes with me on every trip”, “travel and trail essentials”, “keeping energy up when days start early”

4) Combat sports / jiu-jitsu / MMA lifestyle creator

  • **Why they matter:** High-trust male audience, gritty but disciplined
  • **Follower range:** 8k-80k
  • **Content style:** training clips, recovery, weight cuts, routines
  • **Risk note:** avoid creators with heavy controversy or reckless drug talk
  • **Hooks:** “what I use to stay consistent between sessions”, “supplements that actually earn a place in my routine”

5) Founder / operator / high-performance entrepreneur

  • **Why they matter:** Speaks to men carrying responsibility, a key brand lane
  • **Follower range:** 5k-75k
  • **Content style:** business, training, routines, family, personal standards
  • **Risk note:** avoid fake hustle-posting and finance-bro cringe
  • **Hooks:** “energy is an operating advantage”, “my daily stack as a founder”, “habits that stop me feeling flat”

6) Men’s style / grooming / status creator with substance

  • **Why they matter:** Expands the brand beyond pure fitness while staying aspirational
  • **Follower range:** 20k-200k
  • **Content style:** presentation, standards, habits, confidence, lifestyle
  • **Risk note:** must still feel masculine and grounded, not vanity-only
  • **Hooks:** “what clean discipline looks like”, “habits that sharpen how you show up”, “daily standards for men”

7) Animal-based / carnivore / whole-food nutrition creator

  • **Why they matter:** Strong ingredient education fit
  • **Follower range:** 10k-150k
  • **Content style:** food choices, ancestral eating, daily nutrition
  • **Risk note:** avoid fringe medical claims and conspiracy-heavy accounts
  • **Hooks:** “why nutrient density matters”, “how I cover gaps when life gets busy”, “travel nutrition hacks”

8) Dad-performance / family-leadership creator

  • **Why they matter:** Broadens the market to men 30-45 with purchasing power
  • **Follower range:** 8k-100k
  • **Content style:** fatherhood, fitness, routines, responsibility
  • **Risk note:** must feel strong and credible, not cheesy family content
  • **Hooks:** “how I keep energy high with kids and work”, “what keeps me switched on”, “the habits that stop me running empty”

9) Tactical / former military / resilience creator

  • **Why they matter:** Strong alignment with grit, standards, and capability
  • **Follower range:** 15k-200k
  • **Content style:** training, preparedness, routine, resilience, mindset
  • **Risk note:** avoid creators who drift into extreme politics or macho parody
  • **Hooks:** “simple habits that keep me sharp”, “daily gear for performance”, “what earns a spot in the routine”

10) Micro-athlete creator in niche sports

  • **Why they matter:** Often cheaper, more trusted, and more responsive than broad creators
  • **Follower range:** 3k-30k
  • **Content style:** HYROX, CrossFit, endurance, climbing, obstacle racing
  • **Risk note:** some niches skew female or broad wellness; filter carefully
  • **Hooks:** “what supports my training block”, “my daily baseline stack”, “what helps consistency, not just intensity”

Creator Scoring Rubric (100 Points)

Use this before gifting. No one gets product without scoring.

CategoryPointsWhat good looks like
Audience fit25Majority male, US-heavy, interested in performance, discipline, outdoors, strength, vitality, or self-improvement
Brand fit20Feels like Modern Savage: masculine, premium, capable, not cringe
Content quality15Clear camera, strong presence, natural product integration potential
Engagement quality15Real comments, conversation, saves/shares signals, not fake engagement
Conversion potential10Good CTA habits, strong trust, story views or link behavior
Reliability10Posts consistently, replies to DMs, no signs of flakiness
Risk profile5Low controversy, low compliance risk, no obvious brand danger

Scoring thresholds

  • **85-100:** priority send + fast-track affiliate consideration
  • **70-84:** send product if budget allows
  • **55-69:** hold list / revisit later
  • **Below 55:** do not seed

Outreach System

Keep the tone short, confident, and low-friction. Do not sound like a junior influencer manager.

Initial DM template

Hey [Name] — been following your stuff. Strong fit for a new men’s performance brand we’re building called Modern Savage. It’s a premium animal-based formula built around clean energy, resilience, and daily edge. If you’re open, I’d love to send you a pack to try. No heavy brief — mostly looking for creators whose routine and audience genuinely fit the brand.

Warmer DM variant

Hey [Name] — your content feels very on-brand for something we’re building. Modern Savage is a premium men’s supplement brand focused on clean energy, resilience, and physical edge. Want me to send over a pack and a quick overview?

Cold email template

Subject: Modern Savage x [Name]

Hey [Name],

Reaching out because your content feels like a strong fit for a new brand we’re building: Modern Savage.

It’s a premium animal-based formula for men who want clean energy, resilience, and daily edge — positioned more refined than the usual “alpha” supplement noise.

We’re putting together a small first wave of aligned creators and would love to send you product to try if it feels relevant.

If you’re open, I can send a short overview and get a pack out to you.

Best,

[Your Name]

Follow-up 1 (3 days later)

Quick bump on this — think you’d be a strong fit for Modern Savage if you’re open to trying the product. Happy to send details.

Follow-up 2 (5-7 days later)

Last nudge from me — if creator partnerships aren’t a fit right now, no worries. If you want a pack, send over the best shipping email/address and I’ll sort it.

Gifting note to include in package

Built for men who want clean energy, resilience, and daily edge. No cringe. No fluff. Just a better baseline. If you love it, we’d love to see how it fits into your routine.

Affiliate invitation copy

Your content feels like a natural fit for the brand, and we’d love to move from gifting into something ongoing. If you’re up for it, we can set you up with an affiliate code/link and make this a repeat partnership.

Creator Content Brief

This should be a one-page brief sent only after a creator accepts product.

What we want

We want content that feels like real routine integration, not scripted ad reads.

Best-performing angle families

1. Morning routine / daily ritual

“This is part of how I start the day sharp.”

2. Energy / resilience / consistency

“This helps me keep a better baseline when life gets heavy.”

3. Training + recovery lifestyle

“This fits into the stuff I do to stay capable year-round.”

4. Standards / identity

“I care what I put in my body. This fits that standard.”

5. Travel / busy-life utility

“Useful when routines get messy and nutrition slips.”

Preferred content formats

  • 20-45 second direct-to-camera videos
  • morning routine clips
  • training bag / supplement drawer / countertop integration
  • story slides with honest first impressions
  • simple voiceover with B-roll
  • one still image + strong caption if video is not the creator’s strength

Content asks

  • show the product clearly
  • explain why it fits your routine
  • speak like a real user, not a brand rep
  • keep it grounded and masculine
  • mention taste/form/routine only if genuine
  • if it works well, include a soft CTA or code later in the relationship

Do not do this

  • no exaggerated before/after claims
  • no disease, treatment, or medical language
  • no “boosts testosterone by X” type claims
  • no over-sexualized positioning
  • no fake founder story or made-up experience
  • no try-hard “beast mode / alpha king” language

Safe language territory

Use phrases like:

  • supports daily energy
  • fits my routine
  • helps me feel more switched on
  • part of my baseline stack
  • supports resilience and consistency

Avoid phrases like:

  • cures fatigue
  • treats low testosterone
  • fixes hormone problems
  • guaranteed performance increase

Suggested Offer Structure

For initial gifting

  • free product send
  • no guaranteed payment for first touch
  • ask for honest feedback and optional content if genuinely liked

For creators who post quality content

  • affiliate code + tracked link
  • 10-20% commission depending on margin and contribution
  • performance bonuses for content volume or sales milestones
  • repeat product drops monthly

For breakout creators

  • custom landing page or bundle
  • content usage rights for paid ads
  • bonus for raw footage delivery
  • possible fixed fee if conversion is proven

30-Day Action Plan

Week 1 — Build and source

  • finalize brand one-pager and creator brief
  • build first list of 50 creators
  • score every creator before outreach
  • prioritize the top 20 for first contact
  • prepare package inserts and affiliate offer language

Week 2 — Outreach and first sends

  • send first 20 DMs/emails
  • follow up on non-responders
  • collect addresses from interested creators
  • ship first wave of product
  • log all activity in the tracker

Week 3 — Content capture and conversion

  • monitor deliveries and creator posts
  • request raw footage from the best creators
  • identify top 5 pieces of UGC for paid testing
  • invite the best responders into affiliate terms
  • update scorecards based on actual responsiveness and output quality

Week 4 — Double down

  • source 20 more creators based on what worked
  • repeat outreach with refined messaging
  • build a “top creators” shortlist
  • spin winning hooks into ad tests, PDP assets, and email creative
  • review seeding economics and decide whether to scale or narrow

5 Biggest Mistakes to Avoid

1. Sending product to big creators with weak fit

Reach is seductive. Fit converts.

2. Treating creators like ad inventory

The best content feels like routine, not sponsorship.

3. Over-briefing the first post

Give direction, not a script.

4. Ignoring micro-creators

Some of the best early performers will be creators under 25k followers.

5. Failing to operationalize wins

If a creator performs, move them into affiliate, recurring gifting, and paid reuse fast.

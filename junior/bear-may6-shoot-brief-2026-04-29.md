## Shot List

### A. HERO PIECE (90 seconds, piece to camera, autocue)

Single continuous take or best-of edit from 3-4 attempts. Bear facing camera, direct address. Natural light preferred, outdoor setting if possible (expedition/wilderness feel — not a studio). Bear speaks from autocue. Em runs the autocue.

**Marks to hit:**
- Opens: "There's something that happens on a long expedition when the modern world stops being available."
- The organ meat revelation: "Every traditional culture on earth figured this out independently. Ours is the only one that forgot it."
- The close: "This is what I take, what my family takes. Modern Savage is just making it accessible."

### B. SHORT CLIPS (15-30 seconds each, direct to camera)

Each clip is self-contained. Bear should deliver each one 3 times minimum — take selection happens in edit.

**Clip 1 — The 30-year hook (Instagram)**
> "I've been eating like this for 30 years. Not for a trend. On expeditions, when there was nothing else, I learned what serious people have always known — the organs are where the nutrition is. Modern Savage is just the cleanest way I've found to make that practical."

**Clip 2 — The military/hunter line (punchy, wide reach)**
> "Every hunter, every soldier, every explorer who's been in a real field environment has known this. When you're serious about survival, you eat the organ meat first. Not the muscle. The organs. Modern nutrition forgot that. We're putting it back."

**Clip 3 — The no-lab line (anti-supplement positioning)**
> "I'm not a nutritionist. I'm someone who's tested what works in places where what doesn't work has consequences. This is what I actually take. No lab. No manufactured story."

**Clip 4 — The call to action (launch)**
> "Modern Savage launches July 1. If you've ever wanted to eat the way your body was actually designed to — this is how."

### C. FAMILY/MIN ANGLE (60 seconds, piece to camera)

Softer delivery, more personal. Bear talks about the kids range. Autocue.

> "When we were formulating the children's range, I kept thinking — not what the label says is healthy. What is actually, measurably, densely nutritious in a way that their bodies were designed to process. Liver for children has been a staple in every culture that raised physically resilient kids. We've just made it taste like something they'll actually take. Min is ancestral eating, made practical."

### D. B-ROLL (no Bear speaking — product and environment)

Jago captures without Bear speaking:
- Product in Bear's hands (he holds capsule container, pours into palm)
- Product on natural surfaces (stone, wood, outdoors — no clinical white surfaces)
- Bear walking in natural setting (wide + close)
- Bear looking at horizon / landscape (used for social cover images)
- Close-up of Bear's hands / face (B-roll cutaway material)

## Technical Requirements

- **Lens:** 50mm or 85mm equivalent. Slightly compressed look. Not wide-angle.
- **Movement:** Static or very slow push for the hero piece. More dynamic cuts allowed for the 15-30 second clips.
- **Sound:** Lapel mic on Bear. Check levels before shooting. Don't rely on camera audio.
- **Lighting:** Natural light strongly preferred. If interior, large softbox from one side — no harsh ring light, no over-lit studio look. This brand is raw and ancestral, not clinical.
- **Background:** Outdoor natural setting (trees, stone, earth tones) OR stripped back interior with wood/leather texture. No office, no white walls.

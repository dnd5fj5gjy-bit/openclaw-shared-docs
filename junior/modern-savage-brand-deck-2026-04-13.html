The Brand

What Modern Savage is

Modern Savage is a whole-food nutrition brand built around a simple conviction: real food outperforms synthetic supplements every time. Our organ blend powders give families everything they need in one daily scoop - the nutrition humans ate for thousands of years before the supplement industry invented a synthetic substitute.

This is not a niche health brand for men who track macros. It is family nutrition - clean, whole-food, ingredient-led. One product that replaces the shelf of supplements most families are guessing at. 100% owned by the Grylls family, manufactured by ACF Pharma, launching simultaneously in the UK and US in July 2026.

Not a wellness brand. Not a bro supplement brand. Family nutrition done properly.

Narrative Territories

Mission narratives for social strategy

These are the narrative territories Modern Savage can authentically operate in. Each one represents a distinct problem that the product solves for a specific audience. Social strategy should build content pillars from these — each pillar educates on the problem, positions Modern Savage as the natural solution.

Family Nutrition
"The same nutrition, for every member of the family. One product that covers the whole household."
This is the primary territory. The family angle is what separates Modern Savage from every competitor in this space. No other organ nutrition brand has built a product suite for the whole household — adult and child, one philosophy. Content should celebrate family health without being preachy.
Whole Food Simplicity
"Stop guessing. One scoop replaces the shelf."
The convenience angle is underrated. Most health-conscious families are managing 6-10 different supplements with no clear picture of what they actually need. Modern Savage solves that — one daily powder with everything the body needs from whole-food sources. This is not dumbing down — it is the honest case for simplicity.
Real Ingredients, Real Sources
"Your body was built to run on this. Modern food just forgot."
The case for eating the whole animal. Organs as the original superfood. What happened when humans stopped eating like this. The ancestral baseline vs the modern synthetic default. Evidence-led content on why whole-food nutrition outperforms synthetic supplements at the cellular level.
Gut and Long-Term Health
"Vitality is not a supplement category. It is what happens when you eat correctly."
Micronutrient density of organs. Bioavailability of animal-sourced nutrients. Gut health (prebiotic + probiotic stack). Energy, clarity, immunity. Content that takes the science seriously without being dry — accessible for a parent who isn't a nutritionist.
Provide and Protect
"Feed your family the way you were supposed to."
The duty to give children real nutrition from the start. The Kids Blend as an act of parental responsibility. "You wouldn't feed them synthetic vitamins if you knew there was a better option." Strongest angle for the Kids Blend specifically.
Family, Faith and Legacy
"What you feed your family is a statement about what you believe."
Values-driven nutrition. Building family health over generations. The Kids Blend as legacy thinking. Connects with the faith-adjacent, family-first audience that follows Bear — who is openly Christian and known for his family values.
Freedom from Broken Systems
"The supplement industry is broken. This is the alternative."
The case against synthetic vitamins. What the standard supplement industry doesn't tell you. Processed food as a system failure. The relief of finding something that is genuinely what it claims to be. Connects with the sceptic who has tried everything.

Competitive Landscape

Where we sit in the market

The ancestral health supplement market is growing fast — US market moving from $512M to $1.23B+ by 2030. The primary benchmark is Heart and Soil (Paul Saladino). Our opportunity: the same product quality, with Bear Grylls' reach behind it.

Our unfair advantage

Bear Grylls has 100M+ impressions per month across social channels. No ancestral health brand has that kind of distribution. Heart and Soil built to $50M primarily through Saladino's podcast audience. Bear's reach is 10x larger across mainstream channels.

Our positioning gap

Every competitor in this space positions to the same audience: male, US, carnivore-adjacent. Modern Savage is the first organ nutrition brand built deliberately for families — not just a nod to a Kids SKU, but a whole-family mission. That is the whitespace. That is how we win mainstream.

What to avoid

Do not position as a biohacking brand, a performance supplement, or a male health product. That market is already owned by Heart and Soil and Saladino. Our edge is the family and whole-food angle they have left open. Own it.

## Part 1 — What Email 3 Must Do

This is the conversion email. Emails 1 and 2 built identity (origin story) and conviction (proof). Email 3 asks for the purchase.

The psychology here is different from Emails 1 and 2. Those emails were about Bear. This one is about the reader and a specific decision they need to make by a specific time. The emotional register shifts from inspiration to action.

Three things that kill conversion emails in supplements:
1. **Fake urgency.** "Only 47 spots left" when the list knows there are 170 of them. Every supplement brand does this. It feels like a trick because it is. Our founding member pricing works because it's a real commitment — 170 people at a specific price point that we cannot sustain at 1,700 subscribers. That's genuine, and it should be stated genuinely.
2. **Too much copy.** Email 1 earned the right to 400 words. Email 3 has no such patience budget. People who are ready to buy want to click, not read. Keep it under 200 words of body copy.
3. **Burying the link.** The CTA needs to appear twice — once mid-email, once at the end. Not three times (aggressive), not once (easy to miss).

One structural consideration: this email should feel like a natural response to the conversation that started with Email 2. The P.S. in Email 2 invited replies. If any replies came in, Email 3 acknowledges them explicitly — it turns individual questions into shared community moments. Calvin should save reply questions from Email 2 and use 1-2 of them in this email if they arrived.

## Part 4 — Subject Line A/B Test Notes

| Version | Subject Line | Psychology | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (primary)** | `You're in. Here's how to lock it in.` | Affirmation + action. "You're in" confirms belonging before asking for commitment. | Highest conversion hypothesis — affirms before asking. Reduces friction. |
| **B** | `Founding member access opens tomorrow.` | Direct date-based urgency, no tricks. | Strong for list members who are already decided. Lower warmth than A. |
| **C** | `One day left at founding price.` | Time pressure + price anchor. | Works well if the list is cold. Can feel pushy if the relationship is warm. Not recommended here — this list is warm. |

**Recommendation:** A. The "you're in" framing is the payoff on three emails of relationship building. Don't abandon warmth at the conversion moment.

## Part 6 — Implementation Notes for Calvin

- **The link placeholder.** Replace `[Claim your founding member spot →]` with the actual Stripe Payment Link once Jesse generates it Tuesday. The email is ready to send the moment that link exists.
- **Send time.** 8am-9am UK time on Day 7. Most supplement conversions happen in the morning when people are in a decision-making mindset, before the day takes over.
- **Segment correctly.** Everyone on the 170-person list gets this email, not just those who replied "yes" to Email 1. The "yes" replies are your highest-intent segment but you don't want to cut out someone who just didn't reply but is ready to buy.
- **Track the right metric.** Click-through rate on the Stripe link is your primary metric. Open rate is noise at this stage — everyone who opens Email 3 already opened Emails 1 and 2. You want clicks.
- **The Day 14 non-converter email.** If someone opened Emails 1, 2, and 3 but didn't click — they're interested but not converted. That's worth a follow-up. Subject: "Did we lose you?" — simple, honest, one question. No pitch. Just: "Still thinking about it? Happy to answer anything." That email alone typically recovers 8-12% of warm non-converters.

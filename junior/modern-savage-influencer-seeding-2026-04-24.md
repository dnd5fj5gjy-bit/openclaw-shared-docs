# Modern Savage — Influencer Seeding Strategy
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Purpose:** Who to seed, how to approach, and what ROI to expect before and at launch.

---

## The Core Principle

Modern Savage is family nutrition — not biohacking, not carnivore extremism. That positioning determines who you seed and who you avoid. The Liver King audience (2.8M followers, organ meat obsessive) is the target demographic. Liver King himself is not — the steroid scandal makes him a brand toxin for Bear Grylls.

The seeding goal before launch: build 20-30 authentic voices who received the product, tried it, and have something real to say. Not paid promotions — product gifting with zero expectation. The ones who love it will post. Those posts become social proof for launch.

---

## Micro vs. Macro — The Data

Per WeArisma's 2025 influencer report: **70% of budget to micro-influencers (10K-100K followers).** Why:
- Micro engagement rates: 3.5-7% vs macro at 1-2%
- Their audiences trust them more — parasocial relationship, not celebrity
- Cost: product gifting only at this tier. No cash required pre-launch.
- Authenticity: they actually try things, they're not just running ads

For Modern Savage's pre-launch seeding: micro only. No cash. Just product + a personal note from Bear.

## Target Influencer Categories

### Category 1 — Ancestral Health / Primal Nutrition (US-focused)
**Who:** Doctors, nutritionists, coaches who advocate whole-food animal-based nutrition.
**Why:** Built-in ideological alignment. They'll post because they believe in the product, not because they were paid.
**Size:** 10K-200K. Beyond that, you're paying.

**Profile types to look for:**
- "Animal-based diet" doctors on Instagram (not carnivore extremists — family-oriented nutrition MDs)
- Functional medicine practitioners who recommend organ meats to patients
- Registered dietitians who challenge plant-based orthodoxy with evidence

**Approach:** DM with: "We've been following your work. We built a product we think you'll want to try — grass-fed NZ/AU organ blend, no synthetic additives, third-party tested. No ask. Just want to get it in your hands." Keep it short.

---

### Category 2 — Fathers / Family Health (Bear's core territory)
**Who:** Dads who post about raising kids with good nutrition, away from ultra-processed food. Not extremists — just engaged parents.
**Why:** Directly matches Modern Savage's "Kids Blend" positioning. One dad posting "this is what we give our kids" is worth more than 10 product review posts.
**Size:** 5K-50K. These accounts are smaller but hyper-engaged — parents trust other parents.

**Profile types:**
- "Dad of X" accounts where food/nutrition is a recurring theme
- Fathers in the outdoor/adventure community who align with Bear's lifestyle
- CrossFit / functional fitness dads who care about family nutrition

**Approach:** More personal. Reference their content specifically. "We saw your post about [specific thing]. We built something for exactly this reason."

---

### Category 3 — Adventure / Outdoor / Survival Community
**Who:** Overlanders, backcountry hunters, survival instructors, adventure athletes.
**Why:** This is Bear's native community. The credibility transfer is instant. "If Bear Grylls eats this, and this guy who spends 3 weeks in the backcountry eats this, maybe I should too."
**Size:** 10K-150K.

**Profile types:**
- Backcountry hunters (eat what they kill — organ meats are natural)
- Mountaineers / alpinists
- Survival instructors with built audiences
- Overlanding/van life influencers with health-conscious followings

**Approach:** Reference Bear's involvement prominently here — it's the immediate hook.

---

### Category 4 — UK Family / Wellness (for UK launch)
**Why:** Modern Savage launches simultaneously UK/US. UK seeding needs different voices.
**Who:** British nutrition-focused parents, functional medicine practitioners, British fitness community.
**Specific note:** UK audiences are more sceptical of supplements than US. Lean into the "real food, not a supplement" angle. "This is freeze-dried beef liver in a capsule. That's it."

## Seeding Mechanics

**What to send:**
- 1-2 month supply Adult Blend (60-120 capsules)
- Kids Blend if they have children
- A brief handwritten note (or Bear's name on a card) — not a branded media pack
- No contract, no expectation, no branded hashtag requirement

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't require a post. That kills authenticity.
- Don't send a 10-page media kit. That signals "paid ad," not genuine gift.
- Don't send to accounts that post exclusively branded content — they'll file it alongside every other brand and it'll look like what it is.

**Follow-up:**
- After 2-3 weeks: one DM checking if the product arrived and how they're getting on. No ask.
- If they post organically: engage in the comments. Bear or Jesse commenting personally is the best amplifier.

## Budget

Pre-launch seeding for 30 accounts:
- Product cost (60-day supply x 30): ~$45-60 x 30 = $1,350-1,800
- Shipping (DHL international for UK, USPS for US): ~$15-25 x 30 = $450-750
- **Total: ~$1,800-2,550**

This is the cheapest possible customer acquisition at this stage. If 8 of 30 post (26% conversion — realistic for genuine gifting), and each post reaches 20K-50K engaged followers, the waitlist impact could be 500-2,000 new signups. At a $65 AOV, that's $32,500-$130,000 in launch-day revenue pipeline for a $2,500 investment.

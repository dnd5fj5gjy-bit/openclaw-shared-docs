Compliance — Things to Check Before US Launch

FDA (Supplement Labeling)

Supplements sold in the US must comply with DSHEA (Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act). Key requirements:

- Supplement Facts panel (not Nutrition Facts)

- Disclaimer: "This statement has not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

- Country of origin statement

- Manufacturer/distributor name and address (US address needed)

FTC (Marketing Claims)

FTC polices supplement advertising claims aggressively. Watch:

- No unsubstantiated health claims ("cures", "prevents", "treats")

- Testimonials must reflect typical results (or disclose they don't)

- Celebrity/influencer posts must disclose paid relationship (#ad, #sponsored)

- Bear's content counts as advertising — needs to be honest and substantiated

Review the modernsavage.co website copy before taking US orders. Any claim like "boosts testosterone" or "improves immunity" without supporting evidence is an FTC risk. Kylee (or another US attorney) should sign off on US-facing copy before the hard launch.

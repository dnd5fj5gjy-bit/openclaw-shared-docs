## What The Agreement Actually Says

### The Fee Structure (Clause 3.1)
EBG pays Bear Witness **$25,000/month** for social media management services. This is fee-for-service. Jesse invoices monthly; EBG pays within 15 days.

### The Revenue Share (Clause 3.5 — the key clause)

The agreement contains a revenue share in **one specific direction**: Bear Witness pays EBG a percentage of social advertising income.

> "Consultant will remit to the Company 15% of all Distributable Cash received from Class A Activities and 7.5% from Class B Activities **through advertising and sponsorship activities via any social handles managed by Consultant and listed in Exhibit 2.**"

**Exhibit 2 lists the social handles covered:**
1. Bear Grylls personal handles
2. Running Wild show handles
3. Adventure Garage show handles
(Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, Twitter, LinkedIn)

**What this means:**
- The revenue share applies only to advertising/sponsorship revenue flowing through Bear's social media handles
- The FOX Creator Network is a media deal — it is not advertising or sponsorship activity on a social handle
- Nothing in Clause 3.5 or anywhere else creates a right for EBG to participate in TV/streaming deals, appearance fees, or network media arrangements

### The NIL Clause (Clause 8.2 — devastating for Benjamin's argument)

> "Nothing in this Agreement shall grant the Company any rights in the Consultant's or Bear Grylls' name, image, likeness or personal brand without prior written approval."

Benjamin Simon argued that EBG is "the rights holder controlling that commercial input" — Bear's NIL. Clause 8.2 explicitly contradicts this. EBG does not hold or control Bear's NIL. Bear does. This clause was negotiated in and signed by EBG's own COO.

### The Entire Agreement Clause (Clause 13.1 — kills the investor deck argument)

> "The terms set out in this letter constitute the entire and only legally binding agreement between us relating to your engagement and **supersede any previous understandings, arrangements, representations, negotiations or agreements** between us."

Benjamin cited slides 26-27 of the Everest investor presentation as establishing entitlement. Clause 13.1 explicitly supersedes those slides. Investor materials are not contracts. The contract says so.

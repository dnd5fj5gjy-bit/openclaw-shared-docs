# Thursday 23 July: four calls, 09:00 to 11:30

**Two of these are decision calls, two are listening calls.** Aviform (10:00) is the one that moves the Modern Savage critical path. Phoebe (11:00) has one thing on her CV you need to resolve in the first five minutes.

## 09:00 - 09:30 | Ted's Health: weight loss pathway

**Yours. You called it. Purpose: sign off the pathway before it goes live.**

Go-live is 27 July. This is the last working review before it.

### The four things to leave with, in this order

**1. The exact med list and doses, written down, not described.**

> "Before we talk about anything else, read me the list. Which drugs, which doses, which titration steps. I want to be able to repeat it back."

*Good sign:* they read a short specific list without hedging.
*Red flag:* "we'll offer the standard range" or any answer where the doses come with a caveat about the prescriber deciding later. If the pathway is not fixed, it is not ready to go live.

**2. Who is the named responsible clinician on this pathway specifically.**

> "Is Johnathan the named clinician for weight loss too, or is that someone else?"

*Red flag:* a name you have not met, or a locum arrangement.

**3. What happens when a patient is turned down.**

> "Walk me through a patient the pathway rejects. What do they see, what do they get told, and who tells them?"

This is where these services get their worst press. The refusal journey is the one nobody builds and the one that ends up screenshotted.

**4. The go-live gate.**

> "What is still outstanding between now and Monday, and who owns each item?"

Then, the question that actually protects you:

> "If those items are not closed by Friday, do we still go on Monday, or do we move it?"

*Get a yes or a no.* A pathway that goes live with open items is how the first complaint happens.

### One thing to say out loud

> "I would rather launch this a week late and correct than on time and wrong. Nobody is under pressure from me to hit Monday."

Say it early. It changes what people tell you for the rest of the call.

## 10:00 - 11:00 | Modern Savage x Aviform introduction

**Google Meet: meet.google.com/ixc-oyee-sob** (Wendy Greaves organising)
**On the call:** Wendy Greaves, Accounts & Private Label Manager. Likely Robin Rodger, MD, who signed the NDA.

### Where this stands

They signed the NDA 14 July. You sent the full three-product spec the same day with seven numbered questions. Wendy replied within the hour: she was taking it to Robin and the NPD team. Nine days of silence since, then this call.

**Aviform have been formulating supplements since 1977. Their markets are pigeon, equine, dog, cage and aviary, poultry, and human, in that order on their own signature.** Human is last on the list. That is the thing to probe, politely.

### The single most important thing to establish

**They have had the spec for nine days and have not answered any of the seven questions in writing.** Whether they answer them today, with numbers, is the whole test.

Open the substantive part with:

> "You have had the full spec for a week or so. Rather than me talk you through it again, can we just go down my seven questions and take them in order?"

Then hold them to it. If you let this become a general introduction, you will end the hour with a good feeling and no information.

### The four answers that matter

**A. Price per finished pouch at 1,000 / 2,500 / 5,000 / 10,000 units.**

> "Even a range with a wide band is more useful to me than nothing. What is the shape of it?"

*Red flag:* "we'd need to cost it properly." They have had nine days and this is their business.

**B. Can they actually source the organ material.**

> "Which of the organ inputs would you be buying in as finished powder, and which would you be handling as raw material yourselves?"

That distinction is the real answer. Buying in finished organ powder is fine, but it makes them a blender rather than a source, and it changes who you are actually dependent on.

Then:

> "And where would it come from? UK farms, or imported?"

*Context you have:* Calita already told us organs are uncommon in the UK and they may need to import. If Aviform say the same, that is honest and expected. If they claim easy UK sourcing, ask which abattoirs. **Protein Partners are the only lead claiming domestic organ sourcing with segregation, and that claim is still unverified.**

**C. The lung question.** The adult formula contains bovine lung.

> "One specific one. The adult blend contains bovine lung. How would you source and classify that?"

GB permits lung for human consumption. The US does not. You are not testing them, you are finding out whether they know the answer without looking it up.

**D. The regulatory test, which is free and tells you everything.**

Ask it flat:

> "You said you could own the UK regulatory work. Is there anything in the range you would not be able to sell in Great Britain as it is currently formulated?"

**The correct answer is yes, and it is the Summit Stack.** NMN is an unauthorised novel food in GB. Trans-resveratrol is authorised for capsules and tablets only, adults only, 150mg maximum, and Summit is a 12g powder.

*If they raise it themselves before you ask:* they are the real thing, and they are ahead of all three other leads. Weight them heavily.
*If they say no, everything is fine:* they have not read the spec against GB law, and their offer to own regulatory is worth nothing. **Not one of the four manufacturers has raised this. It costs nothing to find out which of them is paying attention.**

Do not lecture them on the answer. Note it and move on.

### What to give them

Timeline: **UK launch from mid-September**, D2C through modernsavage.co.

If they ask what would win them the business:

> "A price we can build a margin on, and a partner who owns the compliance file properly rather than nominally. We are talking to three others and the compliance side is where they are separating."

### Do not commit to

Volumes, exclusivity, or a decision date. You have three other live conversations and Protein Partners is currently the best fit on paper.

### Close with

> "What do you need from us to come back with firm pricing, and when can we have it?"

Get a date. Nine days of silence after the spec went over is the pattern to interrupt.

### The thing to resolve first

**Her CV lists two current roles running concurrently.** Pipers Farm, Head of E-commerce & Marketing, "August 2023 to Present." Align Collective, Founder & Digital Director, "January 2025 to Present."

Ask it directly and without edge, early:

> "Your CV has you at Pipers Farm and running Align Collective at the same time. Talk me through how that actually works week to week."

*Good sign:* a clear, unembarrassed answer. She has gone part time at Pipers, or the agency is two days a week, or she is winding one down. People in growing D2C businesses do build things on the side and it is often a sign of energy rather than a problem.
*Red flag:* vagueness, or discovering Pipers Farm do not know about the agency. If she is doing two full-time jobs, she cannot do a third.

Follow with:

> "If this role happened, what happens to Align Collective?"

You need to know whether you are hiring a person or renting a slice of one.

### The number worth probing

> "You said Align did over £800k in year one. Is that agency revenue, or revenue you managed on behalf of clients?"

*Good sign:* she distinguishes the two immediately.
*Red flag:* she blurs them. £800k of genuine agency fee income from 18 clients in year one while employed full time elsewhere would be remarkable.

### The question that tells you if she gets Modern Savage

> "We are selling freeze-dried organ powder. Liver, heart, kidney, spleen. How would you sell that to someone who has never considered eating an organ in their life?"

*Good sign:* she goes straight to provenance, sourcing, and the specific person who buys it. Pipers Farm sells whole animals to people who did not previously buy whole animals, so she has done this exact job before.
*Red flag:* generic performance-marketing answers about creative testing and CPA.

### The reality check

> "Right now this is a small team, a formula, a manufacturer we have not chosen, and a launch in September. There is no marketing budget to inherit and no team under you. Does that sound like the job you applied for?"

*Good sign:* relief, or enthusiasm.
*Red flag:* a Head of function at an established business looking for a bigger version of the same job.

### Practical, ask before you close

- Devon-based. What does she expect on location and days in London?
- Notice period at Pipers Farm.
- Salary expectation. Get a number on this call so you are not negotiating blind against the Jacob Collins benchmark.

### Do not

Offer anything, or signal where she ranks. Jacob Collins is still live and his fee numbers are in.

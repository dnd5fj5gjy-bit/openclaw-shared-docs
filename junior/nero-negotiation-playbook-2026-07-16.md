## The position in one paragraph

Tyler has been chasing since June and moved unprompted from a vague ask to a structured offer - that is leverage, use it warmly and fast. Bear is the exact asset Nero is missing: the brand runs on rented Meta traffic with no organic community, and Bear is the mainstream bridge that fixes that - which is worth more than the 5% on the table. But the growth engine that built Nero ($3m in 15 months) is testosterone-anxiety advertising, publicly documented as manosphere-adjacent, and that is the one register Bear cannot be near. So the deal inverts: the marketing covenant is not a term IN the deal, it IS the deal. Money-wise: the royalty is the real cheque (~£600k modelled over 3 years), the equity is a lottery ticket (~£377k paper, and ordinary shares get wiped by any future preference stack), and the whitelisting as drafted is a pure giveaway.

## The sequence - five moves

**Move 1 (this week): the gating question.** Founder-to-founder from you, warm, not a lawyer letter. Ready to send:

"We're genuinely interested, and Bear likes the product and what you've built. Before we talk structure, one thing we have to line up. Your paid funnel currently runs on testosterone and hormone claims - the 'gym shorts are killing your testosterone' page, the framing BoF wrote about in February. If it's the core of the business, we should say so now and stay friends."

His answer is the decision, and it tells you what he is like to be in business with.

**Move 2 (parallel, before any number is agreed): buy the audience report.** One HypeAuditor or Modash report on Bear's handle (~$300). Bear's public engagement reads 0.22-0.44% and may skew India/SEA - against Nero's 80% US revenue. Until this lands, do not argue reach. It either arms the counter or stops us overplaying a hand we do not hold.

**Move 3 (if Tyler says yes): the counter.** Term-by-term targets below. Concede the $12m valuation cheerfully - it is a decoy, and under UK tax a higher valuation actively hurts us (dry income-tax charge on illiquid shares). Fight the percentage and the definitions instead.

**Move 4: the DD gate before anything signs.** Management accounts; the margin definition in writing (his claimed 20% net is almost certainly contribution margin - his other company runs 4.2% net); repeat-purchase and cohort data; US trademark secured (80% of revenue is US and the mark is still pending; EU is blocked by a rival); cap table confirmation in writing; and the unnamed comparable partner's actual terms in writing.

**Move 5: paper it properly.** Grant to a corporate vehicle, never to Bear personally; tax advice before signing (s.431 election has a hard 14-day deadline); share rights written into the Articles, not just a shareholders' agreement; BGV counsel drafts.

## Term-by-term: ask / fallback / walk

**Equity (offered: 5% at $12M, services grant)**
Ask: 8-10%, granted to a vehicle, justified on the repositioning Bear delivers, not on valuation. Anti-dilution via the Beckham formula - "the higher of (i) 1.0% of the enlarged share count and (ii) $5,000,000 divided by the share price" - which is a floor and anti-dilution in one line. Named into the "Major Investor" definition (statutory pre-emption has holes: services grants sit outside it and 75% can disapply it).
Fallback: 6-7% if the royalty base widens materially. 5% is acceptable only with the attribution royalty below - one or the other, never neither.
Walk: 5% and a thin royalty base.

**Vesting (offered: 50/25/25 tied to "creative deliverables and continued partnership")**
Ask: objective, scheduled deliverables only - named service days, capped hours, itemised assets, grid posts counted separately from stories. Delete "continued partnership" (it is a unilateral option for Nero dressed as mutuality). Single-trigger acceleration in full on change of control - Bear is not an employee, so double-trigger may be literally unreachable. Pay-or-play: Nero failing to book Bear is not Bear's non-performance.
Walk: any subjective annual clawback.

**Royalty (offered: 10% of undefined "net revenue" on a dedicated BG range)**
Ask: keep the 10%, fix the base. Net Revenue = gross invoiced sales at list price less ONLY documented returns and point-of-sale discounts; aggregate deductions capped at 10% of gross; anti-avoidance (affiliate transfers royalty-bearing, retail computed at retail, sublicence receipts shared 50%); audit right (5 years, licensee pays if under by 5%+); quarterly statements, 30-day payment. PLUS the leak fix: 3-5% attribution royalty on all Nero revenue from Bear-driven traffic (whitelisted clicks, Bear's code, UTM) - otherwise Bear's ads sell Nero's core shorts for zero royalty and Tyler controls what counts as "BG range".
Fallback: minimum annual guarantee of ~£150k from range launch instead of attribution. If Nero believes its own forecast, the floor costs nothing.
Walk: base stays undefined AND no minimum guarantee. The undefined base swings the same 10% from £170k to £20k a year - this is the Water2 "net profit" trap again.

**Ad whitelisting (offered: full account-level for the term)**
Ask: per-post Meta Partnership Ad codes only, every creative pre-approved, no health or hormone claims on any Bear-adjacent surface, claims liability and ASA indemnity sit with Nero.
No fallback - this one is non-negotiable, and the argument is friendly: Meta's own 300-brand study found per-post partnership ads BEAT account-level allowlisting (~3.9% lower cost per purchase), so the ask costs Tyler nothing. Account-level access only buys the right to originate claims in Bear's name that Bear never sees - on a brand already labelled manosphere-adjacent, with Bear named in the eventual ASA ruling.

**Marketing covenant (not in his offer - our condition precedent)**
Ask: written brand-safety covenant on any Bear-associated surface - no testosterone/hormone claims, no false urgency, no inflated review counts - with termination right and full equity acceleration on breach.
No fallback. This is the gate from Move 1, written down.

**Protections bundle**
Information rights (quarterly management accounts), tag-along on Tyler's sale, mutual morality clause, anti-restructuring (benefits follow value to any new parent), rights in the Articles. Standard, cheap to ask, and each one is a Beckham/F45 lesson - he won his argument and still lost ~$9.3m because the protection had to be litigated.

## Talking points that work (and the traps)

**Say:**
- "The $12m is fine. The percentage is the conversation - because this isn't an endorsement, it's a repositioning. Bear takes you from rented Meta traffic to a mainstream brand. That's the asset you can't buy with ad spend."
- "10% of net revenue - agreed. Define net revenue in one paragraph, cap the deductions, and we sign that page. If the deal is what you both believe it is, the definition costs you nothing." (A counterparty who resists both a deduction cap and a floor is telling you what they intend.)
- "On whitelisting, Meta's own data says per-post outperforms account-level. You lose nothing; we keep control of Bear's name."
- "You mentioned another founder coming in at $12m - great, share his terms. Bear comes in on terms no worse." (MFN - turns his own anchor into our lever.)

**Don't:**
- Don't argue Bear's reach until the audience report lands.
- Don't fight the valuation - it is a decoy and a higher number worsens the dry tax charge.
- Don't treat the "£440k biggest month" as a run-rate - it is a six-SKU launch spike (one product for 15 months, then six in 8 weeks). Know it; deploy it only if he anchors on it.
- Don't let the announcement collide with Modern Savage's mid-September launch - Bear fronting testosterone-adjacent activewear in the same quarter undercuts MS's mainstream positioning. Sequence any announcement after MS is out.
- Don't sign anything before the vehicle + s.431 tax structure is set (14-day hard deadline from any share issue).

## Walk-away triggers

1. Tyler will not contractually bar the hormone claims.
2. He insists on account-level whitelisting.
3. The 20% margin proves to be contribution margin AND he will not move on percentage or royalty base.

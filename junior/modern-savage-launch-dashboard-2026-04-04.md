## Why this matters

There was already good strategy work in the workspace:
- positioning and landing-page direction
- launch economics and CAC guardrails
- subscription retention ideas

What was missing was an **operator layer** — something Jesse can open, tweak, and use to make decisions quickly.

This dashboard turns that work into a practical control panel.

## What the dashboard does

### 1. Recalculates launch economics live
Jesse can change:
- 1/2/3-bottle prices
- subscription first-order price
- COGS per bottle
- ops and shipping costs
- good/base/weak CAC
- month-2 and month-3 continuation rates
- offer mix by SKU

The dashboard instantly recalculates:
- weighted AOV
- breakeven CAC
- base contribution dollars
- rough 90-day gross profit per acquired customer

### 2. Shows whether the machine is healthy
It automatically assigns a risk band:
- **Green zone** — ready to scale
- **Yellow zone** — refine before scaling
- **Red zone** — too soft right now

That gives Jesse a simple answer to the real question: should the business buy more traffic or fix the machine first?

### 3. Translates numbers into action
The dashboard generates next-step recommendations based on the inputs. For example:
- if bundle mix is too weak, it pushes the 2-bottle bundle as the hero
- if subscription share is too low, it flags retention/value story work
- if CAC is too high, it points toward creator UGC and offer refinement before scale
- if breakeven room is tight, it warns against hidden cost creep

### 4. Makes scenario planning fast
There is a scenario table for:
- good CAC
- base CAC
- weak CAC

Each scenario shows:
- CAC
- contribution dollars
- contribution margin
- the practical call: scale, refine, or fix

## Best use case

This is most useful as the weekly launch review board for Modern Savage.

Jesse or the team can update actual numbers from:
- paid traffic performance
- landing-page conversion
- bundle mix
- subscription share
- real COGS / shipping data

Then make decisions from the economics instead of gut feel.

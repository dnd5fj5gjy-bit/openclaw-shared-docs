The One-Line Pivot

Ted's Health is stepping back from competing as a consumer men's health brand and repositioning as the operating system behind clinics — the white-label backend that lets other health operators launch a modern telehealth service without building the stack themselves. The opportunity is bigger, the competition is lower, and the margins are better.

Where Ted's Has Been

Ted's Health launched as a UK direct-to-consumer men's telehealth clinic — TRT (testosterone replacement therapy) and ED (erectile dysfunction) treatment online, delivered to the door. The model works: the clinical operation is CQC-registered, prescribing is real, and patients are genuinely being treated well.

But the D2C men's health market is crowded. Numan, Manual, LloydsPharmacy Online Doctor, and dozens of smaller operators are spending heavily on acquisition to compete for the same men. To win at that game requires significant marketing budget and constant performance marketing attention. The economics at early scale are tight.

The New Direction — White-Label B2B Platform

The pivot is simple: instead of trying to be the biggest D2C men's health brand, Ted's becomes the backend that powers other clinics, pharmacies, and health operators.

Most UK clinics already see the demand for men's health services — TRT, ED, hair loss, diagnostics, weight management. What they don't have is the operational stack to launch a modern telehealth offer: online intake flows, prescribing workflows, repeat prescription management, discreet fulfilment, patient retention CRM, conversion-optimised journeys.

Ted's already built that stack. The pivot is to sell it — under their brand, not ours.

Commercial Structure

Three packaging options depending on what the partner needs: Launch-in-a-box (fast start, new service line), Conversion and retention engine (existing service, better economics), or Full white-label backend (ongoing operating partnership with recurring revenue share).

Why This Is the Better Business

B2B economics are significantly better than D2C at this scale. Each clinic partnership generates setup fee + monthly retainer + potential performance share — without the ongoing cost of consumer acquisition. One good clinic partner can generate more stable revenue than 50 individual patients. And Ted's clinical credibility transfers directly: partners get a functioning, regulated operation on day one, not a promise.

Where This Could Go — Seeds for Discussion

The white-label infrastructure Ted's is building is not condition-specific. In theory, the same operating model — online consultation, prescribing pathway, repeat supply, retention — can be applied across any telehealth category where there is proven demand and a prescribing workflow.

We're not rushing into any of these. The focus right now is making the men's health white-label work. But if Calvin has a view on where the opportunity sits — or the relationships to open doors in any of these areas — that's worth a conversation. None of this happens without someone to drive it.

Weight Management / GLP-1 Big market
Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) prescribing online is one of the fastest-growing telehealth categories in the UK right now. Clinics need exactly the infrastructure Ted's is building. Huge opportunity if the right operator or distribution partner is involved.
Women's Health / HRT Big market
Menopause and HRT have had a major cultural moment in the UK. The patient journey is almost identical to what Ted's has built for men — online consultation, prescribing, repeat supply, monitoring. Same infrastructure, different condition, enormous underserved demand.
Peptides Interesting
BPC-157, TB-500, AOD-9604 and others are growing fast in the performance, recovery, and longevity space. Prescribing pathways are evolving. Close to Ted's existing patient demographic — men optimising health and performance. Worth watching and potentially worth moving on early.
Longevity / Preventative Health Interesting
Blood diagnostics, nutrient optimisation, biohacking protocols. Growing fast among high-value consumers. Aligns directly with Bear's brand and the wider BGV health portfolio — strong brand fit, especially with the Modern Savage angle.
Skin / Dermatology On radar
Tretinoin, acne, rosacea. Online derm is subscription-native with high LTV and proven retention. Brands like Skin + Me and Dermatica have shown the category works. Lower clinical complexity than hormone treatment.
Mental Health / Therapy On radar
CBT, anxiety, depression — growing demand, NHS backlog creating private opportunity. Slightly different operational model but the intake and retention mechanics are transferable. Large patient population.

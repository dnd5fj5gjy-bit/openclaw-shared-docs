## 2. THE THREE NUMBERS THAT ARE AMBIGUOUS, AND WHAT THEY COST US

**a) Storage: pallets or shelving?** A pallet at their stated dimensions is 1.92 m3. Charged as a pallet it is £0.72 a day. The same volume on pickable shelving is £2.02 a day, **2.8 times more**. D2C picking normally means a pick face on shelving plus bulk on pallets, and nobody has said what the split is. Range on the same stock: £66 to £184 a month.

**b) Goods-in: does traceability force the per-item scan?** Per-pallet receiving is £15. Per-item scanning is £0.20, which on 3,500 units is £700 rather than £45. Their sheet calls scanning "optional" - but scanning at item level is normally how lot numbers and expiry dates get captured, and we cannot run a supplement without lot-level traceability. **Ask straight out: is lot and expiry capture included in the pallet rate, or does it require the £0.20 scan?** This is the largest single ambiguity in the document.

**c) Which postage class is the default?** RM24 costs £0.97 more per order than RM48. Our own model passes shipping to the customer and gives it free over £80. A single £65 subscription sits under that threshold, so the customer pays - and if we advertise a typical £3.95 delivery charge while SCEND bills £5.01, we lose £1.06 on every single-item order. **Set the customer-facing shipping price off this rate card, and default to RM48.**

## 4. THE THING THAT IS NOT ABOUT PRICE

At the bottom of the proposal, SCEND tell us they have a legal duty to inform us about the **Fulfilment House Due Diligence Scheme**. That is not boilerplate. FHDDS requires a registered fulfilment house to check the VAT registration number of any overseas customer whose goods it stores, keep those records for six years, notify HMRC if it suspects non-compliance, and **stop working with that customer if it is not resolved**.

Modern Savage Inc is a Delaware C-corp. If the UK stock is owned by the US entity, then:

- We are SCEND's overseas customer, and they will need our UK VAT number for their FHDDS file.
- As a non-established taxable person storing goods in the UK and selling to UK customers, Modern Savage must register for UK VAT **from the first sale**. The £90,000 threshold does not apply to overseas businesses - it is nil.
- Until we are registered, the 20% VAT on every SCEND invoice is a real cost, not a recoverable one.

The business plan already flags the UK entity question as an open item for Raemy and Zander Sterling, framed as "must be answered before the first UK sale". **That framing is now wrong.** It has to be answered before SCEND can onboard us, NETP registration takes weeks rather than days, and stock lands with them around late October. It is the longest lead-time item on the UK launch and nobody is holding it.

# Naturecan — Quick Assessment for Jesse
**Prepared:** April 26, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Trigger:** Bear forwarded a Naturecan EIS investment opportunity to Jesse on April 23 asking: "Any interest for us for our Ted's plans here?"

---

## What Naturecan Is

**Founded:** May 2019 by Andy Duckworth (former CEO of Myprotein, one of the UK's largest sports nutrition brands) and Paul Finnegan.

**Product:** Hemp-derived CBD products + vitamins and minerals. Global wellness brand, primarily DTC e-commerce. UK-headquartered.

**Scale:** Global operation. Andy Duckworth scaled Myprotein to a £700M+ exit (The Hut Group acquired it) — so the founding team knows supplement brand-building at scale.

**EIS qualifying:** The investment is structured as EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme) — 30% income tax relief on investment + CGT exemption after 3 years. This is a UK tax-efficient vehicle for early/growth-stage companies. EIS usually applies to companies raising £5-15M.

---

## Why Bear Thinks It's Relevant to Ted's

Ted's Health treats UK men on TRT and health optimisation protocols. CBD is commonly used by that demographic for:
- Sleep quality (a top concern for men on hormone protocols)
- Recovery and inflammation management
- Anxiety/stress reduction

The connection Bear is probably seeing: Naturecan's CBD products could complement Ted's Health's supplement/wellness offering — either as a cross-sell or a white-label product Ted's could give to patients. Andy Duckworth's Myprotein background also means he understands the men's health/performance buyer.

---

## The Honest Assessment

**The case for pursuing it:**
- Andy Duckworth is credible — he built and sold one of the UK's biggest supplement brands
- CBD + TRT is a natural pairing if Ted's Health positions itself as whole-person men's health
- EIS tax relief makes the investment economics more attractive for Bear/Jesse as UK taxpayers
- A relationship with Naturecan could give Ted's a white-label CBD range without building it themselves

**The case against:**
- CBD regulation in the UK is tightening. MHRA increasingly treats CBD as a medicine (not a food supplement) — this creates compliance overhead for any clinic prescribing or recommending it
- Ted's Health is mid-pivot (B2B white-label). Adding a CBD investment/partnership adds complexity to a business that's already changing direction
- BGV bandwidth: Jesse has Modern Savage, Wildernests, Running Wild, Water2, BPS Hub, sailing, all on the boil right now. A new investment due diligence process is expensive in time
- The EIS structure suggests Naturecan needs capital — which means they may be struggling to grow, or in a difficult fundraising environment

**The honest read:** Bear spotted a potential connection and forwarded it. It's worth a 20-minute call with Andy Duckworth to understand the terms and whether a Ted's partnership angle exists. It's not worth a serious time investment until the Ted's B2B pivot is further along.

## ELIZABETH BANKS — Episode 6 (~Late May)

### Who She Is

Fifty-two in 2026. She has done everything Hollywood offers and then decided to direct it herself.

Actress: Effie Trinket in The Hunger Games. Gail in Pitch Perfect. Supporting player who was always sharper than the film she was in.

Director: Pitch Perfect 2 (highest-grossing live-action musical at the time of release). Charlie's Angels (2019). Cocaine Bear (2023) — the one that went viral, that everyone watched, that made her the most talked-about director in Hollywood for a minute. She also created and produces her own production company with her husband, Max Handelman.

In April 2026 she just went public about being told she "can't direct men — they won't follow you." She said: "I think I nailed it." She is in the mode of someone who has nothing left to prove and is saying so clearly.

Married to Max Handelman (producer and sports writer) since 2003. Two sons: Felix and Magnus. She is intensely private about them.

---

### What Connects Her to Bear's Audience

The connection is not Pitch Perfect. It's not Effie Trinket. It's **the person who keeps going when the room says stop.**

Bear's audience responds to this — the practical, quiet version of it. Banks is not a survivor in the Bear Grylls literal sense, but she's spent twenty years being told things she supposedly couldn't do and doing them anyway. That's the same psychology.

Secondary bridge: **Cocaine Bear.** Bear Grylls in the wilderness with the director of Cocaine Bear is inherently funny and Bear's audience will appreciate the meta quality of it. Use this lightly — it's good for an air-night hook — but don't reduce her to a punchline.

---

### What To Avoid

- **Charlie's Angels (2019):** The film failed at the box office. Banks made comments afterwards that were interpreted as blaming male audiences for not supporting female-led films. This became a controversy. Do NOT reference Charlie's Angels at all — not the film, not the box office outcome.
- **"Can't direct men" story:** This is current and active (April 2026). It could easily be read the wrong way in a Bear post. Don't touch it. Banks used it to make a point about her own success — but in a caption for Bear's audience it lands wrong.
- **Felix and Magnus (her sons):** She does not discuss her children publicly in any detail.

## MICHELLE MONAGHAN — Episode 7 (~Early June)

### Who She Is

Fifty in 2026. From Winthrop, Iowa — a town of a few hundred people. Her father was a factory worker and farmer. Her mother ran a daycare. She is the youngest of three children and the only daughter.

She goes back to Iowa every summer. Not for press. Not for Instagram. Because that's home and her kids need to know where they're from.

Career highlights: Mission Impossible series (she played Julia — Ethan Hunt's wife, the person who holds the whole emotional architecture together across six films). True Detective Season 1 (Maggie Hart). White Lotus Season 3 (2025, Jaclyn — her biggest recent cultural moment, played a character whose betrayal was one of the season's most debated plot points).

Married to graphic designer Peter White since 2005. Two children: Willow and Tommy. Twenty-year marriage while maintaining a serious acting career — she talks about it when asked and means it.

---

### What Connects Her to Bear's Audience

**Iowa.** That's the through-line.

Michelle Monaghan is a woman from a small farming town in the middle of America who became a serious actress and still goes home every summer. Bear's audience responds to people who are grounded — not performatively humble, actually grounded. She is.

Secondary bridge: **Mission Impossible.** Julia Meade is not the action hero of those films. She's the person the action hero can't protect — who keeps surviving anyway. Monaghan played that character through multiple films across years. There's something genuine there about resilience that isn't performed on screen.

White Lotus S3 was her re-emergence as a cultural talking point. Jaclyn's story is about what happens when the facade of a perfect life breaks under pressure. In the wilderness, facades break faster.

---

### What To Avoid

- **White Lotus spoilers** in captions — Jaclyn's arc has a specific betrayal plot that some viewers may not have seen. Don't give away the story, just reference the show.
- **Peter White or her children by name** — she keeps her family private. Reference "mother" and "long marriage" as character notes but don't name them.
- **Mission Impossible without context** — casual followers may not connect "Monaghan" to those films. If using MI as a reference, make it clear.

---

The best angle. Simple and real.

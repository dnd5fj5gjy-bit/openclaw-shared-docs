Reading between the lines

1) Paid social is probably too expensive and too generic

If DIRTEA is paying high Meta CAC, the messaging is probably too broad: "functional mushrooms" is crowded, and the ad story may be too educational instead of conversion-led.

2) The brand likely needs a sharper job-to-be-done

DIRTEA should not sell mushrooms as the category. It should sell a specific outcome: focus, calm energy, stress support, better mornings, smarter performance.

3) US expansion needs distribution, not just demand gen

If the US is stalling, it probably needs a combo of creators, practitioner credibility, premium retail or affiliate partners, and founder-led content that travels well.

What I would do next

  • Reframe the positioning — lead with the outcome, not the ingredient.
  • Split the portfolio by use-case — one hero job per SKU.
  • Build a creator + partner engine for the US — trust first, conversion second.
  • Use content as proof, not decoration — answer why mushrooms, why now, why this brand, why trust it.

Immediate strategic moves for Jesse

  1. Audit the current US offer.
  2. Cut one Meta angle and test a tighter outcome-led angle.
  3. Build one hero US founder message.
  4. Recruit 10 niche voices.
  5. Create one partner wedge.
  6. Tighten the proof stack.
  7. Separate education from conversion.

My opinion

DIRTEA probably does not need more marketing. It needs better specificity. The brand should feel less like wellness paste and more like a sharp, credible performance ritual that happens to use mushrooms.

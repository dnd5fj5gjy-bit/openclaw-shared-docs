## The episode structure — Phase 2 ("The Island")

A tight 15-20 minute episode. Working title: **"The Edge."**

**Act 1 — Departure (Southampton, Day 1)**
- Jesse on the boat before casting off. What he's thinking. What he's leaving behind.
- The moment of departure — ropes off, engine on, heading out of the Solent.
- Target feeling: beginning of something.

**Act 2 — The North Sea crossing (Days 2-5)**
- The hardest sailing. North Sea is rough in May. Film the reality.
- Night watch — Jesse alone at 2am, dark water, nothing visible.
- One genuine difficulty moment: weather, exhaustion, a decision that matters.
- Target feeling: this is harder than it looks.

**Act 3 — The Norwegian coast (Days 6-10)**
- Arrival somewhere remote. First sight of land that's genuinely wild.
- One onshore sequence: Jesse walks somewhere, looks at something, says something honest.
- The quiet dawn moment — the thing that makes it worth it.
- Target feeling: this is why people do this.

**Act 4 — The Island (Days 11-14)**
- The destination. Whatever the island is — film it as the answer to the question opened in Act 1.
- One final piece to camera: what did this change? What does Jesse know now that he didn't before?
- Target feeling: arrival and reflection combined.

**Narration approach:** Jesse speaks to camera twice — once at departure, once at the end. Everything else is observational. Don't interview Jesse mid-voyage. Let the footage breathe.

## Practical notes for Jago

**Kit:** Jesse wears Henri-Lloyd Ocean Pro on all on-deck footage. This is contractual and the client (HL) will want brand visible. Don't need to force it — Ocean Pro is the right kit for the conditions anyway.

**Weather:** Don't hide the bad weather. The North Sea crossing in May will be uncomfortable. That footage is more valuable than calm-water sailing. Film everything.

**Battery/storage:** The leg to Norway is 4-5 days at sea with limited charging. Plan accordingly. Prioritise the North Sea crossing footage over the coastal sailing.

**Sound:** Wind noise will be constant. Mike Jesse close when he speaks to camera. Environmental sound (wind, waves, rigging) is an asset — don't fight it.

**The rule:** If in doubt, film it. Storage is cheap. The moment you don't film is the moment something worth keeping happens.

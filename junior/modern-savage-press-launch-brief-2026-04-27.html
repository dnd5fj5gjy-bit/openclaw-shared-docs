The Story (Not "Bear Grylls launches supplement")

Every celebrity has a supplement. The press knows this and most journalists are bored by it. The pitch that works here is different:

The real angle: *"While influencers have sold millions on performance optimization, Bear Grylls is making a family nutrition bet — organ meats as foundational food for parents and children, not biohackers."*

Supporting context that gives it legs:

  • RFK Jr.'s Jan 2026 dietary guidelines shifted US nutrition policy toward red meat, whole foods, and away from synthetic supplements. Modern Savage is ahead of this curve, not chasing a trend.
  • Every other organ supplement (Ancestral Supplements, Heart and Soil, Paul Saladino's line) targets longevity/optimization audiences. Modern Savage is the only brand explicitly positioning organ meats for families with kids.
  • Grass-fed, sourced, third-party tested — not synthetic. The product can withstand scrutiny.

Pitch by publication type:

  • *Daily Mail / Mail Online:* "The survival expert convincing parents to feed kids organ meats — and why the science backs him"
  • *The Telegraph:* "The RFK moment: how ancestral nutrition is going mainstream"
  • *MindBodyGreen:* "Beyond biohacking: why organ meats matter for the whole family"
  • *US News:* "Family supplement brands ditch synthetics for whole-food nutrition — Bear Grylls' new brand leads the shift"

What Press Will Ask (Prepare Answers Now)

  • "Is there evidence organ supplements actually work?"
Answer: Position as whole-food nutrition, not supplement. Liver is the most nutrient-dense food on earth — this is established science, not a claim. Third-party testing confirms nutrient content.
  • "Is this safe for children?"
Answer: Kids Blend has halved organ content versus adult dose. Colostrum in Kids Blend is a well-established infant nutrition ingredient. No proprietary stimulants, no synthetic compounds.
  • "Why does Bear need another brand?"
Answer: Not a licensing deal. Bear is a co-founder and has been developing the formula for 2+ years with ACF Pharma. This is the family nutrition brand he didn't have on the shelf.
  • "Is this just another celebrity supplement cash grab?"
Answer: The product formulation exists before the PR. Bear uses it. The family angle isn't marketing positioning — the Kids Blend exists because he has kids.

**Promise 1 (insert card):** "Give them their first month free. You get a free month when they subscribe."

## 1. The Referral Program

### What It Needs to Do
- Give each customer a unique referral link or code
- Track when a referred friend subscribes
- Automatically trigger a free month for both referrer and referee
- Work with Stripe subscriptions

### The Simplest Build (For July Launch)

**Option A — ReferralCandy ($49/month)**
Integrates directly with Shopify or as a standalone. Customers get a dashboard link, share via email/WhatsApp/social. Automatic reward triggers. Takes 2 hours to set up. This is the right choice if Modern Savage is on Shopify.

**Option B — Recharge Referrals (built-in if using Recharge for subscriptions)**
If Calvin/Drew sets up subscriptions via Recharge (Shopify subscription management), referrals are built in. Check with Drew/Calvin whether Recharge is the subscription layer.

**Option C — Manual codes at launch (free, low-effort, lower-quality)**
Give each founding customer a unique discount code: JESSE170, BEAR170, etc. They share it. When used, both get a free month applied manually. Works for 170 people. Doesn't scale past ~500. Fine for launch if the technical solution isn't ready.

**Recommendation:** Use Option C for the founding 170 (manual codes, one-time effort), build Option A for Month 2+ scale.

### The Referral Economics (Why This Matters)
- 170 founding customers × $591 LTV = $100,470 in total LTV from the founding cohort
- If 20% refer one person each: 34 new customers × $591 = $20,094 in zero-CAC revenue
- The referral program at launch is the most efficient CAC reducer available

## 2. The Community (Where "Let Us Know" Actually Goes)

Bear's insert card says "let us know how you get on." That needs to go somewhere. Options:

### Option A — WhatsApp Community (recommended for launch)
Bear or Jesse creates a WhatsApp Community called "MODERN SAVAGE Founding Members." Invite the 170. Bear drops in occasionally with a voice note or a thought. Jesse shows up. It's not a broadcast channel — it's a real group.

Why WhatsApp over Discord/Slack:
- 170 people already use WhatsApp
- No new app to download
- Bear's voice notes land as they would from a friend, not a brand
- Low moderation overhead (founding members self-police)

The QR code on the insert card back panel goes to a WhatsApp Community invite link (not a group — Communities scale better and Bear doesn't get 170 people messaging him directly).

### Option B — Facebook Group (easier to scale, less intimate)
Private Facebook Group, Bear is a member. Good for SEO, testimonials, photo sharing. Less intimate than WhatsApp. Use this as the long-term community (Month 3+), not the founding launch.

### Option C — Nothing (do not do this)
The insert card creates an expectation. If customers try to "let Bear know" and there's nowhere to go, the disappointment is worse than not making the promise at all.

**Recommendation for July launch:** WhatsApp Community for the founding 170. Brief Bear with 3 example voice notes he could send in Month 1. Facebook Group built but invite-only until Month 3.

## 3. The Recipe Section (Bear's Words)

Bear's insert card says "check out the recipes on our website."

If the website does not have a recipe section on launch day, this is a broken promise on every single order card.

**What the recipe section needs (minimum viable at launch):**
- 5-8 recipes featuring Modern Savage products (beef liver patties, organ smoothies, etc.)
- Bear's voice in the copy ("what I actually make on Sunday mornings")
- One recipe with the Kids Blend (family angle)
- Mobile-optimised — most people will pull this up while cooking

**Who builds it:** Calvin / the design team. Content: brief Bear to send 2-3 voice notes describing how he actually uses the product in meals. Transcribe. Format into recipes.

**Timeline:** Must exist on the website BEFORE the first batch of orders ships. This is Calvin's deliverable alongside the insert card design.

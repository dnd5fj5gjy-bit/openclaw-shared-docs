## 3. The Phased Support Plan

We propose four phases. Each has defined deliverables, cadence, ownership and KPIs, and each phase only unlocks once the previous one has proven itself. This protects everyone: you never pay for promises, and we never dilute Bear's brand on an unproven funnel.

One principle up front, and it matters: **we establish a paid-media CAC baseline before any Bear content goes live.** If Bear starts posting on day one, nobody can ever separate his effect from baseline demand. We measure first, then add Bear, so the incremental lift is provable rather than argued about. Stuart will recognise why this is the right sequencing.

## 5. Measurement and Reporting

We expect to be held to numbers, and we will make that easy.

**Baseline first.** As above: 4-6 weeks of structured paid-media testing before any Bear content, giving us clean baseline CAC, conversion rate and ROAS by channel. This is deliberate discipline, not delay.

**Attribution stack:**
- Unique promo codes and UTM-tagged links on every Bear post, email send and campaign
- Dedicated landing pages for Bear-driven traffic
- Post-purchase "how did you hear about us" survey as a triangulation layer
- Holdout comparisons where volume allows: matched periods and audiences with and without Bear creative, so incremental lift is measured, not asserted

**Reporting cadence:**
- Weekly: performance call with your team (30 minutes, numbers-led)
- Monthly: written report covering spend, CAC vs baseline, revenue by channel, list growth, content performance, next month's plan
- Quarterly: strategy review with Martin and the partners, including honest assessment of what is and is not working

We are happy for Stuart to interrogate the methodology and the numbers at any point. Frankly, we would welcome it; a shared, trusted measurement framework makes every future conversation easier.

## 6. What We Ask in Return

We are deliberately not proposing an agency retainer as the core of this. Agencies get paid whether or not the brand grows. We want the opposite: the overwhelming majority of our upside only exists if Mama Buci wins.

**Proposed structure (as discussed with Martin):**
- Equity participation in Mama Buci Ventures (US): 20% of the US partners' interest and 10% of Martin's interest, alongside the 10% of the UK and wholesale business BGV already holds
- A Bear Grylls branded line as set out in section 4, with an agreed revenue share on that line
- A modest £5,000/month marketing contribution towards hard delivery costs (production, media management, tooling), which we are open to structuring on a deferred or convertible basis so it never strains cash flow

**Why equity rather than fees is the right shape:** market rate for what is described in this pack, bought as services, would run well into six figures annually before you add the value of Bear's endorsement, which is not purchasable at any agency. We are offering it at a fraction of that in cash terms because we want to own a piece of the outcome, sit on the same side of the table as Martin and the partners, and be incentivised for the long term, not the invoice cycle. If we do not grow the business, our equity is worth little. That is the point.

The detailed commercial terms sit with CJ and Martin's advisors; this pack exists to make concrete what the marketing side of the bargain actually is.

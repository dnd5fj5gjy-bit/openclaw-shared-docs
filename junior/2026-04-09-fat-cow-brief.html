Why This Fits Bear Better Than Almost Anything

Tallow is exactly what Bear would use in the wild. Animal fat rendered from grass-fed cattle has been used as a skin protectant for centuries — it's what soldiers and explorers used before synthetic moisturisers existed. It's biocompatible, deeply nourishing, and has no ingredient list full of words nobody can pronounce. That story writes itself.

Ryan founded Fat Cow because he has sensitive skin and got tired of brands claiming "natural" while using synthetic fillers. He started making tallow products at home, started selling them, and it grew. That founder origin is credible and relatable — Bear will respond to that kind of no-nonsense, back-to-basics thinking.

The brand is very early in its growth curve — 46K Instagram, growing TikTok, UK-focused but with a US-facing Shopify store. Bear's involvement could be transformational for them at this stage. That means the leverage to negotiate a real equity stake is there, if Jesse wants it.

The natural/primal wellness positioning also aligns perfectly with where Bear's brand is heading — Modern Savage, organ supplements, ancestral health. Tallow skincare fits the same worldview. Bear could credibly use this product in content and it would feel completely authentic, not sponsored.

Possible Partnership Structures

Equity + Ambassador Most Interesting
Bear takes a small equity stake (3-10% — this is earlier stage than MOTH) in exchange for ambassador role and content. The brand is small enough that Bear's involvement would be genuinely transformational — especially in the US where tallow skincare is a growing movement. If Fat Cow hits its potential, this is the Ryan Reynolds / Aviation Gin model at an earlier, cheaper entry point.
Co-branded Limited Edition
A Bear Grylls x Fat Cow product — a survival balm, a field-ready pack. Test the audience overlap before a longer commitment. Low risk, measurable results. Could be the entry point that leads to something bigger.
Paid Ambassador
Annual fee for content and social. Clean and simple. At this stage, Fat Cow likely can't afford big fees — which is exactly why the equity conversation is more interesting. If they're bootstrapped, the fee ask may not be realistic anyway.
Content Partnership
Bear uses Fat Cow products in content — expedition skincare, field kit, survival routine. One-off to test creative chemistry. Very authentic given the product positioning.

What to Ask / What to Find Out

01What does Ryan actually want from Bear — credibility, reach, US market entry, investment, or all of the above? His answer tells you how to structure the conversation.
02Are they bootstrapped or have they taken investment? If bootstrapped, equity deal is very achievable. If they've already raised at a high valuation, the conversation changes.
03What's the current revenue and growth trajectory? Knowing the run rate helps assess whether equity is worth pursuing — and what % is realistic.
04Are they thinking UK-only or are they looking at the US? If they want to crack America, Bear's profile is genuinely useful — especially the Fox / Running Wild platform.
05Does Ryan have manufacturing locked down at scale? Tallow is not commodity — scaling a tallow skincare brand requires a reliable supply chain. Worth knowing if they can actually deliver on a Bear-sized demand spike.

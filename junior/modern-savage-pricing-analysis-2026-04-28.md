## Why £59/month Works

**Against Ancestral Supplements:** ~97% more expensive. Justified by: Bear's personal story and credibility, UK-made narrative, a blend designed for the 30-45 male professional (not just the ancestral health enthusiast), and the kids blend as a family product. Ancestral targets enthusiasts; Modern Savage targets the mainstream premium buyer.

**Against Heart & Soil:** 10-15% premium. Justified by: UK brand (local buyer preference), the Bear brand pull, and cleaner positioning. Modern Savage has higher name recognition in the UK than H&S.

**Against AG1:** 25% cheaper. AG1 is greens-based, US-founded, and has a different nutritional thesis. The proximity to AG1's pricing positions Modern Savage as premium but not unapproachable. Buyers who looked at AG1 and found £79 too high will feel £59 is the rational alternative.

**One-time vs subscription gap:** £10 premium for one-time (£69 vs £59). This is ~15% — enough to make subscription the obviously better value without making one-time prohibitive. Industry standard is 10-20%.

## Founding Member Pricing Mechanics

**Recommendation: £47/month for founding members — locked for 12 months.**

That's 20% below regular subscription (£59). After 12 months, members receive 60 days' notice that pricing moves to the regular subscription rate. They can cancel or stay.

**Why 20% off, not more:**
- 20% is meaningful (saves £144/year vs regular price)
- 30%+ discounts cheapen the brand before it launches — it signals the product is worth less
- The founding member benefit is the price lock, the early access, and Bear's personal engagement — not a fire sale

**Why 12-month lock, not permanent:**
- Permanent founding pricing creates a two-tier subscriber base indefinitely
- 12 months is a genuine thank-you — a full year at the special rate
- AG1 raised prices 25% in 2022 and retained most subscribers. Modern Savage will have stronger community by month 12.

**What to say in Email 1:** "Founding member pricing is reserved for the people who believed before we launched. It's available to you, and it won't be once we open to the public." No price mentioned in Email 1 — that comes at launch. The urgency is real (170 spots, closes July 1).

## LTV Modelling

At 5% monthly churn (industry standard for new supplement brands), the average subscriber lasts 20 months.

| Scenario | Monthly Price | 20-Month LTV |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Founding member — UK | £47 | £940 |
| Regular subscriber — UK | £59 | £1,180 |
| Family bundle — UK regular | £89 | £1,780 |
| Family bundle — founding | £71 | £1,420 |

**Churn sensitivity:** Dropping churn from 5% to 4% (which good onboarding and community building achieves) increases average subscriber lifetime from 20 months to 25 months — a 25% LTV increase on the same pricing.

**The implication:** Post-purchase onboarding is worth more than pricing optimisation at this stage. A 1% churn improvement adds ~£295 LTV per customer. A £5 price increase adds £100 LTV. Invest in retention.

- This is NOT a discount — it's a credit. Doesn't damage the brand price point.

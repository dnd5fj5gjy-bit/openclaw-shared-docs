# Wildernests — Intro Video Storyboard Concept
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Context:** Jesse asked Calvin to storyboard an improved intro video for Wildernests. This is a concept draft to accelerate that.

---

## The Problem With Most Architecture/Property Videos

They show the building. Drone shots, renders, interior finishes. The viewer thinks: "nice house."

That's not what moves a $200K-HNW buyer. What moves them is identity. The question they're asking isn't "is this a good structure?" It's: "is this the kind of person I want to be?"

Wildernests sells an identity. The intro video needs to sell that, not the building.

---

## Core Narrative Thread

> "Most people spent the last decade building a life that looks right from the outside. Wildernests is for the ones who want to feel right on the inside."

This is the emotional engine. Not "buy a modular home." Not "off-grid living made easy." The line underneath everything: **you built a life others admire. now build one you actually want.**

---

## VIDEO CONCEPT — "The Return"

**Runtime:** 90 seconds (YouTube/Instagram full version). 30-second cut for Reels/ads.
**Tone:** Cinematic. Quiet. Not a sales video. Feels like a short film.
**Music:** Sparse. Something like Nathan Fake, Jon Hopkins, or Nils Frahm instrumental. Not dramatic — contemplative.

---

### STORYBOARD — SHOT BY SHOT

**[0:00–0:05] — COLD OPEN**

*Extreme close-up. Hands. Someone is making coffee on a cast-iron stove. Outside the window: morning fog over tree line. No dialogue.*

Hook: the mundane made extraordinary. Before they know where they are, they feel where they want to be.

---

**[0:05–0:15] — THE CONTRAST**

*Cut. Fast intercutting. Office building. Commuter train. Phone notifications. Email inbox. 3 seconds each.*

VO (quiet, male, mid-40s): "We built something. We worked for it. We're successful. And yet..."

*Cut back to silence. A single wide shot of a Wildernests home at dusk. One warm light in the window. Mountains behind.*

---

**[0:15–0:35] — THE LIFE**

*No VO. Music in. A sequence of real moments in a Wildernests home. All natural.*

- Kids running out the door onto scrub grass, barefoot
- Two adults with coffee watching sunrise from the deck — not performing it, just doing it
- Someone reading in a chair by a window, rain on glass
- A fire being lit. Someone doing pull-ups on a branch. A dog sleeping.
- One wide shot: the home from a distance, surrounded by nothing. It is tiny in the landscape. That's the point.

The point isn't "look at the features." It's: this is what a day actually looks like when you've stopped performing your life.

---

**[0:35–0:50] — THE QUESTION**

*Cut to black. Single line on screen.*

> "When was the last time you woke up and the first thing you thought wasn't a problem to solve?"

*Pause. Let it sit.*

---

**[0:50–1:10] — THE PRODUCT REVEAL**

*Wide drone pull-back. Reveal the home in its landscape — mountains, forest, open sky. The structure itself for the first time.*

VO: "Wildernests. Built for people who've earned the right to choose differently."

*One more close shot: the front door handle. Someone's hand on it, opening it. Going in.*

---

**[1:10–1:30] — THE CLOSE**

*Wildernests wordmark on black.*

Text below, small: "Homes now available. Launching May 2026."

*Website URL.*

*No price. No features list. No "award-winning architecture." Just the name.*

---

## WHY THIS WORKS

### Narrative territory it hits (from brand framework):
- **Control and autonomy** — agency over your own life, rejecting the default
- **Family and legacy** — the children running outside, the unhurried morning
- **Freedom from broken systems** — the commuter train contrast is subtle but pointed
- **Environmental stewardship** — the home is small in the landscape on purpose

### What it avoids:
- No drone flythrough of interior specs
- No "world-class craftsmanship" voiceover
- No celebrity testimonial
- No price reveal (HNW buyers are repelled by cheapening the purchase)

### What it proves:
- This is a quality brand — the production values will signal it
- This is for people like me — identity mirroring, not product listing
- I want to feel what they're feeling — the emotional pull without the sell

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**What you need:**
- 1 real Wildernests home (contact Alexander — the US demo site or the prototype)
- 2-3 actors (a couple, optionally kids). Not models. Real-looking people.
- 1 day of filming. This is not a complex shoot.
- Drone for the reveal shot.
- Color grade: desaturated, warm, analog feeling. No HDR oversaturation.

**What you don't need:**
- VFX or renders
- Celebrity
- Scripted dialogue

**Estimated production budget:** £8-15K for quality execution. Less if Calvin's team does it with a freelance DP.

What Modern Savage is

A 60-second brief so you know what you're representing:

  • What it is: a US health supplement built around "primal" nutrition. The core product is a daily blend of freeze-dried grass-fed organ meats (liver, heart, kidney and more) plus protein, fruit, vitamins and probiotics. The idea: eat the nutrient-dense foods our ancestors did, in a form modern people will actually take.
  • The range: the adult daily blend, a kids version ("Mini Savage"), and a performance add-on ("Summit Stack").
  • The angle: ancestral, rugged, honest, anti-ultra-processed-food. The tone is "your great-grandparents ate this, your snacks would look extreme to them."
  • The connection: it sits in the Bear Grylls world, so adventure, wildness and doing hard things are part of the DNA.
  • Website: modernsavage.co (go look at it before you start).

Your mission

The deliverable

Produce one page that maps 6 to 8 competitor brands in the organ-and-ancestral-supplement space, and ends with the 3 biggest gaps Modern Savage could own. That's it. One page. Clean enough that Jesse could read it in two minutes and learn something.

A great one-pager beats a messy ten-pager every single time. Fight to keep it short.

How to do it — 5 steps

1
Look before you research Spend 15 minutes on modernsavage.co. What does it sell, who is it talking to, what's the vibe? Write down 3 things you notice. You can't judge competitors until you know your own product.
2
Ask the AI to find the players Use Prompt A below. It'll give you a list of competitor brands. Some it'll get right, some it'll invent. Your job is not to trust it blindly (that's step 4).
3
Go one brand at a time Pick your 6-8 and use Prompt B on each one. Don't do them all in one go, the AI gets lazy and vague. One brand, one focused answer, fill in your table, next brand.
4
Check the AI's work — this is the real skill For every brand, open its actual website in another tab and confirm the AI got the pricing and claims right. If the AI can't be verified, it doesn't go on the page. When you catch it being wrong (you will), you've just learned the single most important lesson about using AI.
5
Find the gaps and finish Use Prompt C to help you spot what NO competitor is doing well. Then write the final 3-gap section in your own words. The AI helps you think; the judgement is yours.

The exact prompts to run

Copy these straight into the AI. Notice how specific they are, vague prompts get vague answers. That's a rule you'll use forever.

Prompt A — find the competitorsYou are a market research analyst. I'm researching the market for "organ meat" and "ancestral nutrition" supplements (freeze-dried liver, heart, and similar, sold as capsules or powders), mainly in the US and UK. List the 8 most notable brands competing in this space. For each, give me just: the brand name, its website, and one sentence on what makes it known. Only include real brands you are confident exist. If you are unsure a brand is real, say so.
Prompt B — teardown one brand (run once per brand)Act as a brand strategist. Give me a tight teardown of the supplement brand [BRAND NAME]. Cover only these, one short line each: 1. Who they target (their ideal customer) 2. Their main positioning / the big promise 3. Their flagship product and roughly its price 4. Two health claims they lean on 5. One clear strength 6. One clear weakness or gap Keep it factual. If you don't know something for certain, write "unverified" rather than guessing. I will fact-check everything against their website.
Prompt C — find the white spaceHere is a summary of 7 competitor brands in the ancestral supplement space: [paste your filled-in table]. Modern Savage is a primal organ-based daily blend connected to the Bear Grylls adventure world, targeting people who want ancestral nutrition without the hassle. Based on the competitors, what are the 3 biggest gaps or opportunities that NONE of them are owning well, that Modern Savage could take? Explain the reasoning for each in two sentences.

What "great" looks like

  • It fits on one page. Ruthless editing is a skill.
  • Every fact is checked. You opened the real websites. Nothing on the page is something the AI just made up.
  • The gaps are sharp and specific. Not "be healthier" but a real, ownable angle no rival has.
  • You can explain it out loud in 90 seconds without reading it word for word. If you understand it, you can talk it.
Phase 2 — once the teardown is done

The Modern Savage pitch

Once you know the competitors and the gaps, you'll actually have an opinion worth hearing. So the second half of this mission is to turn that opinion into a real pitch:

The brief

How should Modern Savage promote itself to win? Build your answer as a simple one-page website pitch — a single scrolling web page that lays out your strategy: who we go after first, the core message, the 3 or 4 marketing moves you'd make, and why. You're an AI-native generation, so build it with AI: ask the AI to generate the HTML for a clean one-page site, then keep refining it until it looks and reads the way you want.

Why this is the real test

The teardown proves you can research and check facts. The pitch proves you can form a view and sell it. That's the whole game in business. Nobody expects it to be perfect, they want to see how you think.

## What Source Group Actually Is

Source Group builds what they call "cable-like networks" in specific content categories — launching them directly with rights and capital partners. Their current active deals:

- **Food with Fox** (under Fox Creator Studios) — a vertical food content network
- **Golf** (independent launch, just live as of April 2026)

Their model: pick a category, find the anchor talent/brand, secure capital and rights, sign 2-3 anchor advertisers, launch. Revenue comes from ad sales — either through a sales partner (Fox, UTA) or a direct sales team.

## The Three Scenarios

**Scenario A — Independent Outdoors Network (Source's pitch)**
Bear and BGV co-own a dedicated outdoors content network. Fox optional for sales. Source builds and operates. Bear is the anchor talent and rights holder. Upside is Bear's.
- Pro: Full ownership, no EBG involvement, clean break from the FOX dependency
- Con: $1-3M year-one capital needed. Execution risk. Bears all the downside too.

**Scenario B — Independent network with Fox sales (hybrid)**
Same structure as A but Fox handles ad sales under their existing relationship with Source. Fox gets a sales percentage but doesn't own the network.
- Pro: Fox's ad sales machine without Fox owning the rights
- Con: Still creates friction with EBG's FOX Creator Network claim

**Scenario C — FOX Creator Network (existing EBG relationship)**
Bear anchors the Fox-branded creator network. EBG claims economics. CJ says they don't have a leg to stand on contractually.
- Pro: No capital commitment from Bear/BGV
- Con: Live dispute over economics. Bear is talent, not owner. EBG claims a cut.

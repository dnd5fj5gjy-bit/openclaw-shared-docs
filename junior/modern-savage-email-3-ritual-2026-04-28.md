## Part 1 — What Email 3 Should Do

By now the subscriber has read Bear's origin story and the ingredient breakdown. They believe in the product intellectually. What's missing is behavioural: they can't see themselves taking it. Supplement purchase decisions stall most often at the point of habit formation — people don't doubt the product, they doubt whether they'll stick with it.

The third email solves this by making the daily ritual vivid and achievable. Not "here's how to take four capsules before a marathon." Here's how Bear actually does it — in his kitchen, before anything hard, in under 30 seconds. It's not a protocol. It's a habit that takes no effort once you start.

The secondary function of this email: the family dimension. Bear takes the adult blend. His kids take the children's blend. This is a product for your house, not just for you. That story hasn't been told yet, and it matters for purchase volume — a family subscription is 3-4x the single-person order.

**Psychological mechanics:**
- **Behavioural modelling**: Research shows that showing a respected figure performing a habit significantly increases the adoption rate of that habit. Bear doing this publicly is product marketing at its most efficient.
- **Normalisation of a strange habit**: Organ supplements sound extreme until the person you trust most describes taking them like it's an entirely unremarkable part of their morning. The framing normalises the behaviour.
- **The "tiny habit" effect**: Describing the behaviour as effortless and brief (30 seconds, four capsules, water) removes the friction that stalls supplement habits.

People ask me what I eat before extreme things.

The honest answer is: not much that's different. I don't have a performance stack or a pre-mission ritual that would make good Instagram content. What I have is a quiet thing I do every morning that I've come to think of as the foundation rather than the fuel.

Four capsules. A glass of water. Done before the coffee is brewed.

That's it.

I take Modern Savage the same morning before I film something dangerous as I take it on a school run day. There's no distinction in my mind, and that's the point. The benefit of organ nutrition isn't that it sharpens your performance peaks. It's that it fills in the chronic gaps — the ones that accumulate over months and years when you're not eating the way your body was designed to eat.

I grew up in a family where we ate properly. Real food. Whole animal. Bones boiled for stock. Organ meats at Sunday dinner. Not because my parents were ahead of their time — because that's what people did before the food industry convinced them otherwise. My boys weren't growing up with that same table, and for years I quietly thought that was a problem I hadn't solved.

Modern Savage is partly my attempt to solve it.

The adult blend is what I take. The kids blend — smaller doses, no stimulant compounds, colostrum added for immune function — is what my sons take. We don't make a ceremony of it. It goes next to the water glasses, it gets taken before anything else happens, and then life proceeds. That's the ritual. It's designed to require zero willpower because the habits that last are the ones that don't depend on motivation to execute.

---

There's one question I get from people who are interested but haven't started: *"Will I notice anything?"*

The honest answer: probably not in week one. Organ nutrition isn't a stimulant. It's not going to feel like anything in the way that pre-workout feels like something. What it does is repair the substrate — the foundational nutritional gaps that compound silently over years. Some people notice energy consistency (fewer afternoon crashes) in week two or three. Some notice sleep quality. Some notice recovery from exercise improving before they notice anything else.

What I notice most, after doing this for a while, is what I don't feel. I don't feel the energy wobbles. The mood floor doesn't drop. The hard mornings feel more like the ordinary ones.

I don't know how to separate that from everything else I do to look after my health. But I know this is part of the foundation.

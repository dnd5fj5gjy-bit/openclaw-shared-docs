## Where the factory now stands

The treatment is the single source of truth. The build system has been reset to it:

- Cast is the eight founding characters, under the treatment's names: Stump, Feelon, East, Podcask, Critter, Wiffypiles, Barry, Quadra.
- Each one carries the treatment's own description, catchphrase and stats, turned into a visual prompt and a delivery note the pipeline can build from.
- Runtime moved to 60-90 seconds with a beat structure to match: hook by 6s, setup to 25s, escalate to 50s, turn to 65s, button and end card out.
- Canon tracker rebuilt purely from facts the treatment establishes: Feelon banned from three moons, the rocket always eighteen months away, Barry's crown non-removable, four chairs moved away from East this week, Critter deceased and unbothered.

Voices are cast for seven of the eight. Critter is the one still open, and Wiffypiles needs two clearly different voices or the gag dies.

## The one thing the treatment changes that costs work

Wiffypiles is written as two heads on one body. The existing artwork for that character is one head with two merged faces. Those are different characters to look at and different jokes to write. Treatment wins, so that character needs new art. The other seven are fine.

## Two notes on the treatment itself

**It's good.** The world holds and the tone is specific. The strongest thing in it is "every character thinks they're the main character" - that's an engine, not a premise. It will generate episodes indefinitely without needing plot.

**Nobody in the cast is likeable.** Eight insufferables is a lot of insufferable. Comedy ensembles usually need one normal person trying to get through the day and failing, for the audience to stand behind. Worth adding one before the roster locks.

**One decision to make consciously.** Six of the eight are recognisably one real person each. That's the point of the show and the legal ground is well trodden, but the practical risk is platform takedowns on complaint, not lawsuits. The defence is craft: never the real name, never the real voice, alien read at full strength. The treatment already does this. The separate question is whether Bear's name is publicly attached to it or whether it sits under Bent Antenna. Worth deciding before the first upload rather than after.

## Ten episodes, built to the treatment's engine

One location, two or three speakers, escalates, ends worse and better.

1. **The Rocket Is Ready** - Feelon's launch livestream. The rocket is eighteen months away. It has always been eighteen months away.
2. **Stump Opens A Door** - Stump cuts a ribbon on something he did not build, takes full credit, cannot physically fit through the doorway.
3. **The Prophecy** - East predicts an event that happened last Tuesday. Everyone tells him to shut it. He is, infuriatingly, half right.
4. **Three Hours With Barry** - Barry explains at length why he wants privacy. Podcask has not asked a question in eleven minutes.
5. **The Duet** - Wiffypiles record a love song. The heads cannot agree who it's about. It goes to number one.
6. **Zingtzuno** - Podcask interviews Quadra about ancient combat trivia. Four heads answer differently and simultaneously.
7. **Critter Remembers** - Critter corners Feelon to tell him he knew everyone back in the day. Feelon's brain visibly tries to leave.
8. **The Rival** - Stump discovers Feelon is richer. The whole planet has to sit through it.
9. **Management** - Quadra's manager tries to establish who is in charge. Four answers, three arguments, one accidental hit chorus.
10. **The Funeral** - Someone dies. Critter attends as a guest, not a subject. Nobody raises it.

Pick one and I'll build it end to end as the proof of concept the treatment asks for.

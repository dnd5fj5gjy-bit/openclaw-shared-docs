## Verdict

It's good. The world holds, the tone is specific, and the format discipline (60-90s, vertical, escalate-and-button) is the right call for TikTok/IG/YouTube. Best thing in it: "every character thinks they're the main character." That's a durable engine, not a premise. It generates episodes forever without needing plot.

Two things I'd push on before scripts get written.

**1. Six of the eight are one real person each.** Stump, Feelon, East, Podcask, Critter, Barry read as single identifiable living (or estate-protected) individuals. Wiffypiles and Quadra are closer to archetypes and are the safest characters in the deck. That's not a reason to stop - satire of public figures is exactly the point and the legal ground is well trodden - but it changes three things:
- **Platform risk, not legal risk, is the real exposure.** TikTok and Meta take down parody accounts of named figures on complaint far faster than any court would. Mitigation is craft: never use the real name, never mimic the actual voice, keep the alien read at 100%. The treatment already does this. Hold the line in scripting and voice casting.
- **Estate-protected figures are different.** Critter is a deceased-celebrity parody. Personality rights survive death in several US states and estates enforce commercially. Worth a specific check before Critter carries an episode.
- **Bear's name attaches to whatever this is.** If Zagoria travels, it travels as "Bear Grylls' family office made a cartoon calling a sitting political figure a mound of orange fur." That may be entirely fine, it may not. It's your call, not mine, but it should be a decision you made rather than one you discovered.

**2. Nobody in the cast is likeable.** Eight insufferables is a lot of insufferable. The funniest ensembles have one straight character who is simply trying to get through the day and cannot. Our retired v0 roster had exactly that in Squelch, the janitor who fixes everything and is never credited. He is the only piece of v0 I'd argue to keep - as the audience's foothold, not as a lead. Every scene gets funnier when someone in it is normal.

2. **Art.** Jim's finished art exists for the retired roster, not this one. Every new character needs a model sheet before we can build anything that isn't a placeholder. This is the single blocker on a real proof-of-concept episode.
3. **Who writes.** Treatment says "scripts in development" without naming a writer. If that's open, the factory can generate first drafts to the beat structure and a human punches them up.
4. **Posting.** No channel, no handle, no schedule, no decision on whether this is Bear-attached or arm's-length under Bent Antenna. That decision should come before the first upload, not after.

## Episode slate - 10 premises to the treatment's engine

Each is one location, two or three speakers, escalates, ends worse and better.

1. **The Rocket Is Ready** - Feelon's launch livestream. The rocket is eighteen months away. It has always been eighteen months away. Podcask is covering it live and has questions about a mushroom.
2. **Stump Opens A Door** - Stump cuts a ribbon on something he did not build, will not stop taking credit, and cannot physically get through the doorway.
3. **The Prophecy** - East predicts an event that happened last Tuesday. Everyone tells him to shut it. He is, infuriatingly, half right.
4. **Three Hours With Barry** - Barry explains at length why he wants privacy. Podcask has not asked a question in eleven minutes.
5. **The Duet** - Wiffypiles record a love song. The heads cannot agree who it's about. It goes to number one.
6. **Zingtzuno** - Podcask interviews Quadra about ancient combat trivia. All four heads answer differently and simultaneously.
7. **Critter Remembers** - Critter corners Feelon at a party to tell him he knew everyone back in the day. Feelon's brain, visibly, tries to leave.
8. **The Rival** - Stump discovers Feelon is richer. The entire planet has to sit through it.
9. **Management** - Quadra's manager attempts to establish who is in charge. Four answers. Three arguments. One accidental hit chorus.
10. **The Funeral** - Someone dies. Critter attends as a guest, not a subject. Nobody raises it.

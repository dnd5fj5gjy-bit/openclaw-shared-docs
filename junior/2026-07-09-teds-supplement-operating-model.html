Ted's Health — Supplement Store Operating Model

How we work with a fulfilment partner so the customer feels no handoff, and we earn well.

The headline: a pure Amrita-style deal fails both goals. In that model the patient clicks out to Amrita's own branded site, Amrita takes the payment and ships in Amrita's box, and we get a small commission. That is friction for the customer and thin margin for us. The model we actually want is merchant-of-record plus blind dropship: the patient checks out inside HealthPilot, pays us, and the partner ships direct in plain or Ted's-branded packaging with no trace of themselves. "Someone like Amrita" should really be a blind-dropship own-label supplier. This document builds that out.

The one decision that shapes everything

Referral model — what Amrita and The Natural Dispensary actually are

  • Friction: high patient leaves our funnel, lands on the partner's branded website, creates another account, checks out there.
  • Earn: low commission only, roughly 10–25% of the order, and about 10% of that is handed back to the patient as their discount. Around £5–£12 on a £50 order.
  • The partner is merchant of record. They own the customer and the data, not us.

Verdict: fails both asks. Useful only as a two-week interim so patients can buy something while we build the real thing.

Merchant-of-record + blind dropship — what we actually build

  • Friction: zero patient never leaves the portal. One account, one checkout, Ted's brand from quiz to parcel.
  • Earn: high we keep the full retail-minus-wholesale spread, roughly £25–£30 on a £50 own-branded basket, plus recurring subscription revenue.
  • We are merchant of record. We own the customer, the pricing, the data, the relationship.

Verdict: this is the model. The partner is invisible fulfilment, not a destination.

The customer flow — no friction

  1. Patient takes the health quiz, or gets an AI blood-test review, inside HealthPilot.
  2. The funnel surfaces a matched stack as a care plan, monthly-led price, subscribe-and-save. (Already built in the Harborview demo.)
  3. Add to basket and check out inside the portal. Patient pays Ted's via Stripe. One card, one brand.
  4. The order fires automatically to the fulfilment partner in the background (API, or a middleware/CSV bridge at launch).
  5. Partner picks, packs and ships direct to the patient in plain or Ted's-branded packaging. No partner branding, no partner invoice.
  6. Tracking flows back into the portal. Subscription reorders run automatically.

The patient never knows a third party exists. End to end, it is Ted's store.

The commercial deal we structure with the partner

The negotiation checklist. We need them to agree to:

  • Trade pricing — target 30–40%+ off RRP. On own-label we set the RRP, so the spread is ours to size.
  • Blind dropship — plain or Ted's-branded parcel and packing slip, zero partner literature or branding.
  • Own-label on the core stack — our label on the bottle. Free with several UK dropshippers, no minimum order.
  • Order injection — an API endpoint ideally; a CSV or portal bridge via middleware is acceptable at launch.
  • We are merchant of record — we take the card payment and own the customer.
  • SLA — 1–2 day UK dispatch, batch COAs and heavy-metals testing, returns handling.
  • No MOQ, no stock commitment — true dropship, so zero inventory risk.

The money

Worked example — own-branded men's-health stack, £50/mo basket

Trade + ship cost: ~£20–25  ·  Our price to patient: £50  ·  Gross margin: ~£25–30 (50–60%)

On subscribe-and-save (~10% off), patient pays £45, we still hold ~£20–25 margin, and it recurs monthly. Per subscribed patient that is roughly £20–30/month of near-pure contribution. Even a modest attach rate across the TRT base offsets a large share of TRT acquisition cost.

Referral model for contrast: the same £50 order earns ~£5–12, one-off, with no ownership. 3–5x less.

The platform play — where it gets big

Ted's is a white-label platform sold to clinics. We wire one central fulfilment backend behind every clinic's portal:

  • Each clinic sells its own-labelled stack through its own branded funnel.
  • One negotiated trade deal serves them all, and volume drives the discount down.
  • Ted's / HealthPilot takes an override on every clinic's supplement sales, on top of the clinic's own margin.

That turns supplements from a per-clinic upsell into a platform revenue line that scales with every clinic we onboard.

Regulatory guardrails — non-negotiable

  • Firewall the supplement store from the TRT prescribing offer. Separate lane, separate journey.
  • Authorised claims only. The single permitted testosterone hook is the zinc claim ("contributes to the maintenance of normal testosterone levels in the blood"). No "boosts testosterone", no "natural TRT" — the ASA actively polices this.
  • Register the selling entity as a Food Business Operator if own-label.
  • Written financial-interest disclosure to patients (GMC). The clinician's prescribing decision stays separate from the sale.
  • Keep the partner's batch COAs and heavy-metals testing on file.

Rollout

  • Phase 1 (days): referral link to Amrita or TND as an interim, so patients can buy now while we validate attach rate. Low earn, zero build.
  • Phase 2 (2–4 weeks): the real store. Blind-dropship own-label partner wired into the Harborview funnel — in-portal Stripe checkout, order-injection middleware, tracking webhooks. Frictionless, high-margin, own-brand.
  • Phase 3: central platform backend plus the clinic override.

## Salt of the Earth — the actual numbers

- Pouch ~$0.79 NZD/unit at bulk ≈ ~$0.48 USD; landed ~$0.60-0.75/unit at launch volume
- Air freight $3,600 NZD (~$2,100 USD) for ~15K units = **$0.14/unit incremental** — negligible

- Bundled designer + copywriter offered unprompted; she discounted the quote for brand fit

**The catch that matters:** Salty still has not given a WVTR (moisture barrier) spec for powdered supplements. Compostable kraft typically runs 10-100x higher moisture ingress than PET/foil. Until Melissa provides WVTR + supplement shelf-life data, the eco pouch is a product-quality risk, not just a packaging choice. Cody at Belmark flagged the same thing: they "would not recommend" non-plastic for powders because of the barrier.

## Why Belmark wins the launch run

1. **Timing.** Modern Savage launches July 1. Salty cannot land pouches before late June at the earliest — confirmed June 5. Belmark is US-domestic, same country as the ACF copacker, and ACF already runs these exact pouch sizes. Zero logistics risk.
2. **Barrier.** Belmark's PET/LLDPE gives the moisture barrier the organ powders need. Salty's compostable kraft is unproven on this and may need a conventional liner anyway — which kills the "fully compostable" claim.
3. **Cost is a wash.** Belmark small pouch ($0.54 at 15K) actually beats Salty landed; the large pouch is dearer. Neither is a reason to pick one over the other.
4. **Relationship + speed.** Warm intro via Drew/ACF, integrated supply chain, no MOQ, soft-touch already endorsed by Andrew.

## Why you don't kill Salty

The compostable story is a real differentiator — no other organ supplement has it, and it's authentic to Bear's values. It just can't be the launch pouch. The right home for it:

- **Batch 2 (Sept/Oct)** as the eco upgrade, ordered now to secure the slot, **conditional on Melissa providing WVTR + shelf-life data that clears supplement grade.**
- If the barrier holds: genuine PR hook, faith/TBN stewardship angle, Bear can post it organically.
- If it doesn't: redeploy Salty as the outer carton / insert / secondary packaging where barrier is irrelevant, and keep the plastic pouch inside.

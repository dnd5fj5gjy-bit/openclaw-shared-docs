UK Supplement Regulations (FSA)

What category is Modern Savage?

Organ and gland supplements in capsule or powder form are classified as food supplements under UK law. They are governed by the Food Standards Agency (FSA) and sit under the Food Supplements (England) Regulations 2003, as amended post-Brexit.

This is distinct from medicines. You do not need a medicines licence. You do not need to register with the MHRA. You do not need pre-market approval from the FSA.

However, you are fully legally responsible for the safety and composition of any supplement you import and sell in the UK. The FSA can and does take action against non-compliant products.

What you cannot claim

This is where US and UK diverge. The UK maintains a list of approved nutrition and health claims under Retained Regulation (EC) 1924/2006 (the UK version, post-Brexit).

  • Forbidden: Any claim that a product treats, prevents, or cures a disease or medical condition. "Supports testosterone" is borderline — "boosts testosterone" is not permitted.
  • Permitted with caution: "Contributes to normal immune function" is on the approved UK register. "Supports energy metabolism" is permitted for certain vitamins.
  • Safest framing: Ancestral/ancestral nutrition language and lifestyle framing (the Modern Savage brand voice) sits outside the regulated health claims regime — this is an advantage. Focus on how humans ate, not what the product does medicinally.
  • Organ-specific: There are no specific restrictions on selling organ supplements per se. The restriction is on what you claim they do.

Country of origin + traceability

Grass-fed beef organ supplements need to declare country of origin. If sourced from ACF Pharma in the US, the label should state "Manufactured in USA from US-sourced ingredients" (or confirm sourcing with ACF). UK FSA does not prohibit US-origin animal products in supplements — the BSE concern that historically applied to European beef does not apply to US-origin product.

UK Label Requirements

ⓘ
Important: UK label requirements differ from FDA requirements. If you're using one label design for both markets, you will be non-compliant in the UK. Two label versions are needed, or a combined label that satisfies both.
Product Name
Must include the word "Supplement" or clearly indicate it's a food supplement. "Modern Savage Adult Blend — Food Supplement" is correct.
Net Quantity
Must be declared in metric units (grams, milligrams). UK law requires metric — oz/lbs cannot be the primary unit. Can include both.
Business Address
Must include a UK or EU address for the "food business operator" responsible for placing on the market. You need a UK registered address — BGV London office (3 The Boiler House, Battersea Power Station) works.
Ingredients List
All ingredients listed in descending order by weight. Allergens must be emphasised (bold or capitals). Standard UK food law applies.
Recommended Daily Dose
Must state the recommended portion size and a statement "Do not exceed the stated recommended daily dose." This is mandatory for all supplements.
Warning Statement
Must include: "Food supplements should not be used as a substitute for a varied diet." Keep out of reach of children if applicable. No disease treatment claims.
Best Before / Lot Number
Minimum durability date (BB or use-by) and lot/batch number. Both mandatory under UK food labelling law.
Storage Conditions
Storage instructions if required for safety or quality. "Store in a cool, dry place below 25°C" is standard for supplements.

Nutrition Declaration

UK food law requires a formal Nutrition Declaration table per 100g AND per serving. Format is standardised: Energy (kJ and kcal), Fat, Saturates, Carbohydrate, Sugars, Protein, Salt. For organ supplements with minimal carbohydrates, most entries will be trace or zero.

Micronutrient contributions (e.g., Vitamin A from liver) should be expressed as % of Nutrient Reference Values (NRVs). These are equivalent to the US Daily Values but with UK-specific figures.

Import Duty & Customs Classification

HS Commodity Code

Organ supplements in capsule or tablet form are classified under HS 2106.90 (Food preparations not elsewhere specified or included). This is confirmed by HMRC's own classification guidance for "vitamins, supplements and herbal medicines."

Full UK commodity code: 2106 90 98 00 (other food preparations).

UK Import Duty Rate (US Origin)

Under the UK Global Tariff, food preparations under 2106.90 are subject to varying rates. Many food supplement preparations attract 0% duty under the standard third-country rate. However, some sub-classifications attract 6-12%.

Post-Brexit, there is no UK-US preferential trade agreement. The US is treated as a standard third country. The UK's Most Favoured Nation (MFN) rate applies.

Action required: Confirm the exact rate via HMRC's UK Trade Tariff tool (trade-tariff.service.gov.uk) using commodity code 2106 90 98. Input: "food supplement capsules, animal organ origin, US." A UK customs broker (takes 30 minutes) can confirm this definitively before first shipment.

UK VAT on Import

Import VAT at 20% is charged on all goods entering the UK above the £135 threshold (per consignment). This is reclaimed by the business on the VAT return — it is not a net cost if you are VAT-registered. However, you must be VAT-registered before importing commercially or you cannot reclaim.

For shipments from US manufacturer direct to UK 3PL warehouse: the 3PL will typically act as the importer of record. Agree this with the 3PL during setup.

Key Points

  • Food supplements are standard-rated at 20% VAT. Unlike basic foods, there is no zero-rating exemption. This applies to all three SKUs.
  • You must register before you make your first UK sale, or immediately if you expect to exceed £90K within 30 days. A simultaneous US/UK launch with Bear's reach will almost certainly exceed this in the first month.
  • Register under the legal entity that will trade in the UK. Options: (a) BGV Global Ltd, (b) a new UK entity for Modern Savage, (c) Bear Witness Ltd (IoM — not ideal, may complicate UK VAT position). Discuss with Sara Perry.
  • UK VAT number must appear on all invoices and the Shopify store checkout.
  • VAT collected from UK customers is paid to HMRC quarterly. VAT on purchases (3PL fees, import VAT, packaging) is reclaimed. Net VAT burden on UK revenue is approximately 20% of margin, not 20% of revenue.

UK Trademark

Classes to file

  • Class 5: Dietary supplements; nutritional supplements; food supplements in capsule or powder form. This is the primary class for Modern Savage.
  • Class 29: Processed meat products; prepared animal organs for food; protein supplements derived from animal sources. Covers the organ-based positioning explicitly.
  • Class 35: Online retail services in relation to dietary supplements (optional but recommended to protect the brand's commercial presence).

Filing details

  • Registry: UKIPO (ipo.gov.uk) — same portal used for Sister Swims (Class 25) in April 2026.
  • Cost: £170 first class + £50 each additional class online. Classes 5 + 29 + 35 = £270 total.
  • Timeline: ~2 months to publication, then 2-month opposition period. Total 4 months to full registration.
  • Search first: Run a quick clearance search on the UKIPO trademark register for "Modern Savage" in Classes 5 and 29. Given the distinctive name, conflicts are unlikely but worth confirming before filing.
  • Bear Grylls brand tie: If the brand ever becomes "Bear Grylls Modern Savage" consider whether BG name needs to be on the trademark or as a separate mark.

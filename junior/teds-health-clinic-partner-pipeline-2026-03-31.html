Ted's Health Clinic Partner Pipeline Brief

Prepared by Junior • 2026-03-31

Executive Summary

Ted's Health already has the right strategic pivot: stop fighting for every D2C customer and become the backend engine for existing clinics that already own patient attention. The missing piece was a concrete outbound map. This brief turns the white-label thesis into a practical UK partner pipeline with target segments, example clinics, qualification criteria, and a 30-day founder-led outreach plan.

Bottom line: Ted's should prioritise a 3-wave clinic partner strategy:

  1. Wave 1 — specialist TRT clinics that already believe in testosterone care but may lack scalable operations, follow-up systems, or white-label infrastructure.
  2. Wave 2 — broader digital men's health brands already selling ED, diagnostics, hair, or weight-loss products and can add TRT / hormone optimisation as a higher-LTV line.
  3. Wave 3 — established pharmacy / online doctor platforms that have trust and traffic but move slower; these are better as credibility partnerships once Ted's has pilot proof.

If Jesse wants the fastest path to revenue, start with Wave 1 and Wave 2. They are closest to the pain, easiest to pitch, and most likely to understand the value of outsourced clinical + operational infrastructure.

Strategic Frame

Ted's should not position itself as “another TRT clinic.” That is a red-ocean fight.

The fastest way for a UK clinic or digital health brand to launch or upgrade a profitable men's hormone line without building the full operating stack themselves.

That stack can include clinical assessment workflows, patient onboarding, prescription and pharmacy coordination, fulfilment / logistics, follow-up cadence, blood test workflows, retention systems, and patient comms/support.

UK Partner Market Map

Segment 1 — Specialist TRT Clinics

Examples surfaced tonight: Leger Clinic; The Men's Health Clinic.

Pitch: overflow capacity, operations support, retention infrastructure, and sub-brand expansion without more backend complexity.

Segment 2 — Digital Men's Health Brands

Examples surfaced tonight: Numan; Manual.

Pitch: “you already own male health demand; Ted's gives you a specialist hormone pathway that lifts LTV and retention without forcing you to build the whole clinical/ops stack from scratch.”

Segment 3 — Pharmacy / Online Doctor Platforms

Example surfaced tonight: Boots Online Doctor.

Pitch: a narrow specialist pilot first, then broader rollout once Ted's has proof.

Best ICPs for Ted's First 10 Conversations

  • ICP #1: founder-led or doctor-led TRT / men's clinics with clear category knowledge but operational pain.
  • ICP #2: digital men's health brands already monetising ED, diagnostics, or weight loss that want a high-ticket/high-retention hormone offer.
  • Avoid first: giant retail healthcare groups with long procurement cycles.

30-Day Founder-Led Execution Plan

  1. Week 1: finalise top 20 targets and map decision-makers, gaps, and partnership angle.
  2. Week 2: send tailored outreach to 10 targets only; keep quality high and leverage warm intros.
  3. Week 3: convert interest into a 90-day pilot with setup fee, monthly minimum, revenue share, and clear KPIs.
  4. Week 4: tighten the story from objections and early feedback, then move up-market.

Concrete Recommendation

Start with Numan/Manual-style targets for strategic validation and 3-5 smaller specialist clinic conversations for actual pilot probability.

That combination will show quickly whether Ted's strongest wedge is TRT operations for specialists or hormone expansion infrastructure for broader men's health brands.

Final Take

Ted's does not need more abstract strategy. It needs a clean shot list and a sharper wedge.

Sell Ted's as the operating system for men's hormone care to partners who already have audience trust but don't want to build the whole backend themselves.

# Bear Alignment — Before the May 4 EBG Call
**Prepared:** April 25, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**For:** Jesse — private, do not share externally
**Context:** Bear specifically asked for Jesse to be involved in the FOX Creator Network economics dispute. The EBG/BGV Zoom is May 4 at 3pm UK. Jesse needs to talk to Bear before that call. This brief covers how to have that conversation.

---

## Why This Conversation Matters

If Jesse walks into May 4 not knowing where Bear stands, he is flying blind. Benjamin Simon will read the room instantly — if Jesse and Bear are not visibly aligned, Benjamin will probe the gap.

Bear's last stated position (April 2): "I really would like Jesse involved in these discussions with them too. I hear their argument but I'm not sure it's entirely right."

"I'm not sure it's entirely right" is not the same as "pay them." It means Bear is emotionally uncomfortable taking a hard line against people who contributed to building his brand. That's not the same as believing EBG has a contractual right to the money.

Jesse's job: get Bear from "I hear their argument" to "I'm confident we're handling this right." Not bully him there — bring him there.

---

## What Bear Is Actually Weighing

Bear's hesitation is not primarily legal. It is relational and moral:

1. **EBG helped Bear.** Benjamin and his team worked to grow the Bear platform. Bear is loyal to people who've shown up for him. Telling them "you get nothing" feels like a betrayal even if it's legally correct.

2. **Bear doesn't want conflict.** The phrase "Should we pay my physio?" is Bear's way of acknowledging something complex: many people contributed, and he doesn't want to set a precedent of rewarding contribution beyond what was agreed.

3. **Bear trusts Jesse's judgment.** He asked for Jesse specifically because he doesn't want to take a position alone. He's essentially asking Jesse to tell him what to think.

This is Jesse's leverage: Bear is asking for permission to hold the line, not looking for someone to cave.

---

## How to Open the Conversation

**Medium:** WhatsApp voice note or call. Not text — too cold. Not email — too formal.

**When:** Before May 2. Gives Bear two days to sit with it before the call.

**Opening:**

> "Bear, before the EBG call on May 4 I want to make sure we're on the same page. Can I walk you through where I've landed on this? I've been looking at it carefully."

This signals: (1) Jesse has done the work, (2) it's a shared decision, (3) Jesse is bringing an answer, not a question.

**Do NOT open with:** "What do you think we should do about EBG?" - that puts the burden back on Bear and he'll hedge again.

---

## The Three Points to Land with Bear

Keep it to three things. Bear processes in stories and principles, not legal argument.

**1. What EBG actually has**

> "Ben's argument rests on the investor deck they sent to their investors — slides 26-27. That's a pitch deck. It's not what they're entitled to under our agreement. CJ's looked at the actual contract and there's nothing there that gives them a percentage of the FOX network."

Bear's likely response: "Oh, so they're citing a pitch deck?" Yes. That's the conversation-ender for Bear — he'll understand the difference.

**2. The physio point — but take it seriously**

Bear's physio analogy is actually Bear's way of testing the principle. If you dismiss it, he'll feel unheard. Don't dismiss it.

> "You said something smart about the physio. You're right — a lot of people contributed. But the question is: did they sign an agreement that said they'd get a percentage of future revenue? EBG signed a consulting agreement. Monthly fee, specific scope. That's different from a revenue share. We'd set a terrible precedent for every contractor we ever have if we let this one through."

This is the moral framing that works for Bear: not "they don't deserve it" but "the principle matters and it protects us."

**3. What the right outcome looks like**

> "I'm not trying to blow up the EBG relationship. They've done good work. But I want to walk into May 4 saying: we've looked at the agreement, we don't see the contractual basis, and if they have a specific clause they're relying on we'd like to see it. That keeps the relationship intact and doesn't concede anything we haven't agreed to."

This gives Bear something to agree to: a process, not a verdict.

---

## Bear's Likely Responses and How to Handle Each

**Response A: "Yes, you're right. Hold the line."**
Bear has landed where you need him. Confirm: "I'll say we're looking at the agreement and want to understand which clause they're relying on. That OK?" He'll say yes. Done.

**Response B: "I still think they deserve something."**
Do not argue. Explore instead: "What would feel right to you? A one-off recognition payment, or an ongoing percentage?" Almost certainly Bear means a one-off goodwill payment, not a perpetual revenue share. If so: "OK. That's different from what Ben is asking for. He's asking for a percentage of the FOX network gross — that's $35-80M a year. Is that what you're thinking?" Bear will balk. That's when the number helps.

**Response C: "Do what you think is right."**
This is Bear giving Jesse full authority. Accept it explicitly: "OK. My read is we push back on the contractual basis and don't engage on percentages until we've seen exactly what they're relying on. I'll represent us both on that." Then do exactly that on May 4.

**Response D: No response before May 4.**
If Bear doesn't reply in time: on the call, Jesse says "Bear and I are looking at this together. He specifically asked me to be in this conversation before we take any position. We'll come back with our joint view after May 21." That's still strong — it signals Jesse and Bear are aligned without requiring Bear to have given an explicit position.

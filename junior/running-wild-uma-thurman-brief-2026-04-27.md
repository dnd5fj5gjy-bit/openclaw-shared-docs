## Who Uma Thurman Is

Fifty-five in 2026. Pulp Fiction (Mia Wallace). Kill Bill (The Bride). Two of cinema's most iconic roles — both featuring a woman who survives extraordinary violence through extraordinary will. She knows something about persisting through difficult things.

She is also the daughter of Robert Thurman — the first American to be ordained as a Tibetan Buddhist monk, now one of the world's foremost Buddhist scholars. Uma was raised in a Buddhist household. Her name comes from the Hindu goddess. This is not a footnote; it's the foundation of how she thinks and moves through the world.

She has three children: Maya (with Ethan Hawke, now a rising star herself), Levon, and Rosalind. She is, above everything else, a mother who made choices about what kind of work she did after having children.

In recent years she's been selective, private, and quiet by choice. She doesn't do many interviews. A Running Wild appearance means she chose to be there, in the wild, with Bear. That's worth something.

---

## What Connects Her to Bear's Audience

The bridge is simpler than with MGK or Colman: **survival is her whole story.**

Not survival as a metaphor — as a literal narrative. Mia Wallace. The Bride. She played women who come back from the dead and keep fighting. Her fans know this. And those roles were more than performance — she filmed Kill Bill with a neck injury after Tarantino insisted she drive an unsafe car on set, crashed, and suffered permanent damage. She didn't talk about it for years. Then she did.

Bear's audience responds to the person who doesn't make a fuss about what they endured. Uma is that person.

Secondary bridge: **Buddhism meets wilderness.** Bear's faith-forward moments resonate with his 15M followers. Uma's Buddhist upbringing — the stillness, the non-attachment, the acceptance — translates beautifully into a wilderness context. Nature as a place of clarity rather than threat.

## What To Avoid

- **Harvey Weinstein** — She was among the first to speak publicly (2017). She has said what she needed to say. Do not reference it in social posts. If it comes up in the episode itself, don't lean into it in the caption.
- **The Kill Bill car crash** — She spoke about it in 2018, described permanent injuries. Don't frame her as a victim. She specifically rejected that framing herself.
- **Ethan Hawke / marriages** — She's been single for years and doesn't discuss past relationships publicly.
- **Maya Thurman-Hawke** — Her daughter is now a public figure (Stranger Things). Don't make posts about "like mother like daughter" — Uma has been careful to let Maya have her own career without the mother's shadow.

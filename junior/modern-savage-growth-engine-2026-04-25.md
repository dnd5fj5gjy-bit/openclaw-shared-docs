## Phase 3: Creator/Affiliate Program (Launch at 300-500 Subscribers)

This is Heart & Soil's most documented growth lever: $900K earned media value, $1 CPM through selective affiliate creators.

**The Heart & Soil lesson:** Don't launch an affiliate program on day one. Launch it when you have 300-500 paying subscribers. Why:
1. Product quality needs to be proven before advocates go out
2. Conversion data from the founding member sequence tells you what language converts
3. Founding members are the first-wave affiliates — they already believe

**Modern Savage Creator Criteria** (adapt from Heart & Soil's 5% acceptance rate):
- Audience: 10K-200K (micro-creators outperform mega influencers for supplement conversions)
- Niche: ancestral nutrition, carnivore/paleo, fitness/performance, outdoor/adventure, family health
- NOT: keto as primary identity (different protocol overlap), vegan-adjacent, any "supplements as shortcuts" positioning
- Follower quality over count: engagement rate > 3%

**Commission structure:** 15-20% per subscription (standard supplement affiliate rates). Recurring commission while the subscriber stays active — this aligns incentives. The affiliate gets paid every month the subscriber they brought in stays.

**Tool recommendation:** SARAL (Heart & Soil's reported tool) or Refersion for affiliate management. Budget ~$300-500/month.

**Seeding phase:** Before the affiliate program exists, seed 20-30 targeted creators with product. No ask. No contract. Just: "Bear's been taking organ supplements for 30 years. This is the brand he finally built. We thought you should try it." Founders founders seeding strategy — creators who receive product without an ask are more genuine when they post.

## Phase 4: Email/SMS Lifecycle (Ongoing)

The founding member email sequence is built (Emails 1-3 + onboarding). What comes after:

| Email | Trigger | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Week 2 check-in | Day 14 post-purchase | Engagement, catch potential churners |
| Month 1 refill notice | Day 25 | Manage expectations, confirm next shipment |
| Month 2 review ask | Day 60 | Testimonials, referral reactivation |
| Pre-cancel win-back | Within 24h of cancellation | Recovers 10-15% of cancellations |
| Birthday email | Annual | Retention, 1 free month on anniversary |

**SMS:** Add at checkout for subscribers who opt in. Use for shipping notifications and single high-value nudges (reactivation). Not for marketing. Supplement brands that over-use SMS have high unsubscribe rates.

**The retention number to watch:** Industry benchmark for supplements is 6-7% monthly churn. Heart & Soil runs lower than this (strong evidence from their $60M year-4 revenue — implies high retention). If Modern Savage hits >8% monthly churn, address immediately with the Week 2 check-in and Month 2 review sequence.

## Phase 5: Paid Acquisition (Start at 1,000 Subscribers)

Don't start paid ads before 1,000 subscribers. Why: you don't have conversion data, audience lookalikes, or testimonials. Every pound spent before this is wasteful.

At 1,000 subscribers:
- You have MRR data to calculate CAC that works (target: CAC < 3x monthly subscription value = CAC < £207/$207)
- You have testimonials and user-generated content for ad creative
- You have a Pixel/Meta audience to build lookalikes from

**Channel priority:**
1. **Meta (Instagram/Facebook) — start here.** Supplement category converts well on Meta. Bear's existing audience creates a warm seed for lookalike modeling.
2. **Google Search — second.** Target "organ supplement", "beef liver supplement", "ancestral supplements" — high-intent searchers who've already done the research.
3. **YouTube pre-roll — third.** Bear's video content can be repurposed as pre-roll ads (30-second clips with hard CTA). Reaches the YouTube watchers who are highest-converting.
4. **TikTok — watch but wait.** Ancestral nutrition is growing on TikTok (#carnivorediet, #animalbased have hundreds of millions of views). But Modern Savage's price point ($69) skews older than TikTok's primary demographic. Test with £500 budget after Meta is working.

**Creative formula that converts for supplements:**
- Hook: specific personal story (not "I was tired", "After 30 years of survival expeditions, here's what I've learned about what my body actually needs")
- Evidence: one specific ingredient claim, stated plainly, with a source
- Social proof: subscriber count or testimonial
- CTA: first month free, or founding member price lock

## The One Thing Heart & Soil Did That Most Brands Miss

**30% of their revenue came from word-of-mouth.** Not affiliates with links. Actual word-of-mouth — customers telling people they know.

This is engineered, not organic. The mechanisms:
1. Physical package insert — a personal note from Paul, not marketing copy. Made people want to share the product.
2. Referral program built into onboarding (not added later)
3. Product quality that delivers a noticeable result in the first 2-4 weeks — if people feel a difference, they talk. If they don't notice anything, they quietly cancel.

**The product quality variable is the one thing marketing cannot fix.** If the formulation doesn't deliver a noticeable result in weeks 2-4 — not miraculous, just noticeable energy/digestion/sleep improvement — no growth engine will hold subscribers. The 30% word-of-mouth number at Heart & Soil is only possible because people actually felt something.

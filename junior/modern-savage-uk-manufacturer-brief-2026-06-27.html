Goal: Produce the Modern Savage range (desiccated grass-fed beef organ powder blends, in pouches) in the UK to cut cost and kill the US import/POAO border problem.

The one thing that matters

This is not a normal supplement-sourcing job. Almost every UK contract manufacturer can blend powders and fill pouches. Almost none can legally handle freeze-dried animal organ raw material. Organs are Products of Animal Origin (POAO); processing them into food needs an FSA-approved facility and a willing organ supply chain. Generic vitamin blenders do not advertise this and most do not hold it.

So the real question to every manufacturer is one line:

"Is your facility FSA-approved to handle freeze-dried animal-organ raw material, and have you produced a desiccated beef-organ blend before?"

Expect most to say no. The ones who say yes are the shortlist. The original brief listed four generic blenders that all fail this test, so we start from a better base here.

One spec flag: our blend includes testes, pancreas and spleen. Very few suppliers cover testes, even ones who do liver/heart/kidney. Lead with that ingredient in every conversation.

The three real routes

Route A - Turnkey organ-native maker (fastest). One partner sources organs, blends, and packs. Only credible option found is EU-based (Nutriest, Estonia), not strictly UK, but it removes US import friction and they already make a desiccated organ complex powder.

Route B - Build it domestically (most control, most work). Source frozen grass-fed organs from a UK/EU abattoir, toll freeze-dry and mill at a UK lyophilisation house, then blend and pouch at a generic UK CM that agrees to accept animal-tissue material under its FSA approval. Three moving parts, but keeps it fully UK and protects margin at scale.

Route C - Import desiccated organ powder, blend in UK (fallback). Buy bulk organ powder from a dedicated ingredient house (US), ship in, get a UK CM to blend and pouch. Reintroduces the POAO import problem we are trying to escape, so only if A and B stall.

Regulatory reality (why domestic wins)

  • Organs for human food = POAO. Importing POAO into GB triggers pre-notification (IPAFFS), Border Control Post inspection, and export health certificates. Sourcing the raw organ material inside UK/EU removes that entire layer. The original instinct to manufacture UK is correct.
  • Facility approval. Any site drying/milling organs into food powder generally needs FSA approval under retained Reg. 853/2004 and HACCP. This is the gate most generic CMs fail.
  • SRM/TSE check (clears our blend): banned bovine specified-risk material is mainly skull/brain/eyes, spinal cord (cattle >12m), tonsils and parts of the intestine. Liver, heart, kidney, pancreas, spleen and testes are all permitted in human food. Brain/spinal cord would not be - so don't add them.
  • No pre-market approval needed for the supplement itself, provided no novel-food ingredient and no medicinal claims on label.

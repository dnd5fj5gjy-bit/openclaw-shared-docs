# Modern Savage — Competitor Analysis
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Purpose:** The organ supplement landscape. Where Modern Savage sits, who it needs to beat, and what the actual competitive moat is.

---

## The Market

Beef organ supplements: **$615 million market in 2025**, growing at 5.85% CAGR to ~$980M by 2032. The category is expanding because ancestral nutrition is going mainstream — moving from niche carnivore circles into broader performance and longevity conversations.

The category is fragmented. No dominant brand has built genuine consumer trust at scale since the Liver King scandal in 2022. That's the opening Modern Savage is walking into.

---

## The Main Competitors

### 1. Heart & Soil — Closest Direct Competitor
**Founded:** 2020, Paul Saladino MD + team
**Revenue:** Under $5M (ZoomInfo)
**Positioning:** Doctor-led, science-heavy, "hundreds of thousands of people have reclaimed their health"
**Products:** Multiple specific blends — Whole Body, Bone Marrow & Liver, Primal Energy, etc. Probably 15+ SKUs.
**Price point:** $40-70 per product; customers often buy multiple blends = $80-150/month typical spend
**Audience:** Carnivore/animal-based community, followers of Saladino's Carnivore MD brand
**Social:** Saladino ~1-2M followers across platforms

**Strengths:**
- Saladino is genuinely credible — a practising MD who lives what he preaches
- First mover in the organ supplement space
- Strong community (r/AnimalBased, Fundamental Health podcast audience)
- Multiple SKUs = multiple revenue streams

**Weaknesses:**
- Saladino has publicly moderated his strict carnivore position over time — created confusion and community trust issues with his core audience
- He launched Lineage Provisions simultaneously — split identity between two brands
- Revenue under $5M after 6 years suggests limited scale beyond the core carnivore community
- No mainstream crossover — unknown outside the ancestral health bubble
- Heavy on science, light on story — doesn't have Bear's narrative

**Modern Savage vs Heart & Soil:** Modern Savage wins on story and mainstream reach. Bear Grylls is known by people who have never heard of Paul Saladino. The formula is simpler (one blend, one message). The credibility is lived experience, not medical credentials.

---

### 2. Ancestral Supplements — Category Creator, Now Compromised
**Founded:** ~2016, Brian Johnson (Liver King)
**Revenue:** Reportedly $100M+ ecosystem at peak; core brand compromised post-scandal
**Positioning at peak:** Extreme ancestral lifestyle, hypermasculine, raw organ meat as daily practice
**The scandal:** December 2022 — leaked emails proved Johnson was using $11K/month in steroids while claiming his physique came entirely from "ancestral lifestyle." Community betrayal was significant.
**Current status:** Brand continues but trust is permanently damaged. The Liver King persona that drove growth is now radioactive.

**Why this matters for Modern Savage:**
The Liver King's rise proved the category can scale massively — $100M is not a ceiling. His fall left the community without a trustworthy face. That community is still there, still buying organ supplements, and actively looking for a brand they can believe in.

Bear Grylls is the anti-Liver King: 30 years of filmed, documented, third-party-verified wilderness survival. His credibility cannot be faked — it's literally on camera. The ancestral health community will respond to this if it's positioned correctly.

**Risk:** Modern Savage must never touch the hypermasculine aggression register Liver King inhabited. That territory is poisoned. Bear's lane is capability and family legacy, not dominance and performance-enhancing extremism.

---

### 3. Lineage Provisions — Premium Niche
**Founded:** 2022-23, Paul Saladino MD + Anthony Gustin DC
**Positioning:** Premium, small-batch, 100% regeneratively raised, deliberately DTC (no grocery stores)
**Products:** Protein bars/sticks with organs, supplements — more food-adjacent than pure supplement
**Price:** Premium, deliberate quality signal
**Distribution:** DTC only, podcast guest slots, Saladino's existing audience

**Why they're not the main threat:**
Lineage is very much a carnivore-community-only brand. Saladino's second brand launch also signals he's building a portfolio rather than a dominant single brand. They won't scale beyond the existing ancestral health community.

**Modern Savage vs Lineage:** Modern Savage doesn't compete directly — different positioning (Lineage is doctor + carnivore scientist; Modern Savage is survivor + athlete). The audiences have some overlap but are not the same.

---

### 4. Amazon Commodity Players
**Brands:** Primal Harvest, Wild Lumens, dozens of private-label options
**Positioning:** Price-competitive, Amazon-first, SEO-optimised titles
**Price:** $30-50/month
**Differentiation:** None worth mentioning

**Why they're irrelevant to Modern Savage:**
The Modern Savage buyer is not comparison shopping on Amazon. They're buying identity, not nutrients. The commodity tier competes on price — Modern Savage competes on meaning. These are different markets.

The risk: if Modern Savage ever appears on Amazon without controlling the positioning, it will be compared to $35 private-label alternatives and lose on price. **Do not list on Amazon.** DTC only preserves the premium and the relationship.

## The Real Competitive Moat

It's not the formula. Any competent manufacturer can replicate organ supplements. The moat is:

**1. Bear's credibility is non-transferable.**
30 years of filmed, verified survival experience. You cannot manufacture this. If Heart & Soil signed a celebrity tomorrow, they'd be signing someone whose credibility is acting-based (performed for camera). Bear's credibility is the opposite — it's documented evidence of actually surviving extreme conditions.

**2. Running Wild is an ongoing credibility engine.**
Season 9 is live on Fox right now. Every episode week, Bear is visible on primetime television demonstrating survival skills and resilience. Most supplement brands pay millions for this kind of ongoing brand building. Modern Savage gets it free, embedded in existing content.

**3. The Liver King's shadow is Bear's opportunity.**
The ancestral health community experienced a major betrayal in 2022. They want to believe again — in a real person living the values, not performing them. Bear is the most credible candidate in the world for that role. But only if the product is genuinely good and Bear is genuinely using it.

---

## What This Means for Positioning

**Don't compete with Heart & Soil on science.** Saladino has the medical credentials; that lane is his. Modern Savage's lane is lived experience — 30 years of eating what the field provided, now formalised into a product.

**Don't acknowledge Ancestral Supplements.** Never mention Liver King or that space. Modern Savage exists above that controversy, not in response to it. The community will draw the comparison themselves.

**Don't go broad.** The temptation is to market to "everyone interested in health." The wrong move. The ancestral health community is small, loyal, and high-LTV. Owning 20% of a $615M market is worth more than 0.5% of the broader supplement market because the community builds itself.

**Do use the survivor narrative, not the athlete narrative.** Saladino is an athlete who eats this way. Bear is a survivor who ate this way when it mattered. That's a different and stronger claim. It answers the question "does this actually work when it counts?"

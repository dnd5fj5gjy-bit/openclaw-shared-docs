The Opportunity

Bear's Most Underused Asset

55,000 people have already raised their hand for Bear's world. They signed up for his content, bought something from his website, or opted in to stay connected. They know Bear. They trust Bear. They just don't know Modern Savage exists yet.

This channel sits entirely outside the CPM and algorithm risk of paid social. There's no bid auction, no Meta ad review, no dependency on organic reach. Bear's name in the subject line is the ad.

Done right: 300-600 founding member orders on July 1, $0 in ad spend, and a warm subscriber base that outlasts the launch window. Done wrong: a wave of spam flags that tanks email deliverability for every BGV property before launch day.

This brief covers exactly how to do it right.

Legal

GDPR Reality Check

This list is approximately 7 years old. The core question: can you email them about a product (Modern Savage) from a brand that didn't exist when they signed up?

Short Answer

Yes - but only if you respect three distinct legal bases for three distinct segments. Blasting all 55K with the same email creates legal exposure AND destroys deliverability. Segmenting correctly removes both risks.

Purchasers - Strongest Basis

People who bought something from Bear's website have an existing customer relationship with Bear Grylls Ventures. Under PECR s.22, you can email them about "similar products" without fresh consent - provided the original sign-up had a clear opt-out and you include opt-out in every email. Modern Savage = Bear Grylls family supplement brand = clearly similar to Bear Grylls brand activity. These are your safest contacts. Email them first.

Engaged Subscribers - Legitimate Interest

People who subscribed to BG content and engaged with emails in the last 24 months. Legitimate interest applies for a soft re-engagement email (Bear's personal message, not a product pitch). If they engage with that email, they've signalled intent to hear more and you can move them to the full sequence. Reactivate before pitching.

Dormant Contacts - Re-consent Required

Contacts silent for 3+ years carry the highest legal and deliverability risk. ICO and industry guidance says do not cold-email after 24 months without re-consent. Send one clean opt-in email only. Anyone who doesn't click gets suppressed. Do not add to the main sequence without re-consent.

Three Non-Negotiables (Every Email)

  • 1-click unsubscribe (PECR mandatory)
  • Sender name: "Bear Grylls" or "Bear Grylls Ventures" - not "Modern Savage" (they don't know that name yet)
  • Registered address in footer (BGV Global Ltd or Bear Witness Ltd)

One action before launch: Raemy to confirm BGV's ICO registration covers data processing for associated brand marketing, and that the privacy policy on BG website permits use for "associated brands." If not, update before June 1.

Strategy

Segment the List into Three Buckets

5-8K

Segment B - Engaged

Execution

The Email Sequence

Segment C: Dormant Re-consent (June 1 - before anything else)

Send this first. It cleans the dormant pool before you touch the warm contacts, which protects domain reputation for everything that follows.

Email C1 - Re-consent
"Do you still want to hear from Bear?"
Send: June 1

Brutally simple. "We have something new from Bear's family launching this summer. If you want to hear about it, tap below. If not, no hard feelings - we'll remove you from our list." Two CTAs: "Yes, keep me in the loop" / "No thanks, unsubscribe." Anyone who doesn't click is suppressed permanently. Target: 2,000-3,000 opt-ins from this pool alone.

Segment A: Purchasers (June 2)

Email A1 - Bear's Personal Message
"Something I've been building with my family..."
Send: June 2

No Modern Savage branding. Just Bear. Origin story in Bear's voice: the family conversation about what they were putting in their bodies, not supplements designed for athletes, something whole you could actually give your kids. Ends with: "We're almost ready. And I wanted you to be the first to know." CTA: "Stay in the loop" - click to join Modern Savage pre-launch list in Klaviyo.

Email A2 - The Product
"Meet Modern Savage - our family's daily blend"
Send: June 9

Introduce the brand. Bear and Jesse and family. Why organ blend. Why powder. Why once daily. Show the bag. The family insert card that Bear wrote sitting around their kitchen table. No hard sell - just the story. CTA: "Learn more at modernsavage.co"

Email A3 - Founding Member Offer
"Founding Member price - only until July 1"
Send: June 22

$69/month. What's in it. Genuine scarcity (first manufacturing run, 2,500 units). Founding Member status = 20% better pricing for life, name in the founding community. Auto-ship from July 1. CTA: "Secure my bag" (Shopify/Stripe checkout).

Segment B: Engaged Subscribers (June 8)

Email B1 - Reactivation
"It's been a while - Bear wanted to share this"
Send: June 8

Very short. "We haven't been in touch much lately. Bear has something personal to share before it goes public - no pitch, just a message from him." Links to Bear's 60-second voice note or written message about why the family built Modern Savage. CTA: "I'm curious" (clicks segment them into active flow, move to A2 onwards). Anyone who doesn't open: no further emails.

Those who engage with B1 follow the A2 and A3 schedule. Those who re-opted from C1 join at B1.

Risk Management

Protecting Deliverability

The list is 7 years old. If you blast all 55K with a single email, expect 30-40% hard bounces and a wave of spam complaints. That tanks the sending domain reputation for every BGV email property - Modern Savage, Running Wild newsletters, BGSA - not just this campaign.

Send Segment C first (the dormant pool)

Counter-intuitive, but this cleans the riskiest contacts before they can damage reputation for the warm contacts that follow. A spike in bounces/complaints from the dormant pool harms the domain. Sending C1 first (and suppressing non-responders) means by the time A1 hits, the list is clean.

Domain warm-up if sending from a new Klaviyo subdomain

Week 1: max 500 emails/day. Week 2: max 2,000/day. Week 3: full volume. If Calvin's Klaviyo account has been sending BG content for months already, this may not be necessary - check with Calvin on existing sending reputation.

Watch these numbers

  • Open rate below 8%: domain reputation issue, pause and investigate
  • Spam complaint rate above 0.1%: stop immediately, re-evaluate sender name/subject lines
  • Bounce rate above 10%: list needs additional cleaning before continuing

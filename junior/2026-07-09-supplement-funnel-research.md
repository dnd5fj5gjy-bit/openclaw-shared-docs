# Personalised Health Supplement Funnel — Conversion Playbook (research, 9 Jul 2026)

Governing principle: the results page is a **care plan, not a store**. The same offer, framed as a clinician's reasoning about the patient's own data, converts better *and* is safer (FTC/ASA) than the same offer framed as a sale. Sources drawn from CRO literature, behavioural science (Cialdini), and teardowns of Hims/Ro/Numan/Function/AG1/Thorne.

## Applied in the Harborview demo (results + blood review + basket + supps)
1. **Data-tied reasoning** — each item introduced by the finding that justifies it, then a *permitted* (non-disease) EU/UK health claim. Ashwagandha carries no claim (no authorised claim exists). Never recommend anything you can't tie to a datapoint.
2. **Single recommended protocol as default** (vs à la carte menu) — choice overload kills conversion (Iyengar jam study ~30% vs 3%). À la carte still available for autonomy.
3. **Per-day pricing** headline (£2.17/day) with honest monthly total secondary — lifts subscription take-up 15–40%.
4. **Subscribe-and-save as opt-out default, done legally** — pre-selected pricing toggle (NOT a pre-ticked consent box), unavoidable recurring-charge disclosure adjacent to the button, one-click cancel. FTC still enforces negative-option rules post-vacatur (Amazon $2.5B, Care.com $8M, 2025).
5. **Clinician authority** — named reviewer + GMC# "checked against your results before you saw it."
6. **Transparency / "we did NOT add X"** — declining to upsell is the strongest trust signal; proves the engine optimises for the patient not margin (Function's "grounded in your biology, not margins").
7. **Goal-gradient completeness** — "covers 3 of 3 flagged areas"; reframes checkout as finishing the health setup.
8. **Risk reversal** — 60-day money-back (60d outperforms 30d ~23% without more refunds), cancel-anytime, third-party tested, registered pharmacy badges.
9. **Commitment/sunk-cost + reciprocity** — "based on everything you shared"; "your plan is yours to keep, free" + email-my-plan escape hatch (captures non-buyers for permissioned follow-up, where much quiz revenue lands).
10. **Continuity as care** — 90-day retest cycle framing + Day1/30/90 timeline makes the subscription the *mechanism*, not a trap.

## Hard AVOID list (sleazy + regulatory landmines)
Fake scarcity/countdowns; hidden/buried subscription terms; cancellation mazes; pre-ticked consent boxes (GDPR Recital 32); confirmshaming; **over-medical claims on supplements** ("boosts testosterone", "treats ED", "cures fatigue" — ASA/MHRA illegal, hard line drawn Dec 2025); fabricated reviews/proof; "everything is recommended for everyone".

# Modern Savage — Bear Podcast Guest Brief
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**For:** Calvin / BGV team to execute
**Purpose:** The highest-leverage Modern Savage marketing channel is Bear appearing as a podcast guest — not an ad read. This brief covers who, why, the pitch angle, and the execution sequence.

---

## Why Podcast Guest Slots, Not Ad Reads

The AG1 breakthrough (which grew them to a $1B brand) came from Tim Ferriss genuinely using the product and talking about it on his show — not a paid placement. Paul Saladino's Heart & Soil grew by Saladino appearing on podcasts as an expert in carnivore nutrition, with no sell whatsoever.

The pattern: **trust transfer from a host the audience already respects → product gets the halo of that trust.**

A paid ad read is interruption. A genuine guest conversation is endorsement.

Bear does NOT need to mention Modern Savage in these appearances. He talks about his philosophy of nutrition, what he's learned from survival and wilderness experience, what he's changed about how he eats. The product follows the person. People who find Bear compelling will find Modern Savage.

---

## The Hook for Every Pitch

One line that works for every show:

> "Bear Grylls has spent 30 years learning what the human body actually needs to survive in extremis. He wants to talk about what that's taught him about how we should eat — and why the gap between what we evolved to eat and what we actually eat might be the biggest health problem nobody is talking about."

This is a conversation about ancestral nutrition, survival, and performance. Not a supplement pitch. The supplement emerges naturally if asked about what Bear is personally doing.

## Target Shows — Priority Order

### 1. Andrew Huberman (Huberman Lab)
**Why:** 5M+ YouTube subscribers. The definitive science-based health/performance podcast. Huberman's audience skews 28-45, high-earning, health-obsessed — the exact Modern Savage buyer. A Bear appearance on Huberman would instantly legitimize Modern Savage in the biohacker community.

**Why Huberman would say yes:** Bear is a once-in-a-generation guest. His SAS training, world records, and decades of documented survival make him a unique data point on human performance. Huberman hasn't had Bear. This would do very well for him too.

**The angle for Huberman:** Huberman is interested in stress response, cold exposure, sleep, and nutrition. Bear is a living study of all four under extreme conditions. The conversation writes itself — surviving extreme cold, sleep deprivation, caloric restriction, and what that teaches you about what the body actually needs.

**Contact:** hubermanlab.com/contact — through official booking. Likely has a team/manager. CJ or Calvin should draft a pitch via the contact form and follow up via Bear's team directly (Bear may know Huberman through the performance world).

---

### 2. Paul Saladino (Fundamental Health)
**Why:** Saladino is the leading voice in ancestral/carnivore nutrition and co-founder of Lineage Provisions — the closest comparable to Modern Savage. His audience is exactly: people already convinced that organ supplements work, looking for products they can trust.

**Why Saladino would say yes:** Bear is the ultimate case study for ancestral nutrition lived in practice. Not a theorist — someone who has actually eaten wild prey in survival situations. Saladino would find this genuinely interesting. There's also a business logic: Saladino's products and Modern Savage are adjacent, not directly competing (different customer bases — Saladino is deeply carnivore-coded, Bear is broader).

**The angle:** "You've been writing about ancestral nutrition. I've been living it — not by choice, but by necessity. Here's what 30 years of eating whatever kept me alive taught me about what the human body actually runs on."

**Contact:** Saladino is DTC-focused and relatively accessible compared to Huberman. His team should be reachable via his website (paulsaladinomd.com). This is the most achievable booking on this list.

**Caution:** Don't frame it as a competitive pitch. Bear and Saladino should be natural allies in the ancestral nutrition space. If there's a mutual commercial conversation to be had down the line (Saladino recommends Modern Savage, Bear appears on his podcast), that could emerge — but don't lead with it.

### 3. Peter Attia (The Drive)
**Why:** Attia's audience is the highest-net-worth, most health-obsessed listener base of any podcast in this space. These are physicians, executives, and high-performers who read primary literature and make data-driven health decisions. A Bear appearance on Attia would reach exactly the Modern Savage target buyer in their most engaged state.

**Why Attia would say yes:** Attia is interested in longevity and performance at the frontier. Bear is a unique longitudinal study in how extreme physical stress affects human physiology. The data Attia would want (decades of extreme conditions, blood markers, recovery) is genuinely interesting scientifically.

**The angle:** The science of survival. What 30 years of caloric restriction, cold exposure, altitude, and sleep deprivation in the field have taught Bear about the body's actual nutritional requirements. Attia would want to talk about organ meats through a nutritional density and bioavailability lens — this is where Modern Savage finds its scientific footing.

**Contact:** Through peterattiamd.com. This is a harder booking — Attia is selective and analytical. The pitch needs to be substantive, not celebrity-driven. Lead with what Bear knows, not who he is.

### 4. Tim Ferriss (The Tim Ferriss Show)
**Why:** Ferriss was the podcast that launched AG1. His audience of entrepreneurs, investors, and high-performers has consistently made supplement brands. His format is 2-3 hour deep dives on world-class performers — Bear is an obvious candidate.

**Why Ferriss would say yes:** Bear is exactly the kind of world-class performer Ferriss profiles. The overlap in audience (people who optimize performance, test personal limits, read books like "The 4-Hour Body") is near-perfect. Ferriss hasn't had Bear recently (his recent guests have been more Silicon Valley-focused) — this would be a refresh of the show's adventure/performance axis.

**The angle:** Deconstruct what Bear has learned about human performance from decades at the extreme end. What protocols does he actually use? What has survived the test of time in survival situations? Ferriss listeners love tactically actionable content — give them Bear's actual nutrition and recovery approach.

**Contact:** Via tim.blog/contact (sponsorship/booking page) or through CAA. Ferriss has a booker. The pitch should emphasize what's new — Bear is launching a nutrition brand because of what he's learned, that's the news hook.

### 5. Joe Rogan (JRE) — Stretch Goal
**Why:** The reach is unmatched. 15M+ listeners per episode. Rogan's audience is the purest expression of the Modern Savage buyer — male, 25-45, interested in survival, combat sports, hunting, men's health. A Bear episode on JRE would be the biggest single acquisition event possible for Modern Savage.

**Why Rogan would say yes:** Rogan is interested in survival, hunting, wilderness, masculinity, and challenging the mainstream health narrative. Bear hits all of these. This is a natural fit culturally.

**Why this is a stretch:** JRE bookings are opaque and heavily relationship-driven. Without a warm introduction, it's very difficult to get on. The pitch is perfect — the access is the challenge.

**Contact:** Rogan's team is reachable via CAA. But the best path is a warm introduction — does Bear know anyone in Rogan's circle? Does Bear know Rogan personally? This should be a conversation question for Bear/Jesse before pursuing cold outreach.

## What Bear Should NOT Do

- Mention Modern Savage by name unprompted. Let hosts ask.
- Describe it as a supplement launch. Describe it as something he's been doing for a long time and finally formalised.
- Use supplement industry language ("bioavailability," "nutrient density"). Use Bear language ("this is what I eat when I'm in the field," "this is what kept me going").
- Appear on shows where the host is primarily interested in the Bear Grylls celebrity, not the substantive conversation. That audience doesn't convert to supplement buyers.

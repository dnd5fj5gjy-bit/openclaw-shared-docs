## 10:00-10:30 BST - Zara Lachlan + Jonny Huntington (Long Range Labs)

**Your Zoom, you're the organiser.** Zara is the 3x world-record Atlantic rower, ex-Army, in Gus's Sandhurst platoon. Jonny Huntington is her co-founder, also 3x WR, ex-Army. You'll see her at Gus's commissioning.

**Where this actually stands.** You told her on 14 July: not the right fit for us to invest, happy to help chat it through. She asked for a call with her business partner to get your feedback. So this is a feedback call, not a pitch you need to evaluate. She is not expecting a cheque and you are not obliged to reopen it.

**What you already told them on 1 July** - five points: sponsorship is 90% of revenue with £2.4m assumed in year one; what is the £1.5m actually for when the model is near self-funding; the two files disagree on year-one profit (£463k in the returns sheet is closing cash, real PAT is nearer £200k); back the valuation with comparables; show a downside case at half the sponsorship. **None of those five has been answered in writing.** That is the frame for the call.

### Open with

> "Good to meet you properly, Jonny. Quick bit of housekeeping first so nobody's guessing - we're not investing, that hasn't changed, and this is me being useful rather than diligence. I've got half an hour and I'd rather spend it on the two or three things that would actually change your raise."

### The one question to ask in the first five minutes

> "Of the £2.4 million of year-one sponsorship - how much is signed on paper today, with whom, and over what term?"

- **Good answer:** a number, names, contract lengths, and an honest split between signed and in-discussion. Even £300k signed is fine if they say so plainly.
- **Red flag:** "verbal commitments", "a strong pipeline", or the number moves around during the call. If the answer is soft, everything downstream in the model is decoration and you should say so once, kindly, and move on.

### The feedback worth giving (this is the value of the call)

1. **Fix the profit-versus-cash error before another investor sees it.** The returns sheet labels £462,676 as year-one profit. It is the closing cash position. Real profit after tax is around £200k. Say it as a favour, not a gotcha - the next investor will assume it was deliberate.
2. **The ask contradicts the model.** The cashflow only needs about £179k of bridge. A business that self-funds does not need to sell 25% of equity plus 25% of profits. Either the model is understating what the money is for, or the ask is too big. Pick one and rewrite it.
3. **Sell the asset, not the expedition.** The thing that is actually worth money is a persistent audience across many athletes. Every year of it compounds and the cost base does not. That is the story, and it is not the story the deck currently tells.

### If they ask you to reconsider

> "The honest answer is our portfolio is full in this space and the returns don't work as a pure financial bet. Where I could be genuinely useful is on the audience side, which is the bit you're weakest on and the bit we're strongest on. If there's a version of this where Bear's involvement is the unfair advantage rather than the cheque, that's a conversation I'd take."

Do not put a number, a percentage or a timeline on that. It is a door, not an offer.

### Close

> "Send me the reconciled model and the signed-sponsorship list and I'll read it properly. And I'll see you at Gus's commissioning."

## 16:30-17:00 BST (11:30am ET) - Bear | OpenAI

**This is the Companions pitch.** Thirty minutes, Google Meet. You are the one running it.

### Who is in the room

- **Charles Porch** - organiser, leads creative partnerships and creative strategy at OpenAI, previously ran the equivalent at Instagram. **He is the decision.** Everything is aimed at him.
- **Whitney Glassberg** - his team, on for the substance.
- **Ash Alexander-Cooper** - Head, International Public Sector, OpenAI for Government. Bear's friend of nearly forty years and the reason this meeting exists. He is on your side of the table in spirit.
- **Shardé Green** - scheduling.

### What they have been told to expect

Bear's intro email set it up as: "Jesse leads on a lot of our partnerships and digital... he has a really smart proposal to help develop this and enhance your key pillar ChatGPT query experience." Ash framed it originally as survival and life-skills content, "in line with what you previously scoped with Meta AI."

Two things follow from that. They are expecting a **product idea**, not a sponsorship deck, so lead with the product. And **they know Meta was in this conversation** - Ash put it in writing. Do not pretend otherwise.

### The deck is live and ready

**https://dnd5fj5gjy-bit.github.io/openclaw-shared-docs/junior/companions/**

Six-turn interactive demo, Bear's real cloned voice, works on desktop and mobile. Open it in a tab before the call and share your screen for the demo. Do not send the link in advance - the walkthrough is the pitch.

### Open with (60 seconds, then get to the demo)

> "For over twenty years Bear has been the voice people turn to when they're out of their depth. What we're proposing is that voice living inside the product the world is already talking to. Let me just show you, it's quicker than describing it."

Then run the demo. Click through all six turns and say nothing over the audio. The demo is the argument.

### The three sentences that carry the whole thing

1. "ChatGPT already answers these questions well. What it can't do is answer them in a voice someone already trusts."
2. "This isn't a persona or a custom GPT. It's a handover - the model recognises the territory and offers a name, and the user chooses."
3. "BGV holds the relationships to bring other names to the table for other categories. We can broker it, not just supply Bear."

### The ask - keep it small

Do **not** ask for a partnership, a term sheet, or money on this call. Ask for:

> "One category, one voice, a defined window, and we measure whether people take the handover. Bear's voice is fully cleared, so we could build it without a single rights conversation."

A pilot is easy to say yes to. A partnership needs six people who are not on this call.

### Objections to have an answer ready for

**"How do we handle voice rights? We've been burned on this."** (They mean Scarlett Johansson and the Sky voice. Expect it, possibly not stated that directly.)
> "That's exactly why we'd start with Bear. It's his voice, fully licensed, cloned with his consent, and the clone already exists - you'd be working with the one talent where the rights question is closed on day one. The other names in the deck are illustrative until we bring you signed consent, and brokering that is what we do."

**"Why isn't this just a custom GPT?"**
> "Because a custom GPT has to be found and chosen. The whole value here is the handover happening at the moment of the question, to someone who hasn't gone looking. It's a trust routing decision, not a product someone shops for."

**"What about liability - someone follows Bear's advice and gets hurt?"**
> "Same answer as the rest of the product, plus a real one: Bear's judgement is what makes it safer, not riskier. He's spent twenty years telling people when to turn back. And you keep the guardrails - it's your model, we're supplying voice and judgement, not a bypass."

**"Is this exclusive? What happened with Meta?"** (Ash has told them there was a Meta conversation.)
> "It was scoped, never built, and nothing is signed. We came to you because this is the product people actually use for these questions. We're not going to sign an exclusive on a pilot, but we're not shopping it either."

Careful here. Do not overstate the Meta position in either direction. "Scoped, never built, nothing signed" is true and sufficient.

**"What are the commercials?"**
> "Let's see if the thing works first. If people take the handover, the money is the easy conversation."

### Bear's multilingual idea - hold it in reserve

Bear suggested demoing all four voices across languages ("one deal, every market"). It is strong, and it is a good closer if the call is going well. But lead with Bear in three or four languages only - the other three names are not cleared, and demoing uncleared celebrity voices to OpenAI specifically is the one wrong note you could hit in this room. If it lands, offer to build the language section into the deck for the follow-up.

### Reading the room

**Going well:** Charles asks who else is on the roster, or asks how the handover would be triggered, or brings up measurement. Any question about mechanics means he's already past whether.

**Going badly:** they steer it toward "content partnership" or "brand campaign". That is a smaller, worse deal and it is the default gravity of a creative-partnerships team. Pull it back once: "We can do content, and we'd do it well. But the idea is a product one, and it's the product bit that's worth your time."

### Close

> "I'll send the walkthrough over so you can play it yourself. If there's an appetite for a pilot, tell us the category you'd want to test and we'll come back with what it takes to build it."

Send the link straight after the call, while it is still warm.

# Ted's Health UK Clinic Target List + Outreach Pack

**Date:** 2026-03-28  
**Prepared by:** Junior

## What this is

A practical overnight sales asset for Ted's Health's pivot from consumer telehealth brand to **white-label backend / operating layer for UK clinics**. This pack gives Jesse a short list of relevant UK operators, a prioritisation lens, a wedge for each, and ready-to-use outreach copy.

## Strategic read

The fastest path is **not** trying to outspend Numan or Manual on consumer acquisition. The better move is to sell Ted's as the infrastructure layer that helps clinics launch or upgrade:

- men's health pathways
- repeat prescribing / subscription ops
- clinician + pharmacy coordination
- patient onboarding, reorder, and retention flows
- discreet, conversion-focused front-end journeys

The target customer is not every clinic. It is the operator that already believes in the category but has operational gaps, limited product depth, weak retention, or outdated patient UX.

## Priority target types

### Tier 1 — Independent digital-first men's health clinics
Why first:
- already monetise the exact problem set Ted's solves
- easier founder/operator conversations
- faster partnership cycles than big retail pharmacy chains
- clear value in improving conversion, retention, and fulfilment workflow

### Tier 2 — Broad online pharmacy operators with men's health demand
Why second:
- already have prescribing + dispensing rails
- can bolt on a stronger men's health journey
- likely to value better repeat revenue and lifecycle messaging

### Tier 3 — Adjacent private clinics with a hormone / longevity angle
Why third:
- strategic upside exists
- sales cycle likely longer
- more education needed before commercial agreement

## Notes on the shortlist

### 1) Menschem — best immediate outreach target
**Why:**
- focused on men's health already
- not obviously as scaled or fortified as Numan/Manual
- likely has real operational pain around retention, reorder systems, broader category expansion, and patient experience

**What Ted's can credibly pitch:**
- white-label men's health operating model
- upgraded onboarding and eligibility flows
- subscription / repeat treatment lifecycle design
- better conversion copy and treatment merchandising
- clinic backend support for new pathways

**Best opener:**
"You're already winning on trust and legitimacy. We can help you turn that into higher repeat revenue and a more complete men's health operating system without rebuilding from scratch."

### 2) Pharmica — strongest scale opportunity
**Why:**
- meaningful review volume and broad UK pharmacy footprint
- explicitly talks about automation and affordability
- could have category breadth but weaker specialist storytelling in men's health

**What Ted's can credibly pitch:**
- a specialist men's health layer on top of existing pharmacy infrastructure
- condition-specific journeys for testosterone / ED / hair / broader men's performance
- higher-LTV reorder and adherence programs
- a modular white-label growth engine for partner clinics/pharmacies

**Best opener:**
"You've already built trust and operational scale. We can help you package a sharper men's health experience that lifts conversion and repeat behaviour without needing a separate internal build."

### 3) Numan / Manual — not immediate outreach, but key proof points
These are less likely to convert as partners now. The real use is strategic:
- demonstrate market demand to prospective partners
- show how broad category coverage increases LTV
- show that consumer CAC pressure creates demand for B2B infrastructure plays

Ted's should use them in decks as **evidence of market maturity**, not as the first doors to knock on.

## Positioning Ted's for partnerships

### Simple one-line position
**Ted's Health is the operating layer for clinics that want to launch or scale modern men's health services without building the whole machine themselves.**

### What partners actually buy
Partners are not buying "brand." They are buying:
- faster launch speed
- better patient acquisition conversion
- repeat revenue and subscription logic
- fewer operational gaps between consultation, prescribing, fulfilment, and reorders
- a more modern patient experience
- category know-how without hiring an internal specialist team

### Core offer stack
1. **Launch-in-a-box** — white-label men's health pathway in 30-45 days
2. **Conversion + retention upgrade** — fix onboarding, reorder, and lifecycle messaging for existing clinics
3. **Full backend partner layer** — operating model, patient flows, content, fulfilment coordination, and growth support

## Outreach angles that will land

### Angle A — revenue lift
"You already have the traffic / trust / pharmacy rails. The missed upside is conversion, repeat orders, and patient retention across the men's health journey."

### Angle B — speed to market
"Instead of building a men's health operating layer in-house, you can launch or upgrade with a partner that's already done the hard thinking."

### Angle C — category expansion
"Most operators start with ED or hair loss and leave value on the table. We can help turn a narrow offer into a broader men's health revenue engine."

### Angle D — operational simplicity
"The blocker usually isn't demand. It's stitching together patient intake, prescribing workflow, fulfilment, and repeat engagement cleanly enough to scale."

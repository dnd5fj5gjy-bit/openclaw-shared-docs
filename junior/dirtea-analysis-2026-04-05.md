## Reading between the lines
### 1) Paid social is probably too expensive and too generic
If DIRTEA is paying high Meta CAC, the messaging is probably too broad: "functional mushrooms" is crowded, and the ad story may be too educational instead of conversion-led.

### 2) The brand likely needs a sharper job-to-be-done
DIRTEA should not sell "mushrooms" as the category. It should sell a specific outcome:
- focus
- calm energy
- stress support
- better mornings
- smarter performance

### 3) US expansion needs distribution, not just demand gen
If the US is stalling, it probably needs a combo of:
- creators who can explain the product with authority
- practitioner / wellness operator credibility
- premium retail or affiliate partners
- founder-led content that travels well

## What I would do next
### A. Reframe the positioning
Stop leading with the ingredient. Lead with the outcome. Make the product feel like a modern ritual with a clear purpose.

### B. Split the portfolio by use-case
If there are multiple SKUs, each needs a single hero job:
- focus
- sleep/calm
- energy
- immunity/recovery
That makes paid and creator content much cleaner.

### C. Build a creator + partner engine for the US
The US market usually needs trust before conversion. A better route is:
- micro-influencers with real wellness credibility
- practitioners / founders / coaches
- premium lifestyle creators who can sell the routine, not the gimmick

### D. Use content as proof, not decoration
The content should answer:
- why mushrooms?
- why now?
- why this brand?
- why trust it?

## Immediate strategic moves for Jesse
1. **Audit the current US offer**: what exactly is the promise, and is it too broad?
2. **Cut one Meta angle**: kill the weakest ad angle and test a tighter outcome-led angle.
3. **Build one hero US founder message**: a short, repeatable point of view on focus / modern wellness.
4. **Recruit 10 niche voices**: practitioners, creators, and operators who can credibly talk about the product.
5. **Create one partner wedge**: retail, subscriptions, or bundles that make US entry less dependent on CAC.
6. **Tighten the proof stack**: reviews, routine, ingredients, and why it beats coffee / generic wellness noise.
7. **Separate education from conversion**: use content to build belief, and the ad to convert one clear promise.

## My opinion
DIRTEA probably does not need "more marketing." It needs better specificity.
The brand should feel less like wellness paste and more like a sharp, credible performance ritual that happens to use mushrooms.

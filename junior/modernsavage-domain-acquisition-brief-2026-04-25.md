## Use a Domain Broker. Not GoDaddy. Not Sedo.

The CJ handover mentioned GoDaddy "Make Offer" and Sedo as options. Don't use them.

**Why not GoDaddy Make Offer:**
GoDaddy's tool routes your offer directly to the owner but reveals that the enquiry came via GoDaddy's "buy this domain" prompt. The owner sees interest and prices accordingly. No negotiating intermediary. No confidentiality. GoDaddy also takes 20% commission.

**Why not Sedo:**
Sedo is a marketplace — useful if the domain is already listed for sale. modernsavage.com is not listed on Sedo. You'd have to submit a buyer request, which tells Sedo's team (and potentially the owner) that someone specifically wants this domain. Again, reveals urgency. Sedo charges 15% commission.

**Use MediaOptions (mediaoptions.com):**
MediaOptions is a professional domain broker that conducts "stealth acquisitions." They approach the owner confidentially without revealing who the buyer is or why. The owner doesn't know it's a corporate acquisition on a deadline — they assume it's a speculative buyer with alternatives who will walk away. That compresses price. MediaOptions charges a flat fee (typically capped, not percentage-based) which is more economical on a £1-2K domain.

**Contact:** mediaoptions.com — submit a domain acquisition request. They'll respond quickly and ask for your budget ceiling and timeline.

That's it. Let the broker do the work.

## The Four Things CJ Must Never Say or Imply

1. **Never mention the June 24 expiry.** The moment the owner knows their domain expires in 9 weeks and someone wants it, the price doubles.
2. **Never mention Modern Savage, Bear Grylls, or any brand context.** The domain is worth £800 to a random buyer. It's worth £50,000 to "Bear Grylls's new supplement launch." Keep the buyer anonymous.
3. **Never contact the owner directly.** GoDaddy privacy protection means any direct email goes through a forwarding service — it's logged, it signals a real buyer, it invites a test counter-offer.
4. **Never accept the first counter-offer without a counter.** Domain owners always test with a high opener. Counter at 40-50% of their ask. Walk away if they hold above £2,500.

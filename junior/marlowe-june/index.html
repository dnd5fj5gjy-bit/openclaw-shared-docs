Side Two · the Sound

Standards. Mostly.

Marlowe met June at a winter session in Bordeaux. She was sitting in for the regular pianist; he was filling for a horn that never showed. They played two encores, neither of them rehearsed, and have not gone home alone from a bandstand since.

Their book is built on the American songbook — Porter, Mercer, Strayhorn, a handful of Antônio Carlos Jobim when the room asks for sand under the door, and the originals that fit the same shape.

They favour rooms with low ceilings, a piano that has been loved into being out of tune, and a barman who knows when to stop pouring.

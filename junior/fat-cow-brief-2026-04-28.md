## The "First Move Simple" — What It Actually Means

Jesse said "keep our first move simple" in the email, then the same evening on WhatsApp defined it clearly: 3 co-branded products (Modern Savage x Fat Cow), sold on Modern Savage's UK site plus Fat Cow's site.

That is simple. It is not a full licensing deal or a brand acquisition. It is:
- Fat Cow manufactures (they do this already)
- Co-brand under "Modern Savage x Fat Cow"
- Modern Savage distributes through their UK store (and Fat Cow on theirs)
- Jesse raised the deodorant applicator branding question specifically — Ryan confirmed it's doable (black/silver/green options, one print colour, manufacturer discussion needed)

This is a product collaboration, not a platform deal. The complexity comes in: MOQs, margin split, who holds inventory, and branding rights.

## Questions for the Rescheduled Call

Jesse should come with clarity on:

1. **Can Fat Cow do white-label / co-brand production?** They likely can — tallow brands at this scale often work with manufacturers who allow custom branding. But confirm.

2. **MOQ (minimum order quantity)?** What's the minimum run for co-branded SKUs? Modern Savage is pre-scale — Jesse needs to know what commitment this requires.

3. **Who holds inventory?** Does Modern Savage buy stock upfront, or does Fat Cow fulfil from their warehouse and split revenue?

4. **Margin split?** If Modern Savage is distributing, what does Ryan expect per unit? This defines whether the deal makes financial sense before any volumes are committed.

5. **Tallow source?** Ryan should confirm whether their tallow is grass-fed and UK-sourced — this matters for Modern Savage's brand integrity and will come up in marketing.

6. **Timeline to first product?** Jesse's audience exists now — the sooner a product lands the better.

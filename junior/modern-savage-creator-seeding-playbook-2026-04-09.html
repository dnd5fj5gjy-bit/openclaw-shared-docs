Why This Matters

Paul Saladino built Heart & Soil into a multi-million dollar organ supplement brand almost entirely on the back of creator credibility and his own audience. Modern Savage has something Saladino doesn't: Bear Grylls. That's the unfair advantage — but only if it's used correctly. The creator layer amplifies Bear's credibility into communities that already believe in primal nutrition. Done right, this is the difference between a launch and a movement.

Strategy Overview

Modern Savage should seed product to creators in three waves before launch, with different objectives at each stage. The goal is not "paid posts" — it's authentic product belief from voices the audience already trusts. Creators who genuinely use the product and talk about it in their own language are worth 10x a sponsored post.

Wave 1 — MaySend product to top-tier targets. No ask. Just the product and a personal note from Bear or Jesse. Let them try it.Build genuine believers
Wave 2 — JunFollow up with those who responded. Offer early access / founding partner affiliate code. Lock in content agreements with willing partners.Secure launch voices
Wave 3 — Jul–AugLaunch week push. Coordinated posts from seeded creators. Affiliate codes live. Bear's own content drops. Reviews going live simultaneously.Launch momentum

The Pitch Frame for Every Creator

Never pitch this as "sponsored content." The pitch is: this is the product Bear Grylls actually uses, built on the same ancestral nutrition principles you already believe in. We'd love you to try it first — before anyone else has it. Exclusivity and authenticity over transactional.

What NOT To Do

Don't blast everyone at once with a templated PR email. Don't lead with payment — it signals low confidence in the product. Don't ask for posts before they've tried it. Don't over-explain the formulation in the first message. The first contact is product delivery, not a brief. Keep it human.

The Competitive Edge

Lineage Provisions ($80/bag, Paul Saladino's brand) is the benchmark. They built primarily on Paul's own audience and a small creator network. Modern Savage has something Lineage doesn't: a globally recognised face with 8M+ social followers, a Running Wild platform on Fox, and a story that is genuinely more extreme than "I'm a doctor who eats meat."

Bear Grylls eating organ meat in the wilderness is the authentic origin story. That's not a marketing angle — it's actually true. No other organ supplement brand has that. The creator seeding strategy should keep coming back to that: Modern Savage isn't manufactured credibility. It's what Bear actually eats.

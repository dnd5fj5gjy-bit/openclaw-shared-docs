Route Overview

Total passage approximately 2,850 nautical miles from UK to West Greenland, typically 25-35 days of sailing spread across 6-10 weeks depending on weather holds, exploration time, and how long you want in each place. The Viking Route (UK/Bergen - Faroe - Iceland - Greenland) is well-documented and manageable on a well-found 40ft steel yacht.

Steel hull is a significant advantage: ice contact resistance, structural robustness, and the psychological confidence to push through conditions a fibreglass owner wouldn't. Keep a sharp eye on the hull through-hulls and seacocks before departure - steel boats and saltwater have a long history together.

Weather Windows

North Sea / Faroe Leg

May-June: Ideal

Long days, stable high pressure possible, depressions less severe than winter. Aim to leave on a 5+ day settled window. North Sea can still produce force 7-8 in spring but passages are short enough to be weather-routable.

Faroe to Iceland

Late May - June: Good

North Atlantic depressions track further north in summer. Pick your weather window carefully - minimum 4-day clear window. The stretch between Faroe and Iceland can produce steep uncomfortable seas in SW winds.

Denmark Strait (Iceland to Greenland)

June-July: Best window. Critical timing.

This is the crux of the trip. The Denmark Strait has sea ice retreating June-July. Check DMI ice charts daily (iskort@dmi.dk). The East Greenland current carries ice southward - approach from the northwest, not directly west. July is safest; June possible but monitor ice. Do NOT attempt May without ice pilot advice.

West Greenland Cruising

July-August: Prime Season

Stable conditions, near-24hr daylight, spectacular fjord cruising. Icebergs (calved from ice cap) persist throughout - navigate around them, never between iceberg and shore. Water temperature 2-6 C.

Ice Information Sources - Set These Up Now

1. DMI daily ice charts: email iskort@dmi.dk to subscribe. 2. NASA WorldView (worldview.earthdata.nasa.gov) - near real-time satellite ice imagery. 3. Copernicus Marine Service: marine.copernicus.eu. 4. Canadian Ice Service: ice.ec.gc.ca. Subscribe to all of these before you leave - ice conditions change fast in Denmark Strait.

GREENPOS Registration - Do Before Leaving Iceland

Greenland's GREENPOS system requires pre-registration and mandatory 6-hourly position reports via email (jrcc@jrcc.gl) or HF radio while in Greenlandic waters. Missing a report triggers SAR response. Set up a schedule alarm. If you have Garmin inReach, it can be configured to auto-send position reports on a schedule - discuss this with Amy Parker at Garmin when ordering the kit.

Jesse's Passports - Greenland Entry

UK and Irish citizens are visa-free for Greenland. Jesse has both Irish and UK passports - either works. Greenland is Danish territory but NOT part of the EU/Schengen, so a separate entry is required from Iceland (which IS Schengen). Confirm crew passports are valid at least 6 months beyond expected return date.

Steel Hull and Compass Deviation

A 40ft steel boat will have significant compass deviation error - the steel hull deflects the magnetic field. Have the compass professionally swung (deviation card created) before departure. At high latitudes, magnetic variation also increases significantly (15-20 degrees west in Greenlandic waters). Add this to GPS-based navigation where possible. A properly calibrated fluxgate compass (for autopilot) is worth the investment.

Cold Water Immersion - The Arctic Risk

Water temperature in the Denmark Strait and around Greenland is 2-6 degrees C. Immersion without a drysuit is survivable for approximately 15-30 minutes before incapacitation. Everyone on deck in open water offshore should wear: harness + tether (clipped to jackline), inflatable lifejacket with crotch strap. A horseshoe lifebuoy, throw line, and MOB pole with light/drogue should be mounted and ready. Henri-Lloyd are a partnership - get Henri-Lloyd offshore drysuits or full ocean oilskins for the crew, and Arctic thermal base layers.

Insurance Check - Arctic Coverage

Many standard marine policies exclude waters north of 60° or 65°N - which includes most of Iceland and all of Greenland. Check now. Specialist offshore policies (Pantaenius, Osprey, Craftinsure offshore) cover Arctic. Budget £600-£1,500/year for offshore Arctic coverage on a 40ft boat.

Medical and Offshore Health

Distance from Care in Greenland is Extreme

Nuuk (population 18,000) has a hospital. Smaller settlements have a nurse. Everything else requires helicopter medevac or sailing days to reach care. One crew member should complete an Offshore Medical course (RYA Level 2 or equivalent) before departure. Consider a pre-expedition consultation at OJay Health (Ollie Matthews, ollie@ojayhealth.com) - Jesse's health contact - to discuss expedition medicine kit, prescription antibiotics, altitude/cold adaptation.

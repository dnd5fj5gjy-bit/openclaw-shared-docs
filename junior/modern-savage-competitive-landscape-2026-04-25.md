# Modern Savage — Competitive Landscape
**Prepared:** April 25, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Purpose:** Who Modern Savage is competing against, at what price points, and where the defensible positioning gaps are

---

## The Market Reality

Heart & Soil went from $0 to $50M revenue in three years. The ancestral/organ supplement market is proven, growing fast, and celebrity-driven. The audience already exists — the question is whose story they believe.

Modern Savage's $69/month price point puts it at the premium end. That's defensible if the narrative is right. It will fail if the product just looks like another organ capsule with Bear's face on the label.

---

## Key Competitors

### 1. Heart & Soil — Paul Saladino MD ($50M revenue)
**What they are:** The credibility anchor of the space. Dr. Paul Saladino coined "animal-based eating" and built Heart & Soil as the science-backed organ supplement brand. 20+ SKUs. Flagship "Beef Organs" (liver, heart, kidney, pancreas, spleen) is the category benchmark.

**Pricing:** ~$55-60/month one-time, ~$45/month subscription. Single-organ liver supplement ~$35/month.

**Audience:** US-centric, 30-50 male, functional health obsessed, listens to long-form podcasts, reads PubMed. Saladino's audience respects credentials.

**What they do well:** Science. Saladino can cite the literature. Every SKU has a rationale. They've also built a strong women's product line.

**Weakness:** Saladino himself is polarising and has walked back some extreme carnivore positions. Brand voice is earnest and clinical — not aspirational or adventure-coded.

**Threat level to Modern Savage:** HIGH in the US. They've already trained the audience. Anyone who already buys Heart & Soil needs a compelling reason to switch.

---

### 2. Ancestral Supplements — Brian Johnson ("Liver King")
**What they are:** The brand that made liver supplements mainstream, built by Brian Johnson who built an enormous TikTok persona as "Liver King" — until he was exposed for taking $11,000/month in steroids in late 2022.

**Revenue:** ~$3M (down significantly from pre-scandal peak)

**Pricing:** $30-45/month on Amazon (lower tier)

**Audience:** Young men, gym culture, TikTok native, price-sensitive.

**What they do well:** Amazon SEO, viral content, high volume.

**Weakness:** Trust is permanently damaged after the steroid scandal. The Liver King persona is now a liability. The product is commodity-priced.

**Threat level to Modern Savage:** LOW in the premium tier. Modern Savage's audience is older, higher income, and buys on identity — not on Liver King TikTok content.

---

### 3. Primal Queen — Female Organ Supplement Brand ($2M/month)
**What they are:** Female-focused beef organ supplements. Went from $262K to $2M/month in 2025 with agency scale-up. Capitalising on the ancestral nutrition movement reaching women's health.

**Audience:** Women 30-45, maternal health, hormonal balance, energy.

**What they do well:** Niche clarity. "For women, by women" in the organ supplement space is genuinely underserved.

**Threat level to Modern Savage:** LOW (different primary audience). Note: the "Mini Savage" children's line could intersect with the maternal/family angle if they expand there.

---

### 4. Organised — Bear Grylls (UK, Bison Organs)
**What they are:** Bear Grylls's own organ supplement brand. Already live on beargrylls.com homepage: "Premium bison organ supplements. Ancestral nutrition for the modern world. Fuel the way nature intended." Mentioned in Forbes January 2026.

**Key distinction from Modern Savage:** Bison organs (not beef). UK-based. Bear is the primary face.

**Status:** Live and active. Legal context: there is a dispute Jesse is handling personally — no further Junior action on this.

**Why this matters for Modern Savage positioning:**
Bear Grylls is simultaneously the face of Modern Savage AND runs Organised. This creates a potential brand conflict that Jesse and Bear need to have aligned on before July 1 launch. The clearest resolution: Modern Savage = US market, Grylls family-run, beef blend. Organised = UK/global, Bear's solo brand, bison. But that positioning needs to be explicit and coordinated.

**Threat level:** Directly relevant. Same creator, two organ supplement brands, overlapping audiences. Jesse and Bear need this resolved before press picks it up.

---

### 5. IM8 Health — David Beckham Co-Founded
**What they are:** "All-in-one supplement" co-founded by David Beckham. 90 nutrients in one drink. Clinically tested. Premium lifestyle positioning.

**Price:** Premium tier (£80-100+/month equivalent)

**Why relevant:** This is the celebrity supplement playbook Modern Savage is running — but Beckham is doing it in the multivitamin space, not organs. Shows the model works at scale with the right celebrity.

**Threat level:** LOW (different product category). HIGH as a template — IM8 shows what a premium celebrity supplement brand looks like when it's done right. Study their comms and UX.

## What Modern Savage Has That No Competitor Has

**1. Bear Grylls's 24.8M global following — none of these brands have it.**
Saladino: ~250K. Liver King: damaged. Beckham's IM8: different product. Bear is the most famous outdoorsman alive. This is the unfair advantage.

**2. The "what Bear actually eats" narrative is authentic.**
Bear Grylls was publicly talking about organ supplements in 2023 before Modern Savage existed (Daily Mail, March 2023: "Bear Grylls reveals he takes organ meat supplements"). This isn't a cash-grab. He was already doing this.

**3. The family brand angle is unique.**
Bear + Jesse + Shara + Marmaduke + Huckleberry as the product's first users. No other brand has a family as the authentic origin story.

**4. PRIME Hydration buyer affinity = 49.1x (per UTA data).**
Modern Savage's audience has already proven they buy celebrity supplements at premium prices. The tribe is pre-qualified.

---

## What Modern Savage Has to Get Right

**1. Authenticity of use.** The Liver King scandal destroyed Ancestral Supplements because the founder was performing a lifestyle he wasn't living. Bear genuinely eats this way and has for years. That story needs to be front and centre at launch.

**2. Formula differentiation.** Why is Modern Savage's specific blend better than Heart & Soil's? Drew Kouvel (ACF Pharma) needs to provide the answer. The formula is the product — if it's generic, the brand premium doesn't hold.

**3. The Organised question.** Before press picks up "Bear Grylls launches second organ supplement company," Jesse and Bear need the narrative straight. Ideally: clear market split (US vs UK), clear brand split (family/adventure vs solo/lifestyle), and Bear is publicly associated with both intentionally — not caught straddling.

**4. The US market is the prize.** UTA data shows Bear's US audience (3.6M) is under-indexed relative to global following. Modern Savage can fix that. But US launch needs Stripe, fulfilment (Ships A Lot), and press-ready by July 1.

---

## The One Strategic Conclusion

Heart & Soil proved this market is worth $50M+ and growing. Their audience is trained. Liver King's scandal created a trust vacuum at the top of the market. Modern Savage arrives with the most famous outdoorsman in the world, an authentic origin story, and a premium price point at a moment when the audience wants to believe in something real.

The window to be the dominant premium organ supplement brand is 2026-2027. After that, the market will be crowded with celebrity entrants. **Modern Savage needs to own the story before someone else does.**

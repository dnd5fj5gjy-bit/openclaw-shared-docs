## The Strategic Tension

These two proposals pull in opposite directions.

**FCS/FOX Creator Network:**
- Lower risk: Fox's distribution infrastructure already exists
- Lower upside: Fox takes their cut, EBG claims a piece
- Less control: Fox sets the platform rules
- EBG friction: Every dollar the Creator Network generates is a potential EBG claim
- Jesse is already building content for it: Adventure Garage (vehicle transformation, RealTruck) filmed episode 1 April 1, actively in production April 24-27

**LFG Independent Network:**
- Higher risk: You're building from scratch
- Higher upside: Bear/BGV owns the asset, not just the content
- Full control: You set the brand, the partnerships, the programming direction
- No EBG claim: Entirely outside the FOX/EBG scope
- Requires capital: Very low 7 figures means ~$1-3M commitment in Year 1
- Bear-Delbert complexity: Delbert brought this concept to Source. Natural Studios (39% Bear equity, exit option end of 2026) would likely be the production partner. Delbert has incentive to advance deals that increase Natural Studios' value before Bear's exit option window closes.

**Can both coexist?** Source thinks yes — they're already doing Food (with Fox) and Golf (independently) simultaneously. The question is whether Fox would view an independent Bear outdoors network as competing with the FCS Bear pillar. That's a contractual question nobody has asked Fox yet.

## The Delbert Complication

Delbert Shoopman (The Natural Studios, del@thenaturalstudios.com) is the connector on both deals. Bear owns 39% of The Natural Studios. Bear has an option to sell 18% at end of 2026 — 8 months from now.

Every deal that increases The Natural Studios' value (like LFG production work) is in Delbert's interest. Every deal that increases The Natural Studios' value also increases Bear's 18% stake payout if Bear exercises the option.

This isn't necessarily bad — aligned interests are fine when the deal is right. But Jesse should be aware: Delbert brought the LFG concept to Source specifically. His advocacy for LFG is not purely altruistic.

UTA (Andrew Lear, Ty Flynn, Stephanie Smith) are Bear's YouTube agents. Owen mentioned UTA explicitly as a potential sales partner for LFG:

> "We could partner to launch Outdoors together in a similar fashion... using Fox for sales (a model Source is already working through with Fox) or use an alternative sales strategy like UTA or building a direct sales team."

UTA has already been in conversation with Delbert (Ty Flynn call was scheduled for week of April 27). UTA sent a full "Bear Grylls Digital Dossier" in April. If LFG goes through UTA as the sales engine, it loops Bear's existing talent representation into the deal cleanly — and removes the need for Fox entirely.

**The most interesting scenario:** Bear owns a stake in LFG, Source operates it, UTA sells the brand deals. Natural Studios produces the content. Fox is irrelevant. EBG has no claim.

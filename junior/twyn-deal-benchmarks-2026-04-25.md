## Comparable Deals — What the Market Pays

### MasterClass
- Structure: $100K+ upfront advance per course + 30-35% of revenue from that creator's course
- Bear's angle: MasterClass is a one-time IP contribution (film a course, collect). Twyn requires ongoing involvement and platform exclusivity. Bear's position is therefore stronger.

### Spotify / Joe Rogan
- $250M total guarantee + revenue share on advertising
- This is the upper end of talent/platform deals — only relevant as a "ceiling" benchmark
- The principle: for talent that IS the platform, meaningful upfront + backend participation

### Netflix / Quibi (interactive/premium)
- Quibi: $8-15M per show flat fee + production cost coverage + 20% margin + 7-year license
- Netflix (general): flat production fee + licensing, not revenue share
- Note: You vs. Wild terms not publicly disclosed. CJ would know the actual numbers.

### Creator equity in early platforms
- Public face/anchor talent at seed/Series A stage: 2-5% equity
- Only works if equity vests meaningfully and has reversion rights if company fails

## The Fair Deal for Bear

Given Bear is the anchor (not a contributor), the fair structure has three components:

**1. Upfront advance: £250K-£500K**
- Compensates for exclusivity period
- Provides downside protection if Twyn fails
- Non-refundable regardless of platform performance
- If Tim resists this: ask for a minimum guarantee against future revenue share

**2. Revenue share: 35-45% of platform revenue attributable to Bear's content**
- Benchmark: MasterClass pays 30-35% for one-time IP. Twyn requires ongoing involvement + exclusivity — 35-45% is justified.
- "Attributable" needs to be defined clearly in the agreement: subscription revenue from users who joined for Bear's content, licensing fees from Bear's interactive content to third parties
- Should be calculated after actual platform operational costs, not gross revenue (or it will look enormous on paper and be disputed constantly)
- Minimum per-quarter floor: prevents the maths from working against Bear in slow quarters

**3. Equity: 3-5%**
- Bear is not a passive licensor. He's the founding anchor.
- 3% minimum if ongoing content creation is required; 5% if Bear's persona is foundational to ALL platform content (not just his own)
- Vesting: milestone-based preferred over time-based. Vest on platform hitting subscriber targets, not just on calendar
- **Essential:** Reversion rights on all content Bear creates if Twyn is acquired or collapses — Bear's video content should revert to him after 3 years or on change of control

## What Jesse Should Watch For in Zain's Model

Zain's Year 1/2/3 model will show Twyn's projected revenue. The key questions to ask:

1. **What's the assumed subscriber/MAU growth?** If Year 1 assumptions require Bear to be actively promoting Twyn on his channels monthly, that's Bear's cost — make sure the revenue share reflects it.

2. **What % of revenue does the model attribute to Bear's content specifically vs. other creators?** If Bear accounts for 70%+ of projected revenue (common in anchor-talent models), his revenue share should reflect that concentration.

3. **What's the downside scenario?** If the platform has fewer than X subscribers in 12 months, what happens to Bear's equity and ongoing obligations? Jesse needs an exit right.

4. **What are the exclusivity terms?** If Bear is exclusive to Twyn for interactive video content, that blocks other deals (Netflix, Amazon, etc.). The broader the exclusivity, the higher the price.

## Red Flags — Deal Structures to Reject

- **Equity-only with no cash** — Twyn collapses, Bear receives nothing and has spent 2 years creating content for them
- **Revenue share below 25%** — below MasterClass baseline, which requires less ongoing involvement
- **Exclusivity period > 3 years** without clawback rights or minimum revenue guarantee
- **Revenue share from gross platform revenue** (not Bear-attributed) — dilutes Bear's actual share as Twyn adds other creators
- **No reversion rights** on Bear's content — the most common trap in early-stage platform deals

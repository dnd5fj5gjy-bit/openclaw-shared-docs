## 3. Business Model & Unit Economics

**Pricing (set):** $79.99 one-time / $59.99 subscription per SKU (UK £65/£55). Bundles: 2-product sub $125, 3-product $180/£140. Geo-detected US/UK storefront. Shipping passed to customer, free over ~$100.

**COGS at the 2,500-unit tier:** Adult $24.45, Kids $22.28, Summit $18.90, plus pouch $0.50-1.50 and $0.15 insert. At 10,000 units these fall to roughly $22 / $20 / $17.70 - reordering at scale adds ~4-6 points of margin.

**Margin:** ~63.9% gross at launch mix (my April model), profitable per subscriber by month 2. Jesse's benchmark holds: at 10,000 subs, comfortably ~$500K/month profit; Bear's stated goal is 20,000 subscribers ("then we all have a business forever"), with targets of 5,000 customers in month 1, 20,000 by month 6, 50,000 by year end. Those targets require paid acquisition beyond the owned list - the $100K marketing budget Bear floated in April was never decided and needs resolving (see §10).

**First order economics:** 7,500 units (~$163K COGS) at launch pricing represents ~$450-600K of retail value depending on sub/one-time mix. Reorder discipline: trigger at ~40% stock remaining; next order recommended at 10,000 units (5K adult / 2.5K kids / 2.5K Summit), ~£170-185K.

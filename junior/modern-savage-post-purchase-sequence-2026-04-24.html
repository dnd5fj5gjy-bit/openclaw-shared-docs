Purpose: What happens AFTER someone subscribes. This sequence is what separates 5% monthly churn from 8%. At 170 founding customers, the difference is worth ~$52K in lifetime contribution.



Why This Matters More Than the Launch Sequence


The launch emails (1-4) get them to the checkout. The post-purchase sequence determines whether they stay.


The highest-churn moment for supplements: Month 2. The first bottle arrives, excitement is high. By Month 2 the novelty is gone, the direct impact isn't always visible, and cancellation is only one click away. Every brand that drops from 6-8% monthly churn to 4-5% does it by filling this window with meaning, not just reminders.


The job of this sequence: reinforce the identity purchase. Make them feel like the person who takes this seriously — before they have the results to prove it.

Referral Program Mechanics (launch at Week 2)


Offer: "Give your first month free, get your next month free."

  • Referrer gets one month free credit when their referral subscribes and completes first month
  • Referred friend gets first month free (reducing their barrier to try)
  • No discount codes — credit applied automatically to the account

Why this structure:

  • Referrer is incentivised to actually recommend, not just share a link
  • Referred friend has zero financial risk, which removes the biggest objection ("what if I don't like it?")
  • One-month delay on referrer credit means both parties stay subscribers long enough to form a habit

Launch timing: Surface in OB-5 (Day 28). Also mention briefly in OB-3 (Day 7) as a teaser — "we'll tell you how to share this at the one-month mark."


What to avoid: A complicated tiered referral system. Keep it simple: one referral = one free month. That's it.



Churn Trigger Points and Responses


The two moments when people cancel:

  1. After Month 1 — didn't notice results, forgot they had it, or got surprised by the charge
  1. After Month 3 — results plateaued, subscription fatigue

OB-5 (Day 28) prevents the Month 1 cancel by making the renewal feel intentional and earned — not something that sneaks up on them.


For Month 3: A separate "Quarter done" email (not in this sequence) should hit around Day 90. Content: a progress reflection prompt + a 90-day research insight. Not a save-offer. Save-offers cheapen the brand and attract the wrong customer. Instead, give them something to think about that deepens the investment.


Never: Offer a discount to prevent cancellation. It trains subscribers to threaten cancellation to get cheaper prices. The ancestral health buyer isn't price-sensitive — they're conviction-sensitive. If they're cancelling, the issue is belief, not price. Address belief.

Wildernests Scope Renegotiation

Your Monday 1:1 with Alex · 29 June 2026

You called this 1:1 to renegotiate quality vs quantity. Good instinct. But the way you win this room is not the way you've been framing it in your head. Here's the brief.

1. The one thing to get right

You've been framing this as "the video grind isn't my lane — this is a full-time graphic designer job." That's emotionally true and Bear agrees with you. But it is the weakest card you can play, and if you lead with it Alex can beat it with one document.

Reframe before you walk in. The negotiation is not "this isn't my job." It's:

"What split of this work actually maximises value for Wildernests — and where does my $480k/year of equity time produce the highest return?"

That framing makes you the person solving Alex's problem, not the contractor trying to shrink his deliverables. Same outcome you want. Completely different reception.

2. The uncomfortable truth (hold it before Alex says it)

The signed deal works against the "not my lane" argument. Know this cold so it doesn't surprise you mid-call:

  • The Equity Services Agreement (DocuSigned 22 Apr, backdated 1 Apr) ties continuation to Bear Witness producing "market-ready, luxury-quality, accurate generated videos of the homes."
  • Alex flagged the exact accuracy issues (geometry, layout) before signing and reserved the right to hire another firm if quality isn't delivered.
  • So the Daniel/Gunnar accuracy demands are the contract being enforced — not scope creep. Argue "accuracy isn't my job" and Alex points at the page; you've lost the high ground.

Don't fight on "scope creep." Fight on structure: the deal asks one person to be photorealist + 3D technician + architect-accurate + high-volume producer + brand storyteller. That's not one role, it's a department. That's the unsustainable part, and it's defensible.

4. The proposal to put on the table

  1. Redefine the role to Creative Director + Marketing Lead. You own brand, story, films, hero-shot direction, investor visuals, GTM. You set the standard and approve — you don't execute every variation.
  2. Wildernests owns the production pipeline. Dedicated 3D/graphics capacity (Salman +1, or a hire) executes accuracy/volume to your brief. Daniel QA's against their pipeline, not yours.
  3. Lock the aesthetic spec once. The biggest waste is the spec changing every iteration. One locked visual bible (materials, lighting, furniture, accuracy tolerances), signed off by Alex + Daniel + Gunnar. After lock, changes are change-requests, not free reworks.
  4. Cadence over volume. Sequence environments instead of all five at once, so quality is achievable instead of "rolling the dice."
  5. Amend Section 2 (Duties) to match. The contract calls Duties "adjustable" — use that. Restate the deliverable as creative direction + marketing + final films, with technical production as a Wildernests-resourced function you direct.

6. Tactics

  • Open warm, not aggrieved. Alex is the one you trust. Lead with: "I want this to work and I want to give you market-ready visuals. I've thought hard about how we actually get there, because the current setup won't."
  • Lead with structure, not feelings. Your candid message already landed the emotional point. This call is for the fix.
  • Concede what costs you nothing: you stay fully accountable for quality and standard. That neutralises the contract card and the "rolling the dice" fear in one move.
  • Hold the line on: you stop being the high-volume executor; spec gets locked; production gets resourced.
  • BATNA, used quietly: replacing you means replacing Bear Witness's name on the project. Don't say it — it's why you can be generous in the room.
  • Bear's backing is a reserve card, not an opener. Use the substance — "this is a department, not a person" — as your own line. Keep Bear in your back pocket.

Bottom line

You're not asking to do less. You're asking to be pointed at the work that actually sells homes and raises money — and to stop being a one-person 3D department doing a job Bear correctly called a full-time graphic designer's.

Frame it as solving Alex's market-ready problem, own the quality standard out loud, lock the spec, move the grind to dedicated resource. Same equity, same money, vastly higher return for them and for you.

Go in as the person fixing it, not the person escaping it.

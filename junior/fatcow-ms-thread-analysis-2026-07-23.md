One real concession you may not have clocked: in the 23 July email you dropped the **refund adjustment** and the **invoice floor** that were in the drafted version. Losing the floor is fine, you replaced it with the percentage mechanic and won that. Losing the refund adjustment matters more, and clause 11.4 of the contract puts it back in through the settlement timing instead. Flagged below.

## The one soft spot

Your line "maybe it's easier if you don't pay us any money until it's past the refund possible time" pushes Ryan's cash out by roughly four weeks. He is funding production, pick, pack and postage before a penny reaches him, and he wrote that settlement is "the part that matters most to us". He has agreed to it in writing, but I would not be surprised to see it come back once he models it.

If it does, the trade is: give him the 14-day terms he wanted, and take back the change-of-mind refund adjustment instead, where he credits you his unit price less his actual cost of goods. Same outcome, and he keeps his cash. Do not give up both.

## What nobody has mentioned yet

None of this appeared anywhere in the thread, and all of it is where the actual risk sits:

- How long it runs and what happens when it ends. No term, no notice, no treatment of artwork, listings or leftover packs.
- Who is Responsible Person on the safety file, and whether the CPSR and SCPN notification cover the co-branded pack as sold, not just his existing formula.
- Who actually manufactures it. Top Dog Now Ltd is registered as internet retail at a virtual office in Constance Street. He does not make this himself.
- Product liability and recall insurance. Never raised. Nobody should be selling a skin product under Bear's credit line without a certificate on file.
- Customer data. Dropship puts the name and address of every Modern Savage customer through his 3PL. Without processor terms there is nothing stopping them appearing on a Fat Cow list, which is the same leak the insert restriction is there to prevent.
- Which Modern Savage entity signs. It is a Delaware C-Corp selling UK domestic goods in GBP with UK VAT. That is Raemy's call and it affects VAT recovery and royalty withholding.

All of it is drafted into the agreement. None of it should be controversial.

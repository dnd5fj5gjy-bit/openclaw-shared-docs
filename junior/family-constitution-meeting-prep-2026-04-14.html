Framework

What a Family Constitution typically governs

Understanding the full scope helps Jesse go into the meeting knowing which sections he is confident on and which need more thought.

1

Family Values and Mission Core

A statement of what the family stands for — the principles, values, and beliefs that guide all decisions. For the Grylls family, this likely includes faith, adventure, service, family loyalty, and stewardship of the Bear Grylls brand. This section sets the tone for everything else.

2

Wealth Governance — Who Controls What Key discussion point

How family assets are held, managed, and distributed. This covers trusts, ownership structures, investment mandates, and who has authority over which decisions. With BGV as a family office, assets across the UK and Switzerland, and Jesse managing day-to-day operations, the governance structure needs to be clear and legally sound.

3

Business Succession and Ownership Key discussion point

What happens to the businesses if Bear steps back, if Jesse wants to exit BGV, or in the event of death or incapacity? Modern Savage is 100% Jesse's — how does that sit within the family structure? Who inherits equity in Ted's Health, Wildernests, EBG retainer contracts? These decisions need documenting before they become disputes.

4

Brand and IP Management

The Bear Grylls name and brand is the family's most valuable asset. The constitution should address: who controls the BG brand long-term, what happens to existing licensing deals (Luminox, Running Wild), how new deals get approved, and whether Jesse or other family members have authority to make brand decisions. This intersects directly with Bear's will.

5

Decision-Making and Family Council

How major decisions get made. Does a family council vote? Who has veto rights? What requires consensus vs unilateral authority? For a family where Bear is the public face but Jesse runs the commercial operations, the delineation of authority is critical to avoid future conflict.

6

Next Generation — Education, Opportunity, Responsibility

What obligations does the family have to the next generation? Are Bear and Shara's children (Jesse and siblings) entitled to income from BGV businesses? At what age and under what conditions? What are the expectations in return — involvement in the business, use of the brand, behaviour standards?

7

Philanthropy and Giving

The family's approach to charitable giving. Does BGV have a formal philanthropy mandate? Are there causes Bear or Jesse support that should be embedded in the constitution? This is often linked to the brand — Bear's outdoor/youth work is both personal and commercial.

8

Dispute Resolution

What happens when family members disagree? A good constitution pre-empts conflict by establishing mediation processes, escalation paths, and binding arbitration clauses before they are needed. With multiple businesses and significant assets, this is not optional.

Agenda

Questions worth raising with John

These are the areas most likely to need refinement in a 92-page first draft. Go in with these in mind and see which ones John flags independently.

Is the governance structure legally enforceable?

A family constitution is often a moral document rather than a legal one. What parts of the 92-page draft need to be backed by actual legal instruments (trusts, shareholder agreements, wills) to be binding? John should be clear on what is advisory vs what carries legal weight.

How does the constitution interact with the wills?

Bear and Shara are redrafting their wills with Steven Appleton. The constitution and the wills must be consistent. Has John reviewed the will drafts? Are there any conflicts between what the constitution says and what the wills currently do?

What is the trust structure for the Swiss assets?

UBS Switzerland holds over 900K CHF. Is this held personally or in a structure? Does it sit within the family constitution framework or sit outside it? What are the cross-border implications for UK estate planning?

How does Modern Savage fit?

Modern Savage is 100% Jesse's — not a BGV family office asset. If Jesse builds this to significant value, does the constitution make any claim on it, or is it explicitly carved out? This needs to be clear before the business grows.

What happens to the BG brand licensing if Bear dies?

Luminox royalties ($100K/yr from 2027), Running Wild, and any future licensing are all tied to Bear personally. The constitution should have a clear protocol for who manages and controls brand licensing in the event of Bear's death or incapacity. Jesse needs to be on this governance chain.

Is there a dividend / distribution policy?

As BGV generates income (retainers, royalties, brand deals), does the constitution define how profits are distributed to family members? Is Jesse on a salary, profit share, or equity model? This matters for tax planning and for family expectations.

What are the entry/exit rules for family members?

If Jesse or another family member wants to exit BGV or step back from a role, what are the buyout terms? Does the constitution give any family member the right to force a valuation and sale? These provisions protect everyone — including Jesse — if circumstances change.

Is the 92-page draft ready to share with Bear and Shara?

A 92-page document is comprehensive but may overwhelm family members who are not document-oriented. Ask John if a shorter executive summary version would be useful for getting Bear and Shara's buy-in before finalising.

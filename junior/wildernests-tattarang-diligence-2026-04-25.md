## Question 3 — Competitive Landscape
**ANSWER READY — Alex can send this directly**

---

The four companies Taki named represent four different segments of the modular/prefab space. None do what Wildernests does.

**haus.me** (US, seed stage) offers off-grid capable modular homes with solar, battery, and air-to-water generation. The concept is closest to ours, but "off-grid capable" remains an option rather than the architecture. Grid connection is still available and assumed in their build economics. Raised $4.27M seed in 2022, pre-revenue or very early stage.

**Modscape** (Australia, $6M revenue, 600+ projects) is a premium custom modular builder. Projects are entirely infrastructure-dependent — standard utility connections required. They compete on build speed and design quality, not autonomy. Six million in revenue sounds meaningful until you see their project price points ($2M-$4M+ per home) — it's a low-volume, high-value bespoke business.

**CABN** (Australia, $7.85M Series A) builds net-zero eco-cabins with integrated solar and battery. Off-grid capable, well-executed, but purpose-built for hospitality and tourism (11 operational properties, 90%+ occupancy rate). Not a residential development platform and not scalable in the way Wildernests is designed to scale.

**Dvele** (US, $41.5M Series B) is the most sophisticated technology player in the group. Their Dvele IQ operating system tracks 4M+ data points monthly — health, air quality, water quality. But Dvele homes still assume grid electricity and municipal water/sanitation. The technology sophistication is in monitoring and optimisation, not in eliminating infrastructure dependency.

**What Wildernests does that none of these do:** True zero-infrastructure deployment. No municipal water connection. No septic. No electrical grid. No communications lines. This isn't a feature option — it's the core architecture. The development economics implication is significant: projects that currently take years (and often fail) due to infrastructure permitting timelines can be compressed to months. We eliminate the dependency that kills most off-grid development projects.

The caveat an informed investor will raise: these competitors are all delivering proven, approved technologies at volume. Wildernests' differentiation is real but needs to be demonstrated at scale, and the regulatory pathway for fully autonomous single-unit water/sanitation in different jurisdictions needs to be mapped clearly. We welcome this question — it is the right one to ask, and we have specific answers for each market.

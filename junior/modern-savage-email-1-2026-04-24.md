# Modern Savage — Email 1: Re-Engagement Launch Email
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**List:** 170 warm signups (pre-launch waitlist, no product received)
**Goal:** Re-engage, tell origin story, create founding member urgency, drive reply/click

---

## Part 1 — What Email 1 Should Achieve and Why

**The core tension you're managing:** These 170 people raised their hand. They said yes before a product existed — which is a strong signal. But time has passed, inboxes have filled up, and memory fades. Your first job isn't to sell. It's to remind them why they cared, and make them feel glad they waited.

Welcome emails average 50-60% open rates vs. 20-25% for regular campaigns — the highest of any email type. That window matters. Research on supplement pre-launch sequences consistently shows that education-first sequences (6:2 ratio of educational to promotional content) outperform discount-first approaches. The trust deficit in supplements is real — people have been burned by inflated claims. Your strongest asset here isn't the product; it's the story behind why it exists and who's behind it. Lead with that.

The psychology of the first email is about **commitment and consistency**: the subscriber has already taken an action (signing up). Affirm that action. Make them feel smart for doing it. When you frame them as a founding tribe with privileged access — and that's real, not a trick — it activates reciprocity and exclusivity at the same time. Pre-launch sequences work best when they treat the reader as a co-conspirator in the mission, not a target for a sale.

The specific opportunity here: this list has been waiting. That's a feature, not a bug, if you frame it right. Waiting implies the product was worth getting right. The opening line should honor their patience while creating forward momentum. Bear Grylls' personal voice — direct, warm, grounded in lived experience — is the differentiator. No brand could write this email. Only a person could.

## Part 3 — Subject Line A/B Test Notes

Run an A/B split on subject line with a 30% test window. Pick winner by 4-hour open rate, then send to remainder.

| Version | Subject Line | Psychology | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (primary)** | `I owe you an update.` | Personal accountability, pattern interrupt, curiosity | Feels like a real person, not a brand. "Owe" signals honesty. Strong open rate expected from warm list. |
| **B** | `You signed up a while ago. This is why it took this long.` | Transparency + story hook | Longer subject but tells them exactly what's inside. May outperform A with colder segments of the list. |
| **C (test if budget allows)** | `Still with us?` | Direct question, re-engagement trigger | Shortest option. High curiosity. Slightly riskier — could feel abrupt — but powerful if the brand voice is already established. |

**Recommended winner if open rates are close:** go with B for pure conversion, A for brand voice consistency.

**Preview text should always complement, not repeat:** If subject is "I owe you an update," preview should add new information ("You signed up a while ago. Here's where things stand — and what it means for you.") — never mirror the subject line.

**One thing to track beyond open rate:** Reply rate. This email explicitly asks for a reply ("yes"). Reply rate is both a deliverability signal and a warm list health check. A reply rate above 2% means the list is very healthy. Below 0.5%, the list needs a re-permission flow before launch.

## Implementation Notes for Calvin

- Send from a personal name (`Bear Grylls` or `Jesse Grylls`), not `Modern Savage` — personal sender names increase open rates 15-20%
- Plain text or minimal HTML only — heavy templated emails signal "brand blast," which kills the intimacy this copy requires
- Send Tuesday or Wednesday, 9am-11am recipient local time
- Do NOT include a discount in Email 1 — the research is clear that leading with price signals you don't believe in the product's core value
- Set up a tag/segment for everyone who replies "yes" — this is your highest-intent founding member segment and should receive a separate sequence
- Deliverability priority: if the domain is new, warm it with 30-50 sends on day 1, scale up over 5 days to hit the full 170

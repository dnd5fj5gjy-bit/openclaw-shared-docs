# Wildernests — Who Actually Buys
**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**Author:** Junior
**Purpose:** Rigorous buyer profile. Jesse has equity on the line. The strategy only works if the targeting is right.

---

## The Wrong Way to Think About This

Wildernests is not a tiny home. It's not an ADU. It's not an eco-retreat.

Tiny home buyers are motivated by affordability and minimalism. Wildernests buyers have $200K+ household income and are choosing to spend $200-500K on a second property. These are completely different psychology sets. The Mesocore/tiny home market data (ADU, rental income, downsizing) is irrelevant to Wildernests.

The right lens: **luxury lifestyle real estate driven by identity, not investment returns.**

---

## THE PRIMARY BUYER

**Demographics**
- Household income: $200K-500K+ (one or both high earners)
- Age: 35-55 (peak earning, established career, clarity about what matters)
- Location: Already owns a primary home in a high-cost urban area — SF Bay Area, LA, NYC, Austin, Seattle, Denver
- Profession: Tech executive, finance professional, entrepreneur, senior creative (TV/film/media), doctor
- Remote work capable: Yes — either fully remote or has schedule flexibility
- Family status: Has children (8-18) or recently had first child

**What they've already done**
- Bought the right house in the right neighborhood
- Built a successful career
- Taken the exotic holidays
- Achieved what they were supposed to achieve

**What's missing**
They're describing it as "something real." Not more success. Not more stuff. A place that asks something of them and gives something genuine back.

---

## THE PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE

### The Primary Tension
They built a life that optimizes for everything — career, school district, social proximity, status signals. And it works. And it's not enough. They're not having a crisis. They're having an edit.

The purchase of a Wildernests home is the physical manifestation of that edit. It says: "I'm the kind of person who also does this."

### The Identity They're Buying
Not "environmentalist." Not "minimalist." Not "homesteader."

**The identity they want:** *Someone who built something real for their family in the wilderness.*

That's specific. It implies:
- Capability (they chose something hard)
- Values (they prioritize experience over status)
- Foresight (they built a place before they needed an escape)
- Good parenthood (their kids will grow up knowing this place)

### What Wildernests Needs to Reflect Back
The buyer needs to see themselves in the product. Not the building — themselves. The photo of a couple with coffee watching fog move through a valley. The teenager helping split wood. The fire on a Thursday evening when nobody is performing anything for anyone.

The building is the vehicle. The life is the product.

## BUYER SUBTYPES

### Subtype 1 — The Escape Planner (largest group)
They've been talking about "having a place in the mountains/desert/forest" for years. COVID made it urgent. Remote work made it financially viable. They just haven't found the right thing. The barrier isn't money — it's the friction of custom building (3 years, contractors, land sourcing, design decisions). Wildernests removes that friction.

**What they need to hear:** "Stop planning. We built it."

---

### Subtype 2 — The California Wildfire Buyer (urgent, 12-18 month window)
Malibu, Pacific Palisades, Altadena. They survived or narrowly missed 2025-26 fires. They need a modular structure (faster CA permitting under Newsom's emergency orders), off-grid capable (doesn't depend on PG&E), on land away from fire corridors.

This buyer is making a decision driven by recent trauma and urgency. They're not browsing Instagram. They're calling builders.

**What they need to hear:** "Permitted in weeks, not years. Off-grid ready from day one."

**Market size:** Newsom committed $10M+ to prefab rebuild. CA permitting 3x faster for modular post-Feb 2026. Thousands of displaced HNW homeowners actively seeking alternatives. This is Wildernests' hottest immediate sales channel and Alexander/Gunnar should be in conversations with LA rebuild consultants right now.

---

### Subtype 3 — The Legacy Builder
Age 45-55. Kids are 12-18. Acutely aware that childhood is running out. Wants to create "the place" that defines their family's identity across generations — the way lake houses or mountain cabins do in East Coast family lore. The Wildernests home is the place every family gathering happens for the next 40 years.

**What they need to hear:** "Built for how families actually use it, not how designers imagine it."

---

### Subtype 4 — The Remote Work Optimizer
Post-COVID executive who can work from anywhere and has restructured life accordingly. First home is wherever the partner needs to be. Second home is where they'd actually choose to be. Wildernests is the office with the view they've been building toward.

**What they need to hear:** "Connectivity that works. Architecture that inspires."

## WHAT THEY'RE NOT BUYING

Important for the product team and marketing:

| They're NOT buying | Why it matters |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Minimalism | They want quality. Full-size appliances, real shower, comfortable beds. "Small" is not a feature. |
| Roughing it | Hot water is non-negotiable. Wifi is non-negotiable. They have jobs. |
| Environmental virtue | Green materials are a nice-to-have. "Off-grid" appeals for resilience, not carbon score. |
| Investment return | If they calculate rental income on it, they'll be disappointed. The decision is emotional. |
| Novelty | They're not buying a quirky experience. They're buying somewhere permanent that matters. |

## THE TRIGGER FOR PURCHASE

Wildernests doesn't need to create the desire. The desire already exists. They need to catch the buyer at the moment the desire converts to action.

**Triggers:**
1. **Burnout episode** — bad quarter, near-miss health event, "I'm done with this"
2. **Life stage shift** — new baby, kid turning 16, turning 40/50
3. **Liquidity event** — bonus, equity vest, property sale, inheritance
4. **CA wildfire proximity** — just lost or narrowly escaped (immediate urgency)
5. **Partner alignment** — both finally agree, decision window is brief

**Marketing implication:** Wildernests needs to be *findable* at the trigger moment. Instagram awareness is good, but search intent (someone Googling "modular cabin California permit" or "prefab off-grid home NZ design") is the highest-conversion channel. SEO + targeted Google ads around those trigger searches.

## THE NUMBER THAT MATTERS FOR JESSE

Wildernests is targeting a buyer who represents perhaps 2-3% of US households but accounts for 15-20% of discretionary real estate spending. In the US alone, that's approximately 3 million households in the $200K+ income bracket who own or are actively seeking a second property.

At a $300K average Wildernests unit:
- Capturing 0.01% of that addressable market = 300 units = $90M revenue
- Capturing 0.1% = 3,000 units = $900M

The market is enormous. The constraint is execution, brand trust, and distribution — not demand. The Bear Grylls brand connection compresses the trust-building from years to months. That's the real equity value of the partnership.

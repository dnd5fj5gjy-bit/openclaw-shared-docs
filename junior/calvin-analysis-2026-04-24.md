## 9. Strategic Assessment

### Is Calvin Delivering?

**Yes, broadly - with caveats.** The volume and quality of work is strong. Calvin writes good copy (Jesse: "you guys are getting so good at writing these"), produces YouTube content to a format Jesse is happy with, keeps content flowing consistently, and has proactively expanded his offering (ads, AI content, speaking funnel, 3D work, India account). He is entrepreneurial and brings new ideas regularly (ManyChat, trial reels, speaking engagement strategy, AI UGC ads).

**Gaps:**
- India account quality took time to get right.
- Scheduling discipline was an issue (content coming day-before rather than week-before).
- Internal controls failure on the Wildernests contract was serious and could have damaged Jesse's relationship with a new client.
- Eid staffing constraints caused deliverable delays.

### Is the Deal Good Value?

**Core social retainer ($5,500/month):** Reasonable for a Jakarta-based team handling YouTube editing, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, TikTok, and India across multiple accounts with a senior strategist (Calvin) managing everything. For London market rates this would be significantly more.

**India account ($500/month for 90 pieces/month):** Very good value. Calvin negotiated this down himself and covered 2 months personally.

**Speaking gig (commission only):** Zero cash risk. The Framer website and LinkedIn funnel is being built at no extra charge. Very favourable.

**Modern Savage ($3,000/month + equity):** Pending approval. $3k/month for full marketing operation including ads management is lean for a US supplement brand. The equity structure aligns incentives well. At $500k/month profit at 10k subs (Jesse's projection), 5% = $25k/month - very valuable for Calvin. Main risk: Calvin is being given equity in a business that doesn't exist yet. Performance milestones are subscriber-based, not revenue-based, which is sensible for a subscription model.

**Ted's Health (equity only or equity + cash):** 5-10% equity with milestone triggers is reasonable. The £100k monthly revenue milestone for 10% is ambitious - need to watch whether cash cost structure is agreed to avoid a situation where Calvin does work without adequate short-term compensation and disengages.

### Risks

1. **Dependency:** Virtually all BG social media operations now run through Calvin/Ku Creatives. If the relationship breaks down or Calvin's team has capacity issues, multiple brands are affected simultaneously.

2. **Equity sprawl:** Calvin is now receiving or negotiating equity in three BGV businesses (Modern Savage, Ted's Health, and potentially Wildernests). This is a lot of equity exposure from a single contractor. Vesting conditions and IP protections in the contracts need to be airtight.

3. **Wildernests conflict:** The accidental contract send to Alex was handled but created a moment where Jesse's role as outsourcing intermediary was nearly exposed. Jesse was clear: "You cannot send stuff like that. Your agreement is with me." This is a structural risk if Calvin's team doesn't maintain strict confidentiality around who they are actually working for.

4. **Indonesia timezone/staffing:** Eid delays, food poisoning, travel to Bali - these events slow down deliverables. Calvin has team backup but the single-point-of-leadership through Calvin is a risk.

5. **Modern Savage launch performance:** If Modern Savage doesn't convert, Calvin carries the reputational cost of a failed launch while Jesse's capital is deployed. The equity structure means Calvin has an incentive to perform, but subscriber milestones should have time limits.

6. **Quality floor:** Some AI-generated content and the early India account output show that Calvin can overcommit volume at the expense of quality. Jesse has pushed back each time, but it requires active oversight.

### Bottom Line
Calvin is a high-value partner who has gone from social media editor to multi-project strategic marketing operator in 6 months. The relationship is strong and the personal chemistry is real. The equity deals are sensible in structure but create complexity that needs clean contracts. The main management imperative is: don't let the dependency deepen without airtight contractual protections, and watch the Ted's Health/Modern Savage launch execution closely to determine if the equity deals end up delivering value or creating entitlement without performance.

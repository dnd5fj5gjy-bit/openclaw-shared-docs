DIRTEA Two-Week Plan

2026-04-05 • Prepared by Junior

Objective

Help DIRTEA do more and do better over the next two weeks by reducing dependence on high-CAC Meta spend, tightening the message, and building a better route into the US.

What the email trail says

  • DIRTEA is a real priority in Jesse's world.
  • The brand has already had shareholder, growth, LTV, and CRM discussions.
  • There has been a previous willingness to sell or reshape the business if it helps.
  • The current problem is likely conversion efficiency, message specificity, and US distribution.

Two-week plan

Week 1 — Diagnose and sharpen

  1. Audit the current offer and positioning — what is the hero product, what problem does it solve, what is the one-line promise, and what is the weakest part of the US pitch?
  2. Review Meta CAC by angle — split ads by message angle, not just spend; identify winners and kill the weakest angle fast.
  3. Tighten the US narrative — move from "functional mushrooms" to one outcome-led story; pick the primary hero use case.
  4. Map the trust stack — testimonials, reviews, ingredients, experts, and what is missing for faster trust.

Week 2 — Build new channels and tests

  1. Launch a creator / authority test — 10 niche creators or practitioners focused on credibility.
  2. Test a partner-led US wedge — premium retail, affiliates, wellness operators, or founder-to-founder partnerships.
  3. Create one conversion asset — sharper landing page, quiz, comparison page, or hero guide.
  4. Build a daily content rhythm — founder POV, proof-based post, use-case post, myth-busting post.

My opinion

DIRTEA probably needs less broad wellness marketing and more specific belief-building. If the US is struggling, the answer is probably not more generic spend. It is tighter positioning, better proof, and better channels.

Immediate action list for Jesse

  • Pick the one US use case DIRTEA should own.
  • Cut one wasting Meta angle.
  • Identify 10 credible voices to test.
  • Build one partner wedge.
  • Rewrite the brand around the outcome, not the ingredient.

# Ted's Health Partner Economics Toolkit

**Date:** 2026-03-30  
**Prepared by:** Junior

## Executive takeaway

Ted's Health now needs the asset that turns the white-label thesis into a sale: a simple way for a clinic, pharmacy, or specialist operator to see exactly how the model makes money.

This toolkit gives Jesse three things:
1. a partner-facing economics story
2. pricing and commercial structure options
3. ready-to-use example scenarios that show why a clinic should launch with Ted's instead of building internally

## Core commercial story

The pitch is not "buy software." The pitch is:

> **Add a modern men's-health revenue line with recurring income, fast launch, and lower operational burden.**

Most clinics already understand demand for erectile dysfunction, hair loss, weight, and hormone-related care. What they usually do **not** have is a clean digital intake flow, subscription logic, repeat prescription engine, discreet fulfilment process, or conversion-focused patient journey.

Ted's should sell the gap between clinical demand and operational capability.

## The partner value proposition

A clinic or pharmacy working with Ted's gets:
- a faster route to launching men's-health pathways
- a cleaner digital patient journey
- repeat-order revenue instead of one-off treatment sales
- less internal ops work across triage, fulfilment, support, and refill handling
- a path to expand from one treatment wedge into broader recurring care

## Recommended commercial models

### Model A — Setup fee + monthly platform fee + revenue share
Best for clinics that want low upfront complexity and are comfortable sharing upside.

**Structure:**
- one-time setup / onboarding fee: **£4,000–£10,000**
- monthly platform / support fee: **£1,500–£4,000**
- revenue share on net treatment revenue: **10%–20%**

**Why it works:**
- easy for the clinic to understand
- aligns Ted's to growth
- gives Ted's both upfront cash and ongoing upside

### Model B — Setup fee + fixed monthly retainer
Best for more established operators that want predictable costs.

**Structure:**
- one-time setup fee: **£5,000–£15,000**
- fixed monthly retainer: **£2,500–£7,500**
- optional performance kicker tied to consultation volume or repeat revenue

**Why it works:**
- cleaner procurement process
- easier for groups with budgets and existing finance teams
- positions Ted's more like infrastructure + managed service

### Model C — Low setup + high revenue share
Best for getting pilots live fast.

**Structure:**
- setup fee: **£1,000–£3,000**
- monthly minimum: **£500–£1,500**
- revenue share: **20%–30%**

**Why it works:**
- removes launch friction
- useful for first lighthouse partners
- good when Ted's wants case studies quickly

## Unit economics assumptions

Use these assumptions in early sales conversations unless real clinic numbers are available:

- qualified men's-health leads per month: **100 / 250 / 500**
- consultation completion rate: **55%–75%**
- clinician approval / suitability rate: **55%–80%**
- first-order conversion from qualified lead: **12%–30%**
- average order value: **£85–£140**
- gross margin on first order: **45%–65%**
- retained patients into repeat cadence: **45%–70%**
- monthly gross profit per retained patient: **£25–£55**

These ranges are realistic enough to sell from while leaving room to outperform.

## Objections and answers

### "We could build this ourselves"
**Answer:** Yes, but not cheaply and not quickly. By the time you combine product work, intake logic, clinician workflow design, pharmacy coordination, refill handling, and conversion optimisation, you have built a new business line. Ted's compresses that build time.

### "We're unsure demand is large enough"
**Answer:** Start with one pathway. ED or hair loss alone can prove demand, repeat behaviour, and acquisition economics before broader rollout.

### "We don't want to hand over our brand"
**Answer:** You don't. Ted's should be sold as the backend layer. The clinic keeps the patient-facing brand relationship.

### "We need compliance confidence"
**Answer:** Ted's should package a trust layer into every sale: clinician workflow, pharmacy coordination, regulated-language templates, patient FAQ blocks, and audit-friendly SOPs.

## THE THREE QUESTIONS THAT ACTUALLY DECIDE THIS

Everything else is price. These three are pass/fail.

**1. The one-platform claim (read aloud)**
> "Susan said we would manage US and UK inventory through a single platform because you are on the same WMS. Is that one login and one view across both warehouses, or is it two separate accounts that happen to run the same software?"

- **Good:** he is precise, names the WMS, explains exactly what is shared and what is not.
- **Red flag:** he says "yes, same system" and moves on. If it is two accounts, SCEND's whole advantage over any other UK 3PL disappears and this becomes a straight price comparison.

**2. Food business registration (read aloud)**
> "We are a food supplement. Our stock will be held at your site. Is SCEND itself a registered food business for the warehouse that would hold us, and do we register as the food business operator at your address, or do you carry that?"

- **Good:** immediate, specific answer. He knows what an FBO registration is and names how their other supplement clients handle it.
- **Red flag:** he does not know the term. That means we would be their first food client, and our compliance checklist has an open item nobody owns.
- This one matters beyond the call: Em's UK compliance checklist has FBO registration as an immediate action, and it registers to the local authority **where the stock is held**. His answer unblocks it.

**3. Batch, expiry, recall (read aloud)**
> "Every unit ships with a lot number and an expiry date. Do you pick oldest-expiry-first automatically, and if I needed to trace or pull a single lot, how fast can you tell me exactly which customers received it?"

- **Good:** "FEFO by default, and lot-level traceability, we can report it same day."
- **Red flag:** vague, or "we can look into that." A supplement brand without lot traceability at the 3PL has no working recall. That is not a nice-to-have.

## THE NUMBERS TO GET (ask as a block, get it in writing)

> "Rather than talk price in the abstract, can you send me a per-line rate card this week? I want to see all of it, including the lines people usually leave out:
>
> Pick and pack per order, and per extra item. Storage, per pallet and per month. Receiving, per pallet or per hour. Account setup fee. **Any monthly minimum, or minimum order volume.** Returns handling per unit. Your carrier rates, and whether we can put our own negotiated rates on top. And the cost to leave, if we ever move stock out."

**The three that matter most, in order:**
1. **Monthly minimum.** At 3,500 units this is the line that decides whether SCEND is affordable. Ships A Lot signed with **no minimums** - that is your benchmark and you can say so.
2. **Exit terms.** The classic 3PL trap is cheap to join, expensive to leave, and your stock is the hostage. Ask the leaving cost while you are still the buyer.
3. **Contract length and notice.** Push for month-to-month or a 12-month with 30 to 60 days' notice. Do not sign a multi-year on an unlaunched UK product.

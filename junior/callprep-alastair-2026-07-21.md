**1. Has he ever run this in a room, with real people?**
> "Have you run anything in person - a group, a session, a gathering? What actually happened, how many turned up, did they come back?"

Online following and a live recurring community are completely different skills. This is the single most useful thing you learn today.

**2. What does he want out of it?**
> "If this went well, what does it look like for you in a year? Is this a side project alongside Nuffield, or is it the thing you actually want to be doing?"

- **Good:** he wants to run it, is willing to leave or reduce Nuffield, has a view on how it sustains itself.
- **Red flag:** he wants to be involved but keeps the day job and expects us to build it around him. That is a contributor, not a founder, and this needs a founder.

**3. Where does the faith element actually sit?**
> "Practically, on a Tuesday night, what happens? Who leads the training and who leads the faith part, and what does someone who is curious but not religious experience?"

The answer tells you whether this is a training community with faith in it, or a church with weights. Both are legitimate. Only one is on-brand for Bear, and it is the first. Listen for whether he can hold the door open for the not-yet-convinced.

> "I am less interested in hiring someone to run a class than in finding someone who wants to own this and build it. Does that appeal to you, or does it sound like more than you want to take on?"

This is the real question of the call. His reaction to the word "own" tells you everything.

# Modern Savage Conversion Blueprint

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Prepared by:** Junior

> This is the execution layer that sits on top of the existing Modern Savage messaging work. The goal is simple: turn positioning into a landing page and creative system that can actually convert paid traffic.

---

## Executive takeaway

Modern Savage already has a strong brand lane: **primal nutrition for modern male performance**. The missing piece is a tighter conversion system.

Right now the opportunity is not more brand theory. It is to build a page and ad structure that does three things fast:
1. makes the category obvious
2. makes the product feel premium and credible
3. gives men a clean reason to buy now rather than “come back later”

The winning version of Modern Savage should feel:
- premium, not crunchy
- masculine, not parody-masculine
- performance-led, not medical
- convenient, not educationally exhausting

---

## What to build first

### Recommended page type
Build a **single-product paid-traffic landing page** before worrying about a full multi-page brand site.

The first version should be optimized for cold traffic from Meta, creator seeding, and direct-response content.

### Primary page objective
Get first purchase.

### Secondary page objective
Convert as many buyers as possible into subscription.

### Core conversion promise
**Organ nutrition for men who want a stronger baseline — without eating organs or building a complicated stack.**

---

## Recommended offer architecture

If Jesse wants the fastest path to conversion, the page should not present a weak generic supplement offer. It needs a clearer commercial structure.

### Best first offer stack

#### Option A — safest first launch
- **1 bottle:** standard price
- **Subscribe & save:** 10–15% off
- **2 bottle bundle:** modest savings
- **3 bottle bundle:** strongest savings + free shipping

Why this works:
- simple
- familiar
- increases AOV without gimmicks
- gives subscription a clean default path

#### Option B — stronger DTC test
- **Starter bottle**
- **Starter + travel bottle bundle**
- **Subscribe & save with founder pricing**

Why this works:
- feels premium
- gives a stronger reason to choose the middle/high tier
- helps position the product as ritual, not one-off purchase

### My recommendation
Use **Option A first**, but make **Subscribe & Save the default selected option** on page.

## Landing page wireframe

## 1. Hero section

### Goal
Answer 4 questions immediately:
- what is it?
- who is it for?
- why should I care?
- what do I do next?

### Recommended hero copy

**Headline:**  
**Primal nutrition. Modern edge.**

**Subhead:**  
A premium daily organ blend for men who want cleaner energy, sharper focus, and a stronger foundation for work, training, and life.

**Primary CTA:** Shop Now  
**Secondary CTA:** See What’s Inside

### Hero support bullets
- Organ nutrition without the prep or taste barrier
- Built for energy, focus, resilience, and daily consistency
- Premium sourcing, simple daily ritual

### Visual direction
- bottle front and center
- dark neutral or stone-toned background
- one strong masculine lifestyle image only if it improves clarity
- avoid clutter, ingredient explosions, fake lab visuals, or “biohack” nonsense

---

## 2. Trust strip

### Goal
Reduce immediate skepticism.

### Recommended trust row
- Grass-fed / pasture-raised sourcing
- Third-party tested
- No fillers / straightforward formula
- Subscription available
- Money-back guarantee if offered

If those claims are not fully substantiated yet, swap for only what can be defended.

---

## 3. The problem section

### Goal
Make the buyer feel understood without sounding melodramatic.

### Suggested section headline
**Most men don’t have an effort problem. They have a foundation problem.**

### Body copy
Modern life runs on convenience, stress, bad sleep, and nutrient-light food. A lot of men are training hard, working hard, and still feeling flat. Most supplements either feel synthetic, overhyped, or forgettable.

Modern Savage is designed to give men a stronger nutritional baseline with a ritual they can actually keep.

### Why this section matters
It frames the product as a practical answer to a modern problem, not a fringe organ-meat obsession.

---

## 4. The solution section

### Goal
Explain the product simply.

### Headline
**A simpler way to get the upside of organ nutrition.**

### Structure
Use 3 cards:

#### Card 1 — Energy
Nutrient-dense organ support built to help men feel less flat and more switched on.

#### Card 2 — Focus
A stronger nutritional base for sharper days, cleaner routines, and more consistent output.

#### Card 3 — Resilience
Built for men balancing training, recovery, pressure, and responsibility.

Important: keep this at a benefit level unless product claims are compliance-cleared.

---

## 5. Why organ nutrition / why now

### Goal
Handle the category objection.

A lot of buyers will think one of three things:
- “This sounds weird.”
- “I’ve heard of liver supplements but never took them seriously.”
- “Maybe I should just eat better.”

### Headline
**Why men are paying attention to organ nutrition again**

### Copy angle
Organs have always been among the most nutrient-dense foods available. The problem was never the logic. It was the friction.

Modern Savage makes that nutritional logic practical: no shopping for organs, no prep, no taste barrier, no complexity.

### Support points
- nutrient density
- convenience
- consistency
- premium modern format

---

## 6. Ingredient / formula block

### Goal
Create credibility without overwhelming the page.

### Recommendation
Do not dump an essay here. Use a simple structured layout:
- ingredient name
- plain-English role
- why it matters

### Example structure
- **Liver** — foundational organ nutrition traditionally valued for energy and vitality
- **Heart** — supports a strong whole-body nutritional profile
- **Kidney** — helps round out the organ blend with additional nutrient diversity
- **[other organ]** — short functional explanation

Final wording should be tightened once the exact formula is confirmed.

---

## 7. Identity / lifestyle section

### Goal
Sell belonging, not just ingredients.

### Headline
**Built for men who carry weight well.**

### Body copy
This is for men who care how they show up.

In work. In training. In family life. In the standards they keep for themselves.

Modern Savage is not about hype. It is about building a better baseline so you can perform with more consistency.

### Why this matters
The brand will outperform if it becomes identity-first and ingredient-second.

---

## 8. Social proof section

### Goal
Add evidence without overclaiming.

### Recommended social proof mix
- 3 short customer quotes
- 1 creator-style quote
- average star rating if genuine
- “trusted by X customers” only if true

### Best quote angles
- easier than expected to stick with
- feels premium
- became part of my routine
- convenient way to cover a nutritional gap

Avoid fake macho testimonials or sexual-performance style language.

---

## 9. Offer block

### Goal
Close the sale.

### Recommended structure
Show 3 buy options side by side.

#### 1 Bottle
Best for first-time buyers.

#### 2 Bottles
Best value for consistency.

#### Subscribe & Save
Best for men making it a daily ritual.

### Strong page mechanics
- make subscription pre-selected
- show per-bottle savings clearly
- keep shipping info obvious
- include guarantee near CTA
- repeat “simple daily ritual” language

---

## 10. FAQ section

### Goal
Remove last-minute friction.

### Must-answer questions
1. What exactly is Modern Savage?
2. Why organ nutrition?
3. Who is it for?
4. When should I take it?
5. Is this a replacement for a healthy diet?
6. How long should I take it consistently?
7. Is subscription easy to cancel or change?

### Body
If you want a stronger nutritional foundation without overcomplicating your life, Modern Savage is a clean place to start.

Start Your Daily Edge

## Ad-to-page messaging match

This is where a lot of supplement brands leak conversion.

The first screen of the landing page should map directly to the angle that brought the user in.

### Recommended first 4 creative angles

#### Angle 1 — Anti-softness
**Hook:** Modern life softens men. Build your edge back.

Landing page match:
- stronger baseline
- less flat energy
- daily ritual for disciplined men

#### Angle 2 — Premium masculine identity
**Hook:** Primal nutrition. Modern edge.

Landing page match:
- premium packaging
- premium sourcing
- cleaner, more refined tone

#### Angle 3 — Convenience
**Hook:** Organ nutrition without the prep or taste barrier.

Landing page match:
- no prep
- no cooking organs
- easier to stay consistent

#### Angle 4 — Work + training performance
**Hook:** Built for men who want to show up sharper at work and in training.

Landing page match:
- work, training, recovery, responsibility
- better baseline, not hype spike

## First-round creative test plan

Run a small first-round matrix instead of trying to make one perfect ad.

### Test set

#### Test 1 — Product-led static
- bottle shot
- headline: Primal nutrition. Modern edge.
- CTA: Shop Now

#### Test 2 — Founder/operator identity video
- hook: Built for men carrying real weight
- visual: disciplined morning / work / training
- tone: calm, strong, premium

#### Test 3 — Convenience UGC
- hook: easiest way I’ve found to make organ nutrition practical
- visual: daily routine
- strongest for skeptical cold traffic

#### Test 4 — Contrast ad
- hook: not bro-science. not soft wellness.
- visual: clean premium product visuals
- purpose: sharpen differentiation

### What to look for
- CTR on identity-based angles vs convenience angles
- hold rate on first 3 seconds for videos
- CVR difference between subscription default vs one-time default
- AOV impact of 2-bottle and 3-bottle options

---

## What the page should avoid

1. **Too much education above the fold**  
Don’t make men read a white paper before they can buy.

2. **Over-medical language**  
It kills brand charisma and creates unnecessary compliance drag.

3. **Fake alpha language**  
Anything that sounds like parody masculinity weakens trust instantly.

4. **Busy design**  
The product category is already unusual. The page should feel controlled and premium.

5. **Weak offer framing**  
If the offer looks generic, the brand starts to look generic too.

---

## Best practical next steps

### Immediate
1. Build the single-product landing page first
2. Use subscription as the default selected option
3. Produce 4 creative concepts mapped to the 4 angles above
4. Write 3 short testimonial placeholders ready to replace with real reviews
5. Keep the page concise enough to work on mobile

### Next after that
1. Add creator testimonials and UGC
2. Build a quiz or “is this for you?” selector if conversion data suggests buyers need more hand-holding
3. Expand into a richer brand site once the paid traffic page is proven

If the team ships this properly, the brand can sit in a genuinely attractive lane:

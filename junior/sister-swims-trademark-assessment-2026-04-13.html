3. Professional Opinion on Likelihood of Success

Likelihood of Registration (Absent Opposition)

Based on the clearance searches: high likelihood of acceptance. No registered conflicting marks were identified. The mark "SISTER SWIMS" is inherently distinctive in Class 25 (it is not descriptive of swimwear). The UK IPO examiner will likely accept it without relative grounds objection.

Timeline from filing to registration (assuming no objections and no opposition): approximately 4 months — typically 3-4 months for examination plus a 2-month opposition window. Total: 4-6 months from filing date.

Likelihood of Opposition

The key risk is Sister Swim NY filing an opposition during the 2-month opposition window after the UK IPO publishes your application. My assessment:

  • Probability of opposition: 15-25%. They would need to (a) monitor the UK register, (b) have UK goodwill to rely on (uncertain), and (c) fund the opposition process (costly without a registered mark). Most small US brands with no UK registrations do not actively monitor foreign registers.
  • If opposition is filed: UK IPO opposition proceedings typically cost £2,000-5,000 in professional fees (attorney + filing costs). If the matter proceeds to a full hearing, costs can reach £8,000-15,000. However, the majority of trade mark oppositions settle at the pre-hearing stage via co-existence agreements.
  • Proactive outreach recommended: Before filing, consider a brief informal enquiry to Sister Swim NY to understand their UK position. A co-existence agreement in advance costs far less than opposition proceedings. If they have no UK ambitions, they may be entirely comfortable with co-existence.

4. Filing Recommendations

Goods Specification — Class 25

The proposed specification is well-drafted. My suggested refinement:

Recommended Specification — Class 25

Swimwear; bikinis; swimming costumes; one-piece swimsuits; swim shorts; beach cover-ups; sarongs; kaftans; resort wear; clothing for beach and water sports.

Addition: "clothing for beach and water sports" provides a broader net for future product lines without over-claiming and inviting objection from unrelated clothing brands. The specification is tight enough to avoid objection and broad enough to cover your intended product range.

Class 35 — Retail Services

Recommendation: Do not file Class 35 at this stage. Class 35 adds £50 in official fees and increases the profile of the application. For a brand that has not yet launched, the risk of over-claiming outweighs the benefit. Class 35 can be added in a separate application once trading commences. UK registered marks are valid for 10 years and can be extended — there is no urgency to file Class 35 now.

EUTM Filing

Recommendation: File EUTM within 6 months of UK filing. Under the Paris Convention, you have 6 months from your UK filing date to claim priority on an EUTM application. This means your EUTM application will be treated as if filed on the same date as your UK application — protecting you from any marks filed in the EU in the intervening period. EUTM covers all 27 EU member states in one application at a cost of €850 (one class). Given the brand may sell internationally online, this is good value.

5. Applicant Structure

Filing as a company entity is preferable to filing as an individual if a company structure exists or is planned. Reasons:

  • The trade mark becomes a company asset — easier to sell, licence, or assign
  • Separation of personal and business liability
  • If a company is incorporated after filing as an individual, a transfer/assignment will be required (additional cost and paperwork)
  • If no company exists yet, filing as an individual is perfectly valid and can be assigned to a company later at low cost

Action required from you: Confirm (a) whether a company entity for Sister Swims has been or will be incorporated, and (b) the registered address and contact details for the applicant.

6. Word Mark vs Stylised Mark

Filing as a standard word mark is correct and gives the broadest protection — it covers the words in any font, colour, or layout. A stylised/logo mark would only protect the specific design and provides narrower coverage. Do not substitute a logo mark for a word mark; if you have a distinctive logo, file both separately once the brand identity is finalised.

10. Answers to Your Specific Questions

Q: Would filing a composite mark (word + logo) improve chances versus a pure word mark?

No. A composite mark is easier to register (more distinctive elements) but provides narrower protection. File the word mark first. Add a logo mark separately once brand identity is finalised. Do not substitute one for the other.

Q: Should we reach out to Sister Swim NY proactively before filing?

Recommended. A brief, informal enquiry is low-cost insurance. Frame it as a courtesy notice rather than seeking permission — you do not need their consent to file. The objective is to (a) understand their UK position and (b) open a co-existence dialogue if needed. If they respond aggressively, you can still file — but you will be better prepared.

Q: Should we secure sisterswims.com / sisterswims.co.uk before filing?

Yes, and before any public announcement. Do this first.

Q: Ongoing maintenance requirements?

  • UK renewal: every 10 years, £200 per class
  • Use requirement: the mark must be put to genuine use within 5 years of registration, or it becomes vulnerable to cancellation for non-use
  • No proof of use filing required by the UK IPO (unlike the US where use declarations are mandatory)
  • Watch service: recommend setting up a trade mark watch service (approx. £100-300/year) to monitor for similar filings in Class 25 — allows you to oppose before a conflicting mark registers

Q: Intent to use basis?

The UK (unlike the US) does not require a statement of use or intent to use at the time of filing. You may file before trading commences — there is no requirement to have made any sales under the mark. The 5-year use requirement only kicks in post-registration.

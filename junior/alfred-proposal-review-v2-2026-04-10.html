Two Critical Issues Still in the Proposal

Critical #1 — Must Fix

There is a person named "Alice" in the proposal who does not exist

In the "Why Bear Witness" integration section, this sentence appears: "Bear Witness works alongside Alice and the marketing team, not instead of them."

Alice is not Alfred's marketing director. This looks like a placeholder name that was never updated — possibly carried over from a different proposal or generated by Claude and not caught. If Tom reads this and asks "who is Alice?" the whole proposal loses credibility in a single sentence. This must be fixed before the proposal goes anywhere.

Prompt for Claude:
In the "Why Bear Witness" section, under the Integration subsection, there is this sentence: "Bear Witness works alongside Alice and the marketing team, not instead of them." "Alice" is a placeholder name that should not be in this proposal. Replace the sentence with: "Bear Witness works alongside Alfred's marketing team, not instead of them." Remove the name entirely. Keep the rest of the paragraph exactly as is.
Critical #2 — Must Fix

"Costco for your home" is a brand comparison — exactly what we agreed to avoid

In the "Real Numbers" content pillar, this line appears: "Costco for your home: the framework people already understand and trust. Pay a membership fee, get wholesale prices."

This is a brand comparison — and specifically the one that was a concern from the start. Using Costco as the framework for explaining Alfred is exactly the shorthand Jesse said felt lazy and made founders feel unspecial. Alfred's membership model is its own thing. It doesn't need a retail analogy to explain it. The explanation immediately before it already makes the model clear — this sentence adds nothing and undermines Alfred's uniqueness.

Prompt for Claude:
In the "Real Numbers" content pillar, remove this sentence entirely: "Costco for your home: the framework people already understand and trust. Pay a membership fee, get wholesale prices." The surrounding copy already explains the Alfred membership model clearly and doesn't need this analogy. Remove it and join the preceding and following content naturally. Do not replace it with any other brand comparison.

Either remove it from Phase 5 or reframe it in a way that doesn't conflict: something like "word-of-mouth amplification through genuine member advocacy" makes the same point without the coded incentive connotation.

Consider whether the framing can be bigger.

Prompt for Claude:
In the hero section, update the subtitle text only (keep the headline "The brand that told the truth about household bills" exactly as is). "Household brand" rather than "UK utilities" — larger framing, higher ceiling, positions Alfred as a consumer movement rather than a category winner.

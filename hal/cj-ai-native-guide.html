Module 1

The One Mindset Shift That Changes Everything

Most people ask: "Can I use AI for this?" That's the wrong question. AI-native thinkers ask: "Why would I do this without AI?"

Old thinking
"Let me draft this email, then maybe HAL can clean it up."
AI-native
"HAL, draft me an email to [person] about [topic]. I'll adjust the tone if needed."
Old thinking
"I need to research this company before the meeting."
AI-native
"HAL, give me a 5-point brief on [company] — what they do, who runs it, any recent news, and what they'd want from us."
Old thinking
"I'll put together a rough deal model in Excel."
AI-native
"HAL, build me a 3-year deal model for this: [deal terms]. Revenue, costs, IRR. I'll review and adjust."

The shift is simple: default to AI first, edit second. Your job becomes direction and judgement. HAL handles the heavy lifting.

Module 2

3 Rules of AI-Native Working

⚡

Rule 1: Never start from a blank page

Before you write anything — email, doc, deck, summary — ask HAL for a first draft. Even a rough one. Editing is 5x faster than creating. Your first message should always be "draft me..."

🎯

Rule 2: Give context, not just commands

The more you tell HAL about the situation, the better the output. Don't just say "write an email to Paul." Say "write an email to Paul Gardiner (BGV + Terra Nova) following up on the Deep Real deal — we need his signature by Friday, keep it friendly but firm." Context is everything.

🔄

Rule 3: Iterate, don't restart

If HAL's first draft isn't right, don't start over. Say "make it shorter," "make it more direct," "add a line about X," "change the tone — too formal." One conversation. Multiple drafts. Much faster.

Module 4

Prompts That Actually Work

The difference between a useful AI response and a generic one is how you ask. These patterns work every time.

The Situation + Ask pattern

Give the situation, then the specific ask. Don't make HAL guess what you need.

Situation: [1-2 sentences of context] What I need: [specific deliverable] Constraints: [length, tone, format, deadline]

The "act as" pattern

Put HAL in a role to get sharper output.

Act as a commercial lawyer and review this term sheet. Flag anything I should push back on. Act as a financial analyst. Here are the deal terms — model the IRR. Act as a Bear Grylls brand expert. Does this partnership fit the BGV brand? Why or why not?

The iteration pattern

Never start over. Always iterate on the last draft.

That's good. Now: — Make it 30% shorter — Open with the ask, not the context — Make the last paragraph a clear call to action — Change "partnership" to "collaboration" throughout

The "what am I missing" pattern

Use HAL as a thinking partner, not just a drafter.

I'm about to sign [deal/send this email/agree to these terms]. What am I missing? What could go wrong? What should I have asked but didn't?

One small habit per day. By day 7, AI-first will feel automatic.

1

Email Audit

Tell HAL: "Check my inbox and list anything that needs a reply today." Don't check email yourself first. See what HAL surfaces.

2

Draft Before You Type

The next email you need to send — ask HAL to draft it. Don't write a single word yourself first. Edit HAL's version.

3

Meeting Pre-Brief

Before your next external call or meeting, ask HAL for a brief. Compare what HAL gives you to what you would have prepped yourself.

4

Research Sprint

Pick a company or person you've been meaning to research. Give HAL 5 minutes. See what it digs up that you wouldn't have found yourself.

5

Deal Sense Check

Take any deal or commercial decision on your plate. Ask HAL: "Here's the deal — what are the risks, what's the upside, what would you push back on?" Use it as a thinking partner.

6

Excel Model

Ask HAL to build a simple financial model for anything: a revenue projection, a cost breakdown, a deal structure. See the output. Adjust the inputs and ask it to update.

7

Full AI Day

For every work task that comes up today, ask yourself: "Could HAL do this faster or better than me?" If yes — delegate it. Track how much time you saved.

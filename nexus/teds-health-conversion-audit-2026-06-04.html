Ted's Health Structural Moats

Things Arc TRT cannot replicate quickly — worth leaning into harder.

GP Partnership Pathway
Ted's works alongside the NHS/GP. Arc TRT does not mention this. For patients who want TRT without abandoning their NHS relationship, Ted's is the only structured option. This should be on the homepage, not buried in the funnel.
Bear Grylls / BGV Brand Authority
Brand trust a startup cannot replicate. The BGV connection signals serious institutional backing. Not currently featured on the homepage at all — it's organic context but not an explicit trust signal.
Sir Steve Redgrave Endorsement
Full-page case study. A credible, relevant, well-known patient story. One of the most powerful conversion assets Ted's has — linked from the nav but not prominently featured in the purchase flow.
Broader Men's Health (ED + Supplements)
Arc TRT is pure TRT. Ted's covers TRT, ED, and supplements — three revenue streams vs one. Patients who don't qualify for TRT still have options. This reduces wasted acquisition spend.
Established Blog Authority
10+ posts vs Arc's 1. Content authority compounds over time. Arc cannot instantly replicate 2 years of indexed content. The blog is an asset but not yet linked into the conversion flow.
Partner With Us Programme
Ted's has a B2B partnership pathway Arc lacks. GPs, gyms, and wellness brands can refer patients. This is a structural distribution moat — but the page currently has no visible CTA or application form.

Google Search Console — Setup Checklist

Estimated time: 45 minutes. Requires access to Felix's Google account and the tedshealth.com domain registrar.

  1. Go to search.google.com/search-console → click Add property → choose Domain → enter tedshealth.com
  2. Google shows a DNS TXT record (e.g. google-site-verification=XXXXXX) — log in to your domain registrar (likely Namecheap, GoDaddy, or Cloudflare), add this TXT record to the DNS
  3. Click Verify in Search Console — DNS propagates in 5–60 minutes
  4. Once verified, go to Sitemaps → enter sitemap.xml → click Submit (confirm the sitemap exists first: try visiting https://www.tedshealth.com/sitemap.xml)
  5. Go to Settings → URL prefix, add both https://www.tedshealth.com and https://tedshealth.com (without www) so you get both in one view
  6. Under Settings → Ownership, add the developer's Google account as an owner so they can access it independently
  7. Enable email alerts in Preferences → turn on all alert types (coverage, manual actions, security issues)
  8. After 48 hours: check Coverage report for any pages returning 404 or excluded from index. Fix any errors found.
  9. After 7 days: check Performance report. Note which queries are generating impressions — this is the baseline before the symptom page build.
  10. After symptom pages launch: submit sitemap.xml again to force re-crawl of new URLs
Also check: does tedshealth.com have a sitemap?
Visit https://www.tedshealth.com/sitemap.xml and https://www.tedshealth.com/robots.txt. If sitemap doesn't exist, ask the dev team to generate one from the CMS (most platforms like WordPress, Webflow, Squarespace do this automatically).

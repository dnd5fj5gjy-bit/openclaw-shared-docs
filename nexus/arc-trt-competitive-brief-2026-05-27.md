# Arc TRT — Competitive Intelligence Brief
**Prepared by Nexus | 27 May 2026 | For: Felix Bond / Ted's Health team**

---

## Threat Level: Medium — but the window is closing fast

Arc TRT (arctrt.co.uk) launched their patient acquisition site on April 15, 2026. They are running the same "doctor-led, personalised, specialist-credentialed" play Ted's Health intends to lead with in the UK TRT market.

The good news: they're still very small, their SEO is empty, and their social proof is thin. **The window to outpace them on content and credibility is open, but not for long.**

## Their Positioning

**Tagline:** "Science led. Experience driven. You first."
**Core message:** "We treat the patient, not just the number on a piece of paper."

They target men who have been let down by:
- NHS reference ranges that leave symptomatic men undertreated
- Aggressive "crash oestrogen" protocols used by budget clinics
- Factory-line digital-first services (Manual, Numan)

**Micro-dosing** is their clinical differentiator — they explicitly criticise aggressive protocols. This positions them as the medically conservative, defensible option.

## Their Weaknesses (Right Now)

This is the tactical picture. These gaps are exploitable today.

**1. SEO blog: completely empty**
Site structure exists for a content hub ("Honest, doctor-led writing on testosterone therapy and men's hormonal health") with planned articles (TRT basics, HCG explainer, treatment options). As of launch: zero content published. Zero. Their SEO clock started April 15 and they haven't done anything with it yet.

**2. Social proof is thin**
- 16 Trustpilot reviews (vs. Manual's 2,458)
- 51 Doctify reviews (likely career-aggregate from previous clinics, not Arc TRT-specific)
- The "3,000+ patients" claim is almost certainly Dr. Airey's career total across CJA Balance, Optimale, Manual, and Urban TRT — not Arc TRT patients
- No dedicated Arc TRT social media presence

**3. Brand is new**
Run My Clinics Ltd is recently incorporated. The website is 6 weeks old. They have no earned media — the April 15 press release ran on newswire only (Manila Times, Yahoo Finance wire pickups; no UK health or men's health coverage).

**4. Southampton-anchored for in-person**
Online-first like everyone else, but the Southampton address matters for trust. Patients outside the South of England who want to see a doctor in person have no option.

## What They're Doing Right (Don't Dismiss This)

**Dr. Airey is genuinely well-credentialed.** The Endocrinology diploma alone puts him ahead of most UK TRT clinic doctors. He has personally treated thousands of men and has been Medical Director at two of the biggest names in the sector. His clinical reputation is real, not manufactured.

**The micro-dosing positioning is smart.** It carves out a medically defensible niche — the men who are nervous about TRT's reputation for being "bro-science" are exactly the ones who will respond to conservative, personalised protocols.

**The Ultimate tier at £600/month is genuinely interesting.** There's no direct competitor at that price point in the accessible online market. They're testing whether a premium concierge model works. If it does, it's a high-margin niche.

## The Real Competitive Threat

Arc TRT's biggest weapon isn't what they have now — it's what Dr. Airey knows. He's been inside Optimale and Manual. He understands exactly how the mid-market works, where the margin is, and what patients complain about. He is launching Arc TRT with that institutional knowledge intact.

If and when he starts publishing content, he will write the exact articles that rank. He knows the patient objections cold.

**The clock is ticking, not on Arc TRT's threat today, but on the window Ted's Health has to establish content authority before he does.**

## Strategic Recommendations for Ted's Health

### 1. Publish first. GP-authored content is the differentiator.

Arc TRT has planned content but hasn't shipped it. Ted's Health should start publishing doctor-authored articles on the exact terms Arc TRT intends to target:
- "What is TRT UK" 
- "How to get testosterone on the NHS vs private"
- "TRT risks — honest review"
- "TRT and fertility — what to know"
- "HCG and TRT explained"

**The GP angle is differentiating here.** Arc TRT has a hospital-trained endocrinology specialist. Ted's Health has GPs — which means continuity of care, whole-patient medicine, and the relationship with NHS pathways. That is a different and legitimate trust story that Airey cannot tell.

### 2. GP continuity is the narrative, not credentials.

Arc TRT = specialist expertise. Ted's Health = your GP does your TRT, alongside everything else.

These are different value propositions. Don't try to out-specialize Airey. Win on the "I know you, I manage your health broadly, I'm your ongoing GP" angle. For most men this matters more than an endocrinology diploma.

### 3. Start collecting reviews now, before Arc TRT does.

They have 16 Trustpilot reviews. This race is winnable. Ted's Health should have a systematic review-collection flow from day one of patient intake. First-mover review volume compounds — each new 5-star review makes the clinic appear more established. This is a low-cost, high-trust-signal activity.

### 4. The "honest numbers" play.

Arc TRT claims 3,000+ patients but it's almost certainly career aggregate, not clinic-specific. Ted's Health should be precise and honest about its own numbers — even if smaller, precision reads as trustworthy. "We've treated 47 patients, here are their stories" beats "3,000+ satisfied patients" with a skeptical audience.

### 5. NHS-to-private pathway content.

Many men try the NHS first and get dismissed (testosterone levels in range but still symptomatic). This is the single most common patient journey in UK TRT. Content that explains: "The NHS said I'm fine but I still feel terrible — what now?" is very high-intent. Airey knows this. Ted's Health should own it first.

## Time Estimate

Arc TRT's SEO/content gap is maybe 60–90 days before Airey or a content agency starts publishing. If Ted's Health publishes 8–10 high-quality GP-authored articles before then and builds initial review volume, it will be very difficult for Arc TRT to dislodge that position organically.

**The 30-day sprint that matters:** Named clinician page + 5 published articles targeting informational keywords + Trustpilot/Doctify profile live and collecting reviews.

That's it. That's the whole play.

---

## Summary

Arc TRT is a credible new entrant led by the most experienced doctor in the UK TRT space. Their clinical model is real, their pricing is competitive, and their long-term content strategy is smart. But right now they are 6 weeks old, have no content, thin reviews, and a brand-new company.

Ted's Health has a genuine window — roughly 60–90 days — to establish content authority and social proof before Airey's institutional knowledge gets converted into published material. The differentiating angle is not out-specialising him; it's the GP model, which is genuinely different and defensible.

Strategic Opportunities & Threats

🟢 OPPORTUNITY — Manual's April 2026 Price Rise

Manual raised prices in April 2026 (Cypionate + HCG: £159 → £169). Ted's Health at £115/mo is now £54/month cheaper than Manual's most popular plan. This is an immediate acquisition window — target Manual customers via Google/Meta with "switching" messaging and a simple price comparison.

🟢 OPPORTUNITY — Bear Grylls Activation

No competitor has a celebrity of Bear's calibre associated with their clinic. This is Ted's unfair advantage. A single Bear endorsement post or testimonial (not paid sponsorship — authentic association) could generate significant awareness. Even a quote about men's health on Ted's site would materially differentiate.

🟢 OPPORTUNITY — Review Volume Gap

Numan has 33,000 Trustpilot reviews. Manual has 11,500. Ted's Health has a 4.2/5 rating but low volume. A structured review collection programme (post-treatment email, SMS prompt, incentivised referrals) could close this gap within 6 months and dramatically improve TRTCompare visibility.

🟢 OPPORTUNITY — Affiliate / Influencer Programme

No major TRT clinic runs a strong affiliate programme targeting health influencers, podcasters, or fitness creators. Ted's Health already has Olly managing influencer/affiliate work. An exclusive affiliate scheme with 5-10 well-selected health creators could generate 20-30 new patients/month at low CAC.

🔴 RISK — Optimale on Price

Optimale at £59.99/mo is almost half Ted's price. Price-sensitive patients will compare. Ted's Health needs to make its premium positioning clear — CQC registration, Bear Grylls association, clinical quality — so that the £55/month premium feels justified. Never compete on price alone.

🔴 RISK — Numan's Scale Moat

Numan's 33,000 reviews create a compounding SEO and social proof advantage. At 97 patients, Ted's review count is minimal. This is a credibility gap that takes time to close. In the short term, lean into the "personal" angle — Numan is a big digital business; Ted's is a specialist clinic where you get a real doctor.

🟡 ACTION — Immediate: Boost TRTCompare Profile

TRTCompare is where patients actively comparison shop. Ted's Health currently shows 4.2/5 with limited reviews. Request all existing patients leave a review. Ensure the clinic profile is 100% complete with pricing, treatments, and differentiators clearly listed. This is free marketing to warm, high-intent buyers.

🟡 ACTION — Medium Term: Annual Subscription Tier

Leger Clinic builds strong retention via annual pricing. Ted's Health should offer an annual plan at a discount (e.g., 10 months for 12) to lock in patients, improve cash flow, and reduce churn risk. A retained patient is worth 10x an acquired one at breakeven stage.

🟡 ACTION — Breakeven Path: 128 More Patients

Ted's needs 225 patients to break even; currently at 97. At average £115/month that's 128 more patients = ~£14,700 additional monthly revenue needed. At a conservative 10 new patients/month (achievable with affiliate programme + Manual switchers), breakeven is ~13 months. The Bear Grylls activation could compress this to 6-8 months.

Market Context

The UK TRT market is valued at $2.13 billion globally in 2026, growing at 3.88% CAGR toward $2.57B by 2031 (Mordor Intelligence). The UK private TRT market has expanded significantly since 2020, driven by increased awareness, NHS wait times (often 6-12 months for TRT referrals), and normalisation via men's health media. At least 19 private UK clinics now operate in this space. The NHS threshold for TRT (<8 nmol/L) is stricter than private clinics — creating a large addressable market of men who are symptomatic but don't qualify for NHS treatment. TRTCompare lists clinics for active comparison shoppers — this platform is a significant patient acquisition channel and Ted's Health's profile there is an immediate priority.

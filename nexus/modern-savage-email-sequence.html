Sequence Strategy

The #1 churn driver in this category is "I took it for 30 days and didn't notice a difference." This sequence exists to prevent that by: (1) setting correct expectations upfront, (2) pre-empting the most common anxieties before they become objections, and (3) building emotional attachment to the brand before the customer is prompted to renew.

  • Email 1 (Day 0): Confirm the decision. Establish "this is different" without overselling.
  • Email 2 (Day 2): Onboarding. Pre-empt stomach concerns. Make the first dose easy.
  • Email 3 (Day 7): Bear's personal why. Reinforce 30-day commitment. Give them signals to look for.
  • Email 4 (Day 14): Silence the "I don't feel anything" doubt with the whole-food nutrition story.
  • Email 5 (Day 28): Celebrate 30 days. Subscription CTA. Referral ask.

Email 04

Why You Don't "Feel" It (The Science)

Goal: neutralise "I don't feel anything" doubt at the highest-risk moment for cancellation
Send: Day 14

Subject Lines

RecommendedYou won't feel this working. That's the point.
Alt ATwo weeks in: the honest truth about what's happening
Alt B"I don't feel any different" — let me explain
Preview text: You don't feel vegetables working either. But you'd notice if you stopped eating them.

Email Body

Hi [First Name],

Two weeks in. If you're expecting to feel something dramatic by now — I'll be straight with you: that's not how this works.

Modern Savage isn't a stimulant. It doesn't hit your nervous system the way caffeine does. It's nutrition — the same way eating liver twice a week is nutrition, or eating 9 different berries every morning is nutrition.

You don't feel vegetables working. But you'd notice pretty quickly if you stopped eating them. Modern Savage works the same way.
Why whole-food nutrients are different

Synthetic supplements extract individual vitamins in a lab. Your body gets one piece of the puzzle. Whole-food sourced nutrients come with the co-factors — the supporting compounds that exist naturally in food — which is why they absorb differently and work over time rather than producing an immediate signal.

The grass-fed liver in your daily scoop contains B12, iron, folate, CoQ10, and retinol in their natural forms, alongside the co-factors that help your body use them. You can't replicate that with a synthetic B-complex.

What's actually happening at two weeks

Your body is building reserves. Iron stores take weeks to shift. B12 takes time to accumulate. The gut changes are often the first thing people notice — and that's typically already underway by now, even if subtly.

Two weeks to go. Keep going.

Bear
Modern Savage

Copy notes

  • This email directly addresses the #1 churn reason across the whole supplement category ("didn't notice a difference"). It's sent at day 14 — the highest-risk window before day 28 renewal.
  • The vegetable analogy is simple, accurate, and memorable — customers will recall it when they're tempted to quit.
  • Specific nutrient details (B12, iron, CoQ10, retinol, co-factors) signal scientific credibility without being a lecture.
  • "Two weeks to go. Keep going." is a commitment device — it reframes the remaining two weeks as finishing something they've already started.

Copy notes

  • "Most people don't" creates genuine status for completing 30 days — it's true (industry average churn is ~40% in month 1) and it makes the customer feel they've done something worth continuing.
  • Subscription pitch is minimal: save $10, no lock-in. No manufactured urgency. The work was done in emails 1-4.
  • The referral ask is deliberately anti-referral-programme: "Not because we're running a referral programme — we're not." This mirrors the brand's wider positioning against marketing hype and lands as more credible than a standard referral incentive.
  • "Not by buying more podcast ads" is a nod to the positioning established in Email 3, and makes the customer feel like a collaborator rather than a mark.

Implementation Notes for Klaviyo / Any ESP

  • Trigger: Order placed (any Modern Savage product)
  • Delays: Email 1 = immediate, Email 2 = 48h, Email 3 = 7 days, Email 4 = 14 days, Email 5 = 28 days
  • Suppression: Suppress Email 5 subscription CTA for customers already on subscription at send time
  • Split test suggestion: Run Email 4 subject lines A/B — "You won't feel this working" vs. "Two weeks in: the honest truth" — the first is counterintuitive and should outperform on open rate
  • From name: "Bear Grylls" or "Bear at Modern Savage" — avoid corporate sender names
  • Design: Plain text with minimal branding outperforms heavy-designed emails in this category. Trust = no templates.

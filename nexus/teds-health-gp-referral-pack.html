Three documents for GP practices · Ready to send or print
Prepared by Nexus · April 2026 · For use by Ted's Health clinical team

This pack gives you everything needed to begin building a GP referral network — the single highest-moat patient acquisition channel available in the UK private TRT market. Most competitors are fighting for the same digital audience. Almost nobody is working the GP channel.

What's in this pack

  1. Document 1 — GP Practice Brief — One-page clinical summary to send or hand to practice managers. Explains who Ted's Health is, what it treats, and why GPs should refer.
  2. Document 2 — Patient Referral Letter Template — A ready-to-use letter GPs can personalise in under a minute. Removes friction from the referral decision.
  3. Document 3 — GP Feedback Report Template — The report Ted's Health sends back to the referring GP after each patient's initial consultation. Closes the loop and builds trust.

How to use this pack

Step 1 — Identify target practices

Map every GP practice within 15 miles of Ted's Health's clinic. Prioritise practices with higher list sizes and those in areas with above-average male 30-60 demographics (typically suburban and commuter belt areas). A list of 30-50 practices is a manageable initial target.

Step 2 — Initial outreach

Send Document 1 (the GP Practice Brief) by post, addressed to the Practice Manager, from Ted's clinical director. Follow up by phone 7-10 days later — not to sell, but to offer to answer clinical questions and ask whether they'd like a short conversation. A 10-minute phone call with the practice manager or lead GP is the goal, not an immediate referral commitment.

Note on cold email: Email is easy to ignore. A physical letter on headed notepaper gets opened. Post Document 1 first, then follow up digitally. For GDPR compliance, don't send unsolicited marketing emails to individual GPs — postal outreach to practice addresses is fine.

Step 3 — Make the referral process frictionless

The biggest barrier to GP referrals isn't willingness — it's the time cost of writing a referral letter. Document 2 (the referral letter template) removes that barrier entirely. Make it available as a Word document that GPs can download from your website, or offer to email it on request.

Step 4 — Close the loop

Send Document 3 (the GP feedback report) within 5 working days of each referred patient's first consultation. GPs hate referring into black boxes. A brief, professional summary back to the referring doctor is what turns a one-off referral into a long-term referring relationship.

The compound effect: One GP who refers 3-4 patients per month is worth more than most paid search campaigns — and those patients arrive pre-qualified, trusting the clinic their doctor recommended. GPs also talk to each other: one good relationship can seed others.

Ted's Health: Private Testosterone Therapy

About Ted's Health

Ted's Health is a UK private clinic specialising in testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) for men. We are a regulated private provider with GMC-registered prescribers. Our clinical team has experience in testosterone deficiency assessment, personalised TRT protocols, and ongoing monitoring.

We work alongside NHS primary care — not to replace it. For your patients who fall outside NHS eligibility criteria, or who face lengthy NHS waiting times, we offer a direct route to assessment and treatment.

Why GPs refer to us

The NHS problem What Ted's Health offers
NHS eligibility threshold typically <8 nmol/L total testosterone — many symptomatic men don't qualify We treat symptomatic men with testosterone <15 nmol/L, in line with BSSM guidance
NHS endocrinology referral wait: 6-12 months in most trusts Initial consultation within 1-2 weeks; treatment started within 3 weeks if indicated
GP left with symptomatic patient, no good referral pathway Simple referral process; feedback report sent back to GP within 5 working days

Who we treat

Adult men (18+) with symptoms consistent with testosterone deficiency, including:

  • Fatigue, reduced energy, poor sleep quality
  • Reduced libido and sexual dysfunction
  • Mood changes, low motivation, poor concentration
  • Reduced muscle mass, increased adiposity
  • Morning testosterone <15 nmol/L on two separate tests, with symptoms

Our clinical process

  1. Initial consultation — Full symptom assessment, review of existing bloods or new blood panel if required
  2. Shared decision-making — Treatment options discussed; patient chooses injection, gel, or other protocol
  3. Treatment initiation — Supervised by GMC-registered prescriber
  4. Monitoring — Regular bloods (testosterone, haematocrit, PSA, LH/FSH) per BSSM guidelines; GP kept informed
We will always write back. Every patient referred to Ted's Health receives a consultation summary report, sent to the referring GP within 5 working days. We are always contactable if you have clinical questions about a patient.

Implementation notes

Making This Work

First 30 days: targeting

Start with 20-30 GP practices within a 10-mile radius. Focus on practices with online profiles that mention male health, men's health clinics, or testosterone — GPs who already see this patient type are far more receptive. Use NHS Digital's practice finder to get practice contact details.

What to say when you call

Don't pitch. Ask. "We've sent a clinical brief about our testosterone clinic and wanted to check whether it arrived and whether [clinician name] or your practice manager had any questions." If the answer is no: "Would it be useful if I sent over our referral letter template so you have it on file?" If the answer is yes: send Document 2 and follow up with a thank you.

Tracking

Keep a simple spreadsheet: practice name, contact name, date of first outreach, response, first referral date. After 60 days, you'll have a clear picture of which practices are active referrers and which aren't worth further time investment.

Compounding the relationship

After the first referral from a practice, send a handwritten note (or at minimum a personalised email) to the GP thanking them. At 3 months, share a brief aggregate update: "We've now seen 5 patients referred from your practice — average testosterone on presentation was X nmol/L, all are responding well to treatment." GPs appreciate feedback loops. This is what turns a trial referral into a long-term channel.

The numbers

Ted's Health needs 128 more patients to reach breakeven. A conservative GP referral channel — 5 active referring practices each sending 2 patients per month — delivers 10 patients/month from this channel alone. At that rate, 13 months to fill the gap from GP referrals only. But referral rates compound as trust builds; the real payoff is 18-24 months in, when GPs are defaulting to Ted's for testosterone queries without being asked.

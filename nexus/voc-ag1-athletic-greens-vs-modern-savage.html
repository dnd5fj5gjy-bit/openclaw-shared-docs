Price / Value ● HIGH

Taste ● MEDIUM

Mossy, dirt-like, or bitter/metallic aftertaste reported by a significant portion of users. Grittiness that settles at the bottom unless consumed immediately.

  • "Tastes like dirt mixed with grass."

No Noticeable Results ● HIGH

The most common cancellation trigger: 30+ days in, customers feel nothing different. Influencer hype creates expectations the product cannot meet.

  • "I took AG1 for 1 month daily. I don't feel like anything improved — not a single effect."
  • "Recently started feeling off about an hour after consuming — decided to stop."
Ingredient Concerns ● MEDIUM

Proprietary blends hide actual dosages. High oxalate content concerns. Missing Vitamin D (despite marketing). Potential allergens (green tea, wheatgrass) not flagged clearly.

  • "AG1 doesn't have Vitamin D — the most common deficiency — despite what the marketing implies."
  • "Some ingredients could be high in oxalates — not disclosed clearly."
Misleading Marketing ● MEDIUM

Celebrity and podcast endorsements (Huberman, Rogan, Ferriss) create outsized expectations. Customers feel sold a lifestyle, not a product. Trust collapses when results don't follow.

  • "They use a lot of buzz words and have endorsements from Tim Ferriss — but I haven't noticed a single effect."
  • "The marketing is a little misleading — the reality doesn't match the ad."

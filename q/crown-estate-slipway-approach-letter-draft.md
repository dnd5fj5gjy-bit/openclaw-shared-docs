Re: Lease No. 00019356 / Property No. 0019774 — Slipway, Foreshore and Seabed at St Tudwal's Island West, Abersoch, Gwynedd

We are the tenants under the above lease, dated 9 March 2021, for a term of 30 years from 22 March 2021.

We have noted the outcome of the current rent review and understand this follows the mechanism set out in Part Four of the lease. We are not disputing the basis of the review, but we would like to raise a possible restructuring of the lease that we believe could work well for both parties.

We would welcome the Estate's consideration of extending the term of the lease — for example, by a further 15-20 years beyond the current expiry — in exchange for fixing the Principal Rent at a set figure for the remainder of the extended term, removing the need for further five-yearly rent reviews.

Our reasoning is straightforward: the property is a small, single-purpose structure (a private slipway supporting two non-commercial boats) with a low base rent. We suspect the administrative cost to the Estate of running periodic Open Market Rent reviews and surveyor appointments on an asset of this size is not far off the value those reviews recover. A longer, fixed-rent term would give the Estate certainty of income over a longer horizon while removing that ongoing administrative overhead — and would give us long-term certainty for a structure we built and maintain entirely at our own cost.

We would be happy to discuss a fixed rent figure, or an alternative structure (e.g. a longer indexation-free period with a single review at the extended term's midpoint), whichever is more straightforward for the Estate to accommodate internally.

We would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss this with the relevant member of your Estates team at your convenience.

The Thesis

You already have the IP. The question is how it scales beyond your time.

The Optimal Health Programme is a structured framework — The ONE Method and four foundations, sequenced and ready to teach. Right now it sits inside your one-to-one practice. Everything you deliver is gated by your hours.

Three moves change the shape of the business. Productise the framework into defined offers with set durations, set deliverables and set prices. Layer recurring revenue underneath so income is not tied to new acquisition each month. License the same offers out to clinics that have patient flow but no coaching IP — this is the structural unlock, the move that lets the business grow without you.

This memo lays out what each of those looks like, in what order, and where the commercial leverage actually sits.

A Note on Pricing

The numbers throughout are rough estimates, not fixed prices.

Every figure in this memo — the £1,800–2,400 for Foundations, the £150–200/mo for TRT Optimisation, the £49–79/mo for OJay Inside, the £4–5k for the Performance Partnership, the 35–40% white-label share — is a starting point for conversation, not a recommendation to set in stone. They are benchmarked off publicly visible pricing in the UK functional medicine and men's health market, including:

  • UK functional medicine 12-week programmes (Hampton Wellness, Optimal Health Clinic, Parsley-adjacent operators) sit £2,000–4,000 for comparable scope.
  • TRT coaching adjuncts and premium men's-health coaching (Marek Health, Optimale-tier add-ons, prorated 1-1 coaching) run £100–250/mo.
  • Niche health and fitness group memberships (Marek Health community, Future, premium functional medicine memberships) range £30–150/mo, with the £49–79 band sitting upper-middle.
  • Content and IP licensing splits in fitness/wellness SaaS (Future, Train Heroic and similar models) typically run 30–50%.
  • Premium quarterly cohorts (Heroic-tier programmes £4–6k/qtr; functional medicine intensives like Wild Health Apex £8–15k).

Ollie should set the final numbers against his own current 1-1 rate, his cost-to-deliver, and the price point his existing audience can absorb. Treat the figures as the range to work within, not a price list.

The Licensing Layer · The Unlock

White-labelling is what makes OJay Health scale beyond your hours.

This is the most important section of the memo. The three direct offers are good. They will grow OJay Health into a strong owner-operated business. But they all share the same constraint — they depend on your time, your bandwidth, your attention. There is a ceiling on what they can do.

White-labelling removes that ceiling. You license the framework to clinics that already have the patient flow, take a recurring share per member, and the same Optimal Health Programme reaches hundreds of men through partners — without you delivering the coaching, running the acquisition, or being in the room. This is the structural unlock that turns OJay Health from a coaching business into a licensed IP business.

TRT and men's health clinics have a structural problem. They are good at the medical side and weak on the lifestyle side. Patients drop off, side effects accumulate, outcomes plateau, and the clinic has no real answer beyond "tweak the dose." A white-label coaching programme — yours, under their brand — is the missing tier of their offering. They have the demand. You have the IP. The licensing model joins them.

What you license out

  • The full Optimal Health Programme framework
  • Monthly training, nutrition and education content
  • Community platform and onboarding flow
  • Training the clinic's in-house coach (if they have one)
  • Quarterly content refreshes and updates

The clinic provides: the patient base, the brand wrapper, the front-line communication. Marketing the programme to existing patients is significantly cheaper than acquiring net-new members yourself.

Ideal partners: TRT clinics, functional medicine practices, longevity clinics, high-end gyms with a clinical arm. Anyone with a male health audience and no coaching IP of their own.

Sequencing

What to launch when.

Trying to launch all of this at once would dilute every offer. The sequence matters as much as the offers themselves. Build the IP backbone first, then the recurring layer, then the licensing motion. The high-ticket tier is the slowest to build and benefits from running last.

Months 1–2

Package and launch The Foundations

The flagship offer goes live first. Defined 12-week programme, set price, set deliverables. This becomes the IP backbone — every subsequent offer derives from it. Run 5–10 clients through the first cohort to refine the operational model.

Months 3–4

Open the TRT Optimisation Programme

Once Foundations is running cleanly, the TRT-specific variant launches alongside. Different positioning, different audience, but the operational engine is shared. Start collecting case-study content from week one — this is the marketing asset library.

Months 4–6

Build and launch OJay Inside membership

The recurring layer launches as the back-end of the first two offers. Foundations graduates roll in as the first cohort. Membership platform, monthly content cadence, group call rhythm — all stress-tested with a small founder cohort before opening publicly.

Months 6–9

Open licensing conversations

Once OJay Inside is operationally proven, take it to two or three target clinics as a white-label proposition. Start with one signed partner, validate the partnership economics in market, then expand. Do not pursue licensing until the membership itself is running well — partners will see straight through an unproven product.

Months 9–12

Quietly launch the Performance Partnership

Run the first cohort. Five places maximum. Sold entirely through warm referral. Use it to test the operational rhythm, the residential element and the confidentiality model. Refine before scaling to four cohorts per year.

Track 02 — Prologue

Prologue. Yerushalayim, around AD 33, three days after the torture and execution.

"Tell me what you are discussing," the traveller asked nonchalantly, as he increased his pace so that he was now walking alongside the two. The man seemed not to have a care in the world.

"Why are you so downhearted?" the traveller inquired.

Cleopas rebuked the man. "You must be the only person in all the country who hasn't heard what's been going on." He spat on the ground and wiped his mouth with the sleeve of his cloak. "We had so hoped and prayed that a messiah would prevail against these Romans." He paused. "Today, we taste no victory — only bitter defeat."

The friend with him added quietly, "It is true. These Romans aren't leaving this land anytime soon."

The small group continued to walk the dusty road together. The traveller's cloak and hood covered his head, and in one of his rough hands he rolled some pebbles between his fingers as he walked. He seemed to struggle to contain his zeal. This moment had long been coming. Finally, he could hold himself back no longer.

"How slow you are to believe all that the prophets have spoken." There was an urgency and intensity in his voice. "Did the messiah have to suffer these things?" He paused for effect. "And then — into his glory?"

The two companions turned to look at the man walking beside them.

"I don't understand," Cleopas replied.

The traveller shielded his face and kept walking. The two companions quickened their pace to keep up with the man. And there, on the road from Yerushalayim to Emmaus, with the sun slowly setting in the west, the traveller began to explain to them in scriptures. He told them all the prophets had foretold about the messiah and saviour, and their hearts felt on fire within themselves.

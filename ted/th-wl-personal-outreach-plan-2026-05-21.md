## 2. Target segmentation & prioritisation

The raw pool is the 61 candidate partners in `evidence/legitscript-research-2026-05-12.md`. After de-duplicating and removing out-of-scope entities, the pool segments and tiers as follows.

### Segments
- **Online pharmacies** — already prescribe and dispense; the most natural WL backend match.
- **Men's health / TRT / ED clinics** — same patient, same conditions; highest topical overlap.
- **Diagnostics & blood-test providers** — they own the *trigger* (a low-T or hormone result) but usually have no treatment pathway. Strong "demand leak" fit.
- **Online GP / telehealth services** — have the audience and consults but often no dedicated men's-health hormone pathway.
- **Wellness / screening** — broader health audience with men in it; warmer once a hormone angle is established.

### Tiering logic (this is the bit to defend)
**Tier 1 — LegitScript-certified.** LegitScript certification is the gating credential for partner compatibility. A certified partner has already passed the same regulatory bar TH operates to, which means: (a) lowest onboarding friction, (b) they already understand and value compliant prescribing, (c) far less education needed on "why governance matters." These are the warmest, most-compatible targets — so they go first. **9 unique certified entities** (12 raw rows, three are duplicates of each other — see notes).

**Tier 2 — strong strategic fit, not certified / status unknown.** Real WL fits (men in their ecosystem, plausible appetite for a hormone revenue line) but no LegitScript cert evidence. Higher onboarding friction — they'd need to get comfortable with TH's clinical governance, and some may need to pursue certification themselves. Approach after Tier 1, or in parallel only where there's a genuine warm relationship.

**Excluded — out of scope.** Removed from the target list entirely:
- *Insurers / cash-plan providers* (not WL-compatible, different model): Bupa, Bupa Health Clinics, Benenden Health, Simplyhealth, Vitality.
- *Private hospital groups* (out of LS pharmacy scope, wrong buyer): HCA Healthcare UK, Spire Healthcare, The Harley Street Clinic, Roodlane Medical (HCA umbrella).
- *Defunct / unidentifiable*: Babylon Health (collapsed 2023), Online Doctor UK (generic descriptor, no canonical entity), The ED Clinic (could not ID).
- *Duplicate rows* (folded into their canonical entry, not counted twice): Doctor4U→Doctor-4-U, Medicspot(p-047)→MedicSpot, Mojo Men's Health→Mojo, Zava(p-032)→ZAVA, DrEd Online Doctor→DrEd, The Online Clinic(p-056)→Online Clinic, Dr Felix→Simple Online Pharmacy.

> **Why prioritise on LegitScript and not on size/brand?** Because the constraint on WL onboarding isn't audience size — it's regulatory and operational compatibility. A certified partner can be live faster and is far less likely to stall in compliance review. We can always go up-market on brand later; we start where we can win quickly and cleanly.

## 3. The approach / rationale

**Warm, CEO-to-CEO personal outreach — not cold sales.**

- **Why personal, not a sales team:** WL is a strategic partnership decision (a business deciding to put TH's medical engine behind their brand), not a transactional sale. That decision is made founder-to-founder. A personal note from Olly — peer to peer, "I run strategy at BGV / Ted's Health, I think there's something here for both of us" — lands where a sales sequence bounces. It also signals seriousness and respect for the recipient's business.
- **Why this sequencing (certified first):** We spend the scarce resource — Olly's personal time and credibility — where conversion friction is lowest. Certified partners convert faster and validate the model; early wins create reference cases and momentum for the harder Tier 2 conversations.
- **What "good" looks like:** A *booked partner strategy call*. Not a signed deal, not a long email thread — a 30-minute call on the calendar. That's the single success metric for every touch. Everything in the message copy drives to that one CTA.
- **Defensible to a skeptic:** "You're emailing pharmacies at random" — no: we're starting only with partners who've already passed the same compliance bar we hold, segmented by how their existing audience overlaps with men's-health demand. "Why not just buy ads?" — because WL isn't a D2C product, it's infrastructure sold into businesses that already own the audience; the buyer is a founder, and founders respond to founders. "Why personal, doesn't scale?" — the top tier is small (single digits); personal outreach is exactly right at this volume. Scale comes later, after the model is proven.

## 6. Cadence / timing

- **Spacing:** Touch 1 → +4 days → +5 days → +7 days (breakup). ~16-day window per target, then stop.
- **In flight at once:** Cap at **8–10 active conversations** so every reply gets a genuinely personal, same-day response. This is personal outreach — quality over volume. Work Tier 1 to completion before opening the Tier 2 batch (or run a small parallel Tier 2 LinkedIn track only where there's an existing relationship).
- **When to stop:** After the breakup note with no reply. Don't chase past four touches — log them as "dormant," revisit in ~3 months or when there's a new hook (a TH milestone, a relevant news event, a mutual intro that surfaces).
- **On a positive reply:** Stop the sequence immediately and switch to booking the call. The sequence exists only to *get* the call.

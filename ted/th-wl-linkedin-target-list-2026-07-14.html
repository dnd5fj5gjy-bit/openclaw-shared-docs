Compatibility rule

Include: businesses with an existing engaged health-adjacent audience that do not currently sell TRT, ED treatment, or hormone therapy - where TH's white-label vertical would sit alongside their existing offer as a new revenue line, not overlap it. Exclude: anyone already prescribing or dispensing testosterone, ED medication, or hormone-replacement therapy - those are direct competitors. Where a business's current treatment menu couldn't be confirmed from public sources, it's marked Verify rather than included as clean.

Confirmed competitors - excluded, not silently dropped

  • Medichecks / Leger Clinic - Medichecks (testing) has acquired Leger Clinic specifically to move into direct TRT provision. Worth flagging to Olly as a market signal: diagnostics players are moving into TRT fulfilment, which is exactly the gap TH's WL model should race to fill with others before they follow.
  • The Independent Pharmacy - already sells testosterone testing plus doctor-recommended TRT and ED medication pathways.
  • HUM2N (Dr Mohammed Enayat) - explicitly offers bioidentical HRT and TRT as core services. Looked like a strong longevity-clinic candidate on the surface; would have repeated the earlier list's mistake.
  • Calleva Clinic - markets as "GP-Led Weight Loss & HRT Specialists," likely already runs a hormone pathway. Lean exclude; verify exact scope before any approach.
  • One5 Health - GLP-1 + private GP; hormone scope unconfirmed but flagged as likely-bundled rather than pure weight-loss.
  • Lisa Franklin (Clinic Privé) - not a hormone-treatment competitor, but her HRT/menopause pillar is female-audience-led via a consultant gynaecologist; poor fit for a male TRT/ED vertical. Noted rather than dropped.

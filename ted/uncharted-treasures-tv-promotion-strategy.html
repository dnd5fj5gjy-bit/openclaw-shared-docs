The Show

Eight episodes. One buried truth.

Uncharted Treasures is a real-life treasure hunt for the whole family, starring Coyote Peterson — YouTube's most trusted real-world wildlife creator — alongside legendary shipwreck hunter Bart Michelini and an elite team of divers, historians, and archaeologists on a mission to uncover the true birthplace of piracy in the Bahamas.

With unprecedented access, full excavation permissions, and cutting-edge technology, the team isn't chasing legends — they're pursuing real evidence buried since the Golden Age of Piracy.

Set on Long Island, Bahamas, this eight-episode adventure takes viewers into abandoned plantations, collapsed wells, inland caves, forgotten prisons, and treacherous coastal waters. Each discovery fuels the next, building toward the stunning unearthing of an 18th-century shipwreck long believed lost — and culminating in evidence pointing to a mysterious inland lake rumoured to guard vast pirate treasure, untouched for centuries.

Immersive cinematography and AI-driven reconstructions bring the world of 16th–18th century piracy vividly to life, placing audiences inside every breakthrough.

Executive produced by Bear Grylls, Uncharted Treasures proves: nothing stays buried forever.

“Nothing stays buried forever.” Show line · 1733

This document is a promotion strategy for the series — how we get the right audiences in front of the show, build pre-launch demand, convert Coyote's creator audience into broadcast viewers, and position the title to earn a Season 2 conversation by the end of the run. A separate companion document covers the ecosystem play that follows.

Why this lands now

The genre, the audience, and the talent are all converging.

Treasure-hunt television has spent twenty years in the wilderness of cable. It is returning to the centre of the conversation, lifted by a real-archaeology renaissance, the AI-reconstruction trend in documentary, and a chronic undersupply of family-safe adventure content on the streamers. Uncharted Treasures sits exactly on that meeting point — with a YouTube native lead, a legitimate scientific team, and the most recognisable name in adventure television above the line.

01 / GENRE

Treasure-hunt TV is having a quiet revival.

Oak Island has held the bar for twelve seasons. Expedition Unknown, History's Greatest Mysteries, Lost Cities and the AI-driven recon-docs from Disney+ have all proven a real, durable appetite for slow-burn, evidence-led mystery. What's missing is a flagship with a creator at the centre. We are that flagship.

02 / SUPPLY GAP

Family-safe adventure is structurally undersupplied.

Streamer commissioning has chased true-crime, dating and reality drama. Adventure-doc with co-view appeal — the kind a parent can put on with a nine-year-old — is a genuine open lane. Networks need it. Family streamers (Disney+, Apple Family, Amazon Kids+, Nat Geo) need it. We are built for that lane.

03 / TALENT

Coyote bridges household and feed in one move.

Coyote Peterson is one of the few creators whose audience is genuinely cross-generational and household co-viewed. Kids brought the parents in years ago. He is the cleanest bridge between a YouTube-native Gen-Z/Alpha viewer and the linear/streaming parent buying the subscription — in a single talent.

04 / DISTRIBUTION

Bear's EP credit opens rooms others can't.

Bear Grylls as executive producer is not a vanity line. It is a distribution unlock — the credit that gets the title into top-of-slate conversations at Disney, Nat Geo, Amazon, Apple, Discovery and the global free-TV majors. It also brings BGV's existing partner network (Disney, Netflix, Amazon, NBC) into immediate reach.

The Audience

Four pillars. One household.

Our audience strategy is not one segment with adjacent ones around it. It is four genuine pillars that meet inside a single living room. The whole campaign architecture is designed to bring each pillar in through its own door, and to land them in the same place at the same time on premiere night.

25M+
Coyote's creator universe

The family-creator audience

Brave Wilderness and Coyote's adjacent channels carry a deeply engaged, family-watched audience. He is one of a small handful of YouTube creators whose content is genuinely co-viewed at home — parents trust him, kids return to him. This is the warmest, highest-intent first audience the show has.

Global
Bear Grylls adventure base

The Bear audience — multi-platform

The Bear Grylls audience is broad, international, and pre-disposed to expedition storytelling. They do not need to be educated on the genre, only invited to a new title with a familiar above-the-line credit. We use Bear's own channels and BGV social as the anchor amplifier for every key beat.

12 yrs
Genre runway

The treasure-hunt fandom

Oak Island has run for over a decade. Expedition Unknown, Hunting Hitler, Forged in Fire, History's Greatest Mysteries — this fandom is established, vocal, and underserved by anything new. We earn them through Bart Michelini, the real-archaeology framing, and the inland-lake mystery thread that lives across the season.

New
AI-recon curious viewer

The AI-reconstruction tech viewer

A newer audience — the people who watched the AI-recon Pompeii content, the Ancient Apocalypse crowd, the Lex Fridman / tech-curious tier. They are drawn in by craft and method as much as story. We treat the AI-reconstruction work as a hero asset, not a garnish, and route it through tech-leaning creators and outlets.

The Strategy · Four Phases

From mystery, to reveal, to launch, to legacy.

The campaign runs ninety days before premiere and sustains across the eight-episode run. Each phase has one job, one primary surface, and a clear hand-off into the next. Discipline on the order of operations is the whole game — the worst version of this campaign is a flat-line of trailers and posts. The right version is a curve.

00
Tease · T-90 to T-30

Plant the mystery before the title.

No show name, no logo, no trailer. We start with a question that the right audiences cannot ignore.

  • "1733" cipher campaign across Coyote's channels and Bear's social — clue posts, decoded comments, an in-world Easter-egg site at a dedicated URL.
  • Motion poster reveal as the campaign's first owned asset — the date, the line, no faces.
  • Gated email capture from day one. The cipher campaign is the lure; the list is the asset.
  • Private sizzle to a tight press list — Variety, Deadline, RealScreen, The Hollywood Reporter, Cynopsis — embargoed.
  • Pre-brief Bahamas Ministry of Tourism, dive operators on Long Island, archaeology and maritime press — ensure they don't break the embargo.
01
Reveal · T-30 to T-7

Synchronise the unveil. Earn the press week.

A coordinated trailer drop, talent-led behind-the-scenes, and a press junket designed to convert the genre fans and the Coyote audience in the same week.

  • Full trailer (1:30) and a 0:30 cut released simultaneously across Coyote's main channel, Bear's channels, BGV social, and the broadcaster's owned surfaces.
  • Coyote-led BTS vlog on his main channel within 48 hours — first-person, in-cave, real reactions. The job is to show his audience that he isn't selling the show, he's in it.
  • Press junket: Today Show family slot, GMA, Smartless, Theo Von, a Joe Rogan / Lex Fridman tier long-form on the AI-reconstruction work, and the genre podcasts (Astonishing Legends, History Hit).
  • Bahamas Ministry of Tourism partnership beat — co-branded reels, in-country press familiarisation trip, a dive operator tie-in.
  • Talent reels seeded: Coyote on the cave dive, Bart on the shipwreck moment, Bear on the producer story.
02
Launch · Week 1–2

Convert the warm audience into Week 1 viewers.

An episode-by-episode social bible, live co-viewing on Coyote's surfaces, and a sustain plan that does not fall off after the premiere weekend.

  • Per-episode social bible: 1 hero clip, 3 vertical cutdowns, 1 AI-reconstruction reel, 1 talent-led TikTok. Eight episodes × that bundle · one delivery rhythm.
  • Coyote-channel live watch party for episode one — co-viewing chat, real-time reactions, a tune-in CTA to the broadcaster.
  • Reddit AMA with Bart Michelini in r/IAmA and r/AskHistorians — the credibility audience.
  • Press sustain — recap coverage with maritime and archaeology outlets after each major discovery beat.
  • Paid amplification on platform-specific creative: TikTok Spark Ads off Coyote's BTS, Meta Reels off the AI-recon, YouTube pre-roll on adjacent (Oak Island, Expedition Unknown, Brave Wilderness) content.
03
Sustain & Bridge · Week 3–8

Escalate the discovery arc. Set up Season 2.

The back half of the run delivers the shipwreck unearthing as the central pay-off and re-points the audience toward the inland-lake mystery — the spine of the second season conversation.

  • Discovery-arc storytelling beat-by-beat — we treat each find as a press moment, not just an episode.
  • Mid-season "evidence drop" — a one-off long-form post or YouTube special framing what the team has actually found, with archaeology-advisor on camera.
  • Finale week: shipwreck-unearthing reveal as the social hero asset, a finale-night Coyote live watch.
  • End-of-season teaser pointing at the inland lake — a renewal-conversation hook, not a Season 2 announcement.
  • Hand-off to Phase 2 — the ecosystem strategy that converts the audience we have built into a community and a YouTube franchise. Covered in the companion document.

KPIs · North-star metrics

What we measure. What success looks like.

These are north stars, not contractual commitments. The campaign is unusual — a creator-led distribution model on top of a broadcast premiere — and the right move is to be honest about what we are tracking and why, rather than promising numbers we can't yet underwrite.

250k
Pre-launch list

Target email list size by premiere night, gated through the cipher campaign. The list is a long-term asset that survives the season — it's the seed list for the ecosystem play.

20M+
Trailer views

Aggregate trailer views across Coyote, Bear, broadcaster and paid — in the first 14 days of the reveal phase. Floor, not ceiling.

Top 10
Premiere-night reach

Premiere into the broadcaster's top-10 chart in its home market in week one. The platform-side north star — everything else is in service of this.

30%
Coyote cross-pollination

Of viewers who came in through Coyote's social, the proportion that reach episode one on the broadcaster. The conversion metric this whole campaign turns on.

+25%
Week-over-week retention

Discovery-arc storytelling earns its keep when audiences come back. We track week-over-week completion against genre benchmarks (Oak Island, Expedition Unknown).

5M+
Social-driven tune-in

Attributed tune-in from social touch-points — pixel, lift study, and broadcaster MMM if available. The proof that the creator model paid out.

25
Tier-one press placements

Across trade, consumer, and long-form podcast. Tier-one defined — Variety, Deadline, THR, Today, GMA, Smartless, Theo Von, Rogan/Lex tier.

S2
Season 2 greenlight threshold

The only metric that ultimately matters. By finale plus 30 days, we want to be in a renewal conversation with at least one of the three serious distribution partners.

Every number above is a working target. We will revisit the floor and ceiling once Phase 00 (the cipher tease) closes its first three weeks of data — that gives us the read on creator-audience appetite, and lets us re-grade the rest of the funnel on real ground truth.
Risks & Mitigations

The three things that could go wrong.

A short, honest list. Each one has a real mitigation we can deliver inside the campaign, not a hand-wave.

Risk 01 · Audience

Coyote channel-tone mismatch.

Coyote's audience has expectations — family-safe, first-person, low-cynicism. A glossy promo-led roll-out from him would read as a sell and erode trust. Mitigation: family-safe edit guardrails on every cut that touches his channels; Coyote leads the BTS, not the trailer drop; his audience sees him in the show before they're asked to tune in to it. Promo activity from his surfaces is framed as "what I'm working on" not "what to watch tonight" until launch week.
Risk 02 · Story

Discovery-spoiler leak.

The shipwreck unearthing is the campaign's hero pay-off. If it leaks in Long Island press or via local crew before episode five, the back half of the campaign loses its biggest beat. Mitigation: NDA on local crew and dive operators; embargo on Bahamas press until two weeks after broadcast air of the relevant episode; the Ministry of Tourism partnership is structured to give them tourism upside that depends on the embargo holding.
Risk 03 · Craft

AI-reconstruction backlash.

AI-generated visuals in documentary are an ongoing flashpoint. Without clear handling, the show can be re-framed as "AI slop" by genre press and that framing sticks. Mitigation: every AI-recon sequence carries on-screen disclosure; archaeology-advisor credits run on every recon-led asset; we lead with the methodology in the long-form podcast tier (Lex Fridman, Rogan-tier) and put the AI work front-and-centre as craft, not as garnish. Pre-empt the framing before anyone else gets to write it.

Companion Strategy

From a series, to a franchise.

Once Uncharted Treasures is on air and the hype is genuine, that hype becomes the ignition for the ecosystem. A YouTube franchise built around the show. A community that owns the discovery-arc thread between seasons. An umbrella brand under which other BGV adventures travel onto separate channels — all routed back to one home.

The hormone gap in UK online pharmacy

Every major UK online pharmacy has built ED into its range. Almost none has built TRT. There's a reason — and it's the partnership opportunity.
Full copyEvery major UK online pharmacy has built ED into its range. Almost none has built TRT. There's a reason — and it's the partnership opportunity. ED is a single-molecule, well-precedented, low-management category. A pharmacist with an indemnified prescriber can run it. The protocol is robust, the patient interaction is short, the lifetime value is real but linear. TRT is the opposite. It's a long-cycle, blood-led, dose-titrated, oestradiol-managed, multi-touch category. The patient relationship runs years. The clinical surface is wide — bloods, titration, side-effect management, fertility considerations, repeat audit. You don't build that in a sprint, and you can't run it on a general-prescribing rota. Which is why the UK online-pharmacy category has a visible gap. The audience is there — every operator sees the search and the basket-abandonment data — and the demand is already leaving the brand for D2C men's-health players that built the protocol from scratch. The choice for an established pharmacy isn't whether to enter the hormone category. It's whether to build the clinical engine in-house (twelve months, a clinical team, a regulator conversation), partner with a specialist operator who runs it as white-label behind your brand, or watch the demand quietly bleed. We're the third route. There are others. The first two are the wrong answer for most operators with an audience already in place. — Olly

What a CQC inspection actually checks

CQC-registered is a phrase a lot of operators use. Far fewer have actually sat through an inspection. Here's what one looks like.
Full copyCQC-registered is a phrase a lot of operators use. Far fewer have actually sat through an inspection. Here's what one looks like. The inspector is not interested in your marketing claims. They are interested in evidence — that the service is safe, effective, caring, responsive and well-led. Those are the five domains. Each has its own evidence pack. A short version of what gets reviewed: - Clinical-governance minutes for the previous twelve months - Adverse-event logs and the action taken on each - Prescriber GMC numbers, scope-of-practice documents and CPD records - Standard operating procedures for every clinical pathway you run - Patient-record samples pulled live, reviewed against your stated protocol - Complaints log, response times, action taken - Safeguarding policy and training records for every clinician - Information-governance posture, DSPT submission, data-handling SOPs The inspection is not a one-day visit. The pack is built throughout the year. The visit confirms it. If you're considering putting a clinical brand behind your name, the question to ask any partner is not "are you CQC-registered" — it's "show me the last governance pack you submitted". — Dr Jonathan Andrews

The patient-record audit nobody sees

Every quarter Dr Andrews pulls 30 patient records at random and reviews them against the stated protocol. The point isn't to find errors. It's to know if our SOPs and our actual practice are the same thing.
Full copyEvery quarter Dr Andrews pulls 30 patient records at random and reviews them against the stated protocol. The point isn't to find errors. This is what clinical governance actually is — the discipline of checking that the way you say you operate and the way you operate are within a millimetre of each other. It's also the half a buyer should be asking about before they put their brand on anyone's clinical pathway. — Ted's Health Clinical Team

Why a single-T finger-prick is the wrong starting point

A lot of D2C hormone funnels start with a single-marker testosterone finger-prick test. Clinically, this is the wrong starting point. Here's what good looks like instead.
Full copyA lot of D2C hormone funnels start with a single-marker testosterone finger-prick test. Clinically, this is the wrong starting point. Here's what good looks like instead. A total-testosterone reading on its own tells you very little. It does not differentiate between bound and free fractions. It does not flag SHBG suppression or elevation. It says nothing about oestradiol balance, prolactin, thyroid contribution, or the lipid posture you'll need to monitor once treatment starts. Symptoms of low testosterone are non-specific. Fatigue, low libido, low mood, sleep disruption, training stagnation — every one of those has multiple potential drivers. Some of them have nothing to do with the hormonal axis. A pathway that prescribes against a single number is liable to prescribe against the wrong cause.

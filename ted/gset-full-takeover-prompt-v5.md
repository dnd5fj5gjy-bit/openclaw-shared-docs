## Critical structural rule on the breakdown

When you present the £ allocation, **every line must read as essential to the engine working at all.** No obvious cuts. Jesse's framing on the internal call:

- *"Every part of the breakdown needs to be fucking essential."*
- *"The trick is to make the breakdown such that if they wanted to reduce costs, there's nothing that looks like… okay, where can I reduce costs?"*

Lean into interdependence: content without editing has nothing to ship, distribution without content is empty cadence, the website without tracker infrastructure can't prove sales attribution, every workstream without operations becomes a separate negotiation.

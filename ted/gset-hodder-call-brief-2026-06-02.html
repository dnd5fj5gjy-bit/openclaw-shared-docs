The model

"It's a loop, not a funnel."

Six channels feed each other — Discover, Engage, Relate, Act, Give — and reader testimony flows back to the top to drive new Discovery. YouTube, short-form, Instagram, Substack, Facebook and Bear's anchor posts each pass attention to the next. The shape is cyclical, not linear.

The operational commitment is straightforward: one retainer, one accountable team, one set of deliverables landing every week. Always-on rhythm with calendar anchors that lift the volume — Lent, Easter, Pentecost, the anniversary in June, the UK autumn church push, Advent.

The three priorities, in order

What we're trying to do this year

  • 1 · Close the US gap. 12 months building US Christian audience presence — geo-locked short-form, US-coded YouTube cadence, US faith voices, US retail-velocity tracking against web sessions.
  • 2 · Compound the UK. Sustain the audience we already have through calendar anchors — Lent, Easter, Pentecost, June anniversary, autumn UK church push, Advent.
  • 3 · Set up international. Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese by month 9. Foundations only this year — translation rails, partner shortlist, calendar slot.

Two episode formats, every week, every week. Episode A — The Reading: 8–12 min audiobook passages with editor-added soundscape, ambient audio, atmospherics. Episode B — The Unpacking: 8–12 min with a theologian giving passage context — Hebrew roots, historical setting, fact-checker scholarship.

RecordingThe theologian is filmed in a 3-day batch shoot every 6 months (2x annually). Month 1 priority: lock the studio window and the theologian, prep the question stack across the episode arc.

MonetisationYouTube self-funds at the 8-minute ad threshold. Revenue treatment is open — pooled budget, retainer credit, or a P&L line. Worth flagging on the call; doesn't have to land today.

From Month 3 · the monthly adjust cycle

How we amend each month

Every month follows the same review loop. Direct attribution, engine health, geo-isolated velocity — report, decide, adjust.

  • Direct attribution. Retail clicks tagged by source, geo and campaign. The line between content and a purchase.
  • Geo-isolated velocity. US web sessions read against Hodder US retail velocity, monthly. That's how we know if Priority 1 (close the US gap) is actually moving.
  • Engine health. YouTube watch-time, Substack subscriber growth, Facebook active members, IG saves/shares, short-form view-through. Each channel has its own median line.
  • Format swap. A short-form format that drops below median for two consecutive months gets retired, replaced with a new variant from the testing matrix.
  • Reporting cadence. Monthly summary + weekly ops check-in + quarterly strategy review with Hodder.
  • Cohort cycles. 5-week Facebook cohorts run by eyewitness perspective — Matthew, then Mark, then Luke, then John. Each cycle is a unit of read-and-discuss we can tune separately.

If they ask

Likely Q&A

"Cost vs output — what does Month 1 actually produce?"
Month 1 is build and test, not full launch cadence. Long-form: studio booked, theologian roster locked, audiobook stems in post-production, first Reading + Unpacking pair edited end of month. Short-form: 3–5 clips per platform per week across the testing matrix, ramping up to cadence. Substack: 4 essays drafted, first publishes end of Month 1 or Month 2 Wk 1. Web: build phase. Steady-state output kicks in from Month 2.
"What if the formula doesn't land in Month 1?"
The asset bank is wide and the test matrix runs across three platforms and multiple variant dimensions — we'll see signal. If nothing clears the median by end of Month 1 we extend the test through Month 2 Wk 1–2 and lean harder on the long-form publishing schedule (which doesn't depend on the short-form formula). We'd flag it on the weekly ops check-in, not at month end.
"Is Bear doing any new filming or recording for this?"
No. Bear's contribution is pre-recorded only — audiobook, existing interviews, ChosenCon, archive. His personal channels carry 3 calendar-anchor posts a year (Easter, Christmas, anniversary), written by the team, approved by him. Plus occasional organic story reposts. That's the deliberate structural choice that lets the engine run year-round without consuming his bandwidth.
"What does success look like at 3, 6 and 12 months?"
3 months: locked short-form formula, YouTube monetisation eligible, Substack weekly, first cohort completed, first read on geo-isolated velocity. 6 months: second theologian batch shoot in the can, second cohort underway, US web sessions trending against US retail velocity, calendar anchors banked. 12 months: US gap closing on direct attribution data, UK compounding through anchor seasons, international foundations live (Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese rails).
"How will we know the US strategy is actually working?"
The measurement is geo-isolated velocity — US web sessions read against Hodder US retail velocity, month by month. That correlation is the cleanest signal that content is shifting copies, not just generating views. It depends on the monthly US retail velocity reports from Hodder and the attribution tracker on the rebuilt website.
"Who owns the channels and the audience?"
Hodder retain the book IP. The GSET channel handles and Substack are registered in alignment with Hodder. The marketing assets sit under the GSET brand on the channels we set up, operated by us under the retainer. Worth setting that boundary clearly on this call.
"How does AI fit in — given Hachette policy?"
Hachette's AI policy is respected. AI sits in the backend only — transcription, scheduling, analytics. No AI in published voice, imagery or marketing creative. Every editor signs off on the policy before they commission.
"What's the reporting cadence in Month 1?"
Weekly ops check-in starts Week 1. End-of-Month-1 monthly summary covering: short-form testing matrix and format recommendation, asset audit status, theologian roster status, web build progress, the locked Month-2 content slate for sign-off. Quarterly strategy review starts at the end of Quarter 1.

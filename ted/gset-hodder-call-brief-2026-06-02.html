The picture

The deck Hodder are sitting with proposes BGV running the GSET marketing engine end-to-end at £5,000 / month all-in. This call is about the beginning phase — what we actually do in Month 1, before the Month-2 launch beat. The headline: we don't launch with a guess. We use Month 1 to find a content formula that works, then we launch with it.

The frame for the call is simple. Month 1 is build + test. We record the YouTube long-form content, we trial a high volume of short-form variants, we read the signal, and we lock the formula that wins. Month 2 is the launch beat — website live, first four Reading episodes out, short-form daily, Substack weekly, Matthew read-along open. Then each month after that we adjust based on what's working.

The approach · the through-line

"Rather than picking a format upfront and hoping it lands, we use Month 1 to find the formula. Volume first, signal second, scale third."

"The YouTube long-form is the spine — we batch-shoot it in Month 1 so we have the source library to cut from. On top of that we run a deliberate short-form testing loop: many variants per week, varied on the dimensions we can actually control — hook style, length, narrator vs text-on-screen, pull-out clip vs original cut, platform-native vs cross-posted. We let the data tell us what shape of content earns saves, shares and watch-through on this audience."

"When we see a format hitting the signal we're looking for — usually 2 to 3 weeks in — we lock that as the Month-2 launch formula and ramp to the deck's published volumes. Anything that didn't land gets retired. We don't keep producing in shapes the audience hasn't rewarded."

What "we've found the formula" actually looks like

The signal we're reading for

A format earns the Month-2 slot when it clears these on a meaningful sample. We're not chasing reach — we're chasing audience quality.

Saves & sharesSave / share ratio over likes is the primary signal. Content that travels DM-to-friend is what we're trying to make.
Watch-throughShort-form retention above platform median for the format length. Reading-the-Gospel cuts have a higher bar — the content is the point.
Comment qualitySubstantive comments (questions, scripture references, personal reflection) over emoji-only. Spot-check weekly.
Top-of-funnel CTRClick-through from bio / pinned comment to Substack subscribe or cohort signup. The one number that tells us we're growing the readership, not just the views.
Decision rule: a format gets the Month-2 slot if it clears save/share + watch-through on at least 6 posts across at least 2 platforms. A format is retired if it underperforms on the same sample. Olly signs off the locked formula end of Month 1.

From Month 3 · the monthly adjust cycle

How we amend each month

Once we're in steady state, every month follows the same review loop. Monthly performance report → trigger decisions → next month's content brief.

  • What we measure. Per-format: saves, shares, watch-through, CTR to Substack/cohort, follower growth, comment quality. Per-channel: subscriber growth, top performers, drop-off points. Per-pillar: which of Reading / Unpacking / Short-form / Reader-testimony is doing the lifting.
  • Format swap. A short-form format that drops below median for two consecutive months gets retired and replaced with a new template variant. Operational — Olly signs off the swap.
  • Content-pillar swap. If a whole pillar (e.g. Reader-testimony) underperforms for two months, we reweight the calendar — fewer of that pillar, more of what's working. Surfaced in the monthly report, agreed with Hodder.
  • Voice / tone swap. If commentary signal says we're missing the audience (too academic, too casual), we adjust the theologian roster mix or the audiobook excerpt selection. Olly + Andy decide.
  • Sign-off chain. Operational publishes — BGV decides. Calendar anchors (Lent, Advent, etc.) — pre-approved via a brief 2 weeks ahead. Sensitive items (contested theology, brand-line crossings) — escalated to Andy.

If they ask

Likely Q&A

"What if the formula doesn't land in Month 1?"
The testing loop is wide enough that we'll see something. Five formats × multiple variant dimensions across three platforms gives us at least one shape that clears the signal bar. If nothing clears it by end of Month 1, we extend the test by 2 weeks and push the launch beat to Month 2 week 3 — long-form publishing still goes out on schedule (the Reading episodes don't depend on the short-form formula). We'd flag it on the weekly check-in immediately, not at month-end.
"How much content does Month 1 actually produce?"
Long-form: 4 Reading episodes in post-production + 52 Unpacking episodes captured (4 in edit by end of month). Short-form: meaningfully fewer than the 36/month steady state — call it a 50–70% volume ramp across Weeks 2–4 while we test. Substack: 3 articles drafted, first one publishes end of Month 1 or Month 2 Wk 1. The deck's full Section-09 cadence kicks in from Month 2.
"Cost vs output — are we paying full retainer in a build month?"
Yes, and the deck explains why. Months 1 and 2 are front-loaded — website rebuild, Substack stand-up, 52-ep batch shoot, brand + audio one-offs, the testing loop. That's why the 3-month minimum exists. Month 3 onwards is the steady-state engine the retainer was sized for; Months 1–2 are the build investment that makes Month 3+ possible.
"Bear's commitment — is he doing anything new?"
No new filming and no new recording. Everything Bear is in is pre-recorded — audiobook, Big Church Read, archive, podcast bank. He's the voice and patron, not the host of every post. That's a deliberate structural choice — it's how the engine stays sustainable across a full year.
"What does success look like at 3 months? At 6?"
3 months: locked formula running at deck Section-09 volume, YouTube channel monetisation eligible, Substack to 4 articles/month, Matthew cohort completed its first run, first real growth data in the monthly report. 6 months: 100+ short-form pieces published, Reading + Unpacking running weekly, theologian batch refreshed for the next 26 weeks, second cohort (Mark) underway, audience growth trend established across all channels.
"Who owns the channels and the audience?"
Hodder retain the book IP. Channel handles and Substack registered in alignment with Hodder. The marketing assets we build sit under the GSET brand on the channels we set up, operated by us under the retainer. Worth being careful here — don't promise more than what's in the deck.
"Why won't AI just do this cheaper?"
It can't, on this brand. Hachette policy bans AI-generated voice, imagery and marketing creative. AI sits in our backend only — transcription, scheduling, analytics — never in published output. Every Ku editor signs off on the policy before they commission.
"What's the reporting cadence in Month 1?"
Weekly Friday sample to Hodder for sight (not gated). End-of-Month-1 dashboard covering: short-form testing matrix results, formula recommendation, asset audit status, theologian roster status, build progress, the locked Month-2 launch slate for sign-off.

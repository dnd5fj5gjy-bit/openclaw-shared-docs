The Opportunity

Demand isn't the constraint — deal flow is.

Bear Witness can already win and deliver — Alfred and MamaBucci are live. But every client has come inbound or via a warm relationship. Nothing goes out and creates the next ten.

Delivery is sorted: senior strategy from Jesse and the team, plus effectively unlimited make capacity in-house. The missing piece is the front end that turns that into a steady flow of paying clients.

Today

Inbound only

Clients arrive by referral or relationship. Good work, but the pipeline depends on luck and timing, not on a system we control.

The gap

No outbound motion

Nobody owns going out, researching the right businesses, and opening the conversation that brings them onto Bear Witness.

The prize

A scalable agency

A predictable client engine plus recurring revenue is what turns Bear Witness from a project shop into a real, growing business.

The Unlock

Engine, not face — so brand alignment stops being a limit.

Why this opens the market

We sell the operating system, not the name.

We do the strategy and creative, then hand off — the Bear Grylls name never goes on the front of a client's work. So we're not limited to brands that fit Bear or the outdoors world. Any growing business that needs a marketing engine is a fair target.

It also needs none of Bear's time, content or involvement. The engine runs invisibly behind the client's brand — growing revenue without touching what makes Bear Witness valuable.

The one thing we have to get right. Hand over strategy and creatives and the client runs their own channels — clean, low-risk. Run their pages and socials ourselves and we carry their brand voice in public daily. That's where reputation risk and ops load sit — so it's gated to the premium tier: selective intake, higher price.
Who We Target

Two clear audiences.

Audience A

Growing businesses, no agency yet

  • Small but scaling — past the founder-doing-everything stage
  • Not yet using an agency; marketing is ad-hoc or in one person's head
  • Need a proper marketing engine but can't justify a full in-house team
  • Best fit for the Campaign Blueprint — a plan and assets to run with
Audience B

Bigger businesses scaling or shifting

  • Established, with budget, but stuck or plateaued
  • Wanting to scale, rebrand, or move harder into digital
  • Have in-house people but need senior strategy and creative firepower
  • Best fit for the Full Engine — higher value, longer relationships
Because we're white-label, neither audience needs to be Bear-aligned or outdoors-adjacent. The filter is simple: do they have an audience and a budget, and a marketing gap we can fill?
The Offerings

Two products, deliberately different.

Same engine behind both. The difference is how much of the running we own — and that sets the price and the risk.

Product 1 · Volume play

Campaign Blueprint

Lower cost · project or light retainer · scalable
  • Full campaign strategy, delivered as a clear document
  • All creatives built to support it — ready to ship
  • The client runs and manages execution themselves
  • Optional add-on: ongoing creative support from the editing team
Best for Audience A. Light on our ops, easy to deliver many in parallel, low brand risk.
Product 2 · Value play

Full Engine

Much higher cost · recurring · selective intake
  • Everything in the Blueprint, plus
  • We run the channels — paid, social, content, the calendar
  • We own execution end-to-end behind their brand
  • Only taken on for the right clients we can do justice to
Best for Audience B. Recurring revenue — the bit that compounds into a real business — but ops-heavy and carries brand risk, so it's priced and gated accordingly.
The strategic point: the Blueprint builds volume and proof; the Full Engine builds recurring revenue. Lead generation comes from the first, the long-term business value from the second. We need both — and should be honest internally about which one we're really trying to grow.

The Motion

The one thing we borrow from tech sales — nothing else.

The only parallel to tech sales is this: we proactively open a conversation with someone and aim to convert them into a paying client. That's it. Everything else is Bear Witness — quality-led, senior in tone, never a cheap blast.

01

Research

In-depth work on exactly who to contact — sector, size, and a real marketing gap. The targeting is considered, not scraped.

02

Approach

Cold outreach, but to a Bear Witness standard — specific, credible, worth their attention. Done at volume off the research.

03

Converse

A real conversation that diagnoses their gap and frames which product fits — Blueprint or Full Engine.

04

Convert

Land them on the right tier and hand to delivery cleanly.

05

Grow

Move Blueprint clients up the ladder into retained and Full Engine relationships over time.

Standard, not spam. Volume comes from the depth of the research — knowing exactly who to contact and why — not from blasting. Every approach has to clear the Bear Witness quality bar, because the engine carries our name even when the client work doesn't. Anything that reads as cheap or pushy is off the table.
The Role

Why this is worth my time — and what I'd own.

An additional remit alongside my current Market & Strategy work — it's where strategy meets commercial growth, and right now nobody owns it.

What I'd own

Lead the growth engine

  • Define the target audiences and build the prospect lists
  • Own the outbound motion end-to-end — research, approach, convert
  • Shape and price the two products and the package ladder
  • Carry the relationship until a client is landed and handed to delivery
  • Report on pipeline, conversions and revenue contribution
Why me

The fit is direct

  • Market & strategy is already my lane — this extends it commercially
  • I can speak credibly to growing businesses about their marketing gap
  • Senior delivery and editing capacity already exist to back me up
  • It's a clear, ownable initiative with a measurable growth path
Why it's worth the time. If this works, it adds a new, scalable revenue line to Bear Witness without needing Bear's involvement and without diluting the brand. If it doesn't, we'll know cheaply — because the first phase is validation, not build. The downside is a few weeks of research; the upside is a repeatable client engine.
The Ask

A green light to validate — not to build.

Not asking to build anything yet — just the go-ahead to spend focused time proving whether this is real before we invest.

What a yes unlocks — the validation phase
  • Research the two target audiences and confirm the demand is genuinely there
  • Pressure-test the two products and the package pricing with real businesses
  • Book and run a handful of real conversations with prospective clients
  • Come back with evidence — would they pay, for what, and at what price
  • Only then, if it stacks up, propose the actual build and launch
Decision needed: a green light for me to spend time on the validation phase above. No spend, no build, no commitment beyond my time — just permission to find out if this is worth Bear Witness pursuing properly.

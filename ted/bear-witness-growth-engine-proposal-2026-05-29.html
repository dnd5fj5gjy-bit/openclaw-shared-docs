The Opportunity

Demand isn't the constraint — deal flow is

Bear Witness has already proven it can win and deliver client work — Alfred and MamaBucci are live. But every client so far has come inbound or through a warm relationship. There's no engine that goes out and creates the next ten.

If the goal is to grow Bear Witness into a serious business, the missing piece is a repeatable, outbound way to fill the pipeline — not more delivery capability. The delivery is sorted: senior strategy from Jesse and the team, and effectively unlimited make capacity through the in-house editing team. What's missing is the front end that turns that capability into a steady flow of paying clients.

Today

Inbound only

Clients arrive by referral or relationship. Good work, but the pipeline depends on luck and timing, not on a system we control.

The gap

No outbound motion

Nobody owns going out, finding the right businesses, and walking them through onto Bear Witness — the way a SaaS sales team does.

The prize

A scalable agency

A predictable client engine plus recurring revenue is what turns Bear Witness from a project shop into a real, growing business.

The Unlock

Engine, not face — so brand alignment stops being a limit

This is the insight that makes the whole thing bigger than it looks.

Why this opens the market

We sell the operating system, not the name

Because Bear Witness does the strategy consult and the creative build and then hands off, we don't put the Bear Grylls name on the front of anyone's work. That means we don't have to limit ourselves to brands that fit Bear or the outdoors / nature world. Any growing business that needs a marketing engine becomes a fair target.

It also protects the brand the other way: this requires none of Bear's time, content or personal involvement. The engine runs invisibly behind the client's brand. Done right, it grows revenue without ever touching the thing that makes Bear Witness valuable.

The one thing we have to get right. When we only build the strategy and creatives and hand them over, the client runs their own channels — clean and low-risk. The moment we also run their pages and socials, we're carrying their brand voice in public, day to day. That's where reputation risk and operational load both live — so it's gated to the premium tier, with selective intake and a higher price that pays for the care it needs.

Who We Target

Two clear audiences

Audience A

Growing businesses, no agency yet

  • Small but scaling — past the founder-doing-everything stage
  • Not yet using an agency; marketing is ad-hoc or in one person's head
  • Need a proper marketing engine but can't justify hiring a full in-house team
  • Best fit for the Campaign Blueprint — gives them a plan and assets to run with
Audience B

Bigger businesses wanting to scale or shift

  • Established, with budget, but stuck or plateaued
  • Wanting to scale, rebrand, or move more aggressively into digital
  • Have in-house people but need senior strategy and creative firepower
  • Best fit for the Full Engine — higher value, longer relationships
Because we're white-label, neither audience needs to be Bear-aligned or outdoors-adjacent. The filter is simple: do they have an audience and a budget, and a marketing gap we can fill?

The Offerings

Two products, deliberately different

Same engine behind both. The difference is how much of the running we own — and that difference sets the price and the risk.

Product 1 · Volume play

Campaign Blueprint

Lower cost · project or light retainer · scalable
  • Full campaign strategy, delivered as a clear document
  • All creatives built to support it — ready to ship
  • The client runs and manages execution themselves
  • Optional add-on: ongoing creative support from the editing team
Best for Audience A. Light on our ops, easy to deliver many in parallel, low brand risk.
Product 2 · Value play

Full Engine

Much higher cost · recurring · selective intake
  • Everything in the Blueprint, plus
  • We run the channels — paid, social, content, the calendar
  • We own execution end-to-end behind their brand
  • Only taken on for the right clients we can do justice to
Best for Audience B. Recurring revenue — the bit that compounds into a real business — but ops-heavy and carries brand risk, so it's priced and gated accordingly.
The strategic point: the Blueprint builds volume and proof; the Full Engine builds recurring revenue. Lead generation comes from the first, the long-term business value from the second. We need both, and we should be honest internally about which one we're really trying to grow.

The Motion

How a tech company would sell it — our version

This is the part that's genuinely new for Bear Witness: a deliberate, repeatable outbound motion rather than waiting for referrals.

01
Target

Build a list of growing businesses in the two audiences — by sector, size and obvious marketing gap.

02
Approach

Warm, consultative outreach — like a tech AE, not a cold spammer. Lead with a specific insight about their business.

03
Consult

A discovery call that diagnoses their gap and frames which product fits — Blueprint or Full Engine.

04
Onboard

Land them on the right tier with a clean onboarding, exactly like getting a customer live on a SaaS product.

05
Expand

Grow Blueprint clients up the ladder into retained and Full Engine relationships over time.

Reputation guardrail. The outreach is tailored and senior in tone — it should feel like a partner reaching out, never a volume sales blast. Anything that could read as cheap or pushy is off the table, because the engine carries the Bear Witness name even when the client work doesn't.

  • Only then, if it stacks up, propose the actual build and launch

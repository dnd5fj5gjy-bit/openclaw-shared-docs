The Opportunity

Demand isn't the constraint — deal flow is.

Bear Witness can already win and deliver — Alfred and MamaBucci are live. But every client has come inbound or through a warm relationship. Nothing goes out and creates the next ten.

Delivery is sorted: senior strategy from Jesse and the team, plus effectively unlimited make capacity in-house. The missing piece is the front end that turns that into a steady flow of paying clients.

Today

Inbound only

Clients arrive by referral or relationship. Good work, but the pipeline depends on luck and timing, not a system we control.

The Gap

No outbound motion

Nobody owns going out, finding the right businesses, and opening the conversation that brings them onto Bear Witness.

The Prize

A scalable agency

A predictable client engine plus recurring revenue is what turns Bear Witness from a project shop into a real, growing business.

The Unlock

Engine, not face — so brand alignment stops being a limit.

We do the strategy and creative, then hand off — the Bear Grylls name never goes on the front of a client's work. So we're not limited to brands that fit Bear or the outdoors world. Any growing business that needs a marketing engine is a fair target.

It also needs none of Bear's time, content or involvement. The engine runs invisibly behind the client's brand — growing revenue without ever touching what makes Bear Witness valuable.

The one thing we have to get right. Hand over strategy and creatives and the client runs their own channels — clean, low-risk. Run their pages and socials ourselves and we carry their brand voice in public, daily. That's where reputation risk and ops load sit — so it's gated to the premium tier: selective intake, higher price.
Who We Target

Two clear audiences.

Because we're white-label, neither audience needs to be Bear-aligned or outdoors-adjacent. Every business needs marketing — so the field is wide open. These are simply the two shapes of client worth going after.

The right businesses, well chosen
Audience A

Growing businesses, no agency yet

  • Small but scaling — past the founder-doing-everything stage
  • Not yet using an agency; marketing is ad-hoc or in one person's head
  • Need a proper marketing engine but can't justify a full in-house team
  • Best fit for the Campaign Blueprint — a plan and assets to run with
Audience B

Bigger businesses scaling or shifting

  • Established, with budget, but stuck or plateaued
  • Wanting to scale, rebrand, or move harder into digital
  • Have in-house people but need senior strategy and creative firepower
  • Best fit for the Full Engine — higher value, longer relationships
The Offerings

Two products, deliberately different.

Same engine behind both. The difference is how much of the running we own — and that sets the price and the risk.

Product 1 · Volume play

Campaign Blueprint

Lower cost · project or light retainer · scalable
  • ◇ Full campaign strategy, delivered as a clear document
  • ◇ All creatives built to support it — ready to ship
  • ◇ The client runs and manages execution themselves
  • ◇ Optional add-on: ongoing creative support from the editing team
Best for Audience A. Light on our ops, easy to deliver many in parallel, low brand risk.
Premium
Product 2 · Value play

Full Engine

Much higher cost · recurring · selective intake
  • ✓ Everything in the Blueprint, plus
  • ✓ We run the channels — paid, social, content, the calendar
  • ✓ We own execution end-to-end behind their brand
  • ✓ Only taken on for the right clients we can do justice to
Best for Audience B. Recurring revenue — the bit that compounds into a real business — but ops-heavy and carries brand risk, so it's priced and gated accordingly.
The strategic point: the Blueprint builds volume and proof; the Full Engine builds recurring revenue. Lead generation comes from the first, the long-term business value from the second. We need both — and should be honest internally about which one we're really trying to grow.

The Approach

The one thing we borrow from tech sales — nothing else.

The only parallel to tech sales is this: we proactively open a conversation with someone and aim to convert them into a paying client. That's it. Everything else is Bear Witness — quality-led, senior in tone, never a cheap blast.

No. 01

Target

Pinpoint exactly who to contact — sector, size, and a clear marketing gap. Considered, not scattergun.

No. 02

Approach

Cold outreach, but to a Bear Witness standard — specific, credible, worth their attention. At volume off the research.

No. 03

Converse

A real conversation that diagnoses their gap and frames which product fits — Blueprint or Full Engine.

No. 04

Convert

Land them on the right tier and hand to delivery cleanly.

No. 05

Grow

Move Blueprint clients up the ladder into retained and Full Engine relationships over time.

Standard, not spam. Volume comes from the depth of the targeting — knowing exactly who to contact and why — not from blasting. Every approach has to clear the Bear Witness quality bar, because the engine carries our name even when the client work doesn't.
The Role

My time, spent on growing the business.

This sits on top of my current marketing role — the consultation and the campaign and document building I already do. The added time goes into the company's growth: bringing new clients in, not just delivering for the ones we already have.

What the role involves

Building the growth engine

  • Build the client-growth strategy and the outreach programme
  • Identify and approach the right businesses — Audience A and B
  • Run the conversations that turn prospects into paying clients
  • Shape the two product offerings and the pricing ladder
  • Hand landed clients to the delivery team, and track the growth
On top of what I already do. This doesn't replace the marketing consultation and document building — it adds a growth lane alongside it. If it works, it brings Bear Witness a new, scalable revenue line without needing Bear's involvement or diluting the brand. If it doesn't, we'll have spent some time, not money.
The Ask

Make client growth part of my role.

The ask is simple: a green light to spend some of my time on the company's growth — building the client-growth strategy and starting to action it (reaching out to and calling prospective clients) — on top of the marketing consultation and document building I already do. Nothing gets built upfront; it's my time going into bringing new clients in.

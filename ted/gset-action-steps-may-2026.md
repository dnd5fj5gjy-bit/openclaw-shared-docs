## What Jesse's Strategy Doc Says (the parts that matter for execution)

- **Frame:** Not a series — a single-title marketing system. "Never sell the product. Educate people on the problems it solves."
- **Audience:** Five segments, all entered emotionally — curious non-believers, awkward evangelists, intimidated readers, drifted Christians, parents/youth leaders.
- **Channel mix:** YouTube (anchor), Substack (owned audience), Instagram (daily), TikTok/Reels (clip factory), church partnerships, Facebook Group, "Give One Away" gifting.
- **Cadence:** 3–5 posts/week steady state. Calendar anchors: Lent, Easter, Pentecost (24 May), anniversary (19 Jun), autumn church push, Advent/Christmas.
- **Hard constraint on Bear:** Will not film new content or do dedicated recording sessions. Two to three calendar-anchored posts/year on Bear's own channels — that's it.
- **KPI posture:** Flow metrics over vanity metrics. Track cross-platform movement (TikTok → YouTube → Substack → church groups). No revenue or sub-count targets stated.
- **Named owner in the doc:** Andy Lyon at Hodder. That ownership has now defaulted to BGV/Olly.
- **Verbatim principles to preserve:** "Content travels down, stories travel up." "Never cross-post. Adapt." "A camera, a reader, a quiet room." "Track the flow, not just the numbers." Five-voice casting (different performer per disciple).

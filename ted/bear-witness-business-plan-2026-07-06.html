Bear Witness · Internal Business Plan

The Business Plan for Bear Witness

A single, definitive account of what Bear Witness is, why it works, and what it needs to grow - built for one conversation with Jesse and Felix, not three separate decks.

Read the plan
01 · Executive summary

An AI-run marketing and agent consultancy, operating inside BGV, already proven on BGV's own brands.

Bear Witness is a small team - Jesse, Olly and Felix - that has spent the last several months running marketing and operations for GSET, Ted's Health and Modern Savage almost entirely on an internal AI stack. That work is not a pilot. It is live, ongoing, and already extended to one paying external client, Alfred. This plan sets out how to turn that capability into a real business with two distinct revenue streams, sold in the right order, without pretending either is further along than it is.

Stream 1

Agent Workforce

An installed product - named AI agents engineered into a client's own business, priced like headcount. Proven internally across BGV's operations, but needs new multi-tenant hosting and isolation infrastructure before it can be sold to clients outside BGV. Growth-limited today by Felix being the only person who can build one end to end.

Stream 2

AI-Integrated Agency

A managed service - Bear Witness runs a client's ongoing marketing function the same way it already runs GSET, Ted's Health and Modern Savage, sold as a monthly retainer. Needs no new build. This is the one that can be sold and delivered starting now.

Why now: the AI capability already exists and is already earning proof points inside BGV every week. The only thing missing is the decision to sell it properly, starting with the stream that needs no engineering work to open for business - Stream 2 - while Stream 1's infrastructure gets built in parallel.

02 · The problem

Agencies only grow by hiring. That is exactly what caps how big they can get.

Every marketing agency sells the same thing underneath: people's time. More clients means more staff, and every new hire eats into the margin the client's fee was supposed to protect. That is why agencies are expensive, why onboarding takes months, and why quality drops the moment an account team is stretched too thin across too many clients.

Bear Witness does not have that constraint. It already runs the exact same marketing work - strategy, content, paid media, reporting - for three live BGV brands, using an AI stack instead of an account team. Taking on another client does not mean hiring another person. It means engineering and oversight time, not payroll.

Growth without headcount
03 · The offer

Two streams. One built, one not. Sold in that order.

Both streams come from the same underlying AI capability, but they are different products, at different stages of readiness, sold to different buying motions. Confusing them is the single easiest way to slow both down.

Needs build

Stream 1 · Agent Workforce

Installed product · Single Agent / Team Agents / Full Workforce

Named AI agents engineered into a client's own business - a colleague with unlimited capacity, built for a specific role. Sold with a one-off build fee plus a monthly retainer, the same way a business would price a new hire.

  • Proven internally: four live named agents running Bear Witness's own team today
  • Needs new infrastructure before external sale: multi-tenant hosting, per-client isolation, credential vaulting, watchdog coverage
  • Growth-capped by Felix being the only person who can build a new install end to end, until that process is documented
No build needed

Stream 2 · AI-Integrated Agency

Managed service · Channel Management / Growth Retainer / Full Marketing Function

Bear Witness runs a client's ongoing marketing - strategy, content, paid media, reporting - the same way it already runs it for GSET, Ted's Health and Modern Savage. Sold as a straight monthly retainer, no engineering to stand up per client.

  • Already proven: this exact work runs today for three live BGV brands plus Alfred, externally
  • No new infrastructure required - the same AI stack, pointed at a new client's accounts
  • Not capped by Felix's build capacity - the constraint on Stream 1 does not apply here

How the Client Acquisition Engine fits in: a paid-ads-plus-AI-nurture funnel, initially pointed at filling Bear Witness's own pipeline rather than sold externally, feeds qualified leads into the Stream 2 tiers once client #1 has proven the model through warm channels. It is the mechanism that lets Stream 2 grow past the first client without depending on personal connections indefinitely.

04 · Market and target customer

Mid-size brands already spending on marketing, either in-house or through an agency.

The ideal customer profile for Stream 2 is a mid-size brand that already knows it needs ongoing marketing - it is currently either running a lean in-house function that cannot cover every channel, or paying a traditional agency retainer and getting standard agency turnaround and margins in return. These are businesses with an existing marketing budget line, not ones who need persuading that marketing matters. The pitch is a straight swap: the same or better output, without the headcount cost or the agency margin.

05 · Competitive landscape

Not competing with agencies on price alone, or with AI tools on features alone.

Two very different types of competitor sit either side of Bear Witness, and the position only makes sense set against both at once.

Traditional agencies

  • Grow by hiring - every new client needs more account team hours
  • 4-6 week, £10k-£15k full marketing/competitor audits before work even starts
  • 90-day ramp to a fully optimised campaign is the industry standard
  • Quality drops as account teams get stretched across more clients
  • Expensive relative to output, slow to onboard, hard to scale down if the relationship isn't working

AI marketing tool vendors

  • Sell software, not outcomes - the client still needs someone to run it
  • Point solutions: one tool for ad copy, another for reporting, another for scheduling
  • No accountability for the strategy or the result, only the tool working as specified
  • Client still carries the coordination cost of stitching multiple tools into one function
Bear Witness

Full function, AI-run

  • Runs the whole marketing function, not a single tool inside it
  • AI does the labour, so more clients costs engineering time, not payroll
  • No build fee for Stream 2 - the core managed-service offer needs no new engineering per client
  • Accountable for the output and the strategy, the way an agency is, at a fraction of the cost

The actual moat is cost-to-serve, not the tool set or the network. Because AI does the labour, Bear Witness can undercut a traditional agency's fee and still run a healthier margin, indefinitely, on any client. That is a structural advantage neither a headcount-scaling agency nor a point-solution vendor can copy without becoming a different kind of business themselves.

06 · Business model and pricing

Retainer-based, priced against the salary or agency fee it replaces. No build fee for Stream 2.

Stream 2 is sold as a monthly retainer across three tiers, scaling with how much of a client's marketing function Bear Witness takes on. Unlike Stream 1, there is no build fee - it is a service, not an installed product, so there is nothing to engineer and deploy on the client's side before billing starts.

07 · Go-to-market plan

Two different mechanisms for two different growth stages.

Client #1 and every client after it are not won the same way. Treating the bootstrap channel as a repeatable strategy is the mistake this plan explicitly avoids.

Stage one · Bootstrap, warm channels

Landing client #1

Hodder Faith and the wider GSET publishing network, Ted's Health white-label clinic prospect lists, Modern Savage supplier and retail contacts, and Alfred as a named, specific proof case to point every one of those networks at. Each name in this list gets used once - it is a fast, low-cost way to skip months of cold outreach while the repeatable channel is being built, not a source of client #2 or beyond.

Stage two · Scalable, repeatable

Client Acquisition Engine

Paid ads plus an AI nurture agent, feeding qualified leads directly into the Stream 2 tiers. This is the mechanism that has to carry growth from client #2 onward, because warm connections run out and cannot be manufactured to order. Prioritising this build early is the mitigation against the connections channel quietly becoming the permanent strategy.

The sequencing matters more than either channel alone: warm intros buy time and a proof case. The Acquisition Engine is what turns that proof case into a client base large enough to matter, without Bear Witness's growth rate being tied to who Jesse, Olly or Felix happen to know.

08 · Team and operations

Three people. AI does the labour, not more headcount.

Bear Witness runs on a deliberately small team, and the business model depends on that staying true.

Strategy & direction

Jesse

Team lead. Cross-cutting decisions on where Bear Witness spends its effort and which stream gets priority at any given stage.

Operations, sales, delivery

Olly

Runs client relationships, sales conversations and day-to-day delivery oversight across both streams - the AI-fluent operator turning capability into revenue.

Engineering

Felix

The one person who can build or configure almost any agent end to end. This is what makes "productise what we already run internally" the right call instead of building from scratch in a new vertical - but it is also the team's single sharpest constraint.

Why three people can service a growing client base: AI does the marketing labour for Stream 2 and the agent labour for Stream 1's clients once installed. Each new Stream 2 client costs engineering and oversight time, not a hire - that is the entire premise this plan rests on, and it is already proven true for the three internal BGV brands plus Alfred.

The one real operational constraint: Felix is currently the only person who can build a new Agent Workforce install end to end. That caps how fast Stream 1 can grow until that build process is documented into something a second person could run. Stream 2 does not carry this constraint - it needs no build, so it is not limited by Felix's hours in the same way, which is the main reason it is the stream to lead with.

09 · Financial outlook

Fixed AI costs amortise across a growing client base. Exact figures are in the pricing-model deck.

This section is deliberately qualitative. A dedicated pricing-model deck carries the full unit-economics breakdown - real cost-to-serve, UK salary and agency-fee benchmarks, and starting-price recommendations per tier - and this plan defers to it on exact numbers rather than inventing figures that would need correcting later.

Cost structure

Fixed AI/compute costs, amortising down

The core cost of running Bear Witness's AI stack does not scale 1:1 with client count the way payroll does. As more Stream 2 clients are added, the same fixed infrastructure and tooling spend is spread across a larger revenue base, improving the margin on every client after the first.

Marginal cost per client

Engineering and oversight time, not payroll

Taking on a new Stream 2 client costs Olly's onboarding and account-management time plus whatever engineering configuration is needed to point the AI stack at the client's own channels - not a new salary. That is the mechanism behind the "growth without headcount" claim made throughout this plan, not a separate assumption.

Directional anchor, not a forecast: a traditional agency's full marketing/competitor audit runs £10k-£15k and an SME retainer runs £1k-£5k a month - Stream 2 is priced to undercut those figures while running a materially better margin, because the labour behind the work is AI rather than an account team. The pricing-model deck turns this into real numbers once actual cost-to-serve is measured against the first paying client.

10 · Risks and mitigations

The honest list, not the flattering one.

Client concentration

One paying external client today

Risk

Alfred is currently the only proof case outside BGV. Losing that single relationship removes the entire external evidence base at once.

Mitigation

Prioritise landing a second external client through the warm bootstrap channels this quarter, and stand up the Client Acquisition Engine so future client count is not dependent on any one relationship.

Engineering single point of failure

Felix is the only Stream 1 builder

Risk

Every new Agent Workforce install currently depends on one person's hours and availability. Illness, burnout or a competing priority stalls Stream 1 entirely.

Mitigation

Document the build process into a repeatable playbook before selling Stream 1 externally at any volume - this is already flagged as the Phase one priority in Stream 1's own roadmap, and this plan does not shortcut it.

Margin risk

AI cost growing faster than modelled

Risk

The economics story depends on fixed AI/compute cost amortising down per client. If token usage per client grows faster than assumed - heavier accounts, more agents, more back-and-forth - the margin advantage narrows.

Mitigation

Meter usage per client from the first paying account onward, feed real cost-to-serve data into the pricing-model deck, and re-price tiers if actual usage diverges from the assumption before it compounds across a larger client base.

Connections-bootstrap trap

Warm intros becoming the permanent strategy

Risk

It is easy to keep leaning on Hodder Faith, Ted's Health and Modern Savage contacts past client #1 because it is the path of least resistance - at which point growth quietly caps at whoever the team happens to know.

Mitigation

Treat the bootstrap list as explicitly single-use per name, and prioritise building the Client Acquisition Engine now rather than after the warm channels run dry, so a repeatable channel is already live before it is needed.

12 · The ask

A decision to greenlight, not a request for capital.

Bear Witness does not need new money to start - it is bootstrapped off the AI stack the team already runs and pays for. What it needs from Jesse and Felix right now is a decision: greenlight leading with Stream 2 while Stream 1's infrastructure is built in parallel, back the Client Acquisition Engine build ahead of the warm channels running dry, and commit to documenting Felix's build process so Stream 1 is not permanently capped at one person. Everything else in this plan follows from that one decision.

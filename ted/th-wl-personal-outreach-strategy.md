**Owner:** Olly Westmacott

**Goal:** Onboard LegitScript-certified UK partners to Ted's Health White-Label, via direct CEO/MD-level relationships.

---

## 1. Why personal, why CEO/MD

White-label partnerships are commercial, brand-linked decisions. They aren't bought by a procurement lead — they're decided by the founder, CEO, or MD. So we skip the contact form and go to the decision-maker on day one. Two reasons:

- **Speed.** One conversation with the right person beats four weeks of being routed through partnerships inboxes.
- **Signal.** A founder-to-business-lead approach signals we're serious and that this is a strategic partnership, not a vendor pitch.

## 3. Find the decision-maker (10 min per partner)

For each partner, find one named human on LinkedIn:

- Job titles to target, in order: **Founder → CEO → Managing Director → Commercial Director → Head of Partnerships.**
- Pharmacy-side targets are often *Superintendent Pharmacist* — they hold the regulated authority but aren't always the commercial decision-maker. Take them only if no founder/CEO is findable.
- Capture: name, exact job title, LinkedIn URL, company email if discoverable.

Log each one in the dashboard's **Contact Name / Job Title / Email** fields before sending. If a partner has no findable decision-maker after 10 minutes of looking, park it and move on — don't burn a day on someone unreachable.

---

## 4. Channel — LinkedIn first, email second

**Primary: LinkedIn direct message** to the named decision-maker.

- Higher open and reply rates than cold email at this seniority.
- Lets them check you out (BGV, Ted's Health, Bear) before replying — which is the point.
- Send a connection request with a one-line note. If they accept, follow with the full message. If they don't accept in 5 days, send the message as an InMail OR move to email.

**Secondary: direct email** when LinkedIn is unavailable or unanswered.

- Use a clear subject line, no clickbait.
- Send from your BGV address. Don't BCC anyone or send via a tool — this is hand-typed feel.

## 5. Message template — three touches, two weeks, then stop

### Touch 1 — Opening (LinkedIn or email)

> Hi [first name],
>
> [Their company] keeps coming up as one of the few UK [pharmacy/men's health] businesses doing this properly — LegitScript-certified, good range, strong reputation.
>
> I lead market & strategy at Bear Grylls Ventures. We've built **Ted's Health** — Bear's men's health platform — and we're launching a white-label programme so a small number of partners can offer the Ted's Health experience under their own brand, with our clinical and supply infrastructure behind it.
>
> Worth a 20-min call to see if it's a fit? I'd rather come straight to you than route through partnerships.
>
> Olly

### Touch 2 — Follow-up (7 days later, only if no reply)

> Hi [first name] — circling back on the note above.
>
> Quick angle: we're picking the white-label launch partners now, and [Their company] is on a short list of 10. Happy to send a one-pager if it's easier than a call.
>
> Olly

### Touch 3 — Final (14 days after touch 1)

> [first name] — last one from me. If white-label isn't a fit for [Their company] right now no problem, just say and I'll close the loop. If it might be and the timing's off, tell me when to come back.
>
> Olly

After touch 3 with no reply: status → **No response**. Don't re-pester for at least 90 days.

## 6. Cadence

- **Per week:** 5 new partners contacted (touch 1). Don't blast all 10 in one day — staggers the follow-ups and keeps replies manageable.
- **Per partner:** 3 touches max, spaced 7 days apart, over 14 days.
- **Wave 1 completes** in roughly 4 weeks (open, follow, close).

## 8. What gets a "yes" — be ready

Three things you'll be asked on the first call. Have answers ready:

1. **What is white-label, exactly?** Their brand, your front-end. Ted's Health clinical infrastructure, supply, fulfilment. They get the patient relationship and the margin on top.
2. **Commercials.** Have a one-line on revenue share / fee structure before the first call. Don't wing it.
3. **Time to live.** How long from signature to a partner running on TH-WL. Be honest — Calvin / engineering can give a real number.

If you don't have firm answers on 2 and 3 yet, get them from Calvin before week 1 starts.

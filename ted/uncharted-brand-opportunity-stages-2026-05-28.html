00The idea

We can start selling the opportunity before a single frame is shot. The storylines exist on paper. Bear Grylls and Brave Wilderness already have the audience. That means we can build hype now, sell that hype to brand partners now, and have it all land together when the expedition delivers.

The franchise monetises in three connected stages across its lifecycle. Pre-show hype builds and sells the anticipation before production. Broadcast turns that into in-show sponsorship across two distribution channels. Post-broadcast digital compounds it on YouTube and across the wider digital ecosystem. Each stage opens its own brand-partnership opportunities, and each one makes the next easier to sell.

Why this works

Uncharted Treasures' own channel has no audience or performance data yet — on its own it's hard to monetise early, because partners price digital on proven views. The unlock is piggybacking the reach we already have: Brave Wilderness' established audience and Bear Grylls' short-form distribution carry the hype while the franchise builds its own following underneath it.

So we don't wait for the show to exist before we earn. We sell the hype — in the hope, and with the storylines, that it all blows up at the same time.

01Two types of sponsor

Brand opportunities across all three stages fall into two categories. Knowing which a partner is shapes what we offer them and how we price it.

Type A

Conversion sponsors

Transactional. They want measurable response — clicks, codes, sales. Best served with integrated, performance-led placements: dedicated segments, trackable offers, direct calls to action woven into the content.

Type B

Mention / brand sponsors

Less transactional. They want association, awareness and credibility from the franchise. Served with brand mentions, presence and alignment — named partner of the expedition, logo and reference placements, halo from the adventure.

How the ad work actually runs

Worth setting partner expectations early: the established model is that a script gets approved, then the talent produces the ad themselves in their own direction — rather than the brand handing down a finished blueprint. Budget roughly a day to two days of time per ad, end to end (script, production, edit). That creative autonomy is part of why the integrations feel authentic and perform.

02Stage 1 — Pre-show hype

1 Pre-hype Before & during production

Generate revenue and excitement before the show exists. We write a creative hook that's relevant to the show but adjacent to it, run a hype series across the audiences we already have, and sell brand partnerships against that anticipation — all while building the content library we'll need later.

How it works
A "hype series" distributed on borrowed reach

An adjacent-to-the-show content series — built from the storylines we already have on paper — distributed across Brave Wilderness and Bear Grylls short-form channels. It gets people excited about the potential and warms up the audience before any expedition footage exists.

Brand oppEarly-bird sponsors back the hype series itself — getting in before the show at a lower entry price, in exchange for being there at the ground floor.

Sell the anticipation in advance

Get partners excited and buying in before we go into production. We're not selling proven views yet — we're selling the storylines, the talent, and a credible path to a moment that all lands at once.

Brand oppFounding-partner / pre-launch packages — discounted, multi-stage commitments locked in before the audience (and the price) climbs.

A funded BTS team building sellable content

A behind-the-scenes team and budget runs in tandem with production, so we're constantly generating content we can sell — not just the show itself, but the making of it. More content means more inventory, which means more brand-sponsor opportunities.

Brand oppBTS and "road to the expedition" content offers a second, parallel sponsorship stream alongside the main show.

03Stage 2 — During broadcast

2 Broadcast When the show airs

When the show goes out, the pre-sold anticipation converts into in-show sponsorship. The key advantage here is control — because we hold two distribution channels, we can guarantee a sponsor their delivery one way or another.

How it works
In-show sponsorship & promotion

Partner promotion is built into the show itself — integrations, placements and named-partner moments inside the broadcast. This is the premium, headline inventory the pre-hype stage was selling toward.

Brand oppHeadline / presenting partner of the broadcast — the most visible, highest-value slot in the model.

Two channels = a sponsor guarantee

We control sponsorship delivery because we have two places to put it: the TV show and our YouTube series channel. If a placement doesn't hit a sponsor's quota on one, we can place it on the other. That makes the offer to a brand far more dependable than a single-channel buy.

Brand oppCross-channel packages with a guaranteed-delivery promise — TV reach plus a digital make-good, sold as one.

04Stage 3 — Post & during-broadcast digital

3 Digital On YouTube & digital platforms

The show's life on YouTube and digital is where the franchise compounds. The audience built through the first two stages becomes the owned asset — and the model widens by bringing other creators under the umbrella to multiply what's there to sell.

How it works
YouTube monetisation — built on borrowed reach first

A monetising YouTube channel takes roughly nine months, often longer, to build. Because the Uncharted channel starts with no audience or performance data, early ad deals tend to be flat-rate against expected views — so we piggyback Brave Wilderness to accelerate past the cold-start and reach meaningful monetisation faster.

Brand oppAlways-on digital integrations and ad-share partnerships that grow in value as the channel's proven numbers climb.

A sub-vertical of creators under one umbrella

Bring multiple other influencers in underneath the franchise umbrella — an adventure / discovery sub-vertical that shares the brand. More creators means more content, more reach and more inventory, which maximises total monetisation across the network.

Brand oppNetwork-level deals — a brand sponsors across several creators at once, with the franchise as the trusted umbrella.

05How the three connect

The stages aren't separate plays — they're one continuous build. Each one creates the audience, the content and the proof that makes the next stage worth more to a brand partner.

Pre-hype
Borrowed reach builds anticipation; sell early founding partners
Broadcast
Anticipation converts to in-show sponsorship across two channels
Digital
The show's audience compounds on YouTube; the umbrella widens
Back to the top
Proven numbers reset the pre-hype price higher for the next cycle
The thread that runs through all three

Every stage is designed to be sold against before it's proven — hype before production, in-show before the audience exists, digital before the channel monetises — by borrowing the reach we already have in Bear Grylls and Brave Wilderness. The franchise's own audience builds underneath, and once it's proven, the whole thing reprices upward.

The Idea

Sell the hype before a single frame is shot.

The storylines exist on paper. Bear Grylls and Brave Wilderness already have the audience. So we build hype now, sell it to brand partners now, and have it all land together when the expedition delivers.

Monetisation runs in three connected stages. Pre-show hype sells the anticipation before production. Broadcast turns that into in-show sponsorship across two channels. Digital compounds it on YouTube. Each stage opens its own brand opportunities.

Why this works

The Uncharted channel has no audience or data yet, so it's hard to monetise alone — partners price digital on proven views. The unlock is piggybacking reach we already have: Brave Wilderness and Bear Grylls' short-form distribution carry the hype while the franchise builds its own following underneath.

The Bahamas · Where the story lives
Two Types of Sponsor

Know which one a partner is.

Every brand opportunity falls into one of two types. Which one a partner is shapes what we offer and how we price it.

Type A

Conversion sponsors

Transactional. They want measurable response.

Clicks, codes, sales. Best served with integrated, performance-led placements: dedicated segments, trackable offers, direct calls to action in the content.

Type B

Mention / brand sponsors

Less transactional. They want association.

Awareness and credibility from the franchise. Served with brand mentions, presence and alignment — named expedition partner, logo placements, halo from the adventure.

How the ad work actually runs

Set expectations early: the talent gets a script approved, then produces the ad themselves in their own direction — not to a brand's finished blueprint. Budget one to two days per ad, end to end (script, production, edit). That autonomy is why the integrations feel authentic and perform.

Stage One

Pre-show hype

Before & during production

Generate revenue and excitement before production starts. A creative hook adjacent to the show runs as a hype series on the audiences we already have, and we sell partnerships against that anticipation while building the content library for later.

A "hype series" on borrowed reach

A content series adjacent to the show, built from the storylines on paper, distributed across Brave Wilderness and Bear Grylls short-form. It warms up the audience before any expedition footage exists.

Brand oppEarly-bird sponsors back the hype series — in before the show, at a lower entry price, for being there at the ground floor.

Sell the anticipation in advance

Get partners buying in before production. We're not selling proven views — we're selling the storylines, the talent, and a credible path to a moment that lands at once.

Brand oppFounding-partner packages — discounted, multi-stage commitments locked in before the audience (and the price) climbs.

A funded BTS team building sellable content

A behind-the-scenes team runs in tandem with production, generating content we can sell — not just the show, but the making of it. More content means more inventory, means more sponsor slots.

Brand oppBTS and "road to the expedition" content is a second, parallel sponsorship stream alongside the main show.
2
Stage Two

During broadcast

When the show airs

When the show airs, pre-sold anticipation converts into in-show sponsorship. The advantage is control: we hold two distribution channels, so we can guarantee a sponsor's delivery either way.

In-show sponsorship & promotion

Partner promotion built into the show — integrations, placements and named-partner moments inside the broadcast. The premium, headline inventory the pre-hype stage was selling toward.

Brand oppHeadline / presenting partner — the most visible, highest-value slot in the model.

Two channels = a sponsor guarantee

Two places to put a placement: the TV show and our YouTube series channel. Miss a sponsor's quota on one, make it good on the other — far more dependable than a single-channel buy.

Brand oppCross-channel packages with a guaranteed-delivery promise — TV reach plus a digital make-good, sold as one.

Unseen footage = a parallel channel

The shoot is filmed far beyond what airs — unseen behind-the-scenes footage captured throughout production. Every piece of content we film is another route to sell: cut-downs, extras, the making-of — all sellable inventory on top of the broadcast.

Brand oppA standalone BTS / making-of partnership — a sponsor backs the unseen footage as its own channel, separate from the in-show slot.
3
Stage Three

Post & during-broadcast digital

On YouTube & digital platforms

YouTube and digital is where the franchise compounds. The audience built across stages 1 and 2 becomes an owned asset — and bringing other creators under the umbrella multiplies what there is to sell.

YouTube monetisation — borrowed reach first

A monetising YouTube channel takes roughly nine months, often longer. With no audience or data yet, early ad deals are flat-rate against expected views — so we piggyback Brave Wilderness to clear the cold-start and monetise faster.

Brand oppAlways-on digital integrations and ad-share partnerships that grow in value as the channel's proven numbers climb.

A sub-vertical of creators under one umbrella

Bring other influencers under the franchise umbrella — an adventure / discovery sub-vertical sharing the brand. More creators, more content, more inventory — more total monetisation across the network.

Brand oppNetwork-level deals — a brand sponsors across several creators at once, with the franchise as the trusted umbrella.

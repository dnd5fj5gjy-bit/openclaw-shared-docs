01The picture in one paragraph

Uncharted Treasures is a franchise, not a show. A real-permit archaeological expedition in Long Island, Bahamas — birthplace of Caribbean piracy. Coyote Peterson hosts. Bart Michelini is the expert. Bear Grylls is Executive Producer. Bear Witness Media owns the IP, the production, the distribution, and everything after it airs.

It runs as year-round Coyote-hosted digital treasure hunts on YouTube that build audience all year, culminating each year in one flagship broadcast expedition — the 8-episode TV season. That season is the prestige anchor. The owned ecosystem (channel, social, community, list) is the asset that endures.

02The model — Model B, locked

Olly settled this on the 22 May Coyote call: it's a combination, B-led. Coyote hosts year-round smaller-scale digital hunts (builds audience/hype) → these build up to the FINALE = one big broadcast TV season per year (the flagship). Plus the repurposing engine: every piece — both the smaller hunts AND the broadcast episodes — turned into weeks of content across every platform, best material run as Meta ads, all fed back into the YouTube channel.

06The 4 phases — promotion arc

16-week promotion window — T–12 weeks through +8 weeks post-premiere.

Phase I · T–12 to T–6

Tease

Establish legitimacy

Expert community outreach (archaeologists, maritime historians validate publicly). Coyote introduces himself to the show audience via BTS. Permit announcement = hero hook. Bear's EP credit on all materials. Earned media pitching to adventure/history podcasts.

Phase II · T–6 to T–1

Build

Convert awareness to belief

Character intro films (Coyote, Bart, dive team). Audience-specific cutdowns (Family / Coyote / Bear variants). Paid social via Meta + YouTube pre-roll. Podcast circuit — Coyote and Bart long-form in trusted environments. Tension-building: raw footage, teaser clips, unanswered questions.

Phase III · Launch week

Premiere

Make it unavoidable

Hero campaign film on owned and earned. Headline trailer (90-sec for TV/pre-roll). Connected TV buys final 2 weeks. Bear's social posts (if schedule allows). Live social reactivity. Press coverage maximised around air date.

Phase IV · Weeks 2-8

Sustain

Retention & ecosystem

Weekly repurposing: recap cuts (YouTube), Reels (IG), TikTok clips, podcast unpacking. BTS vlogs (Coyote-fronted, one per episode). Always-on social: 4 pillars × min 4 posts/week. Meta/YouTube shifts to binge messaging + retention.

11Q&A bank — anticipated questions

What Coyote (or anyone else) is most likely to ask, with the answer you give.

How much posting/time is required from Coyote?

Hosting digital hunts year-round, episode-fronted content, podcast circuit at launch. His reach launches it; the loyal audience build, paid media, and Bear Witness portfolio carry it. Hosting accelerates rather than sustains the franchise alone.

What's Bear's actual involvement?

Executive Producer credit (opens broadcaster doors at Disney, Nat Geo, Amazon, Apple) plus 8.5M-follower launch amplification via his existing channels. No new filming required from Bear — repurposes of existing assets only.

What does the network pitch look like?

We don't go in with a concept. We go in with an engaged year-round audience showing proven loyalty. Networks are buying de-risked demand, not an unproven idea.

Why will this sustain when other treasure shows haven't?

Three reasons: (1) credibility — real permits, real archaeology team, real finds, not manufactured drama; (2) year-round engagement vs. one-off broadcast format; (3) owned ecosystem (YouTube channel, social, community, email) keeps audience between seasons.

Are other adventurers / creators getting involved?

Right now we're building this brand with Coyote hosting. Long-term creator strategies developing — but unfunded presently, so a light "in time" framing, not the spine.

What's the timeline?

Open. Flagged for post-call resolution. Don't commit to specifics in the room.

Whose channel do the digital hunts live on?

Suggested: build the Uncharted channel as franchise home + cross-promote on Coyote's. His channel is his asset — flag as his call. Open decision.

What's the commercial / what's in it for me?

Defer. "Core partner not hired host." Steer off commercials in the vision call. Park for a separate conversation.

Where's the show being filmed?

Long Island, Bahamas. Birthplace of Caribbean piracy. Real archaeological sites, real permits, two centuries untouched.

Why Bahamas piracy specifically?

Long Island is where Caribbean piracy was born — culturally resonant, untold story. Stands independent of production quality. Bart Michelini (our expert) anchors the historical credibility.

What if the season finds nothing big?

"Whatever we found = more questions." That's Ep 8 by design. Series finale plants the season-two conversation. Open-ended narrative is the franchise's friend.

12Diplomacy — never say to Coyote

Red lines

Internal-only framings. Do NOT say in the room.

  • "The host seat is swappable" — true internally, never out loud. He's the founding face.
  • "We don't need you" — even gently. Always frame Bear as amplifier, Coyote as core partner.
  • Any commercials / money specifics — defer. "Core partner not hired host." Park for a separate conversation.
  • "Audience transfers to the IP" — true, but phrased to Coyote sounds like a downgrade. Phrase as "the audience grows with both you and the franchise."
  • The umbrella / other-creators idea as a roadmap — Olly de-emphasised this. Mention only lightly as "strategy in development" if pressed; can't pay other creators yet so don't promise.

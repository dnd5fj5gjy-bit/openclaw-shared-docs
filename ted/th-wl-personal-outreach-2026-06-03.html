4 · Discovery Call

10-minute structure (block 30 in the diary, keep 20 in reserve)

The call's one job is to qualify whether this is real and earn a second meeting with the right people on it. Don't pitch the commercials in the first call — pitch the fit, then arrange Calvin.

0:00–1:00
Open

Thank them, frame the call, set the time.

"Thanks for the 30 minutes — I'll keep us to ten and leave the rest as buffer. What I'd like to do: a few questions on your side first, then I'll walk through how the white-label actually works, and we'll agree the right next step. Sound good?"

1:00–4:00
Qualify — 4 questions, listen hard
  • What does the men's-health side of your business look like today — anything live, anything planned?
  • Roughly what share of your audience is male, and what age band?
  • If a member or patient asked you tomorrow about TRT or ED, where do they go?
  • What would have to be true for you to put a men's-health clinic behind your brand in the next 6–12 months?

You're listening for: (a) demand they already see, (b) the gap they admit to, (c) whether they have authority or whether you need to talk to someone else.

4:00–7:00
The pivot — what the WL actually is

Three sentences, no slides yet:

"Ted's Health is CQC-registered with GMC doctors, MHRA-compliant pharmacy, and a live prescribing pathway for TRT, ED and hormone optimisation. The white-label is exactly that — your brand on the front, our clinical engine underneath. The patient only ever sees your brand; we handle clinicians, scripts, pharmacy and repeats."

Then connect it to what they just told you. Use their language back at them. "You said [X] — that's the gap white-label fills, and here's what that looks like in practice…"

7:00–9:00
Handle the obvious objections live

One or two will come up in every call — handle them in the room with the objection map below. Don't dodge commercials if they ask: give the rev-share floor, say full numbers are Calvin's call on the next session.

9:00–10:00
Close — book the second meeting

One ask, specific.

"Based on what you've said, I think the right next step is a 45-minute commercial session with Calvin Lefferts who runs the white-label side — he'll walk through partnership terms, time-to-live and the integration. I can hold three slots next week and email them this afternoon. Are you the right person for that call, or should I include someone else?"

Get the second meeting in the diary on the call. If they need internal sign-off, agree the date you'll have an answer.

What you do NOT do on the discovery call: walk through a deck, quote firm commercials, promise integration timelines, name other partners. All of those belong on the Calvin call.

5 · Objection Map

The eight you'll hear — crisp answers

Read these out loud once before Monday. Each answer is one or two sentences — say it, then ask a follow-up so the call doesn't stall on the objection.

ObjectionAnswer
"We already prescribe." Most of our partners do too, in some categories. The white-label fills the gap on the men's-health pathway specifically — TRT, hormone optimisation, ED — where the clinical protocols and ongoing management are different from general prescribing. Where's that pathway today for you?
"Compliance / regulatory risk." We carry the regulatory load — CQC registration, GMC doctors, MHRA-compliant pharmacy, all in our name. The patient sees your brand; the clinical responsibility sits with us. We can share the regulatory pack on the Calvin call.
"Will my patients still trust me?" The patient relationship stays with you — comms, retention, brand. We're invisible to them; they see your clinical pathway. We hold the medical responsibility; you hold the relationship.
"What about my brand?" Your brand on every screen — intake, consultation interface, prescription dispatch, repeat reminders. We provide the engine; the wrapper is yours. We can mock a [Company]-branded demo storefront for the Calvin call so you can see exactly what it'd look like.
"The rev share is too low." Worth doing the maths on what it replaces. You're not running a clinic — no clinicians on payroll, no pharmacy licence, no regulatory overhead, no clinical PI. Recurring rev on patients you'd otherwise lose to D2C. Calvin will walk through the numbers properly on the next call.
"What's the integration lift?" Lighter than people assume. We've designed it so partners can start as a referral / co-branded path and graduate to full white-label without re-platforming. Calvin will give an honest time-to-live for [Company]'s setup specifically.
"Why Ted's Health vs Zava / Phlo / building it ourselves?" Zava and Phlo are general telehealth WLs — we're a specialist men's-health clinic, which is the harder and higher-margin piece. Building in-house is 12 months and a clinical team before patient one. We're a clinical engine you can be live on inside [time-to-live].
"Send us a deck / put it in writing." Happy to — I'll send the partner one-pager today. The next-step ask is the same: a 45-minute Calvin call so we can talk specifics for [Company]. Should I hold something next week while you read?

7 · Olly → Calvin Handover

The exact moment a conversation graduates

Olly owns the front of the funnel and the relationship; Calvin owns the commercial close. The handover is its own checkpoint, not a default. A premature handover wastes Calvin's time and signals the wrong urgency to the partner.

All three must be true

Qualification criteria — only hand over when

1 · Compliance fit confirmed. Partner is LegitScript-certified, regulated equivalent, or has the route to compliance (e.g. cannabis clinic with existing GMC prescribing). Not a vague "we'll sort it."

2 · Genuine commercial intent. Partner has said, on the discovery call, that this is a real consideration for the next 6–12 months and they want to see numbers. Not "interesting, let's stay in touch."

3 · Named decision-maker confirmed. Either you're already speaking with the founder/CEO/MD, or they've named the person who decides and committed to bringing them on the Calvin call.

What Olly keeps doing after handover
  1. Stay in copy through the Calvin call — the partner expects continuity, and Calvin may need real-time context only Olly has.
  2. Own the relationship until HoTs are signed. Calvin owns the commercial doc; Olly owns the partner.
  3. Full handover at signature. Once heads of terms are signed, Calvin / ops take it from there. Olly transitions to a relationship-only touch point.

Go deep on one or two verticals before expanding — same pitch, tighter message, faster conversion.

Go first

Premium Gyms & Fitness Clubs

Members are already health-invested and male-skewing. Clinical services are a natural addition. Third Space, Fitness Worx, Athlete Lab are leads today.

Why now: Fastest conversion — they have the clients, need the product.
Go first

Private GP Clinics (Independent)

Already seeing male patients who need TRT. They test and refer out — losing revenue. Owner-operated = fast decisions. Harley Street cluster is a half-day visit.

Why now: Highest clinical fit. One visit can convert multiple practices.
Month 2

Blood Testing Networks

Goodbody Clinic (40+ UK locations) tests for testosterone but has no treatment pathway. The pitch is literally "close the loop on your existing results."

Why then: B2B sales cycle, need proof point from Phase 1 first.
Month 2

Pharmacies (Independent)

Pivoting hard into clinical services post-pharmacy-first. TRT is a high-margin, recurring service. Manchester Pharmacy already doing it manually.

Why then: Independent pharmacies move faster than chains; use them as a template for larger groups later.
Month 3+

Corporate Wellness

Huge volume potential. FTSE 250, engineering firms, law firms. Access via employee benefits brokers. Longer sales cycle but high ACV.

Why then: Sales cycle is longer. Set up the channel while clinic deals close.
Month 3+

Sports Clubs

Championship rugby and football clubs. Performance and recovery framing. Bear's credibility unlocks doors here that are closed to competitors.

Why then: Decision-making is complex (club doctor + board). Good for PR, not quick wins.
Later

Fertility Clinics

Male factor infertility is 50% of cases. IVF clinics have no men's endocrine offering. Most under-served vertical — but niche and slower to move.

Why later: Powerful pitch, but clinical procurement cycles are long.
Later

Military & Veterans

Bear's ex-SAS background makes this almost frictionless. SSAFA, Help for Heroes. Long sales cycle but high PR and brand value.

Why later: High brand value, low volume. Good Phase 3 play once the commercial model is proven.

Weekly Rhythm

~3 hours of focused WL work per week. Fits alongside the rest of your role.

Mon
Pipeline review (15 min). Check where every active lead is. Move anything stale. Decide who gets outreach this week. 15 min
Tue
Outreach block (45 min). 2–3 new contacts reached. Email or LinkedIn DM. Use the templates (see Outreach section). One personalised line per message. 45 min
Wed/Thu
Meetings / visits. All in-person pitches and calls booked here. Use the booklet. Take notes immediately after — Ted logs them. as booked
Fri
Follow-ups + content (30 min). Follow up any meetings from this week. Post on TH LinkedIn if it's a content week. Log everything to pipeline. 30 min
Total: ~1.5–2 hours of desk work per week plus meeting time. The rhythm prevents the most common mistake in B2B sales: inconsistent outreach — a burst of activity, then silence while you wait for replies, then starting from zero again. Consistent beats intense.
04

Pipeline Stages

Every lead lives in one of five stages. Move things forward — if it's stuck, it's dead.

1. Target
Identified, not contacted Research done. Right person found. Not yet reached out.
2. Contacted
First touch made Email / DM / walk-in sent. Awaiting response. Follow up after 3 days if no reply.
3. Meeting
Call or visit booked Meeting confirmed. Prepare pitch, bring booklet, know their business.
4. Proposal
Terms shared Partner has shown interest. Specific proposal / pricing sent. Chase weekly.
5. Signed
Partner onboarded Agreement signed. Onboarding started. Ask for a referral.
Pipeline health rule:

At any given time you want: 5+ in Stage 1, 3+ in Stages 2–3, 1+ in Stage 4. If Stage 2–3 is empty, you've stopped outreaching. If Stage 4 has been sitting for 3+ weeks with no movement, it's probably dead — move on.

05

Outreach Playbook

The sequence to take a target from first touch to meeting booked. Works for email and LinkedIn.

1
Day 1 — First Touch
Short, direct intro. One line that shows you know their business.
"Hi [Name] — I run the white-label programme at Ted's Health (part of Bear Grylls Ventures). I noticed [specific thing — e.g. "you're already doing blood testing for members" / "your Well Man package is excellent but TRT management seems to go elsewhere"]. We built a fully managed men's hormone health service that clinics and wellness businesses white-label under their own brand — no clinical build, just revenue. Worth a 20-minute call?"
2
Day 4 — First Follow-Up (if no reply)
Shorter. Bump the thread. Add one piece of value.
"Just bumping this up — happy to send over a one-pager if easier. The short version: you keep the client relationship, we handle the clinical ops, you earn on every patient. No set-up cost."
3
Day 10 — Second Follow-Up
Final attempt. Assume they're busy, not disinterested. Give them an easy out.
"Last nudge on this — if it's not the right time or not a fit, no problem at all. If the idea of a white-label men's health programme is interesting at any point, drop me a line. Happy to send the info across regardless."
4
Meeting — In Person or Call
Lead with their problem, not your product. Then present Ted's Health as the answer.
Open: "What does your current process look like when a male patient flags low testosterone or asks about TRT?" → Let them talk → Present Ted's Health as what closes that gap → Leave the booklet → Agree a clear next step before you leave.
5
48h Post-Meeting — Follow-Up
Thank you + specific proposal or next step. No vague "let me know."
"Great to meet you / speak today. As discussed, I'm sending over [the one-pager / a draft proposal / pricing options]. Happy to get on a call this week to go through it — does [day] work?"
The in-person advantage: Walking into a clinic or gym unannounced, being friendly and leaving the booklet, is often more effective than email — especially for small independents where the decision-maker is on-site. Don't underestimate it. Bring the booklet. Be brief. Ask for 10 minutes.

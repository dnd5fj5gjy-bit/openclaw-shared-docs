Brief 03 of 04

The Five Minutes Before the Day Starts

Benchmark: Reference video 3 (15.7s) — a macro hand/product close-up (fingers holding a capsule against warm light), a short punchy line, a category statement, then a wordmark resolve with "start your journey" CTA. Benchmarked for its intimate macro framing and its trick of downplaying a claim ("it's kind of a small deal") before landing the real one.

Hook (0-3s)

Macro shot, warm backlight: a hand stirring a spoon through water/milk in a glass, condensation catching the light. Text: "It's kind of a small habit."

Visual style / mood

Intimate, tactile, golden-hour backlight. Everything shot close (hands, glass, steam/condensation) rather than wide lifestyle. Feels like a private morning ritual, not a gym ad.

Beat-by-beat structure

TimeBeat
0-3sMacro: hand stirring a scoop of powder into liquid, backlit. Text: "It's kind of a small habit."
3-7sCut to a close, slower shot of the drink being lifted, light catching the glass. Text builds: "It's kind of a small habit that changes how the whole day goes."
7-11sLarge type card, no visual distraction, cream background: "Whole-food nutrition. Built for men."
11-15sWordmark resolves centre-frame on Strength teal background. CTA text beneath.

On-screen text / copy

"It's kind of a small habit." → "...that changes how the whole day goes." → "Whole-food nutrition. Built for men." → "ted's HEALTH — Start the day differently."

Voiceover / caption script draft

VO: It's kind of a small habit.
VO: One that changes how the whole day goes.
VO: Whole-food nutrition, built for men.

Visual / stock footage direction

Premium morning-routine stock: hands, glass, steam, natural window light. No branded packaging visible, use a plain glass or neutral shaker, never a labelled product. Skin tones and setting should read as approachable, not aspirational-model, in line with the brand's "friend not medical" characteristic.

Music / pacing note

Soft, warm acoustic guitar or minimal piano, slow tempo throughout (this is the calmest of the four briefs). One tempo lift at the wordmark resolve. 4 beats over 15 seconds, mirroring the reference's slow build to a short, punchy payoff line.

CTA

"Start the habit. [PRICE — TBC] — ted'shealth.com"

Why this works: it reuses the reference's "small claim, bigger payoff" copy structure and intimate macro framing, but roots the emotional driver in daily ritual and vitality rather than treatment.

Brief 04 of 04

The Numbers Men Don't Talk About

Benchmark: Reference video 4 (11.0s) — a swipeable stat-card carousel (World Obesity Day awareness post): bold data points in clean cards over a soft gradient, one message per card, ending on a brand card. Benchmarked for its data-led credibility structure and its confidence in letting numbers carry the emotion.

Hook (0-3s)

Soft teal-to-cream gradient background, small pill label "MEN'S HEALTH DATA". Bold statement fades in: "Most men have no idea what's actually in their diet."

Visual style / mood

Card-based, editorial, data-forward. White stat cards with rounded corners (matching Ted's Health's 16-24px corner radius system) float over the gradient background. Feels credible and slightly clinical without losing warmth, closest of the four to the brand's "expert-led" side.

Beat-by-beat structure

TimeBeat
0-2sPill label + hook line over gradient background: "Most men have no idea what's actually in their diet."
2-5sStat card 1 (white card, teal accent icon): "Only 1 in 3 UK men hit their daily protein target."
5-8sStat card 2: "Men are 40% less likely than women to track what they eat."
8-9.5sStat card 3, shorter beat: "Most 'healthy' snacks are still ultra-processed."
9.5-11sBrand card: wordmark, tagline, CTA. "ted's HEALTH — Maximising Men's Potential."

On-screen text / copy

"Most men have no idea what's actually in their diet." → "Only 1 in 3 UK men hit their daily protein target." → "Men are 40% less likely than women to track what they eat." → "Most 'healthy' snacks are still ultra-processed." → "ted's HEALTH — Maximising Men's Potential."

Note: stats above are placeholders for the script draft. Final data points need sourcing and verification before this brief goes into production.

Voiceover / caption script draft

VO: Most men have no idea what's actually in their diet.
VO: Only 1 in 3 hit their daily protein target.
VO: And most "healthy" snacks are still ultra-processed.
VO: Ted's Health. Built to close that gap.

Visual / stock footage direction

No lifestyle footage needed, this is a graphics-first format. Background: soft Momentum-gradient (teal into white/cream) as a static or very slow-moving plate. Stat cards use the brand's rounded-corner system, Strength teal for numbers, Balance near-black for supporting text, small line-icon accents (heart rate, protein, packaging) in teal.

Music / pacing note

Fast, clipped pacing versus the other three briefs, a beat every 2-3 seconds with a light percussive "tick" as each card lands, keeping energy up for 11 seconds total. Matches the reference's rapid swipe-through rhythm even though this is a fixed-length video, not a real carousel.

CTA

"Close the gap. [PRICE — TBC] — ted'shealth.com"

Why this works: it's the one brief that leads entirely with credibility and data rather than mood or ritual, giving the campaign a sharper, more skeptical-audience-facing option alongside the other three.

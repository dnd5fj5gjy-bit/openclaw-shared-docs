Standing rules for all four briefs

  • No final packaging exists yet. Every brief now includes an on-screen product beat, like the Voy ads, marked [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER]. Editors block that beat out for the real Ted's Health bottle to be swapped in once packaging is ready.
  • Pricing is undecided. Every CTA carries a [PRICE] placeholder.
  • Keep it simple and generic. No invented stats, no made-up ingredient lists, no jargon.
  • Voice: short lines, punchy, premium, sleek. No metaphors, no em dashes, just plain, clean sentences.
Brief 01 of 04

You're Not Average

Benchmark: Reference video 1, 10.7s. Minimal and quiet: a soft gradient, one line of text at a time, no product, no face. Benchmarked for its restraint and pacing, not its subject.
You're Not Average
Premium stock: a soft light gradient plate (teal into white). Product beat needs a clean, still tabletop shot ready for the bottle to be dropped in. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER]
You're not average.
Highlight is the pacing. Quiet, confident, one short line at a time over a slow-moving gradient, closing on the product and logo, product held on screen the same way Voy's ads do. Keep every line short.
  1. 0-3s (hook, above): "You're not average." over the gradient.
  2. 3-6s: "So why start with an average plan?"
  3. 6-9s: "Ted's Health starts with you."
  4. 9-11s: Product beat, bottle sits centre-frame on the gradient plate. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER - swap in final Ted's Health bottle] Logo resolves beside it.
  5. CTA: "Find your fit. [PRICE]. ted'shealth.com"
Why this works: short, confident lines carry it, and closing on the product itself, like Voy does, keeps it recognisable and buildable now with a placeholder.

Brief 02 of 04

Made With Real Food

Benchmark: Reference video 2, 15.3s. A slow, cinematic product reveal, focus pulling onto a hero shot before landing on a logo card. Benchmarked for its reveal rhythm and confidence in one clean hero beat.
Made With Real Food
Premium stock: warm, natural ingredient shots (generic, no specific list), a clean lit turntable surface for the hero product shot. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER]
Most blends start in a lab.

  1. 0-3s (hook, above): "Most blends start in a lab."
  2. 3-7s: "Ours starts on a farm."
  3. 7-11s: Hero shot, slow turn on the bottle. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER - swap in final Ted's Health bottle] "Real food. Nothing else."

Why this works: one clean claim, one hero product shot exactly like Voy's, no detail to get wrong. Premium and simple.

Brief 03 of 04

The Small Habit

Benchmark: Reference video 3, 15.7s. An intimate macro close-up, a short line that downplays the claim before landing the real one. Benchmarked for its intimacy and its small-claim, bigger-payoff structure.
The Small Habit
Premium stock: a hand stirring a drink, backlit, close and warm. Include the bottle standing beside the glass in frame. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER]
It's a small habit.
Highlight is the build: a downplayed opening line, then the real payoff, then the product itself. Close, warm, backlit shots throughout, feels like a private morning routine. Calm pacing, the slowest of the four briefs.
  1. 0-3s (hook, above): "It's a small habit."
  2. 3-7s: "That changes how the whole day goes."
  3. 7-11s: Product beat, bottle picked up off the table beside the glass. [PRODUCT PLACEHOLDER - swap in final Ted's Health bottle]
  4. 11-15s: Logo resolves on teal background.
  5. CTA: "Start the habit. [PRICE]. ted'shealth.com"
Why this works: the small claim, bigger payoff structure is simple to follow, and putting the bottle in hand grounds it in something real rather than just mood.

## The proposition

> **BGV becomes Hodder Faith's full marketing and brand operator for GSET, end-to-end, for £5,000 per month.**

Everything in the hodder doc that Hodder would otherwise have to build, hire, or coordinate — BGV runs it. Single team. One retainer. Already inside the brand world.

## Scope (from the call) — everything is included

- All platforms — content creation across the lot
- All editing and post-production
- All posting and scheduling
- **Website redevelopment** and ongoing site management
- **Substack** content and management (the depth engine in the existing strategy)
- Brand management and voice stewardship
- Project management / single point of contact
- Reporting

## Critical structural constraint on the breakdown

When you present the cost / scope breakdown, **every line must read as essential to the engine working at all**. Avoid line items a procurement-minded reader could cross out without breaking the offer. The point Jesse made in the call: *"you can make it, but you can't post it"* — production without distribution is worthless, distribution without editing is worthless, etc. Frame the breakdown so the package is the package; there is no "lite" version sitting inside it waiting to be carved out.

This is not about hiding work — it's about presenting an integrated engine where each component depends on the others.

## What I want you to produce

A clean, opinionated pitch document Hodder Faith can read end-to-end and say yes to. Match the **depth, structure, sectioning, and tone** of the reference hodder doc itself (https://bgv-gset-hodder.netlify.app/). Sections (rename / re-order to fit, but cover all of this):

1. **Where we are** — short setup: launch is done, the engine in the handover doc needs an operator, here is who that should be.
2. **What we're proposing** — the £5k/month full-operator model, in two paragraphs. Plain language.

9. **Phasing** — month 1 onboarding (asset audit, website rebuild kickoff, Substack stand-up, channel claim/cleanup), month 2 ramp, month 3+ steady-state engine.
10. **Commercial** — £5,000 / month all-in. What's inside the retainer. What sits outside (paid media spend, third-party tooling licenses, additional commissioned shoots if ever needed). 12-month or rolling, simple termination. Then a single-page SOW summary.
11. **Why BGV** — we wrote the strategy, we know the brand, we already sit inside Bear's world, we are one team rather than four agencies. Short. Confident.
12. **Next steps** — how Hodder says yes and what happens in the first 7 days.

## Tone & constraints

- Match the hodder doc — warm, direct, opinionated, no jargon, no agency-speak.
- This is not a generic marketing pitch. The book is a Gospel narrative; the engine is built around faith audiences and the Christian calendar. Honour that.
- Don't re-litigate decisions already made in the handover doc (audiences, platform stack rationale, Bear's boundary, the "loop not funnel" metaphor). Build on them.
- Make recommendations, don't present menus. Pick the cadence, pick the deliverable counts, pick the operating rhythm.
- Don't ask clarifying questions before producing v1. Write the full first draft. At the end, list any assumptions you made and any genuinely blocking unknowns.

Clean Markdown, section by section, ready to drop into Notion or a slide deck. Headings, short paragraphs, bullets where they earn their place. No filler.

Hard guardrail · across every brief

Hachette / Hodder AI policy applies absolutely. No AI-generated voice. No AI-generated imagery. No AI in covers or marketing creative. AI may be used for backend processes only — transcription, scheduling, analytics, draft outlining — and never to generate published content. Every editor signs this off before commission.

Month 2 is the launch beat. By the end of Month 2 the website is live, first 4 YouTube episodes are published, the 52-episode theologian batch is in the can, short-form is publishing daily across three platforms, Substack is at weekly cadence, the read-along cohort is open for sign-up, and the monthly reporting loop is running. Every brief below is anchored to that moment. Month 1 = onboarding & build; Month 2 = launch; Month 3+ = steady state at the Section 09 volumes.

The eight workstreams below map 1:1 to the deck. Volumes in each meta block are the Section 09 monthly floor at steady state — over-performance triggers a rebalance conversation, never a price uplift. Calendar anchors (Lent, Easter, Pentecost, June anniversary, autumn church push, Advent) raise volumes for the relevant fortnight; that's not in the table here.

Assets supplied to every editor on Day 1: the Bear archive bundle (catalogued in Month 1 — Big Church Read videos, audiobook stems, press, podcast appearances, reader reviews, the Sol Fuel chapter + intro the BW team wrote), the brand identity system (Brief 07), the signature audio motif & mastering chain (Brief 08), and the voice & content-pillar guide (one-pager already drafted).

Brief 01 · YouTube long-form

Reading + Unpacking Episodes

The spine. Eight long-form episodes/month — four Reading, four Unpacking. Designed to compound over years on search and watch-time.

Volume8 / month (52 Unpacking filmed in one Month-2 batch)
Length8–12 min · ≥8:30 for mid-roll monetisation
OwnerLead long-form editor (1 FTE equivalent)
RefreshTheologian batch every 6 months

Episode A — Reading (4/month)

  • Audiobook chapter audio laid over photographic-style visuals — candlelit interiors, hands turning pages, dawn landscapes, scripture overlays in DM Serif Display.
  • One signature audio motif at open and close (per Brief 08).
  • End card: episode number, subscribe prompt, Substack pointer, soft retail link in the description.
  • Devotional pacing — closer to a meditation app than a documentary. Generous in silence.

Episode B — Unpacking (4/month, batch-filmed)

  • Single theologian on camera, intercut with referenced scripture, historical imagery, simple maps.
  • Filmed in one 3-day batch shoot in Month 2 (52 episodes = enough for a full year). Refreshed every 6 months.
  • Theologian roster spans tradition (Anglican, Catholic, evangelical, Orthodox) — credibility on the show hinges on this. Roster locked by end of Month 1.
  • Editor cuts the whole batch into ~26 episodes over Month 2, the other 26 spread across the year as ongoing releases.

Visual treatment — locked once, replicated 104×

  • LUTs and grade locked in week 1 of Month 1 via a 40-second mood-film test (the only one-off in this brief).
  • After Effects template for title card, scripture overlay, lower-third, end card — handed to the team so any editor can hold the line.
  • Thumbnail system (three variants — Reading / Unpacking / Special) provided by Brief 07.
Production pipeline: audiobook stems → Reading rough cut (Day 1–2) → grade (Day 3) → finish + thumbnail (Day 4) → Hodder sample check Friday → publish Tuesday/Thursday. Unpacking cuts pulled from batch reel, same finishing pipeline.
Sign-off: Operational. Olly checks Friday sample. Calendar-anchor episodes (Easter, Pentecost, anniversary, Advent) pre-approved via content brief 2 weeks ahead.
Brief 02 · Short-form

YouTube Shorts · TikTok · Instagram Reels

The discovery engine. 36 clips/month across three platforms, geo-locked for UK/US, each pointing deeper into the engine.

Volume12 YT Shorts + 12 TikTok + 12 IG Reels = 36/mo
Length30–60s · 9:16 vertical
OwnerShort-form editor (1 FTE)
Source mix~60% YT episode re-cuts · ~40% archive originals

Source breakdown

  • ~6 clips per Reading episode (audiobook moments, scripture beats, ambient takes recut as standalone reflections)
  • ~6 clips per Unpacking episode (theologian one-liners, scripture explainers, "what most people get wrong about…")
  • The remainder pulled from existing Bear archive — interviews, podcast appearances, retreat footage, audiobook recording session
  • Each clip exists in UK and US text-card variants where geography meaningfully changes the framing

Platform-native grammar

  • YT Shorts: tighter narrative arcs, hook in 1.5s, ends pointing at the long-form episode
  • TikTok: more conversational, more first-person, more comment-bait — but never trending audio (Hachette AI policy & brand restraint)
  • IG Reels: more visual, more scripture-overlay forward, more saveable

Craft non-negotiables

  • Captions in Outfit Bold, bottom-third safe zone, white with 30% black shadow. No karaoke text.
  • No meme overlays, no trending audio, no "POV when…"
  • Every clip ends with a soft pointer card (1s) — channel, Substack, or episode. Not retail. We do not sell the book on social.
Production pipeline: Tuesday — pull source clips for the week from new long-form + archive. Wed/Thu — cut. Fri — caption + geo-variants. Schedule for Mon–Sat publishing across three platforms (one TikTok/Reel/Short per weekday + Saturday TikTok).
Sign-off: Operational. Weekly sample (3–5 clips) sent to Hodder Friday but not gated.
Brief 03 · Instagram main feed

Instagram — The Daily Heartbeat

Twenty feed posts/month plus stories. Where saves and shares matter more than likes. Designed to travel via DM.

Volume20 feed posts + daily stories
Format mixQuote cards · audiograms · reader testimonies · theologian spotlights · BTS
OwnerSocial editor + graphic designer (shared)
Success metricSaves & shares · not likes

The twenty / month

  • 8 quote cards — verses + book lines, set in templated Outfit + DM Serif Display, designer-produced from theologian transcripts and audiobook excerpts
  • 4 audiograms — Bear or theologian voice over a waveform + scripture treatment, 45s, sound-on, captioned
  • 4 reader testimonies — pulled from the testimony curation stream (4/mo, see Brief 09 within Brief 05)
  • 2 theologian spotlights — short profile reel introducing each new face from the Unpacking batch
  • 2 BTS / process — footage from the theologian shoot, the studio set, voice recording, etc.

Stories — daily

  • Episode drop reminder · Substack of the week · reader DM screenshots (with consent) · scripture-of-the-day · poll/question
  • Always sliders or polls — drives algorithm weight; doesn't need creative every day
Production pipeline: designer batch-produces 5 quote cards every Friday. Audiograms cut by short-form editor (Brief 02) on Wednesday. Testimonies routed in from curation stream. Theologian spotlights produced day-of from batch-shoot offcuts.
Sign-off: Operational. Bio always points to Substack.
Brief 04 · Substack

The Depth Engine

The only non-algorithmic asset — and, by Month 12, the most valuable thing we own. Four articles + two reader letters/spotlights every month, plus seasonal companions.

Volume4 articles + 2 reader letters/spotlights /mo
LengthArticles 1,200–2,000 words · letters 600–900
OwnerSubstack editor (writer) + Hodder editorial pass
SeasonalLent / Pentecost / Advent companions

The recipe

  • 2 theologian essays/mo — Unpacking transcripts edited into long-form essays. Hodder editorial pass before publish.
  • 2 thematic articles/mo — original writing from the in-house editorial voice: doubt, gospel readings, faith and culture, Bear's perspective on a given passage
  • 2 reader letters / spotlights/mo — curated reader stories, sometimes Q&A format, sometimes mini-profile of a church group or reader who's used the book

Seasonal companions (additional to the 4+2)

  • Lent: 40-day daily series, short-form, gated to subscribers
  • Holy Week + Easter: 8-piece series
  • Pentecost: 7-piece series
  • Advent: 24-day series
  • Church discussion guides: free, email-gated PDFs released at each calendar anchor

Voice rules

  • Warm, direct, invitational, never preachy, never salesy — same tone as the audiobook.
  • Never AI-drafted in final form. AI permitted for outline scaffolding only, never published copy (Hachette policy).
  • Each post auto-pulls onto the website — no manual republishing.
Production pipeline: Mon — write article 1. Tue — Hodder edit pass. Wed — schedule. Thu — write/curate reader letter. Fri — publish + cross-post tease to IG/FB. Friday cohort week — additional 2x cohort companion posts (see Brief 05).
Sign-off: Each theologian essay edited by Hodder. Thematic articles operational unless theological line is contested.
Brief 05 · Facebook · Community

Facebook Page + GSET Reading Community Group + Read-Along Cohorts

Two mechanics on one platform. The Page is broadcast and discovery; the Group is community and cohort reads. Self-sustaining by day 90.

Volume12 Page posts + 8 Group prompts + 1 rolling cohort
Cohort cadence5-week read-along, one per Gospel — Matthew, Mark, Luke, John, Acts
OwnerCommunity lead (part-time, ~10 hrs/wk)
Self-sustaining targetDay 90 — 8–12 volunteer moderators

Facebook Page (12/mo)

  • Episode drops · Substack share · reader testimonies · cohort invite · church leader spotlight · seasonal anchor moments
  • Geo-locked variants where audiences diverge (UK / US framing on the same core post)

Group prompts (8/mo)

  • 2x weekly discussion prompts (Mon + Thu) — one scripture, one cultural / lived-experience
  • Church leader networking thread (monthly)
  • "Reader of the month" feature

Read-along cohort (1 rolling)

  • Five-week structured read — Matthew first, then Mark, etc. Daily passages, weekly live thread, private cohort space.
  • Cohort companion emails: 2 per cohort week, sent via Substack (cohort-only segment).
  • Goal: several thousand readers through one cohort by year-end.

Reader-testimony curation (4/mo, feeder)

  • Community lead scans Group, IG DMs, Substack replies for usable testimonies (with consent process)
  • Surfaces 4 per month — feeds Brief 03 (IG) and Brief 04 (Substack reader letters)
Production pipeline: Mon AM — community lead sets the week's prompts + Page posts. Thu PM — cohort week's passage live. Continuous — moderation, testimony curation.
Sign-off: Operational. Sensitive theological exchanges escalated to Hodder per Section 08.
Brief 06 · Website & attribution

thegreateststoryevertoldbook.com

Central hub, conversion layer, attribution backbone. Designed Month 1, live by end of Month 2.

Build windowDesign wk 1 · build wks 2–4 · live end of Month 2
StackAstro or Next + Substack RSS pull + Cloudflare edge geo-routing
OwnerIn-house dev + designer (one-off) → ongoing maintenance retainer
SLAChanges live in 7 working days (non-structural)

Build spec

  • Geo-routed retail link tree — Amazon UK/US, Waterstones, Bookshop.org, St Andrews, Barnes & Noble, region-defaulted
  • Geo-detected cover swap — UK / US / future editions
  • Substack auto-pull — server-rendered, no manual republishing
  • Social feed embeds, events page (church partner events, US tour if confirmed), tracker links
  • Subscribe block above the fold — Substack double-opt-in
  • Theologian roster page — refreshed each batch

Attribution layer (the non-obvious requirement)

  • Every outbound retail click tagged: ?utm_source=gset_web&utm_medium={geo}&utm_campaign={section}
  • Click events POSTed to a lightweight log endpoint — we own the data, not GA
  • Surface in a simple Looker / Metabase dashboard, weekly auto-email to the single Hodder POC
  • Same scheme applied to outbound links from Substack, IG bio, FB Page

Month-1 build sequence

  1. Wk 1 — design (cover-swap states, retail tree wireframes, Substack pull treatment)
  2. Wk 2 — Astro/Next scaffolding + Substack RSS integration
  3. Wk 3 — geo-routing + attribution layer + dashboard endpoint
  4. Wk 4 — content load, QA, soft-launch behind staging URL
Ongoing pipeline: weekly Substack auto-pull (zero touch). Monthly tracker review. Per-campaign UTM scheme additions. Theologian roster refresh on each batch.
Sign-off: Structural changes escalated to Hodder POC. Operational copy (event listings, retail link adds) on our judgement.

One-off pack in Month 1, then a rolling template-support retainer. Locks the look across YouTube, Instagram, Substack, Facebook, Web.

One-offMonth 1, week 2
Ongoing~25 templated assets / month (with social editor)
OwnerGraphic designer (1 FTE eq. across GSET + ongoing brand support)
AestheticEditorial · restrained · type-led · cream/ink/burgundy/gold

One-off pack (delivered end of Month 1)

  • YouTube: channel banner, end-screen, three thumbnail templates (Reading / Unpacking / Special), watermark, lower-third
  • Instagram: 5 feed quote-card variants, audiogram template, 3 Story templates, Reel cover template
  • Substack: header artwork, 3 section dividers, post hero illustration style

Brief 08 · Audio system

The Audio Identity

The signature motif, the mastering chain, the audiogram template. The audio is what's consistent across every channel — picture changes, sound doesn't.

One-offMonth 1, weeks 2–3 — 90s pilot + system
OngoingQC pass on each Reading episode, audiogram support
OwnerAudio engineer / sound designer (part-time retainer)
No AI voiceHachette policy — Bear's real voice only

One-off deliverables

  • 90-second audio pilot — first 90 seconds of audiobook ch. 1 mastered with the full bed (wind, distant water, single bell, parchment, fire). The spec the visual editor cuts to, not the other way round.
  • Signature audio motif — single sustained drone or low bell, used at chapter open and close. Identifiable across all 104 episodes.
  • Mastering chain — voice -24 to -28 LUFS, slight warmth on low-mids, light de-essing, no compression that flattens breath.
  • Audiogram template — Premiere/After Effects template the short-form editor and social editor reuse 12+ times/month.
  • 1-paragraph audio brief — the spec the next 103 episodes follow.

Ongoing

  • QC pass on each Reading episode bed mix (15 min per episode)
  • Master any seasonal companion (Lent daily, Advent daily) where ambient design is meaningful
  • Refresh signature motif annually if/when it gets stale
Sign-off: Audio identity locked once by Olly + Jesse. Ongoing QC operational.

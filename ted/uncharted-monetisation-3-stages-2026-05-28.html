00The frame

The three stages map to the franchise's three growth horizons — Capture, Compound, Expand. They are not three separate businesses; they are one continuous build where each stage funds and de-risks the next.

Stage 1 brings money in early off the launch — ad revenue, sponsorship, the broadcast licensing deal, tourism, merch. Stage 2 converts that into recurring, owned, higher-margin revenue — memberships, renewals at proven-audience rates, repeat season licensing. Stage 3 scales the moat — format and IP licensing, geographic expansion, an owned ecosystem that means a broadcaster deal is acceleration, not oxygen.

The principle underneath all three: the owned audience is the asset. Talent and broadcasters amplify; the channel, social, community and email list are what compound and what we monetise repeatedly. Year 1 spends to acquire that audience. Years 2 and 3 harvest it at rising margin.

01Stage 1 — Capture · how money comes in early

1 Capture Year 1 / launch

Year 1 monetises the launch moment. Revenue is mixed and front-loaded across five levers — the point is to fund the build and establish the brand while the real prize, the owned audience, is being acquired underneath it.

YouTube ad & partner revenue — year-round hunts

The always-on digital hunts monetise directly through the YouTube Partner Programme: pre-roll and mid-roll ad share, plus channel memberships and Super Thanks as the audience grows. Volume of year-round uploads — not one annual spike — is what makes this meaningful from month one.

De-riskAlways-on cadence means revenue isn't hostage to a single broadcast window; the channel earns between TV seasons.

Brand sponsorship & integrations

Real-expedition gear, dive, travel and outdoor brands pay for integration into hunts and the flagship season — kit placement, sponsored segments, named-partner episodes. The real-permit, real-archaeology credibility is the sell: brands buy authentic adventure, not staged drama.

De-riskBart's expert credibility and the genuine permits make this defensible to brand-safety teams in a way reconstruction shows are not.

Broadcast network licensing — the flagship season

The 8-episode flagship is licensed to a network. Bear's Executive Producer credit opens the doors — Disney, NatGeo, Amazon, Apple — and we go in with a proven year-round audience, so it's a de-risked demand pitch, not an unproven concept. This is the single largest Year-1 line.

De-riskWe sell engaged audience, not an idea. And because the channel earns independently, the licence is a premium, not a lifeline — which strengthens our negotiating position.

Bahamas tourism partnership

The Bahamas tourism board as a flagship cultural partner — Long Island as the birthplace of Caribbean piracy is a destination story they want told. Co-funding, location support and co-promotion in exchange for authentic placement of the location.

De-riskOffsets production cost directly and earns reach at an authenticity we couldn't buy.

Launch merchandise

A tight launch drop tied to the franchise identity and the find — limited, on-brand, sold to the most engaged audience first. Modest in Year 1; its real job is to test demand and seed the owned-store infrastructure that Stage 2 scales.

De-riskPrint-on-demand / pre-order keeps inventory risk near zero; it validates merch appetite before any committed spend.

02Stage 2 — Compound · recurring & owned revenue

2 Compound Year 2+

By Year 2 the audience built in Stage 1 is a predictable, owned asset — channel, social, community and email list. Stage 2 monetises that asset on repeat, at higher margin and with far less dependence on any single deal. This is where the franchise starts to look like a business rather than a launch.

Owned-audience monetisation — memberships & community

The owned audience is monetised directly via a community layer — YouTube channel memberships, a paid Substack / member tier, behind-the-scenes access and member-first drops. The owned email list is the highest-margin re-engagement channel we have. Recurring revenue with no platform-deal dependency.

De-riskRecurring monthly revenue we own end-to-end — not subject to a broadcaster's schedule or an algorithm change.

Repeat annual TV season licensing

The flagship becomes an annual prestige beat licensed season after season. Each renewal is negotiated off a larger, proven audience and a track record — so terms rise rather than reset. The relationship moves from one-off sale to a standing line.

De-riskProven Season 1 performance and a year-round audience between seasons make each renewal lower-risk for the network and higher-value for us.

Sponsorship renewals at higher rates

Year-1 sponsors renew against real, demonstrated audience numbers rather than projections — so rates step up. New brands enter at the proven-audience price. Multi-season and category-exclusive deals become possible once the numbers are real.

De-riskPricing moves from speculative to evidenced; competitive tension between brands for proven slots lifts rates.

Paid-experience & expedition tie-ins

The franchise's authenticity supports premium real-world experiences — expedition-adjacent trips, fan dives, location experiences, run with the Bahamas tourism partner. High-ticket, low-volume, and they deepen the community while they earn.

De-riskDemand is pre-validated by the engaged community before any experience is committed; runs on partner infrastructure, not ours.

Format licensing — first steps

With a proven format, early format-licensing conversations become credible — selling the show structure, not just the finished episodes. This is the bridge into Stage 3 and starts here once Season 1 has performed.

De-riskLicensing a proven format carries far less execution risk for a buyer than commissioning a new one.

03Stage 3 — Expand · scale the moat

3 Expand Future

Stage 3 takes a proven, owned franchise and scales it — without proportionally scaling the cost or the broadcaster dependency. The owned ecosystem is now the moat: the franchise earns across multiple lines whether or not any single broadcaster says yes.

Franchise / format licensing to new geographies

License the Uncharted Treasures format to new locations and markets — the structure travels, the IP stays ours. Each licensed territory pays for the format and returns franchise fees while extending the brand at no production cost to us.

De-riskLicensees carry production cost and local risk; we earn on proven IP. The brand grows without our capital growing with it.

IP, merch & licensing extensions

The franchise IP supports a real merch line, publishing, educational tie-ins (school-network and museum / aquarium partnerships) and family-attraction or toy crossovers. These extend the brand into durable, owned-IP revenue that isn't tied to any episode airing.

De-riskDiversified IP income decoupled from the broadcast calendar; each extension is optional and demand-tested first.

Geographic & flagship expansion

Runway to bigger flagship expeditions and new expedition locations under one franchise identity audiences already trust. Larger spectacle commands higher licence and sponsorship value; new geographies open new tourism partners on the Bahamas template.

De-riskEach new location launches into an existing, trusting audience rather than from a cold start.

Secondary creators — light, in time

In time, additional expedition leads can come under the franchise umbrella. Kept deliberately light — a future option, not the spine of the model and not a near-term revenue line. Mentioned for completeness, de-emphasised by design.

De-riskOptional and unfunded for now; nothing in the model depends on it, so it carries no downside.

The Stage 3 principle

By Stage 3 the owned ecosystem — channel, social, community, list, IP — earns across multiple lines independent of any broadcaster. A broadcaster deal becomes acceleration, not oxygen. That independence is the whole point of building owned audience first.

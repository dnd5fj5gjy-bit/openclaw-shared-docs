Q+A prep

Most likely questions in order of probability. Concise answers — if they want depth, they'll ask again.

"What does Month 1 actually produce?"
Build and test, not full launch cadence. Studio booked, theologian roster locked, audiobook stems in post-production, first Reading + Unpacking pair edited by end of month. Short-form at 3–5 per platform per week across the testing matrix. Substack — 4 essays drafted, first publishes end of Month 1 or Month 2 Week 1. Web build phase. Steady-state output starts Month 2.
"What if the short-form formula doesn't land in Month 1?"
The asset bank is wide enough and the matrix runs across three platforms with multiple variant dimensions — we'll see signal. If nothing clears the median by end of Month 1 we extend through Month 2 Week 1–2 and lean harder on the long-form publishing schedule, which doesn't depend on the short-form formula. We'd flag it on the weekly ops check-in, not at month end.
"Is Bear doing any new filming or recording?"
No. His contribution is pre-recorded only — audiobook, existing interviews, ChosenCon, archive. His personal channels carry three calendar-anchor posts a year — Easter, Christmas, anniversary — written by the team, approved by him, posted by him. That's the deliberate structural choice that lets the engine run year-round without consuming his bandwidth.
"How will we know the US strategy is working?"
Geo-isolated velocity. US web sessions read against US retail velocity, month by month. That's the cleanest signal that content is shifting copies, not just generating views. It depends on monthly US retail velocity reports from Hodder and the attribution tracker on the rebuilt website.
"Who owns the channels and the audience?"
Hodder retain the book IP. The GSET channel handles and Substack are registered in alignment with Hodder. Marketing assets sit under the GSET brand on the channels we set up, operated by us under the retainer. Worth setting that boundary cleanly today.
"What does success look like at 3, 6 and 12 months?"
3 months: locked short-form formula, YouTube monetisation eligible, Substack weekly, first cohort completed, first read on geo-isolated velocity. 6 months: second theologian batch shoot in the can, second cohort underway, US web sessions trending against US retail velocity. 12 months: US gap closing on direct attribution, UK compounding through anchor seasons, international rails live for Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese.
"How does AI fit in given Hachette policy?"
Backend only — transcription, scheduling, analytics. No AI in published voice, imagery or marketing creative. Every editor signs off on the policy before they commission.
"What's the reporting cadence?"
Weekly ops check-in from Week 1. End-of-Month-1 monthly summary covering the short-form testing matrix and format recommendation, asset audit status, theologian roster, web build progress, locked Month-2 content slate for sign-off. Quarterly strategy review starts end of Quarter 1.
"How is YouTube revenue handled?"
YouTube self-funds at the 8-minute ad threshold. Revenue treatment is open — pooled budget, retainer credit, or a P&L line. Worth flagging today but doesn't have to land in this call.
"What's excluded from the retainer?"
Paid media spend, third-party tools in Hodder's name, additional commissioned shoots beyond the theologian batch and existing Bear material, translation costs. Everything else — content, editing, posting, community, website ops, Substack, brand management — sits inside.
"Can we start before contracts are signed?"
Asset audit and theologian roster lock can start the moment we have the green light. The 3-day batch shoot needs the contract because it commits production budget. Aim to be in the studio by Week 3.
"What about The Chosen, Passion of the Christ, RRR — are those clearable?"
That's one of the Month 1 asks. We've scoped the use; Hodder's confirmation on what's clearable and on what terms feeds the short-form testing matrix.

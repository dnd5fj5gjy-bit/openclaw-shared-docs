What the Ambassador Role Looks Like

A straight rundown of what the role actually involves and how it works day to day, so you can see whether it's a fit before we get into the specifics.

The idea

Why we work with ambassadors at all

Ted's Health grows through people whose audience already trusts them on health and performance, not through generic ads. An ambassador is someone who goes through the patient pathway themselves, has a genuine result or experience worth talking about, and is comfortable sharing that with their audience in their own voice. We're not looking for a script read out loud; we're looking for real people who care about the same cause and want to promote men's health more broadly. That shared cause is what makes the alignment work: think of it in the same spirit as how Sir Steve Redgrave represents Ted's Health, a public advocate for men's health, not a referral partner running a discount code.

What it involves

What we're actually asking for

  • Go through the patient pathway yourself and have a genuine experience worth talking about, good or middling. We're not asking you to oversell it.
  • Content, compatible with your platform and at the volume you feel comfortable with: a post, story or short video about your results and experience, in your own words.
  • Be a visible advocate for men's health. Lend your voice and profile to the cause, publicly associated with Ted's Health, in the same way Sir Steve Redgrave represents us.
  • Stay in touch periodically. A re-share or mention every so often, not a one-and-done. This is an ongoing relationship, not a single transaction.
  • Represent it accurately. Your own experience only, no medical claims made on our behalf.
How it works

Day to day, what this actually looks like

1
You go through the patient pathway

Same process any client goes through: blood test, then a consultation to talk through your results.

2
You share your take

Once you've had the results and consultation, you post about it in your own voice. No script, no brand-speak.

3
Light-touch agreement

A short written agreement covering what's expected on both sides and how long it runs. Nothing heavy.

4
Ongoing, not one-off

You keep it in rotation over time rather than a single post and done, and we're on hand for anything you need along the way.

What this isn't

Not a paid influencer contract with fixed deliverables and a monthly invoice. It works differently to that.

Not a medical endorsement. You're speaking to your own experience, not making claims on our behalf. Our clinical team owns everything clinical.

Not exclusive by default. If that ever becomes relevant for either side, we'd talk about it separately.

01 / The idea

We show how food used to be earned, then flip to the modern world. That is the whole video - one text line, one hard cut, carried by beautiful footage and a euphoric trending sound. Modern Savage is never shown or named on screen. The brand lives only in the caption, as the way back.

The note that matters most

Both halves are equally beautiful

This is not premium footage versus bad footage. Both acts are cinematic and luxury-grade - slow motion, shallow focus, drone, considered light. The modern world should look stunning but feel hollow, the way a car ad makes a factory look gorgeous.

Because Act 2 is just as beautiful, the viewer never consciously clocks "good vs bad" - they simply feel the warmth and connection drain away. That feeling is the entire point. The contrast comes from content and colour temperature, never from production quality.

02 / The structure

One flip, two worlds

Part 01

Then - how food was earned

Warm, primal, alive. All the aesthetic firepower goes here: vibrant green, open land, real food got by hand. This is the scroll-stopper.

  • Hunter running across savannah, backlit, dust catching the light
  • Riders on horseback crossing open plains, slow motion
  • Hands turning meat over an open fire, embers rising
  • Aerial drone over a lush green valley at golden hour
  • A community eating together around a fire, passing food, laughing
Stock search terms
tribe hunting savannah slow motion horseback riders plains golden hour cooking meat open fire cinematic community eating around fire

Grade: warm, golden, rich saturated greens.

The turn

Cut on the beat drop

The single most important second. A hard cut (or a match cut if an easy one presents itself - fire into a flickering strip light, hands lifting meat from the fire into hands lifting shrink-wrapped meat off a shelf) landed exactly on the music drop.

Part 02

The modern world

Same cinematic craft, colder soul. Processed, repetitive, isolating - longer queues, worse food, shot as elegantly as Part 1.

  • Slow dolly down an endless fluorescent aisle, identical packets repeating
  • Plastic-wrapped meat on a styrofoam tray under cold light, condensation on the film
  • Long queue through self-checkouts, overhead, everyone on their phones
  • Neon drive-thru at night, a bag of food passed through a window
  • Macro slow motion of a nugget or seed oil dropping into a fryer
Stock search terms
supermarket aisle dolly fluorescent packaged meat plastic tray macro self checkout queue overhead drive thru neon night fast food fryer slow motion

Grade: cool, desaturated blues and clinical whites - same richness, opposite temperature.

06 / Concept variations

Same format, different cut

The benchmark below is the hero concept - build to that. These are alternative ways to run the exact same "then vs now" format if we want to test other angles or make a series. Every one keeps the rules: both halves equally beautiful, one text line, trending sound, brand only in the caption.

01 - The Hunt Benchmark How food was got. Hunting, horseback, fire and the open land, cut against the supermarket, the drive-thru and the fryer. This is the concept detailed above.
02 - The Table
How we ate. The ritual around the food, not the getting of it.
ThenFamily gathered, cooked from scratch, shared for hours, firelight and full plates.
NowEating alone, lit by a laptop, a delivery carton, scrolling between bites.
03 - From the Land
The journey of the food itself, from source to plate.
ThenAnimal on open pasture, whole and respected, butchered by hand, nose to tail.
NowProcessing line, conveyor, shrink-wrapped fillet, a barcode and a use-by date.

What the food was for. Identity and strength (kept cultural, never medical).
ThenStrong, moving, capable people working the land, fuelled by what they caught and grew.
NowSedentary, screen-lit, running on vending-machine fuel and something in a wrapper.

The same daily beats, two eras, cut side by side.

Brief 01 · Trailer

The Channel Trailer — 60 seconds

The film that lives at the top of the YouTube channel and plays at the bottom of every Hodder meeting from here on. Sells the engine in one minute.

Format60–75s film · 16:9 + 9:16 cut
DeadlineFri 6 June
Rate envelope£1,200–1,800
OwnerLead video editor

The Job

A one-minute film that introduces The Greatest Story Ever Told as a YouTube channel and makes the viewer feel they have to come back. No exposition. No "welcome to my channel." A mood piece that earns trust through restraint.

The Feel

  • Devotional, not documentary. Closer to a meditation app's onboarding film than a Netflix doc trailer.
  • Bear's voice (from existing audiobook chapter 1) over still-quiet visuals — candle, hands, scripture page, dawn, footpath, ink on paper, baptism water.
  • Slow. Generous in silence. The viewer can hear themselves think.
  • One title card at the end: The Greatest Story Ever Told. Underneath: A reading. A reckoning. A return.

Structure

  1. 0:00–0:10 — Cold open. Single audio line from Bear, lifted from audiobook ch. 1 ("In the beginning…" or equivalent). Black with slow visual fade-in.
  2. 0:10–0:35 — Visual sequence. ~8 photographic-style shots, 3s each, married to ambient bed.
  3. 0:35–0:50 — Brief textual reveals on screen — three lines of the book, set in DM Serif Display, fading in over slow visuals. "This is not a sermon." / "This is the oldest story." / "Told plainly, for once."
  4. 0:50–1:00 — Title card. Hold three seconds. End frame: book cover + retail prompt.

References

Tone: Calm app's "1 Year of Meditation" trailer · The Bible Project's Genesis 1–11 overview opening · Apple "Shot on iPhone — Faith" spot.
Avoid: Hillsong-style sweeping aerials, voiceover guarantees, multiple voice clips intercut. One voice. One mood.

Deliverables

  • 60–75s master, ProRes + H.264
  • 9:16 vertical cut for IG / TikTok / Shorts (60s hard cut)
  • 15s teaser cut for paid sampling later
  • Open-project file handed back to BGV

Forward-ready · attach Bear ch. 1 audiobook stem + archive bundle on commission.

Brief 02 · Visual Treatment Film

Episode A — "Reading" Look Proof

Not an episode. A 45-second mood film that locks the visual treatment of the Reading series before we commit to 52/year.

Format40–50s film · 16:9 · graded master
DeadlineFri 13 June
Rate envelope£1,500–2,200 incl. colourist
OwnerLead video editor + colourist

The Job

The Reading episode is the spine of the channel — 52 of them in year one. Before we cut episode 1 properly, we need a 40-second proof film that locks the look: LUTs, type, transitions, lower-thirds, scripture overlay style, pacing, music bed. Once this is approved, every Reading episode follows the same template.

What it shows

  • Cold open: chapter number + title in DM Serif Display, fade up over black.
  • ~30 seconds of audiobook narration laid over a single coherent visual sequence — candlelit interior, hands turning pages, a window at dawn, ambient outdoor cutaway.
  • One scripture overlay moment — clean white serif type drifting up the frame.
  • Closing card: title + episode marker + Substack subscribe nudge.

Look brief for the colourist

  • Warm, low-contrast. Cream and ink. Highlights pulled, blacks lifted slightly.
  • No saturated colour beyond candle / lamp / dawn. Skin tones natural-warm.
  • Subtle film grain, no aggressive halation.

Music / sound

  • Ambient bed only — drone, soft strings, occasional acoustic guitar pluck.
  • No percussion. No swell at the end.
  • License through Musicbed or Artlist — supply 2 options for sign-off.

References

Closest: The Bible Project's long-form Hebrew-prophets episodes (visual restraint) · Apple TV+ The Mosquito Coast opening titles (warmth) · Calm's Story narrations (pacing).
Avoid: stock-footage feel, lower-thirds with motion, sweeping camera moves, dramatic music build.

Deliverables

  • 40–50s master, ProRes
  • LUT(s) handed back as .cube files
  • Typographic system file (After Effects template for title card, scripture overlay, end card)
  • 1-page written treatment summary so the next 51 editors can hold the line

Forward-ready · pair with Brief 01 if commissioned to same editor for shared bed/grade savings.

Brief 03 · Short-Form

Launch Clip Pack — 5 Shorts from the Archive

A pre-loaded fortnight of short-form before episode 1 is even live. Tests the discovery engine and gives the launch a head of steam.

Format5 clips · 30–60s · 9:16 vertical
DeadlineFri 6 June
Rate envelope£600–900 (£120–180/clip)
OwnerShort-form clip editor

The Job

Five short-form clips, each pulled from existing Bear footage and audiobook audio. Each clip works as a standalone — strong hook in 1.5 seconds, on-screen captions, ends pointing at the channel or Substack. The goal is to validate the engine at zero cost: we publish these in the run-up to launch, watch what travels, and refine the formula before the long-form drops.

The Five Clips

  1. "Why I wrote it" — Bear talking about the book in an existing interview. ~45s. Hook: "The story everyone half-knows."
  2. "The moment I came back to faith" — audiobook excerpt over visual. ~50s. Built for high-emotion scroll-stop.
  3. "What the Bible actually says about fear" — Bear interview + scripture overlay. ~40s. Theology-curious hook.
  4. "This is the part Hollywood always gets wrong" — audiobook excerpt re-cut as provocative hook. ~55s. Engagement-bait but truthful.
  5. "60 seconds with the loudest book in Britain" — book stat + 3 reader testimonies (text on screen) + Bear voice tag. ~60s. Social-proof variant.

Craft notes

  • Captions: sans-serif (Outfit Bold), bottom-third safe zone, white with black 30% drop shadow. No karaoke-bouncing words.
  • Hook is in the first 1.5s. No build-up. No "hey guys."
  • Each clip ends with a soft 1-second card pointing at the channel or Substack — not retail. We are not selling the book on social.
  • Provide UK and US text-card variants of clips 1, 3 and 5 (different stats / different framing).

References

Studied: Bible Project shorts · Jordan Raynor TikTok · Father Mike Schmitz "Bible in a Year" clips · The Chosen behind-the-scenes shorts.
Avoid: meme-text overlays, trending audio, anything that needs context to land.

Deliverables

  • 5 final clips (UK), 3 US variants — H.264 vertical, with and without captions burned in
  • Caption SRT files per clip (for accessibility + platform auto-translation)
  • 1-line hook copy per clip for caption-text-on-post

Forward-ready · supply archive bundle + audiobook stems on commission. Captions in Outfit Bold (font supplied).

Brief 04 · Brand Identity

Channel & Cross-Platform Visual System

One designer, one pack. The visual language that holds across YouTube, Instagram, Substack, Facebook and the web.

FormatStatic + template pack · multi-platform
DeadlineFri 13 June (full pack)
Rate envelope£2,500–3,500 one-off
OwnerGraphic designer (faith / publishing background pref.)

The Job

Build the visual identity system that every editor, community lead and freelancer will work from. Not a logo exercise — a working kit that locks the channel's look across six surfaces, anchored in the typographic and colour decisions already in the deck.

What's in the pack

  • YouTube — channel banner, end-screen, three thumbnail templates (Reading / Unpacking / Special / Seasonal), watermark, lower-third
  • Instagram — feed quote card (5 variants), audiogram template, Story template (3 variants), Reel cover template
  • Substack — header artwork, three section dividers, post hero illustration style
  • Facebook — group banner, cohort-week post template, discussion-prompt card
  • Cross-cutting — full type system (DM Serif Display + Outfit), colour palette (cream, ink, burgundy, gold, forest), iconography (simple line-mark cross / book / dove)

Brand brief

  • Restrained. Editorial. Closer to a literary publisher than a megachurch.
  • Cream as base, ink as type, burgundy as accent, gold sparingly.
  • Type-led. Imagery is rare and earns its space when it appears.
  • Reads as old — like the book has been around longer than it has.

References

Studied: Penguin Modern Classics covers · Plough Quarterly · Comment magazine · Faber poetry editions · The Bible Project's typographic minimalism.
Avoid: evangelical worship-night gradients, scripted handwriting fonts, anything that reads "Christian merch."

Deliverables

  • Figma file with all templates, editable, organised by surface
  • Exported PNGs for immediate use (thumbnails × 6, quote cards × 10, banners × 4)
  • 1-page brand guidelines PDF (type, colour, spacing rules, do/don't)
  • Font licences passed to BGV

Forward-ready · attach this HTML doc as visual reference (same family).

Brief 05 · Audio Pilot

Episode 1 Cold-Open — 90-Second Audio Pilot

The first 90 seconds of episode 1, finished. Proves the audio engine — ambience, narration treatment, scripture entry — works without picture.

Format90s audio file · stereo · mastered
DeadlineWed 4 June
Rate envelope£500–800
OwnerAudio engineer / sound designer

The Job

Take the opening 90 seconds of audiobook chapter 1 and build the full ambient bed underneath it — wind, distant water, a single bell, parchment, fire. Master it as if it were episode 1 of a finished podcast or the cold open of a finished YouTube episode. This is the spec the visual editor cuts to, not the other way round.

Why this matters

The channel's audio identity is the only constant across YouTube, Substack audiograms, Facebook clips and Instagram Reels. If the audio works on its own, the visual treatment becomes interchangeable. If it doesn't, no amount of grading saves us.

Sound design notes

  • Ambient bed sits at -24 to -28 LUFS — present but always under the voice.
  • Bear's voice mastered for clarity, not loudness — slight warmth on the low-mids, light de-essing, no compression that flattens breath.
  • One signature audio motif (a sustained drone or a single low bell) repeated at chapter open and close — the audio version of the title card. The motif is identifiable across all 104 episodes.
  • Stem-deliver everything so the same bed can be reused under shorts and audiograms.

References

Closest: The Truth podcast cold opens · BBC Drama on 4 · Pray As You Go opening seconds · Hans Zimmer's Time as ambient grammar.
Avoid: Sermon-style swells, choral pads, anything that announces "this is religious."

Deliverables

  • 90s mastered stereo WAV + MP3
  • Full session stems (voice / bed / motif / FX) handed back
  • 1-paragraph audio brief that the next 103 episodes follow

Forward-ready · audiobook ch. 1 stem required. If Hodder hasn't confirmed by Tue 26 May, run this brief on a chapter we already have licensed access to.

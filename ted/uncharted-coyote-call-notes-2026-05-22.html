Your part · the franchise plan

3 The points to make

1 — A franchise, not a campaign

"We're approaching this as a year-round franchise, not a one-off campaign to promote a single season. The aim is to build something that runs all year and keeps growing."

2 — How it's structured (lead with this)

"You host a series of smaller-scale treasure hunts on YouTube through the year — that's what builds the audience and the hype — and they build up towards the finale, which is the one big broadcast season. The digital comes first and builds the audience; the broadcast is the peak it leads to."
  • Year-round — you host smaller-scale digital YouTube treasure hunts → builds hype and a loyal audience
  • The finale — those build up to the one big expedition = the broadcast TV season (the flagship)

3 — The content engine (how it all gets made)

"And every piece of it — the smaller hunts and the broadcast episodes — gets turned into weeks of content. We repurpose it across every social platform, run the best of it as Meta ads, and feed it all back into the YouTube channel. So both halves are constantly generating digital content that grows the audience."

4 — The audience is the asset

"That audience is what makes the broadcast far easier to sell to a network — we'd be bringing a proven, loyal audience rather than just a pitch — and it builds the demand for each year's finale."

5 — What we bring, and building your brand

"This is what we do well — we're experts at activating promotion around Bear Grylls, and we'll bring exactly that to Uncharted Treasures and to your brand, working alongside your team. The goal is to build the Uncharted brand and your brand together, with you as the host of the digital series."

6 — Underpinned by genuine credibility

"And it's all underpinned by real credibility — real excavation permits, a real archaeology team, real finds. That's what makes the audience trust it and stay, and it's what most treasure-hunt shows can't offer."
On reach · say it plainly

"Your reach and Bear's are how we launch quickly and at scale. That loyal audience is what makes each season easier to sell, and what lets the franchise stand on its own over time."

In one line: the reach gets us started fast; a loyal Uncharted audience — built with your brand — is what holds the value long term.

Real permits, a real archaeology team and real finds, plus a year-round audience rather than a one-off. Credibility over manufactured drama.

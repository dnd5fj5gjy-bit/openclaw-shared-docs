3 The four beats

Beat 1 — It's a franchise, not a campaign

"This isn't a marketing campaign for a TV show. It's chapter one of a franchise. The show is how we plant the flag — the franchise is how we keep it flying."

Sets the whole tone. Everything else hangs off this.

Beat 2 — The three pillars (give him the shape)

"It stands on three pillars: the TV series as the prestige anchor, a year-round digital series that keeps people engaged between seasons, and always-on social so the brand never goes quiet. That stack is what a franchise looks like — a single launch is just a show."
  • TV series — the prestige anchor / the flag
  • Year-round digital — keeps the audience between seasons
  • Always-on social — min 4 posts/week, 4 lanes: Behind-the-Expedition · Franchise Lore · Discovery Moments · Community Challenges

Beat 3 — The flywheel (why it compounds)

"Every single episode becomes weeks of content — recaps, behind-the-scenes, dive POV, shorts, a podcast, the newsletter. That feeds the YouTube channel, the social, the community — and that owned audience compounds into demand for season two and beyond. The finale isn't an ending, it's the start of the next conversation."

One TV episode → YouTube + IG + TikTok + Shorts + podcast + newsletter + ads. No extra production cost.

Beat 4 — Where the value actually lives

"The value isn't in any one of us posting. It's in the community and the platform we build and own — held together by something no other treasure show has: real permits, a real archaeology team, real finds. That credibility is what makes people believe it and stick around."

Real access = the moat. It's why this community holds when other treasure-hunt shows fizzled.

2 · The strategy in one line

"We're not just marketing one show. We're using the show to build a long-term, owned franchise — a brand, an audience and a platform that keeps growing between and beyond seasons. The TV launch is the catalyst; the franchise is what we build on the back of it."
Your part · the franchise plan

3 Four points to make

1 — A franchise, not a campaign

"We're approaching this as the first season of a franchise, not a one-off promotional campaign. Every decision is about building something that continues after the broadcast ends."

2 — It's built on three pillars

"It rests on three pillars: the TV series as the anchor, a year-round digital series that keeps the audience engaged between seasons, and always-on social that keeps the brand visible week to week. Those three together are what make it a franchise rather than a single show."
  • TV series — the anchor
  • Year-round digital — keeps the audience engaged between seasons
  • Always-on social — min 4 posts/week: Behind-the-Expedition · Franchise Lore · Discovery Moments · Community Challenges

3 — How it compounds

"Every episode produces weeks of content across YouTube, social, podcast and the newsletter. That content drives people to the channels we own, which builds an audience and community that compounds over time — and becomes the demand for the next season."

One episode → recap, BTS vlog, dive POV, shorts, podcast, newsletter, paid. No extra production cost.

4 — Where the value sits

"The long-term value sits in the audience and community we own, underpinned by genuine credibility — real excavation permits, a real archaeology team, real finds. That's what makes the audience trust it and stay, and it's what most treasure-hunt shows can't offer."
Be clear here · he's in the room

4 On reach — amplify, not depend

This is the "not reliant on Bear or Coyote long-term" point. Make it plainly, and as a benefit to him:

"On reach — your audience and Bear's are how we launch this quickly and at scale; that's the acquisition engine in the early phase. The strategy then converts that audience into a community that belongs to the franchise, so it becomes self-sustaining rather than dependent on any one person's reach. For you, that means this isn't a one-off promotion — you're a founding partner in something with recurring, long-term value."

The point in one line: the reach gets us started fast; the owned platform is what holds the value long term.

7 Likely questions

"How much does this rely on me posting?"
Your reach launches it at scale; the plan then runs on the owned audience, paid media and the wider portfolio. Your involvement accelerates it rather than carrying it.
"What's Bear's role?"
Executive producer credit, which opens broadcaster doors — Disney, Nat Geo, Amazon, Apple — plus stories and reposts to his 8.5M followers at launch. No new filming required; worth being straight on that.
"What do you need from me?"
The YouTube rollout, behind-the-scenes vlogs, episode reactions, and the podcast circuit at launch.
"Why will this last when other treasure shows haven't?"
Real permits, a real archaeology team and real finds. We lead on genuine credibility rather than manufactured drama.
"What makes the launch land?"
We target three audiences in parallel — yours, Bear's, and the family co-viewing audience via paid — each with its own creative. Every episode is planned as its own moment, not just an airing.

The picture · refresher

GSET is The Greatest Story Ever Told — Bear's 288-page Gospel narrative published by Hodder Faith, told through five eyewitness perspectives. It debuted at #1 on the Sunday Times (Hodder Faith's first ever Christian title to do that) and sits on 4.8 stars across 1,400+ Amazon reviews. The book is live. The audiobook and the Big Church Read video assets are the unlock for everything that follows.

The state of play is this: BGV wrote Hodder's marketing-handover doc, and we're now pitching to run that engine for them at £5,000 / month all-in across six channels. The deck Hodder are looking at is the Bear Witness × Hodder v2 build — full marketing operator, single point of contact, steady-state from Month 3. Today's call is about social specifically: what we run, how we grow it, and what we need from them to switch it on.

2 · Where social sits today

Honest read of the current state

  • Instagram — no dedicated GSET account live. Bear's own channels carry the book at launch moments only (~3 calendar anchors per year). That's the ceiling under the current setup.
  • Substack — not stood up. It's in the v2 deck (4 articles + 2 reader letters per month) but the channel doesn't exist yet.
  • Short-form (Reels / Shorts / TikTok) — no dedicated GSET pipeline. Reference creative direction exists (see the 28 May ad-benchmark doc) but no posting cadence is running.
  • YouTube long-form — the Reading the Gospel series (Bear voice-over, archive footage) and the Unpacking series (theologian sit-downs) are scoped in v3 briefs. Production not started — gated on the theologian batch shoot.
  • Reader-testimony format — designed, not running. Needs the consent flow live before it can post.
  • Facebook Page + Group — in the deck, not yet stood up.
The honest line: the strategy is built. Almost nothing is actually running yet on dedicated GSET channels. That is precisely what the £5k/month retainer turns on. The pitch is "we switch this engine on."
3 · The direction · read this as one flow

"The way we'd grow social for GSET is to treat the book as the start of something — not the end. The book has done its job: it's put the story at #1. What it needs now is a content engine that keeps the conversation going every week, in the places where people are already looking for something deeper."

"The audience we're growing is specific. Faith-curious readers who are open but not in church; spiritually fatigued adults coming back to it; churched but Bible-intimidated people who'd never read the Gospels straight; and the slice of Bear's existing audience who are open to faith content when it comes from him. Men 25–45 leaning into purpose is a real cluster inside that. We're not trying to reach everyone. We're trying to reach the readers the book hasn't found yet."

"The content shape is four pillars that feed each other. Reading the Gospel — short YouTube long-forms cut from the audiobook, Bear's voice over archive footage. Unpacking — paired episodes where a theologian opens up what Bear just read; we cut all 52 in a single batch shoot. Short-form — five repeatable Reel / Short / TikTok formats running daily across IG, YT and TikTok. Reader testimony — real readers, what the book changed, in a templated format with a proper consent flow."

"That's wrapped with a Matthew read-along cohort — a five-week guided read, then Mark, then Luke, then John, then Acts — which gives us a rolling community we can serve. And a Substack sequence that lives at the centre of it: weekly articles, reader letters, the editorial home."

"The cadence we'd run at steady state is in the deck: 8 long-form YouTubes a month (4 Reading + 4 Unpacking), 36 short-form pieces (12 each across Shorts, TikTok, Reels), 20 Instagram feed posts, 4 Substack articles + 2 reader letters, and the rolling read-along cohort. That's volume we can hit because we run it as one engine — same team, same brief stack, same approval flow."

"The one constraint we work inside: no new Bear filming. Everything Bear is in is pre-recorded — audiobook, Big Church Read, archive. That's by design — it's how the engine stays sustainable. Bear is the patron and voice, not the host of every post."

"And it all runs at £5,000 a month, all-in. Single point of contact, weekly samples to you, calendar anchors pre-approved through a brief, operational content publishes on our judgement. That's the deal we're proposing."

6 · If they ask

Quick Q&A

"How is the £5k a month broken down?"
Designed so every line is essential — content production across six channels, single-POC management, weekly reporting, calendar planning, community management. Excluded: paid media spend, third-party tool licences in Hodder's name, additional commissioned shoots beyond the theologian batch, translation. The breakdown is in section 11 of the deck.
"Why £5k — what makes it the right number?"
It's the floor we can run the full six-channel engine on at the volumes in section 09 — 8 long-forms, 36 short-forms, 20 IG posts, weekly Substack, rolling cohort, every month. Anything lower means dropping a channel or cutting cadence. 3-month minimum, rolling monthly after that with 30 days' notice.
"When does the first measurable result land?"
Month 2 is the launch beat — first four Reading episodes published, short-form daily, Substack weekly, Matthew cohort opens. First monthly report follows Month 2. Month 3 is steady-state and the first real read on growth.
"What's Bear's role / filming commitment?"
Zero new filming. Everything Bear-on-screen is pre-recorded — audiobook, Big Church Read, archive. He's the voice and patron, not the host of every post. That's deliberate — it's how the engine stays sustainable across a year.
"Who owns the assets and the audience?"
Hodder retain the book IP. The marketing assets we build sit under the GSET brand and live on the channels we set up — they're operated by us under the retainer. Channel handles and Substack registered in alignment with Hodder. Worth flagging on the call: don't promise more than is in the deck.
"What's the reporting cadence?"
Weekly samples (not gated — published on our judgement, sample for sight). Monthly performance report. Calendar anchors pre-approved via a content brief 2 weeks out. Sensitive items (contested theology, brand-line crossings) escalated.
"Why won't AI just do this for cheaper?"
It can't, on this brand. Hachette policy bans AI-generated voice, imagery and marketing creative. AI sits in our backend only — transcription, scheduling, analytics — never in the published output. We can show that policy in writing.

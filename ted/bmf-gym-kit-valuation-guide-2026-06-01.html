01 · Inventory Approach

Walk the gym zone by zone. Don't loop.

Start at the door and work in a single loop — clockwise or anticlockwise, doesn't matter, just commit to a direction and don't double back. The mistake people make is walking the floor randomly and counting the same plates twice.

Open Notes (or a fresh spreadsheet) on your phone with one row per item. Don't aggregate "all kettlebells" — log each weight separately, because a 16kg and a 32kg are worth very different money.

1

Strength floor

Racks, rigs, barbells, plates, dumbbells, kettlebells, benches. The biggest-value zone — start here while you're fresh.

2

Cardio line

Rowers, assault bikes, ski-ergs, sleds, treadmills. Note brand + model on each — these are serialised and the brand drives 80% of the value.

3

Functional / military

Battle ropes, tyres, sandbags, slam balls, medicine balls, plyo boxes. BMF-signature zone — high volume, lower unit value.

4

Bodyweight & accessories

Rings, parallettes, pull-up attachments, mats, bands, foam rollers. Easy to undercount — sweep the walls and storage hooks.

5

Storage & fixtures

Plate trees, dumbbell racks, kettlebell stands, wall hooks, anchor points. Don't skip — racks alone can be £500–£2k of value.

6

Back-of-house

Storeroom, cupboards, outdoor area. Anything stashed or seasonal. Easy to forget; worth checking last so you don't get distracted mid-walk.

03 · Pricing Approach

Two prices per item. Always.

Don't try to land on one "true" value — log replacement (new) and resale (used) separately. Replacement is for insurance and asset valuation. Resale is what you'd actually clear if you sold the lot.

Source 1

Replacement (new)

What a UK retailer charges today. Use these for prosumer / commercial kit — they cover 95% of what's in a BMF gym.

  • · Mirafit — prosumer, mid-tier, broad range
  • · Wolverson Fitness — competition standard
  • · Strength Shop UK — heavy lifting / strongman
  • · Fitness Superstore — broad retail
  • · Rogue Fitness UK — premium, gold standard
  • · Decathlon (Domyos) — budget end

Commercial-grade: Eleiko (Olympic), Hammer Strength, Watson, Pullum, Concept2 (rowers / ski-ergs / bikes).

Source 2

Realistic resale (used)

What you'd actually get if you sold it. Search "sold" listings, not asking prices — asking prices on Gumtree are aspirational.

  • · eBay UK — filter Sold items, last 90 days
  • · Gumtree — UK local pickup, biggest used market
  • · Facebook Marketplace — fast-mover, local
  • · Gymrats UK / used-kit Facebook groups
  • · Used-Gym-Equipment.co.uk — trade-in / clearance

Good condition typically clears at 40–60% of new. Fair / worn drops to 25–40%.

The depreciation heuristic — use this when you can't find a comp

Heavy iron (plates, dumbbells, barbells, kettlebells) — holds 60–70% of new. Steel doesn't wear out.

Cardio machines (rowers, bikes, treadmills) — drops to 40–50%. Hours / wear / digital displays date them. Concept2 holds best — closer to 55–65%.

Racks & rigs — 50–60% if uninstalled and complete; less if it's a bespoke wall-mount that needs cutting out.

Rubber & textile (mats, ropes, bands, sandbags, plyo boxes) — drops to 20–30%. Hygiene + wear kill the market.

05 · Cumulative Value

Report two numbers, not one.

The replacement total is what BMF would pay to fully rebuild the gym today — the right number for insurance, asset valuations and capex planning. The resale total is the realistic liquidation value if you walked everything out tomorrow. Both matter, for different reasons.

The two formulas

Total replacement £ = Σ ( Qty × New unit £ )
Total resale £ = Σ ( Qty × Used unit £ × condition factor )

Condition factor: New = 1.0 · Good = 0.85 · Fair = 0.65 · Worn = 0.40. Apply to the resale column only — the replacement column is always "new".

BMF-branded / military-themed kit (logo'd sandbags, custom-painted tyres, BMF-branded crates) — knock another 20–30% off resale. Niche branding hurts the second-hand market. Replacement value is unaffected.

06 · Photo Every Zone

Snap as you go.

Take a wide shot of each zone before you start counting it — that's your audit trail and your sanity check if the spreadsheet later doesn't add up. Close-up every serial-numbered piece: Concept2 monitors, assault bikes, treadmills. Serial + model number = the resale price.

If you spot anything unusual or rare — Eleiko bars, vintage iron, BMF-custom rigs — get a clear photo and we'll price it properly afterwards.

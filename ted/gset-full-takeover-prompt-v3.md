## Critical structural rule on the breakdown

When you present the scope or cost breakdown, **every line must read as essential to the engine working at all**. There must be no obvious place a procurement-minded reader can cross out a line without breaking the offer. Jesse's framing on this call was explicit:

- *"Every part of the breakdown needs to be fucking essential."*
- *"The trick is to make the breakdown such that if they wanted to reduce costs, there's nothing that looks like… okay, where can I reduce costs?"*
- *"You can make it, but you can't post it."*

Lean into the *interdependence* of components — content without distribution is invisible, distribution without editing has nothing to ship, the website without channels is a billboard in a desert. Present the package as a loop, not a menu.

## Value framing — touch lightly

Jesse noted that £5k/month is artificially low for this scope ("it would be way more"). Do **not** state this. Instead, build the pitch so the value is self-evident — list the workstreams, the deliverable volumes, the operating model, and let the reader arrive at "this is a lot for £5k" on their own. The single biggest commercial benefit to Hodder is **no back-and-forth** — one team, one number, one decision. Make that visible in the operating-model and commercial sections.

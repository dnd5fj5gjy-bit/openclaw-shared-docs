The Mission
"Adventure television has spent two decades replacing real discovery with manufactured drama.
Uncharted Treasures is the correction."

This is not a marketing campaign for a TV show. It is the opening chapter of a mission: to prove that real discovery - real permits, real archaeology, real finds - beats manufactured drama every time. When audiences watch Uncharted Treasures, they are not watching a format. They are watching history happen.

That mission shapes everything. The channels we use, the tone we take, the content we make, the communities we build. Every tactical decision in this document flows from one conviction: authentic access is the unfair advantage that no competitor can replicate.

Three content pillars power that franchise indefinitely: the TV series as the prestige anchor, a year-round digital series that keeps audiences engaged between seasons, and always-on social content that ensures the brand never goes quiet. That stack is what a franchise looks like. A single TV launch is what a show looks like.

Market Context

Why this lands now

The window is specific. The conditions that make Uncharted Treasures timely will not last forever. Four forces are converging - and the combination has not existed before.

Genre fatigue is peaking

Oak Island, Expedition Unknown, Lost Gold of World War II - the treasure-hunt format has run the same playbook for fifteen years. Audiences are literate in every beat. They want the format but they are exhausted by the execution. A show with genuine discovery access breaks the cycle at exactly the right moment.

The creator-to-TV migration is real

Creator-native shows are crossing into broadcast for the first time at scale. Platforms are actively seeking talent with pre-built audiences. Coyote's twenty million subscribers are not just a marketing asset - they are proof of concept for the broadcaster that the audience already exists and has already opted in.

Discovery + family content outperforms

Post-pandemic viewing data is consistent: family co-viewing content with educational hooks outperforms single-demographic formats in retention, week-on-week, and recommendation rate. Uncharted Treasures is structured exactly this way - the adventure hook brings in Coyote's audience, the historical depth keeps them.

Bahamas tourism has distribution appetite

The Bahamas Tourism Board is actively seeking flagship content partnerships following the post-pandemic travel recovery. The moment for a high-profile production set in Long Island, Bahamas - with full tourism board backing - is now. That partnership adds legitimate distribution and co-marketing reach we cannot replicate through paid media alone.

Strategic Objectives

Three stages. Each one unlocks the next.

The campaign is built as a sequence, not a set of parallel activities. Stage one acquires the audience. Stage two converts that audience into genuine believers in the show and builds the brand from zero. Stage three capitalises on the momentum of a credible, talked-about show to grow the owned ecosystem - the YouTube channel, the social following, the community - that the franchise is built on. You cannot skip a stage. Each one is the condition for the next.

I.

Acquire

Get the right audience in front of the show

Uncharted Treasures launches with no existing audience of its own. Every viewer has to be acquired from scratch. The primary acquisition engine is the franchise itself — the story, the permit, the real archaeology — amplified across Coyote Peterson's reach, the Bear Witness portfolio, paid media, and earned press. No single talent relationship is the plan. The story is the plan. The objective: make premiere night unavoidable for the audiences most likely to become long-term franchise fans — fans of Uncharted Treasures, not fans of any individual presenter.

II.

Convert and credibilise

Turn attention into belief, belief into a brand

Getting people to watch once is acquisition. Getting them to believe in the show, talk about it, share it and come back for the next episode is conversion. That requires credibility - and credibility is Uncharted Treasures' single biggest differentiator. Real excavation permissions. A genuine archaeological team. Actual historical evidence. Expert voices validating it publicly. This is what separates the show from every other treasure-hunt format, and it has to be built consistently from T-12 onwards.

III.

Capitalise and build

Ride the momentum into a franchise that outlasts broadcast

A credible, talked-about show with a hooked audience is the foundation. Stage three activates the full franchise architecture: the TV series as the prestige anchor, a standalone digital series that generates content year-round between seasons, and always-on social that keeps the brand alive 365 days a year. This franchise does not go quiet after the finale. The repurposing engine turns every episode into weeks of content. The digital series fills the gaps. The social calendar never stops. A franchise with two million average viewers, a growing digital series following, and an always-on social presence is worth licensing and merchandising that runs for five-plus years. The TV launch is the seed event. The franchise architecture is what it grows into.

Phased Launch Plan

A sixteen-week arc in four movements

Each phase has a specific job. The phases are sequential - you cannot run Phase II without Phase I having landed. The tactics below are specific to the show's actual hook: the access, the permits, the real finds. Not a generic launch playbook.

Phase I: Tease — Drop the Permit Announcement

The Permit Drop We have legal permission to dig in sites no one has touched in two hundred years. Lead with the permission, not the show. The permit announcement is the story. Everything else is downstream of this.
Coyote on His Own Channel Coyote posts a direct-to-camera video on his channel explaining what the permits are, what they allow, and why it is unlike anything he has done before. No trailer. No production polish. Authentic.
Historical Evidence Video Bart posts one standalone deep-dive on the actual historical evidence for piracy on Long Island. A real content piece, not a teaser. It stands alone and earns views on its own merits.
Bear Trade Press at T-8 Bear's one confirmed interview window. The angle: his EP credit and what the permits mean for the show. This runs in trade press and adventure media simultaneously for maximum reach.

Phase II: Build — Real Content That Stands Alone

Coyote's Deep-Dive Documentary One full-length YouTube video (20-30 mins) on the actual historical evidence for the pirate treasure. Not a trailer. A real piece of content that lives independently and drives organic discovery.
Expert Community Circuit Bart goes on archaeology and maritime history podcasts with the actual evidence. Not to promote the show - to talk about what they found. The show is the follow-up.
Bahamas Tourism Partnership Activation Co-branded content with the Bahamas Tourism Board. Their channels carry the real discovery story to an audience with travel intent already attached.
Paid Media Goes Live Paid campaigns launch targeting the three audience pools. Paid follows organic - we know what is resonating before we spend. The best-performing organic moments become Spark Ads.

Phase III: Premiere — Make It Unavoidable

Premiere-Night Live Dig Stream Coyote and Bart livestream bonus content from a discovery site during the premiere broadcast window. Drives tune-in anxiety and extends the premiere moment into a multi-platform event.
All-Portfolio Coordinated Drop Every Bear Witness channel posts simultaneously on premiere night. Coordinated, not staggered. The amplitude matters.
Bear Social Post (Upside) If Bear's schedule allows, he posts his genuine reaction to the discoveries. Not a promotional post - his authentic response to seeing what the team found. Worth 10x produced content.
Press Pick-Up Activation Pre-pitched long leads land simultaneously with premiere. Genre press, adventure media, and one national broadsheet if the permit story lands with the right journalist.

Phase IV: Sustain — Every Find Is a News Story

Episode-by-Episode Discovery Announcements Every major find in every episode becomes its own announcement. The shipwreck found in episode four is a news story, not just a show moment. Treat each revelation as a standalone press event.
Weekly BTS Vlog Series Coyote's vlog from each episode shoot drops the week of that episode's broadcast. Drives tune-in for existing viewers and gives new viewers a discovery path into back episodes.
Repurpose Engine Running Each episode generates: one recap, one BTS vlog, three Reels, five TikTok shorts, one newsletter deep-dive, and boosted Spark Ads from top-performing organic. All coordinated, all from one source episode.
Season 2 Setup By episode six, plant the seed publicly. What did we not find? Where do we go next? The end of season one is the beginning of the franchise conversation.

Franchise Architecture

Built to last. Not just to launch.

A show lives and dies with its broadcast slot. A franchise compounds forever. Uncharted Treasures is designed from the ground up as a standalone franchise IP — not a vehicle for any individual talent. The three pillars below are what separate a franchise from a one-season wonder.

Pillar 1 — TV Series

The prestige anchor. Each season is a new expedition — new location, new discovery, new chapter in the world's untold treasure history. The TV series earns the franchise legitimacy, press coverage, and the broadcaster relationships that make everything else possible. Season one is the Bahamas. The franchise outlasts it.

Prestige Anchor

Pillar 2 — Digital Series

The year-round engine. Between TV seasons, Uncharted Treasures keeps its audience engaged with a standalone digital series — shorter episodes, faster-paced, released on YouTube and owned digital channels. Each digital series explores a lead that did not make the TV cut: a secondary dig site, a historical thread, a preparation story. The audience stays subscribed 365 days a year, not just during broadcast windows.

Year-Round Engine

Pillar 3 — Always-On Social

The franchise never goes quiet. A structured social content calendar runs continuously — not just in the weeks around a premiere. Three categories: behind-the-expedition (raw, unscripted moments from production), franchise lore (historical deep-dives, location context, discovery reveals), and community challenges (audience sent on their own local treasure hunts, best submissions featured on the franchise channels). Minimum four posts per week, every week, in season or out.

Social Content Engine

The brand never goes quiet.

The most common franchise mistake: treating social media as a campaign tool that switches on around launch and off again between seasons. Uncharted Treasures runs a continuous social presence that builds audience depth year-round — turning casual viewers into committed followers, and committed followers into franchise advocates.

Behind the Expedition

Raw, unscripted content from production: kit checks, travel days, near-misses, crew life, dive prep, site surveys. Shot on phone or handheld. No production overhead — this footage already exists from filming. Posted across Reels, TikTok, Shorts. The audience gets access that no broadcast show gives them.

Franchise Lore

Evergreen historical content: the real history of Bahamian piracy, the archaeology of treasure preservation, the stories of past expeditions that never found what we found. These posts live forever and drive organic discovery long after premiere night. Each one deepens the world the franchise inhabits.

Discovery Moments

Structured reveal content tied to the dig: "what we found this week," "what this object tells us," "the question this discovery raises." Creates a drumbeat of franchise news that gives media outlets and fan communities something to write about between episodes.

Community Challenges

Audience participation at scale: local treasure hunt challenges, historical mystery Q&As, "where would you dig next?" polls. Best submissions featured on the franchise channels. Builds a UGC layer around Uncharted Treasures that generates content without production cost — and creates the emotional ownership that turns viewers into superfans.

Posting cadence: Minimum 4-5 pieces per week, every week of the year — in season and out. The goal is that the audience never feels the franchise has gone quiet, and the algorithm never deprioritises the channel. Social presence is not a campaign tactic. It is a franchise infrastructure decision.

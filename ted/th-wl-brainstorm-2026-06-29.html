01 — Partner Models

Commercial Structures

Revenue Share (15–30%)

Best for partners with existing patient flow (GP clinics, pharmacies). Low barrier to entry; TH earns on outcomes. Risk: slow ramp if partner is passive.

SaaS Platform Fee (£500–£2,500/month)

Best for larger operators (corporate wellness providers, insurance). Predictable revenue for TH; partner owns the patient relationship. Add a per-patient activation fee on top.

White-Label Franchise (setup fee + monthly)

Best for independent clinics or physios wanting a turnkey men's health offering. Upfront £3–10k setup, then £500–1k/month. TH does the clinical backend; they bring the brand and footfall.

Per-Patient / Per-Test Model

Best for pharmacies and walk-in clinics doing high volume. Keeps fixed costs low for partners. Clean unit economics for TH.

Equity / Joint Venture

For strategic partners (military orgs, sports clubs) where TH wants long-term brand association. Share upside, share delivery.

02 — Verticals

Who to Target

Corporate Wellness Military & Veterans Sports Clubs Pharmacies Fertility Clinics Private GPs Men's Health Clinics Biohacking

Corporate Wellness (high volume opportunity)

Companies with male-heavy workforces (construction, logistics, finance, tech) paying for employee health benefits. HR buys it as a retention perk. Think: FTSE 250, engineering firms, law firms.

Military & Veterans (best brand fit)

Testosterone and hormonal dysregulation is heavily prevalent post-service. SSAFA, Help for Heroes, RFEA. High trust environments. Bear Grylls is culturally credible here (ex-SAS).

Rugby / Football / Cricket Clubs

Professional and semi-pro squads. Performance + recovery framing replaces the clinical framing. Partnership with the club physio/medical team. Start with Championship or Premiership rugby.

Pharmacies (fastest distribution)

Boots, Day Lewis, or large independent groups already pivoting to clinical services. Blood testing + TRT management is a logical extension of their pharmacy-first model.

Fertility Clinics (most under-served)

Male factor infertility is ~50% of cases; testosterone and sperm health are directly linked. IVF clinics lack a men's endocrine offering. Natural bolt-on referral relationship.

Weight Loss / Metabolic Health Clinics

Testosterone and metabolic health are deeply linked (GLP-1 + TRT is a growing clinical combination). Clinics doing Ozempic/Wegovy could add TRT as a natural complement.

Occupational Health Providers

Medigold, Optima, PAM Group — they service large employer contracts. Adding a men's hormone health module is low friction for them.

Biohacking / Longevity Clinics

Bioniq, LongevityX, Harpal Clinic types. Their customers already want TRT. White-labelling TH fills their clinical gap.

Full White-Label Platform

04 — Why Us

Differentiation Angles

  • Fully built, fully regulated — CQC-registered clinical pathway already in place. A partner building from scratch is 18+ months and £500k+.
  • End-to-end ownership — blood testing → diagnosis → prescribing → ongoing management, all in one system.
  • Men's-only specialism — not a generalist telehealth platform. Resonates with patients, credible niche for partners.
  • Proven UK patient model — de-risked. Partner inherits a working system.
  • Speed to revenue — operational in weeks, not months. That's the core white-label sell.
  • Bear Grylls brand halo — see below.
Bear Grylls / BGV angle: Bear's brand carries massive credibility with the exact demographic TH serves — 35–60, physically active or aspirationally so, achievement-oriented men. His endorsement removes the stigma barrier around TRT. White-label partners co-brand without paying for a celebrity ambassador. For military/veteran verticals, the ex-SAS credibility is unmatched. Caveat: use pre-recorded/archive Bear content only — no new filming commitments.

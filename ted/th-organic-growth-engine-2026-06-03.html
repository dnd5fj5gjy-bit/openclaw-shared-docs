01 · The Strategic Spine

One brand. Two audiences. Same posture.

Ted's Health is a regulated men's-health clinical service that grows organically by being credible in the rooms it shows up in. Two rooms matter: the room where patients are, and the room where partners are. Each room gets its own surface — but the operator behind both is the same operator.

The strategic one-liner
"A regulated men's-health clinic credible enough to be the patient's choice on Instagram and the partner's white-label engine on LinkedIn — built around the same protocol, the same doctor, the same governance pack."
Why this matters operationally. Two separate strategies would have meant two voices, two governance positions, two budgets. One engine with two surfaces means every clinical asset (Dr Andrews to camera, a protocol explainer, a patient-safety note) earns its place twice. The work compounds.

03 · Cross-Channel Mechanics

How IG and LinkedIn feed each other.

Two surfaces are only one engine if the work compounds. These are the four mechanics that take a single piece of work and earn its place on both channels.

Mechanic 01

Dr Andrews cross-cast

Every Dr Andrews Wednesday Reel is shot to camera as a clinical-authority asset. The same shoot produces a LinkedIn-native quote-card / longer caption version. One filming session, two channel-specific edits, both voices.

Mechanic 02

Partner proof lands on LinkedIn first

Signed partner stories, integration milestones, governance-pack updates — these break on LinkedIn (where the buyer audience reads it as proof). Two weeks later they become a single-card carousel on Instagram (where the consumer audience reads it as credibility).

Mechanic 03

Patient stories land on Instagram first

Composite anonymous patient journeys (the IG weeks-5-8 cluster) live natively on Instagram. The ones that resonate become Pillar 02 LinkedIn posts framed as "what the patient pathway actually looks like — for a buyer thinking about whether to put their brand on it".

Mechanic 04

Olly's personal account is the bridge

Olly's personal LinkedIn (separate doc) is the bridge surface. He reshares TH company-page posts with operator framing, and his personal IG presence — where it exists — backs the brand without competing with it. The personal account is the amplifier, not a third channel.

The rule. No content is created for one channel without the question "is the cross-cast obvious?" being asked. If the answer is no, the content still ships — but the question gets asked, so the easy compounding wins are not missed.

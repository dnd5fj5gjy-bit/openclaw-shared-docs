Personal Outreach Strategy

Onboarding LegitScript-certified UK partners to TH White-Label, founder to founder. No partnerships inbox. No vendor pitch.

Owner Olly Westmacott Drafted 15 May 2026 Wave 1 size ~10 partners
In one screen
  • Target the 14 LegitScript-certified partners only — LegitScript is the gating cert
  • Find the Founder / CEO / MD on LinkedIn; skip the partnerships inbox
  • LinkedIn DM first. Three touches, seven days apart, then park
  • Five new partners per week. Wave 1 closes in about four weeks
  • Every touch logged in the dashboard — single source of truth

Principle

Why personal, and why CEO-level

White-label is a commercial, brand-linked decision. It's not bought by procurement — it's decided by the founder, CEO or MD. So we skip the contact form on day one.

01

Speed

One conversation with the right person beats four weeks of being routed through partnerships inboxes.

02

Signal

Founder-to-business-lead approach reads as a strategic partnership, not a vendor pitch.

03

Control

You set the frame in the first message, before anyone slots TH into a category that doesn't fit.

The Sequence

Four steps, repeatable per partner

Step 1 · 10 min

Find the human

LinkedIn search. Target titles, in order: Founder, CEO, MD, Commercial Director, Head of Partnerships. Log name, title, LinkedIn URL, email in the dashboard.

Step 2

LinkedIn first

Send a connection request with a one-line note. If accepted within five days, send the full opener. If not, switch to email or send as InMail.

Step 3 · over 14 days

Three touches

Opener → seven-day follow-up → fourteen-day close. After touch three with no reply, status moves to "no response" and the lead rests for 90 days.

Step 4 · always

Log everything

Update the dashboard status, contact info, notes and next action after every touch. The dashboard drives Wave 2 prioritisation.

Cadence target: five new partners contacted per week (don't blast all ten in one day — stagger so follow-ups stay manageable). Wave 1 completes in roughly four weeks.

The Messages

Three-touch sequence

Templates below. Personalise the opening line for each partner — that's the bit that matters.

Touch 1 — Opener Day 0 · LinkedIn or email
Hi [first name], [Their company] keeps coming up as one of the few UK [pharmacy / men's health] businesses doing this properly — LegitScript-certified, good range, strong reputation. I lead market & strategy at Bear Grylls Ventures. We've built Ted's Health — Bear's men's health platform — and we're launching a white-label programme so a small number of partners can offer the Ted's Health experience under their own brand, with our clinical and supply infrastructure behind it. Worth a 20-min call to see if it's a fit? I'd rather come straight to you than route through partnerships. Olly
Touch 2 — Follow-up Day 7
Hi [first name] — circling back on the note above. Quick angle: we're picking the white-label launch partners now, and [Their company] is on a short list of ten. Happy to send a one-pager if it's easier than a call. Olly
Touch 3 — Close Day 14
[first name] — last one from me. If white-label isn't a fit for [Their company] right now no problem, just say and I'll close the loop. If it might be and the timing's off, tell me when to come back. Olly

Prep

Three things you'll be asked on the first call

Q1

What is white-label?

Their brand, your front-end. Ted's Health clinical infrastructure, supply and fulfilment behind it. They keep the patient relationship and the margin on top.

Q2

Commercials?

One-line on revenue share or fee structure. Don't wing it. Get the number from Calvin before Week 1.

Q3

Time to live?

From signature to partner running on TH-WL. Be honest. Calvin and engineering can give a real number.

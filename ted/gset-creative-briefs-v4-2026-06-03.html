Hard guardrail · across every brief

Hachette / Hodder AI policy applies absolutely. No AI-generated voice. No AI-generated imagery. No AI in covers or marketing creative. AI permitted only for backend processes (transcription, scheduling, analytics, draft outlining) — never the published artefact. No new Bear filming. Every clip, frame and voiceover that features Bear is pulled from the existing archive. Every editor signs both lines off before commission.

Month 2 is still the launch beat. v4 briefs do not move that anchor. Brief 10 (paid) goes live in Month 2 once organic baselines exist; Brief 11 (anchors) governs every seasonal moment from Lent onwards; Brief 12 (cohorts) takes over once Matthew (v2 B05 / v3 B04) closes; Brief 13 (church partners) runs continuously from Month 1; Brief 14 (international) opens in Month 6; Brief 15 (measurement) is live by end of Month 1 and runs forever after.

The six workstreams below extend the deck's Section 09 monthly volumes — they do not replace them. Volumes in each meta block are steady-state floors at the phase they activate. Calendar anchors raise the relevant volumes for the relevant fortnight; over-performance triggers a rebalance conversation, never a price uplift. Everything is sized to in-house Ku Creatives make capacity — brief by volume and spec, never by £ envelope.

Each brief assumes the v2 foundations: the Bear archive bundle, the brand identity system (v2 B07), the audio motif & mastering chain (v2 B08), the website + attribution layer (v2 B06), the voice + content-pillar guide. Where a v4 brief produces a feeder asset, it routes back into a v2 workstream cleanly.

Brief 10 · Paid media · the distribution layer

Meta + YouTube Paid System

The paid funnel that buys reach against the organic engine. Live from Month 2 once a first organic signal exists. Always testing, always within the AI guardrail.

Volume12 ad variants live at any time · 4 fresh creatives/mo
PlatformsMeta (FB+IG) · YouTube TrueView/In-feed
OwnerPaid lead + short-form editor (creative) + analyst (Brief 15)
SpendHodder budget — sits outside £5k/mo retainer · we manage

The funnel shape

  • Top — reach + sub: short-form variants from Brief 02 with retail-region geo-routing. Goal: cheap subscribed reach into Substack + YouTube + IG.
  • Mid — consideration: 60–90s narrative cuts of the Reading episodes (audiobook moments + scripture overlay), pointing at the full episode and Substack.
  • Bottom — retail conversion: testimony-led cards + audiogram excerpts pointing at the geo-routed retail tree on the website. Tagged with the attribution scheme from v2 B06.

The creative production rhythm

  • Four fresh ad creatives per month — two narrative (Reading-led), one testimony (from Brief 05 curation), one theologian one-liner (from Unpacking).
  • Each creative shipped in 9:16 + 1:1 + 16:9, UK + US text-card variants, three hook variants.
  • Always re-cut from existing material — never bespoke filmed. Paid creative is a recombination job, not a production job.
  • Hachette guardrail re-applied: no AI voice, no AI image, no AI-generated B-roll, no trending audio.

The testing protocol

  • 2-week cycles: launch fresh batch Mon week 1, review Fri week 2. Kill ≤0.6x ROAS, double-down on ≥1.6x.
  • Hook-length-platform matrix mirrors Brief 03 short-form testing — paid amplifies the formats that organic already validated.
  • Geo-locked from Day 1: UK and US run as separate ad accounts/regions with separate budget pots.
  • Calendar anchors (Lent, Easter, anniversary, US push, Advent) trigger spend pulses — see Brief 11 for the volume uplift.
Production pipeline: Mon — analyst (Brief 15) hands paid lead the week's top organic signals. Tue–Wed — short-form editor cuts the four fresh creatives + variants. Thu — paid lead loads, geo-tags, schedules. Fri — last-week review + kill/scale decisions. Continuous Hodder visibility via the weekly dashboard auto-email.
Sign-off: Operational on creative once Brief 11 anchors are pre-approved. Spend allocation reviewed monthly with Hodder POC. New retail destinations (i.e. anything not in the current retail tree) require POC approval before linking.
Brief 11 · Calendar anchors · seasonal uplift

Lent · Easter · Pentecost · Anniversary · Autumn Church Push · Advent

The six moments in the year that demand more output, not different output. Each anchor has a pre-approved content brief and a volume uplift on top of the Section 09 floor.

Anchors / year6 (Lent · Easter · Pentecost · 19 Jun anniversary · autumn · Advent)
Uplift volume+50% short-form, +1 long-form, +2 Substack /anchor fortnight
OwnerEditorial lead + community lead (cohort tie-ins)
ApprovalEach anchor's content brief signed off 2 weeks prior — Section 08 rule

The six anchors — what each one is

  • Lent (40 days, late Feb–Apr): daily devotional short-form series. Companion 40-day Substack track (gated to subscribers). Cohort tie-in: Matthew or current Gospel.
  • Holy Week + Easter (8 days): the biggest moment. 8-piece Substack series, daily short-form, one Reading special (Passion narrative), one Unpacking ("What the four Gospels say differently about the resurrection"). Paid spend pulse from Brief 10.
  • Pentecost (1 week): 7-piece Substack series on the Acts opening + early church. Cohort tie-in if Acts cohort is mid-flight.
  • 19 June anniversary (publication day): launch-day commemoration. Year-1 results piece on Substack, "reader of the year" testimony reel, one short Bear-channel uplift post (existing footage), retail spend pulse. The yearly Bear-channel moment per the project_gset_bear_role rule.
  • Autumn church push (Sep–Oct): Big Church Read / church discussion guide drop. Free PDF cohort guides released. Outreach pulse via Brief 13.
  • Advent (24 days): daily Substack devotional series. Daily short-form pulled from Reading episodes covering the Nativity. One Bear-channel uplift post (Christmas Eve) — the second yearly Bear moment.

What an anchor uplift looks like in practice

  • Steady-state monthly: 8 long-form, 36 short-form, 20 IG, 4+2 Substack. Anchor fortnight: 9 long-form, 54 short-form, 25+ IG, 6+ Substack.
  • Daily seasonal series (Lent, Advent) run inside the short-form lane as a dedicated daily track — they don't replace the discovery short-form, they sit beside it.
  • Each anchor has a free email-gated PDF discussion guide attached, released to the Substack list 48hrs early.
  • Brief 10 paid spend pulses by +40–60% across the anchor fortnight, weighted to the bottom-of-funnel testimony creative.

The pre-approval content brief — what gets signed off

  • Theological line for the anchor (e.g. Easter framing, Pentecost emphasis) — locked with Hodder editorial 2 weeks ahead.
  • Volume table for the fortnight per workstream.
  • Specific scripture moments / Reading-episode cuts used across the anchor.
  • Whether Bear's own channels get an uplift post (anniversary + Christmas Eve only — confirm clip selection with Bear's social team).
  • Paid spend pulse and creative slate.
Production pipeline: 6 weeks ahead — editorial lead drafts anchor content brief. 4 weeks ahead — Hodder editorial pass. 2 weeks ahead — anchor brief locked, sign-off recorded, all editors briefed. Anchor fortnight — uplift volumes ship per the brief, daily reporting to the dashboard. Anchor +1 week — post-mortem note added to Brief 15 monthly report.
Sign-off: Each anchor content brief pre-approved by Hodder POC per Section 08. Bear-channel uplift posts confirmed with Bear's social team before scheduling.
Brief 12 · Cohort series · Mark → Acts

The Four Cohorts After Matthew

Matthew is the launch cohort (v3 Brief 04). This is the rolling system that runs Mark, Luke, John and Acts after it — each a 5-week structured read, each compounding subscriber depth.

Volume1 cohort always live · 5 weeks each · Mark → Luke → John → Acts
Cadence~6-week gap between cohorts (5 wks live + 1 wk reset)
OwnerCommunity lead + Substack editor (cohort companion posts)
ReuseMatthew kit (v3 B04) is the template — same shape, fresh scripture

The repeating five-week structure

  • Wk 1 — Open: sign-up window closes, welcome email sequence fires (5 emails, day 1–5), first weekly live thread Thursday.
  • Wks 2–4 — Read: daily passage emails (Substack cohort-only segment), two cohort companion essays per week, weekly live thread Thursday, mid-cohort short-form pulse.
  • Wk 5 — Close: graduation thread, "what this Gospel changed for you" testimony curation pulse (feeds Brief 05), early sign-up window opens for the next cohort.
  • +1 reset week: swap kit assets (banner, calendar, weekly threads, passage emails) for next Gospel. Roll learnings from last cohort's post-mortem into next kit.

The four cohort kits

  • Mark — "The urgent Gospel": framed for first-time readers and busy professionals — 16 chapters, fastest read. Cohort emphasis on pace, action verbs, the shortest Gospel as the entry point.
  • Luke — "The Gospel of the outsider": framed for the spiritually-fatigued + the Bible-intimidated. Cohort emphasis on the parables (Good Samaritan, Prodigal Son), women and outsiders in Luke's account.
  • John — "The Gospel of meaning": framed for the lapsed and the philosophically-inclined. Cohort emphasis on the I AM statements, signs, and the prologue.
  • Acts — "The story keeps going": framed as the bridge from Gospel to lived faith. Cohort emphasis on Pentecost, Paul, the early church. Sequenced for autumn so it overlaps the Pentecost anchor.

Each kit's contents (cloned from Matthew template)

  • Cohort landing page (Substack section), banner artwork (Brief 07 templated), 5-week reading calendar PDF
  • 35 daily passage emails (one per day, 200–300 words framing + the passage)
  • 5 weekly thread prompts, 10 cohort companion essays (Substack cohort-only), graduation thread + testimony prompt
  • Welcome email sequence (5 emails) + early sign-up reminder sequence (3 emails for the next cohort)
  • Short-form pulse pack: 5 cohort-themed clips per cohort, ahead of week 1 (re-cut from Reading episodes covering that Gospel)

Year-1 cohort calendar (illustrative)

  • Month 2 — Matthew opens (v3 B04 kit)
  • Month 4 — Mark opens
  • Month 6 — Luke opens (anchored against autumn church push)
  • Month 8 — John opens
  • Month 10 — Acts opens (anchored against the next Pentecost if calendar permits)
  • Year 2 — repeat with refreshed companion essays + testimonies
Production pipeline: 6 weeks before each cohort opens — community lead and Substack editor clone the Matthew template, swap scripture + companion essays, brief Brief 07 designer for banner refresh. 4 weeks ahead — Hodder editorial pass on companion essays. 2 weeks ahead — early sign-up opens to current cohort + Substack list. Week 5 of previous cohort — sign-up opens publicly + short-form pulse fires.
Sign-off: Each cohort's companion essay slate edited by Hodder per Section 08. Cohort calendar pre-approved annually with Hodder POC.
Brief 13 · Church partnerships · institutional outreach

Big Church Read + Church Partner Engine

The institutional channel — pastors, small-group leaders, church comms managers, theological colleges. Continuous from Month 1, scaling steadily. Not paid media. Earned relationships and free useful tools.

Volume10 partner conversations/mo · 4 free tools/year · 1 monthly leaders' digest
OwnerPartnerships lead (part-time, ~12 hrs/wk) + community lead
Free toolsDiscussion guides · sermon companion · cohort kit · Lent/Advent guides
ChannelsEmail · LinkedIn · church-leader Facebook group · industry events

What "partnership" means in practice

  • Free email-gated discussion guides for the book, released at each calendar anchor (Lent, Easter, Pentecost, Advent) — ungated for any church that wants to use them.
  • Cohort kit shareable with churches running their own read-along (the v3 B04 / Brief 12 kit, repackaged as a "run this in your church" pack).
  • Sermon companion notes — one-page outline notes for each Gospel, designed for pastors preparing sermons on those passages.
  • Monthly leaders' digest email (separate Substack section / list) — what's coming, free resources, reader stories from other churches.
  • Theological college outreach — offering free copies + discussion guides for "Gospels in popular culture" / "evangelism in contemporary publishing" modules.

The outreach cadence

  • 10 partner conversations/month: warm outbound to UK + US pastors, denominational comms leads, parachurch organisations (UCB, Premier, Hope Together, Alpha, US equivalents). Track in a simple CRM (Airtable / Notion) — name, denomination, region, last touch, status.
  • 1 monthly leaders' digest: sent first Monday of the month to the partner list + opt-in church leaders. ~600 words, plain, no merch.
  • Quarterly partner spotlight: Substack reader-letter format featuring a church or leader who's used the book. Feeds Brief 04.
  • 1 industry event/quarter: conference attendance or sponsored side-room — partnerships lead represents. Existing Bear archive talks usable as session content.

The four free tools / year

  • Lent discussion guide — 40 sessions, mapped to the Lent Substack track
  • Easter sermon companion — Passion narrative across the four Gospels, with cross-references
  • Cohort run-it-yourself pack — full 5-week kit for any church to run a Matthew (or any Gospel) read-along
  • Advent family devotional — 24 days, designed for use in homes and small groups, plus a church-service liturgy version
Production pipeline: ongoing — partnerships lead works the CRM weekly, books calls, tracks status. Mon week 1 — monthly leaders' digest ships. Quarterly — partner spotlight + free tool releases. Continuous — feedback from partners feeds testimony curation (Brief 05) and Substack reader letters (v2 B04).
Sign-off: Partner outreach copy and free tools approved by Hodder POC on first release; subsequent personalised emails operational. Endorsement asks (e.g. denominational leader quote) routed through Hodder.
Brief 14 · International · Spanish · Portuguese · Brazil pilot

International Expansion — The Month 6 Stand-Up

The deck's Month 6+ phase. Three new language/region pilots without a separate retainer line. Lean — translated content, partner-led distribution, lightweight local channels.

ActivateMonth 6 (Spanish + Portuguese channels) · Month 7 (Brazil Substack pilot)
Volume Y14 long-form/mo per language · 12 short-form/mo per language · monthly Substack
OwnerInternational producer (PT-time per language) + translation partner
TranslationExcluded from £5k/mo retainer · Hodder budget line per deck Section 11

What each language stands up

  • YouTube channel — translated audiobook tracks (where rights permit) over the existing Reading visual template. Translated end-card scripture overlays.
  • Substack section — translated thematic articles (2/mo) + one translated theologian essay. Original locale-specific reader letters where possible.
  • Short-form lane — 12 clips/mo per language, translated captions + region-specific text cards, scheduled to local prime times.
  • Local partnership — at least one institutional partner per region (publishing imprint, church network, theological college) identified by month 5.

What Brazil-pilot looks like

  • Substack-led, not video-led. Cheaper to test, faster to read demand.
  • Translated thematic articles + Pentecost / Easter anchor companions in PT-BR.
  • Local reader-testimony curation (Brief 05 process, PT-BR consent flow).
  • Decision point at Month 9 — if Substack growth crosses a defined threshold, stand up the YouTube channel; if not, archive cleanly.

Quality + voice

  • Translation by humans, not AI (Hachette policy applies absolutely — published copy never AI-generated).
  • Native speaker / theological reviewer in each language signs off the launch slate before publish.
  • Voice rules from v2 B04 carry across translated: warm, direct, invitational, never preachy.
  • Imagery and brand identity (v2 B07) cloned exactly — no regional re-brand.

What we are explicitly not doing

  • Not standing up paid media in international regions until organic baselines exist (mirror UK/US logic from Brief 10).
  • Not commissioning new Bear filming for any region — archive only.
  • Not launching all three regions on day 1 — Spanish and Portuguese in Month 6, Brazil pilot in Month 7 to keep producer load real.
Production pipeline: Month 4 — international producer scoped + translation partner shortlisted. Month 5 — local partnerships identified, launch slate translated. Month 6 — Spanish + Portuguese channels open with 4 episodes + 12 clips + first Substack post live. Month 7 — Brazil Substack pilot opens. Month 9 — Brazil decision point.
Sign-off: International launch slate approved by Hodder POC. Translation partner contracts sit with Hodder (not BGV). Ongoing translated content operational once launch slate is locked.
Brief 15 · Measurement · the reporting loop

The Monthly Hodder Report + Always-On Dashboard

The engine has to be legible to Hodder — not as a vanity scoreboard, but as the case for what's working and what should change. Built in Month 1, live by end of Month 1, runs forever after.

CadenceWeekly auto-email · monthly report deck · quarterly review meeting
OwnerAnalyst (part-time, ~12 hrs/wk) + Olly review
StackLooker / Metabase + Substack API + YouTube Studio API + Meta Insights API
SLAWeekly email Mon AM · monthly deck first Tue of new month

The four signals that matter

  • Subscribed reach — Substack subs, YT subs, IG follows, FB Page + Group members. Net new, not vanity total. Cohort breakdowns.
  • Engagement quality — Substack open rate + paid conversion rate, YT average view duration + retention, IG save+share ratio. Comment quality flagged by community lead.
  • Retail attribution — UTM-tagged clicks from each surface (v2 B06), conversion rate where the retailer surfaces it (Amazon Attribution where possible). Weekly geo split.
  • Cohort completion — read-along signups, week-4 retention, graduation rate, testimony volume produced.

What the weekly email contains

  • Top-line numbers — 4 signals above, last 7 days vs trailing 28d average
  • One "what's working" callout — fastest-growing format or piece
  • One "what's not" callout — biggest miss + the hypothesis for next week
  • Calendar anchor / cohort status if mid-flight
  • One specific decision the analyst would make if running the engine alone — flagged so Hodder sees the operational instinct, not just the data

What the monthly deck contains

  • Section 09 volumes shipped vs target (per workstream)
  • Engagement deltas vs last month — what moved and why
  • Retail attribution summary — UK + US split, per-channel attribution share
  • Cohort report — sign-ups, completion, testimonies
  • Brief 10 paid efficiency — spend, ROAS, fresh creative slate
  • Next month's adjust calls — what we're changing, what we're holding
  • One open question for Hodder — what we'd like a steer on

The quarterly review meeting

  • 30 min, virtual, Olly + Hodder POC + analyst
  • Three slides only: what worked, what didn't, what's next
  • Output: one written decision memo signed off by both sides, filed in the content library

What we don't measure (deliberately)

  • Vanity reach metrics that don't tie back to engagement or retail
  • Per-post performance below the trend line — noise, not signal
  • Cross-platform follower overlap until Year 2 — premature for what it costs to compute
Production pipeline: Month 1 wk 2 — dashboard stack stood up, APIs connected. Month 1 wk 3 — first internal dry-run weekly email. Month 1 wk 4 — first weekly email shipped to Hodder POC. End of Month 2 — first full monthly deck shipped. Month 3 — first quarterly review meeting scheduled.
Sign-off: Dashboard + weekly + monthly templates pre-approved by Hodder POC in Month 1. Ongoing reports operational. Quarterly review decisions co-signed.

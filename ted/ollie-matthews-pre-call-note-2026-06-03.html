Where you actually are

What you've got is more than you're giving yourself credit for.

OJay Health is a credible, established 1-1 functional health coaching practice. You've got the proper credentials, a strong founder story, sixteen named-client testimonials including recognisable names, and a working lead magnet in the Optimal Health Test. The product is solid. The brand is solid.

What you don't have yet is a repeatable marketing system. Right now everything depends on you personally being visible, posting, replying, taking calls. So when you get busy or tired, the pipeline goes quiet. That's the thing behind the message — it isn't really a lack of capital, it's a lack of structure.

The honest reframe

Investor might be the wrong frame here.

Worth pressure-testing what you'd actually get from an investor before going to look for one. The maths usually doesn't land for a service business that depends on the founder's time — and what you're describing wanting them to fund, you can fund yourself out of monthly profit.

What "find an investor" really means

Giving up ownership to buy a marketer.

Investors want equity in exchange for capital, and they want a return. A 1-1 coaching practice doesn't scale without you in it — so there's no obvious exit, which makes it unattractive to most investors and expensive to anyone who is willing. You'd dilute the business you built to fund something you can run for under £2k a month.

What you actually need

A funded marketing function, paid from profit.

Productise the offer so it converts without you in every call. Run one clean funnel end-to-end. Hire one operator on a retainer or profit-share to run it. You keep 100% of the business, and the system pays for itself within a few months. The "marketing engine" you're describing — you just labelled it "investor."

First steps · next 30 days

Where I'd start, in this order.

Five concrete moves. Each one removes a specific blocker. The point is you don't have to wait for capital or hire a whole team — these are all things you can start this week.

01

Publish pricing on the site.

Right now the site sends every interested person to "Book a call" with no price. That filters out everyone who isn't ready to commit to an unknown number, and it makes you personally sell every single lead. Publish three tiers — even just a starting-from price — so high-intent people self-qualify before they get to you.

Why first: this single change halves your sales-call load and doubles call-show quality.
02

Build the funnel around the Optimal Health Test.

The lead magnet already exists and it's good. What's missing is what happens after someone takes it. Add a five-email nurture sequence that walks them from "I scored badly" to "book the call," plus an automatic call-booking link inside the result page. This is the spine of the whole system.

Why next: you already have the asset — you just need to build the conveyor belt behind it.
03

Pick one channel. Run it properly.

Instagram is the right channel for your audience of high-performing men. Three posts a week, one format (reels works), one hook style, one CTA pointing to the Optimal Health Test. Stop scattering across LinkedIn, Facebook, blog. One channel done well beats four done badly every time.

Why: focus is the only thing that creates compounding. Spreading thin is what's making it feel overwhelming.
04

Hire one operator — not a whole team.

A part-time VA or content-led freelancer at £800–£1,500 / month who runs the IG calendar, replies to DMs, follows up unbooked leads, and reports weekly. Pay them retainer plus a small per-client kicker. You stay the face; they run the engine. This is what "an investor would let me hire" really looks like — you just fund it from cashflow.

Why: removes the founder bottleneck without ceding ownership. Reversible if it doesn't work.
05

Track one number weekly.

Leads → calls booked → clients signed, every Monday. That's it. No dashboards. The point is to give you a single signal of whether the system is working, so you stop feeling reactive and start feeling in control. Decisions about what to change come from this number, not from how busy you feel.

Why last: until 1–4 are in place, there's nothing to measure. Once they are, this is the steering wheel.
The bigger question

If you actually want scale, you have to productise.

The above gets you a steady client flow inside 1-1 coaching. But if the real ambition is the kind of business an investor would back, that means moving beyond pure 1-1 — a group programme, a course, a productised diagnostic package — anything that doesn't trade your hours for revenue. That's a different conversation, and a much bigger one. Worth flagging here so you can think about it, but probably not the focus of our first call.

Two paths worth considering

Grow it on your own terms, plug into ours, or both.

Two clean options. Most people end up at a hybrid, but they only get there if they see both laid out. Path A is the foundation either way — Path B doesn't work without it.

Path A

Grow OJay Health on your own terms.

The five-step system above. No investor, no dilution. You use cashflow to fund growth and stay 100% owner.

  • Publish pricing — stop being the only filter
  • Build the funnel behind the Optimal Health Test
  • Pick one channel (IG short form) and run it weekly
  • Hire one part-time operator at ~£1k/m + kicker
  • Track leads → calls → clients every Monday
My take: this is the floor. Six months of focused execution and you have a calm, predictable practice — and a base from which any next move is cheap to fund.
Path B

White-label with Ted's Health.

You plug OJay into BGV's regulated men's health infrastructure. We provide the clinical engine; you keep your brand, your audience, your customer relationship.

  • Adds a productised, prescription-grade tier above 1-1 coaching
  • Captures clients you currently lose to "I'd need TRT for that"
  • No CQC build, no GMC hiring, no fulfilment headache
  • Recurring revenue share that scales without your hours
  • Compliance, doctors, intake, CRM — all handled
My take: this is the scale tier. The thing an investor would have funded — except you don't need an investor because it's already built.
The smart play

Path A first. Then Path B as the lever.

My honest answer is you probably want both, in order. Path A is the foundation — you need a working funnel, a clear offer and one operator running it before anything bolts on top. Once that's humming (call it 60–90 days), Path B becomes the productised scale tier: same funnel, same audience, but now an OJay × Ted's Health clinical pathway sits above the 1-1 coaching. That stack is what gives you a constant client stream, a scalable revenue line, and the freedom to either reinvest in growth or — if you still want to — have a much more interesting conversation with an investor, because now there's something investable to back.

Ted's Health · White-Label Partnerships

Sales Call & Onboarding Guide

Everything needed to run a white-label partner sales call end-to-end, and what happens next once they say yes - from first conversation to a live branded clinic.

Overview

What the white-label offer actually is

Ted's Health is the clinical engine. A partner (clinic, gym, diagnostics provider, or community) puts their own brand on top and keeps the patient relationship. We build the platform, run the clinical pathway, and handle prescribing - they never have to build any of it themselves.

1

We build and run the clinical platform

Full white-label infrastructure under their brand - intake, triage, eligibility screening, clinical review by GMC-registered doctors, and prescribing coordination. Already built, nothing for the partner to create.

2

We get the product to market

The pathway is configured and embedded for their specific audience and business type - clinic, gym, diagnostics, or community. Their brand is front and centre; Ted's Health is the engine behind it.

3

We intake and manage the patients

Once men are in the pathway, Ted's Health handles the full clinical journey - consultation, prescribing, repeat prescriptions, fulfilment, retention. The partner keeps the trusted front-end relationship; they don't manage patients.

Part One

The Sales Call Script

Run in order. Don't skip to pricing before objections are handled - the close only works once there's a verbal yes in principle.

1. Discovery - get them to confess the need

Opening question - let them talk, don't interrupt. The goal is to hear them say, unprompted, that they have men in their world who need this.

Say

"Yeah so I just wanted to start and ask - what actually made you want to have this conversation today?"

Follow-up probes - use 1–3, not all:

"Do you feel you're already seeing your customers or community members asking about testosterone, ED, energy levels - anything in that space? Even quietly?"

"If you were to open up a pathway tomorrow - even informally - do you think men in your world would actually use it?"

"And if this worked - do you see this as a serious revenue line for the business, or more of an added-value thing for your audience?"

Reconfirmation before moving on - don't proceed until they say yes. This is their verbal commitment that the conversation is worth having.

"Okay great - so just to make sure I've got this right: you've got [X type of audience], you're already seeing [signal of demand], and you do believe there's a real commercial case here. Is that a fair summary?"

2. The Three Pillars - what Ted's Health actually does

"So I'm sure you've seen the videos, but let me explain what we actually provide - I'll keep it to three things."

Walk through Pillars 1–3 above, then pause and check in:

"Does that make sense as a model? Do you have any questions on how it actually works before we keep going?"

3. Objection handling

Don't rush to this - let objections surface naturally after the Three Pillars. If they don't, prompt:

"Is there anything about the model that feels like it might not work for your business?"

"I don't want to become a clinic / take on regulatory burden"

"You're not becoming a clinic. You're not prescribing anything. You're not making clinical decisions. Your role is exactly what you already do - hold the relationship with your audience. Ted's Health holds the CQC registration, employs the doctors, and manages the clinical pathway. You bring the men. We bring the infrastructure."

"Could this damage my brand?"

"That's the right question to ask - and it's exactly why we're selective about who we work with. The risk of doing this badly is real. The risk of doing it properly, through a CQC-registered, doctor-led service, is actually the opposite - it builds trust. Men are already searching for this. The question is whether they find a responsible pathway through your brand, or they end up on a random forum."

"I'm not sure my audience actually wants this"

"Men don't tend to talk about this openly - that's the nature of it. But privately, they're searching. The demand is already there. What most businesses find is that once there's a credible pathway, the uptake is faster than expected - because you're not creating demand, you're just giving existing demand somewhere to go."

"I need to think about it / speak to my business partner"

"Totally fair. Who else needs to be in the room for a decision like this? If it makes sense to get them on a second call, let's do that - I'd rather take an extra week and have the right people involved than move forward with half the picture."

"What does this cost me?"

"Good - let's get into that. Before I do, can I just confirm: if the numbers made sense, is there anything else that would stop you moving forward?" (Flush remaining objections before moving into negotiation.)

4. Negotiation - commission and pricing

Only enter this section once all objections are handled and there's a clear verbal yes to moving forward in principle.

"Okay - so let's talk about the commercial structure."

Frame it correctly first

"The first thing to know is there's no setup fee and no monthly platform cost. You're not paying anything upfront. The model is purely performance-based - you only pay when patients convert onto treatment. That means there's no financial risk in getting started."

Explain the structure - it's two-tier

"Pricing works in two tiers, depending on the item. For medication - TRT and tadalafil - you buy each item from us at a wholesale price, which is our cost plus a 37% uplift. You then set your own patient-facing price with no restriction at all, and you keep everything above that wholesale price. Our income on medication is fixed the moment you buy it - it doesn't depend on what you charge the patient. For everything else - consultations, blood tests, phlebotomy, consumables - you're supplied at cost, and the existing margin split applies: you keep 63% of whatever markup you add on top, we take 37%. Sell at cost and you pay us nothing beyond cost."

Worked example, if useful

"To make that concrete - say a medication item costs us £140.41. Your buy price would be £192.36. If you then sell it to the patient at £150, we're still fine - we already collected our wholesale price at the point you bought it. The only way we're ever out of pocket is if you price below our own cost, and even then it's only the gap, never the full value of the product."

No floor, no branded ED meds

"Two other things worth knowing: there's no minimum patient price - you're completely free to set your own retail price, we don't restrict or control what you charge. And branded ED medications like Viagra and Cialis aren't part of the range - tadalafil, the generic, is what's available."

Anchor on value, not cost

"Think about what one retained patient on a 12-month TRT programme is worth to your business. Even a small percentage of your existing audience converting would create a meaningful recurring revenue stream - without you having built any clinical infrastructure to get there, which in itself is a lot of time and money."

Handle pricing pushback

"What's your concern - is it the wholesale price on medication, the margin split on services, or the retail price you think you can charge your audience?" (Isolate the specific objection and address it directly.)

5. Close

"Well, thanks so much for taking the call today - and based on everything we've talked about, does this feel like something worth exploring properly?"

Part Two

Client Onboarding - 6 Steps

Once a partner says yes, this is the self-serve flow they go through to launch their branded clinic on the platform.

1

Name your clinic

Partner enters their clinic name. Used across the platform - patient dashboard, emails, login page. Can be changed later in settings.

2

Pick your URL

Partner chooses the platform URL/subdomain their clinic will live on and that patients will use to access it.

3

Branding - make it yours

Upload logo (landscape, ideally 600×200, PNG/SVG, max 2MB) and favicon (32×32, ICO/PNG/SVG). Set primary colour, accent colour, sidebar/header colour, button background and button text colour - with a live preview showing how each choice looks across the dashboard. All shown wherever patients see the clinic: login page, dashboard, emails. Editable later in Clinic Settings.

4

Create admin account

Partner sets up the account they'll use to sign in and manage the clinic - first name, last name, work email, password (min. 8 characters). Teammates can be invited later. Continuing agrees to data processing for clinic setup; full terms come at the next step.

5

Connect Ryft payment account

Partner connects a Ryft account so they get paid out through the platform - this is how their share of revenue from converted patients actually reaches them.

Reference

Commercial Model - Quick Reference

Key terms

  • No setup fee - nothing paid upfront by the partner.
  • No monthly platform cost - purely performance-based.
  • Pay only on conversion - partner is charged a cost price per treatment, only once a patient converts onto treatment.
  • Partner sets retail price - full control over what patients are charged.
  • Margin split - confirmed 63/37: partner keeps 63% of the margin above cost price, Ted's Health takes 37%.
  • Full cost breakdown - sits in the commercial proposal sent after the call, not disclosed in full on the call itself.

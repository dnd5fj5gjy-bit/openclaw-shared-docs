1 · Strategy

What Olly's feed is for

LinkedIn is not a personal brand exercise. It's the credibility surface his outbound lands against. Every post needs to read like it was written by someone the partner already wants to take a call with.

Positioning · one sentence

"An operator inside BGV building regulated, partnership-led health businesses — Ted's Health, Modern Savage, GSET."

Not "growth marketer." Not "founder." Not "thought leader." Operator — the word senior partners respect. Inside BGV — borrows the Bear halo without performing it. Regulated, partnership-led — the lane that matters to Pharmica / Well / Third Space / Equinox. Named businesses — concrete, not abstract.

Why LinkedIn specifically
vs Instagram

Wrong audience

IG is consumer reach for TH (already running, separate plan). Partners don't open it. Use IG for product audience; LinkedIn for the people who sign deals.

vs X

Wrong tone

X rewards loud takes. The WL audience reads X-loud as a red flag. LinkedIn lets him sound thoughtful without performing it.

vs Substack

Wrong frequency

Substack is a quarterly artefact. LinkedIn is the weekly drumbeat — same surface as the Touch 1 → Touch 3 window. Add Substack later if a long-form essay earns one.

3 · Five Content Pillars

The frame every post sits inside

Each post belongs to one pillar. The ratio across a month is the discipline — not "post when inspired". Pillar 1 carries the most weight; pillar 5 keeps the algorithm fed without burning credibility.

1 · Operator perspective OP

~35% · ~1 post / week

What Olly is learning right now building TH WL / GSET / MS. Specific, in-the-moment, with the lesson at the end. Highest leverage — partners read it and think "he's actually doing this."

Audience servedPartners + BGV peers
Works whenThe detail is concrete
Skip whenYou'd be inventing a lesson

2 · Sharp lane opinions POV

~25% · 1 post / week

Opinionated takes on men's health, brand-building inside a portfolio, regulated growth, the D2C → clinical-credibility shift. Earns the algorithm and earns partner respect. Strong view, calmly held.

Audience servedPartners + industry peers
Works whenYou disagree with consensus
Skip whenIt's a take you don't actually hold

3 · Behind-the-scenes BTS

~15% · ~1 post / fortnight

Building-in-public glimpses — a deal that almost happened, a pivot, an honest miss. Trust builders. Used sparingly; over-used they become content marketing.

Audience servedPeers, future hires, ecosystem
Works whenThere's a real lesson, not a vibe
Skip whenIt crosses into venting

4 · Partner / category POV CAT

~15% · ~1 post / fortnight

Observations about the industries TH WL sells into — pharmacy economics, clinic operations, gym monetisation, the publishing-to-audience handoff. Positions Olly as someone who understands the partner's world before he asks for time.

Audience servedSpecific target partner segments
Works whenYou spot a pattern they live with
Skip whenIt reads like a pitch dressed up

5 · Engagement / question ENG

~10% · 1 post / 3 weeks

Short, low-stakes prompts. A question to the network, a poll, a "what would you do here". Algorithm fuel that doesn't dilute the feed. Never the lead post in a week.

Audience servedThe whole network
Works whenThe question is genuinely open
Skip whenIt's a rhetorical setup for your view

A small thing that's working better than it should: writing every brief twice.
A small thing that's working better than it should: writing every brief twice. First version: what we want. Long, detailed, with all the context. Sent to the team that'll execute. Second version: one paragraph. What the brief is actually trying to achieve, in language a smart outsider could understand. Sent to no one — kept at the top of the doc.

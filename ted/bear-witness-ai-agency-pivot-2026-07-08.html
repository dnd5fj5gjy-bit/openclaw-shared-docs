Bear Witness · The Pivot · Business Plan

One business: build agents for people

Jesse's steer, turned into a plan. Bear Witness stops selling two things and sells one - engineered AI agents, built into any business, the same way they already run ours. This is the case for making that the whole focus, and what it takes to bring it to market.

Read the plan
01 · Executive summary

Bear Witness becomes an AI agent consultancy. One product, one pitch, one motion.

Jesse's call, from a conversation with Olly: instead of Bear Witness selling two separate things - a marketing retainer and an installable agent product - it sells one thing, properly. We build named, engineered AI agents into a business, the same way we've already built them into ours. A business owner doesn't buy "AI agents" in the abstract; they buy back their own time, or a colleague they couldn't otherwise afford to hire. Marketing was never the business - it was the first proof case, running inside GSET, Ted's Health and Modern Savage. This plan makes that proof case one example among many, not the whole offer.

What changes

From two streams to one

The 6 July business plan ran two parallel streams - an installed agent product and a managed marketing service - and flagged the confusion between them as a real risk. This plan resolves it: there is one product, engineered agents, sold at three depths. Marketing is a proven vertical inside it, not a separate business.

What stays true

Zero new capital, zero new hires

Nothing about team, infrastructure or capability changes. Three people - Jesse, Olly, Felix - still run this on the same rented multi-tenant cloud stack, the same Claude API, the same live proof already running inside BGV.

What's new

A market big enough to be worth the focus

The agentic AI market is worth roughly $9.9bn globally in 2026, forecast to grow at 42-50% CAGR through the early 2030s - and professional-services adoption specifically (consulting, legal, accounting) is the fastest-growing segment, forecast at 46.3% CAGR. This is a bigger, faster-growing wave to ride than the UK marketing-agency market alone.

Why this is the right call, not just a simpler story: Bear Witness's actual advantage was never "we're good at marketing." It's that AI does the labour, so taking on a new client costs engineering time, not a hire. That advantage transfers to any function a business would otherwise pay a person or an agency for - not just marketing. Naming the business what it actually is widens the market without changing a single thing about how the team works.

02 · Why this pivot, why now

The market is moving faster than the marketing-agency category ever did.

The UK marketing agencies market - the frame the previous business plan sized itself against - is growing at a steady but unremarkable 4.82% CAGR. The market this pivot points at is growing 8-10x faster, and it's still early enough that "we already run this on ourselves" is a genuinely rare claim, not a marketing line.

Every business owner has the same problem marketing had inside BGV: more work than hours, and hiring as the only lever anyone reaches for. That problem isn't unique to marketing - it's universal, and it's the one Bear Witness is already equipped to solve at scale.

03 · The offer

One product. Three depths. Priced like headcount, because that's what it replaces.

A business doesn't need to understand AI to buy this - it needs to describe a role it wishes it could clone or afford to hire for. That's a founder question, not a technical one, which is what makes it sellable to any business owner, in any sector, not just BGV's existing brand-safe circle.

The old framing

Two products, competing for attention

  • Stream 1: Agent Workforce - an installed product, needing new infrastructure before external sale
  • Stream 2: AI-Integrated Agency - a marketing retainer, ready to sell today
  • Two pitches, two buyer types, two sets of collateral - and the 6 July plan itself flagged "confusing them is the easiest way to slow both down"
The pivot

One product, three depths

  • Single Agent - one engineered role, the entry point, fastest to sell and prove
  • Team Agents - two to five agents coordinating across a function, marketing being the proven example
  • Full Workforce - a whole operation run alongside the human one, priced against headcount displaced

Marketing doesn't disappear - it becomes the reference case. "We already run a Marketing Agent Team on our own brands" is the single best proof point in every pitch, whatever role the prospect is actually buying for. It just stops being sold as a separate business line with its own pricing logic.

04 · Live proof

We're not describing this. We're running it.

Four named, engineered agents already run inside Bear Witness's own team today, each built around a specific role - not a shared chatbot, a dedicated colleague per function. Every pitch can open with "here's our own agent team, running our own business, right now."

Proven vertical

Content, paid media, reporting

Runs live across GSET, Ted's Health and Modern Savage. This is the Marketing Agent Team example - one of many roles this can be engineered around, not the whole product.

Proven pattern

Partner and customer ops

Onboarding flows, referral tracking, follow-up sequences running for Ted's Health's white-label pipeline - proof this extends past marketing into sales and operations roles.

Proven pattern

Admin, scheduling, personal assistance

Inbox triage, invoicing, receipt logging, calendar management - the exact shape of a Single Agent sale to a solo founder or small team, already running inside BGV's own operations.

05 · Market and target customer

Any business owner who's said "I need another pair of hands" - and can afford to buy one.

The ICP widens under this pivot rather than narrows: it's no longer "a mid-size brand with a marketing budget line," it's any founder or small operator who currently either does a job themselves that's capping their growth, or is paying agency/consultant day rates for work AI can now do at a fraction of the cost.

Segment one

Solo founders and small teams

Capped by their own hours, not their ambition. The Single Agent tier - one role, engineered and deployed - is the natural entry point: low commitment, immediate and legible time-back.

Segment two

Growing operators buying agency/consultant time

Already paying £500-£2,500+/day for consultants, or £25k-150k for AI implementation projects (UK 2026 benchmarks) - the Team Agents tier undercuts this dramatically while delivering a built, running product rather than a report.

Segment three

Larger operators with real headcount to displace

Businesses spending £75k-250k on full AI production implementations, or carrying salaried teams for functions that don't need to be human-run - the Full Workforce tier, priced against what it replaces.

UK AI consultancy benchmarks from Helium42 "AI Consultancy Pricing UK 2026," OpenClaw Consultant's "AI Agent Costs UK 2026," and Nicola Lazzari's "AI Consultant Hourly Rate UK 2026," cross-checked against Bluecanvas and KlarifAi's 2026 pricing guides.

Why this ICP works better than the marketing-only version: the pitch no longer depends on a prospect already having a marketing budget line - it depends on them having any role, function or team they'd hire for if cost and hours weren't the constraint. That's a far larger, faster-qualifying conversation for Olly to open.

06 · Competitive landscape

Not an agency, not a consultancy, not a SaaS tool. A built, running colleague.

Three categories sit around this offer, and the position only makes sense set against all three at once.

AI consultancies

  • Sell diagnosis and strategy, not a working product - £12k-35k strategy workshops, £25k-85k proofs of concept
  • Big Four and mid-tier firms charge £900-3,000+/day, with 40-60% hidden costs on top for data/infra work
  • Client is left holding a roadmap, still needs someone to build and run it

Point-solution AI tools

  • Software, not an outcome - the client still configures and operates it themselves
  • One tool per task; the client carries the cost of stitching several into one working function
  • No accountability for whether the output is actually good, only that the tool ran
Bear Witness

Built, running, accountable

  • Ships a working, named agent - not a report or a raw tool - engineered for one role, live from day one
  • Priced like a hire, not a project: build fee plus a retainer, not a scoped consulting engagement
  • Live proof running our own business, on every call, before a single external client is signed

The moat is cost-to-serve, not the category. Because AI does the labour, Bear Witness can build and run an agent for a fraction of what a consultancy charges just to scope one, and still hold a strong margin. Neither a day-rate consultancy nor a tool vendor can match that without becoming a different kind of business.

08 · Go-to-market plan

Same two mechanisms as before - now pointed at one product, not two.

The bootstrap and scale channels already planned for Stream 2 carry over unchanged - they just sell Single Agent / Team Agents / Full Workforce instead of a marketing retainer specifically.

Stage one · Bootstrap, warm channels

Landing client #1

Hodder Faith and the GSET publishing network, Ted's Health white-label prospects, Modern Savage supplier contacts, and Alfred as a named proof case - each used once to skip months of cold outreach while the repeatable channel gets built. The pitch is now "what role would you hire for," not "do you want a marketing retainer" - a wider door into the same relationships.

Stage two · Scalable, repeatable

Client Acquisition Engine

Paid ads plus an AI nurture agent, feeding qualified leads into the three tiers. Unchanged from the prior plan - it's the mechanism that has to carry growth from client #2 onward, because warm connections run out and cannot be manufactured to order.

One extra advantage the pivot creates: the pitch no longer needs to qualify a prospect as "has a marketing budget line" before it's worth a conversation - any founder describing a role they can't afford to hire for qualifies. That's a shorter qualification step for every warm intro and every paid lead.

09 · Risks and mitigations

The honest list, not the flattering one.

Engineering single point of failure

Felix is the only builder

Likelihood: highImpact: high
Risk

This risk gets sharper under the pivot, not smaller - every new client now needs a build, where the marketing-retainer stream needed none. Every Single Agent, Team Agents or Full Workforce sale depends on Felix's hours until the build process is documented into something repeatable.

Mitigation

Owner: Felix. Document the build process into a templated playbook before selling at volume - this is now the single highest-priority piece of work in the plan, not a background task. Target: a first-cut playbook from the next build, refined on the second and third.

Client concentration

One paying external client today (Alfred)

Likelihood: highImpact: high
Risk

Alfred remains the only proof case outside BGV, and was proven under the marketing-retainer framing, not yet as an "agent build." Losing it removes the entire external evidence base at once.

Mitigation

Owner: Olly. Land a second external client through the warm bootstrap channels this quarter, and stand up the Client Acquisition Engine in parallel so client count doesn't depend on any one relationship.

Positioning risk

"Agents for people" is a broad claim to make credible

Likelihood: mediumImpact: medium
Risk

Widening from "marketing agency" to "AI agent consultancy" is a bigger claim, and a crowded one - AI agent building is being pitched by everyone from Big Four consultancies to solo freelancers. A generic pitch drowns in that noise.

Mitigation

Owner: Olly. Lead every pitch with the one thing generic AI consultancies can't say: "we run this on our own business, today, and here's exactly what it does." Specificity and live proof are the differentiator, not the category name.

Margin and infrastructure risk

Compute cost and multi-tenant isolation

Likelihood: mediumImpact: medium
Risk

Same risk already flagged in the pricing-model deck - if usage per client runs heavier than modelled, or oversight hours run higher, margin narrows. Each client's agent workspace also needs genuine isolation on shared rented cloud infrastructure.

Mitigation

Owner: Jesse. Meter usage per client from the first paying build onward, feed real cost data back into this pricing model, and re-price if usage diverges before it compounds. Isolation pattern already proven internally (Ted serving multiple users from one machine).

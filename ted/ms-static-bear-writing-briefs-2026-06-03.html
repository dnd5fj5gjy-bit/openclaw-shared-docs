Yesterday's audit doc gave you eight image-led variations to break the shoot-footage repetition. This is the next layer — ten more, each picked to occupy an environment, copy register and type treatment the existing twelve ads (Jago set + Batch 1) don't touch yet.

The deliberate variety is the deliverable — Ku gets a brief set that can't possibly look like one ad in ten outfits.

Section · The ten briefs

One Bear photo · one piece of writing · one job to do.

Numbered 09–18 so they extend the audit doc's series cleanly. Ku Creatives can pick these up directly — each brief carries enough to ship a static without further input. Copy is plain professional throughout, no metaphors.

Brief 09 · Jungle question

Jungle · "When did your supplement shelf last grow in soil?"

Hook (on-image copy)"When did your supplement shelf last grow in soil?"
Visual conceptExisting jungle / rainforest photograph of Bear — wet leaves, deep greens, humid light. Bear in the lower-third, looking into the frame or off-camera. Type: big DM Serif italic, set across the top half of the image, white with a soft drop shadow. No product. No URL. Subtle Modern Savage wordmark bottom-right.
Type treatmentLarge italic serif question · single line if crop allows, two lines max.
Caption (3-line)A question worth sitting with. Most supplements are built in a lab, not grown. Modern Savage is whole-food nutrition from real ingredients, real sources. Launches July.
Pillar / funnelP3 Real food, not lab food · TOF awareness.
PlacementIG / FB feed (1:1 + 4:5). Strong as a paid TOF test against the shelf-clutter hook.
Why it's different — Batch 1 had no jungle imagery and no question-form hooks. This is the only ad that asks rather than states.
Brief 10 · Desert dare

Desert · "Try replacing what you take for two weeks."

Hook (on-image copy)TRY REPLACING WHAT YOU TAKE FOR TWO WEEKS.
Visual conceptExisting desert / arid Bear photograph — ochre tones, dust, hard sun. Bear walking out of frame or mid-stride. Type treated as a stamped sticker overlay — Special Elite font, slight tilt, looks like it's been pressed onto the photograph rather than typeset over it.
Type treatmentStamp / sticker · monospaced caps, slightly rotated, paper-cream block behind the type so it reads as a physical sticker.
Caption (3-line)Two weeks. One bag instead of your current stack. Notice what changes — your morning, your gut, what the bottles were actually doing. Modern Savage launches July.
Pillar / funnelP2 Replace the shelf · MOF / BOF.
PlacementIG feed + Story (4:5 and 9:16). The stamp treatment travels well to Stories.
Why it's different — first instruction-register hook in the set (most are statements). Stamp treatment is a new typographic register entirely.
Brief 11 · Arctic instruction

Arctic · "Cold weather, hot food, real fuel."

Hook (on-image copy)COLD WEATHER · HOT FOOD · REAL FUEL
Visual conceptExisting arctic / snow / mountain expedition photograph of Bear — white-out conditions, frost on beard, breath visible. Type set as a single horizontal Montserrat caps line across the bottom-third, gold underline rule between each phrase. High contrast — black or navy plate behind the line so it reads cleanly on snow.
Type treatmentSans-serif caps tile · three short phrases separated by middle-dot.
Caption (3-line)What you put in your body matters more, not less, when conditions get harder. Real food, dense nutrition, no synthetics. Modern Savage is the bag we built for that. Launches July.
Pillar / funnelP5 Proof not promises · TOF / brand.
PlacementIG feed + Reels static cover. The white-on-white challenge means it will hold the eye in a busy feed.
Why it's different — first arctic imagery, first three-beat caps lockup. Visual register is "field credentials" without saying it.
Brief 12 · Family table

Around the table · "We pass the same bag round our table."

Hook (on-image copy)"We pass the same bag round our table."
— ADULT BLEND · KIDS BLEND · ONE STANDARD
Visual conceptExisting photograph of Bear with his family around a table — warm domestic light, food on the table, no posed product placement. If a posed shot doesn't exist in the archive, a candid family shot in a kitchen or garden works too. Type: serif headline overlaid in the negative space, small-caps strap line underneath.
Type treatmentHeadline serif quote + small-caps strap line · two-tier hierarchy.
Caption (3-line)One brand, two bags, one standard. Adults get the full organ blend, kids get a smaller scoop with colostrum added. Same real food, same family table. Contains milk — honest on the label.
Pillar / funnelP1 One family, one brand · MOF · sells Adult + Kids together.
PlacementIG feed (4:5) — the strongest single image for "one brand, the whole family" pillar.
Why it's different — Batch 1 V05 had Bear with one son. This is the whole family unit at a table — the brand's deepest visual asset and we haven't used it once yet. Olly to confirm we have rights to a family-table photo before we brief this one.
Brief 13 · Training

Training shot · the data tile.

Hook (on-image copy)21G PROTEIN · 6 ORGANS · 1B CFU · 0 FILLERS
Visual conceptExisting training photo of Bear — kettlebell, pull-up bar, push-up, climbing rope, something physical and unposed. Sweaty / mid-movement, not lifestyle-clean. Type: four data tiles stacked along the right side, each a small caps line with a gold rule above the number. Bear takes left two-thirds of the frame, tiles fill the right strip.
Type treatmentData tiles · four stacked stat blocks, each with the number in DM Serif and the descriptor in small caps Montserrat.
Caption (3-line)The numbers behind one scoop. Real protein, the full organ stack, gut-health bacteria, nothing synthetic. Built for the body you actually use. Modern Savage launches July.
Pillar / funnelP3 Real food, not lab food · MOF.
PlacementIG feed (1:1) + carousel slide-one. Designed to be screen-grabbed and forwarded.
Why it's different — Batch 1 V02 had numbers too, but as a stacked top-right block on a portrait. This is a different layout (right strip), different photo (training, not portrait), different stats (protein / CFU / fillers, not organs / servings / synthetics).
Brief 14 · Urban contrast

Urban / suit · the handwritten scrawl.

Hook (on-image copy)same body whether I'm in a suit or in a swamp.
Visual conceptExisting urban / suited / city photograph of Bear — a rarer register, deliberately. Crisp clean grade, formal context. Type: a single handwritten line in Caveat across the lower half, white or off-white, like it's been scrawled directly onto the image. Modern Savage wordmark bottom-right.
Type treatmentHandwritten scrawl · Caveat or similar, looks personal not designed.
Caption (3-line)What you put in doesn't change when the context does. Real food, real fuel — whatever room you're in. Modern Savage launches July.
Pillar / funnelP5 Proof not promises · TOF · brand-personality post.
PlacementIG feed (1:1) — performs well as a pinned-style post, breaks the wilderness-only Bear stereotype.
Why it's different — first urban / non-wilderness Bear image in the set, and the only handwritten-type post. Shows the audience the brand isn't only for survivalists.
Brief 15 · Camp dawn

Camp · the italic serif quote, at dawn.

Hook (on-image copy)"What you eat in the morning sets the day."
Visual conceptExisting camp / fire / dawn photograph of Bear — warm orange light, smoke, low sun, kettle on. Type: italic DM Serif quote in the negative space (sky / smoke), attributed in small caps underneath. No product, no URL. Modern Savage wordmark small bottom-right.
Type treatmentItalic serif quote · attribution in small caps. Same family as Batch 1 V03 but a completely different photo register (dawn / camp, not action / expedition).
Caption (3-line)The first thing in the body matters most. One scoop, real food, every morning. That's the bag we built. Modern Savage launches July.
Pillar / funnelP3 Real food · TOF · daily-ritual angle.
PlacementIG feed (4:5) + Story (9:16). Particularly strong as a morning-slot post.
Why it's different — same type family as Batch 1 V03 by design (to build the brand's serif-quote signature), but the photo is unmistakably dawn / camp, not mid-action expedition. Builds a recognisable quote-format Bear can return to weekly.
Brief 16 · Candid · eating

Candid in the field · the caption strip.

Hook (on-image copy)BEAR · IN THE FIELD · EATING WHAT HE BUILDS.
Visual conceptA genuinely candid photograph — Bear eating in the field, mid-bite, unguarded. Looks like a behind-the-scenes still, not a posed shot. Type: a single Special Elite caption strip across the bottom of the image, paper-cream plate behind the type, like a documentary still caption.
Type treatmentCaption strip · monospaced, lower-third, looks like a film-still caption.
Caption (3-line)No production. No styling. Bear eating the same way he has for thirty years — and the same way Modern Savage was built. Launches July.
Pillar / funnelP5 Proof not promises · TOF · documentary register.
PlacementIG feed (4:5) + Reels static cover. Reads as a real moment, not an ad.
Why it's different — the only "documentary" treatment in the set. Tells viewers this isn't a celebrity licence without ever saying so.
Brief 17 · Coastal claim

Coastal · "Nothing in this bag was made in a lab."

Hook (on-image copy)NOTHING IN THIS BAG WAS MADE IN A LAB.
Visual conceptExisting coastal / shoreline / open-water photograph of Bear — wide horizon, sea-grey or sea-blue palette. Bear small in the frame, lots of negative sky. Type: single big Montserrat 800 caps line set huge across the upper-third, breaking onto two lines if needed. White on cool grey-blue.
Type treatmentSans-serif big claim · one sentence, set as a billboard.
Caption (3-line)Whole-food nutrition. Real ingredients, real sources, no synthetic forms. The bag we'd want our families on. Modern Savage launches July.
Pillar / funnelP3 Real food / P2 Replace the shelf · TOF.
PlacementIG feed + FB feed (1:1, 4:5). Cool palette gives the feed contrast against the warm jungle / desert / camp grades.
Why it's different — first coastal / open-water image in the set. Cool palette intentionally — gives the feed a wider colour range, not all warm earth tones.
Brief 18 · Quiet · faith-adjacent

Reflective · the smallest type in the set.

Hook (on-image copy)"Look after the body you've been given."
Visual conceptExisting quiet / reflective photograph of Bear — interior or low-light outdoor, contemplative register. Could be book in hand, head down, or a moment of stillness. Type set small and centred, italic DM Serif, plenty of whitespace around it. The deliberate restraint is the point. No product, no URL. Modern Savage wordmark tiny, bottom-centre.
Type treatmentSmall italic serif · centred · the quietest post in the brand.
Caption (3-line)A simple line, worth coming back to. Modern Savage is built on the same idea — give the body what it was designed to run on. Launches July.
Pillar / funnelP5 Proof not promises · Brand · faith-adjacent audience without overclaiming.
PlacementIG feed (1:1) — anchor post, low-volume, high-dwell. Strong Sunday slot.
Why it's different — first deliberately quiet post in the set. Touches Bear's Christianity / faith-adjacent territory (named in the brand deck) without quoting scripture or alienating the secular audience.
Section · Open questions for Olly

Three decisions I need from you before Ku starts.

All three are quick — but each one gates a subset of the briefs above. Reply with answers and Ku gets a clean brief pack.

Open questions

Three yes/no calls.

  • Family table photo (Brief 12). Do we have rights to a usable photo of Bear at a table with the family? If not, swap to a candid kitchen / outdoor family shot, or drop the brief and lean harder on Batch 1 V05 (Kids Blend with one son).
  • Training photo (Brief 13). Same question — is there an archive training shot we have rights to use commercially? If not, drop and we re-use one of the existing field photos with the same data-tile treatment.
  • Urban / suit photo (Brief 14). Less certain we have one in the archive. If not available, we keep the handwritten-scrawl treatment but apply it over a candid wilderness shot instead — still hits the "personal hand" register.

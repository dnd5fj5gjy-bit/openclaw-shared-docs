Bear Witness · Internal Strategy

Client Acquisition Engine

Not a new service to sell. A systemised, AI-run pipeline that keeps Bear Witness's own retainer business fed with qualified conversations - built with the tools and team we already have, no new infrastructure required.

Read the plan
01 · The bottleneck

Bear Witness's constraint isn't delivery. It's deal flow.

The Growth Retainer and Full Engine tiers already work - Alfred proves the shape and the AI-leveraged team can run more clients without more headcount. New clients still arrive almost entirely through network and referral, which is exactly the failure mode the "local business" ads-and-nurture model exists to fix at a different price point: not predictable, and it caps growth at who Jesse and Olly happen to already know.

This is the same fix, aimed inward. Instead of building and selling an ads-and-nurture system to local businesses, we build one for Bear Witness itself - to keep our own pipeline of qualified conversations full and predictable.

02 · The model

Ads plus an AI nurture agent, running one funnel: into our own calendar.

The mechanics are simple and proven - paid ads to a lead magnet, then an always-on AI sequence that qualifies and books a call, instead of a human chasing every reply. We're not reselling this to local trades; we're pointing it at our own growth.

Step one

Ads to a lead magnet

LinkedIn/Meta ads targeting founders and marketing leads at mid-size brands, pointing to a proof asset - a case study built from GSET, Ted's Health, or Modern Savage's own growth engine, or a free AI marketing audit offer.

Step two

AI nurture, not manual chasing

Anyone who engages drops into an automated sequence that qualifies them (budget, current setup, pain points), educates them on the AI-Integrated Agency model, and books the call the moment they're ready.

Step three

Warm call, straight into the tiers

Jesse/Olly only ever speak to pre-qualified, pre-sold leads. The call sells into Channel Management, Growth Retainer, or Full Engine - this funnel creates no new product, it fills the ones that already exist.

03 · Why it fits our current team and tech

This is the one AI-agency idea that needs nothing new to build.

Compare this to selling the Agent Workforce externally, which needs new multi-tenant infrastructure, per-client isolation, and credential management before a single client can be onboarded (see the Agent Workforce deck's "What's needed" section). This funnel needs none of that, because it only ever runs for one tenant: us.

Selling Agent Workforce externally

  • Needs multi-tenant hosting for client agents
  • Needs per-client credential and data isolation
  • Needs a documented build process beyond Felix
  • Needs watchdog coverage across every client instance
Ready to build now

Client Acquisition Engine

  • One tenant only - Bear Witness itself, no isolation problem
  • Reuses the messaging/nurture patterns already built for the internal agent team
  • One ad account, one landing page, one nurture agent
  • Felix's time is a single build, not a per-client rebuild

In short: this pivots the "AI Growth Consultancy" idea away from a new service line we'd sell to strangers, and into the thing that actually removes Bear Witness's real constraint - deal flow - using tech and team capacity we already have today.

04 · The funnel, in detail

What actually needs to exist.

Target

ICP

Founders and marketing decision-makers at mid-size brands who currently run marketing in-house or through a traditional agency - the same buyer the Growth Retainer/Full Engine tiers already target.

Hook

Lead magnet

A proof-led asset: "how we run GSET/Ted's Health/Modern Savage's growth engine" case study, or a free AI marketing audit - something that demonstrates the model rather than describes it.

Channel

Paid ads

LinkedIn first (matches the B2B buyer), Meta as a secondary/cheaper test channel. Modest spend to start - this is a pipeline-filler, not a mass-market play.

Backend

AI nurture agent

One agent, built once, running an email/DM sequence: qualify, educate, book. Reuses the same conversational and scheduling patterns already running across the internal agent team.

05 · What's needed

Small, concrete list - not a rebuild.

Assets

One proof-led case study or audit offer

The lead magnet itself - built once from work Bear Witness has already delivered internally.

Media

An ad account and modest test budget

LinkedIn (and/or Meta) account set up, a small initial spend to validate cost-per-qualified-call before scaling it.

Build

One nurture agent, engineered once by Felix

A single build - not a per-client rebuild - covering qualification, education, and booking into Jesse/Olly's calendar.

Tracking

A simple pipeline log

Where leads, qualification status, and booked calls get tracked - so we can see cost-per-call and conversion into signed retainers.

06 · Roadmap

Small, fast, and testable.

1
Weeks 1-2

Build the lead magnet and nurture agent

Package one proof asset, engineer the nurture sequence, set up the ad account and landing page.

2
Weeks 3-6

Run a small test

Modest ad spend, measure cost-per-qualified-call and show-up rate before committing more budget.

3
Month 2 onward

Scale spend against proven conversion

Once cost-per-signed-client is known, treat ad spend the same as any other CAC decision - scale it as retainer signings justify it.

The ask

Greenlight a small test - lead magnet, one ad account, one nurture agent.

This needs no new infrastructure and no new hire - just a decision to point the same AI-agency mechanics that work for local businesses at Bear Witness's own pipeline.

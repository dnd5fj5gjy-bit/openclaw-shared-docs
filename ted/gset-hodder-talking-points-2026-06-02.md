## Act 1 — This is our plan (~90 seconds)

"So the plan we're putting forward is **one engine, six channels, one team** running all of it. The reason it's shaped that way is because the book has already done the hard part for us.

300,000 UK copies in eight months. Number-one Sunday Times debut — the first time a Hodder Faith Christian title's done that. 4.8 stars across more than 1,400 reviews. So the question for us isn't really 'how do we promote this book' anymore. It's how do we operate the brand of the biggest Christian title Hodder Faith has published in a generation, while staying true to what the book actually is.

The strategic gap, and the biggest opportunity, is the United States. The US Christian book market is roughly fifty times the size of the UK's, it's up 40% since COVID, and appetite is at a generational high. Bear's universally known in America, but he's known there as an explorer — not yet as a person of faith the way he's known here. That work has been done in the UK. It hasn't been done in the US yet.

So we've set three priorities, and we mean the order. **First — close the US gap.** That's the biggest opportunity on the table and it's time-sensitive. **Second — compound the UK** through the calendar: Lent, Easter, Pentecost, the June anniversary, the autumn church push, Advent. **Third — start setting up international.** Spanish, Portuguese and Brazil-targeted foundations this year. Scale lands in year two."

---

## Act 2 — This is how we start + how the year unfolds (~3 minutes, your longest act)

"For Month 1, we'd have five workstreams running in parallel.

**The first** is starting the YouTube long-form recording. That's two formats every week. Reading episodes, where we take passages from the audiobook and layer them with editor-added soundscape and ambient audio. And Unpacking episodes, where a theologian sits with the same passage and gives you the context — the Hebrew roots, the historical setting, the scholarship. The theologians are filmed in a three-day batch shoot, twice a year. 52 episodes per batch.

**The second** is the short-form testing loop. Three to five clips per platform per week, across TikTok, Reels and Shorts. Never cross-posted, and geo-locked so we can isolate UK and US activity separately. The source bank is the audiobook stems cut into immersive moments, Bear's existing interviews — Eldredge, Brierley, Metaxas, Will Cain — plus licensed visuals where we can clear them. By the end of Month 1 we know what's actually moving the audience.

**The third** is starting the website rebuild. Geo-routed retail links, a UK and US cover swap, and the attribution tracker layer underneath everything. We design and build in Month 1, and the site goes live by the end of Month 2.

**The fourth** is standing up Substack. A standalone GSET account, separate from Bear's personal one. Theologian transcripts edited into proper essays, reader letters, seasonal series. First four essays drafted in Month 1. Substack matters because it's the only owned email list in the stack — algorithms can take TikTok or Instagram away tomorrow, they can't take Substack away.

**And the fifth** is the community side. Facebook group up, with structured five-week cohort read-alongs designed around the book's eyewitness perspectives — Matthew first, then Mark, Luke, John, Acts. Instagram on a steady five-posts-a-week rhythm. And the full asset bank audited and locked so the engine has stable source material for the year.

Quickly, to show how the year unfolds from there:

**Month 1** is the build phase. **Month 2** is when the engine goes live — the batch shoot's in the can, the website launches, YouTube publishes the first four episodes, short-form turns on at full cadence, Substack's weekly, and the first cohort opens for sign-up. **From Month 3** we're at steady state, and we get the first measurable signal on engine health and US traction. **From Month 6 onward** we open international — Spanish and Portuguese architecture goes up, the Brazil pilot starts, and US tour support folds in if the tour confirms."

---

## Act 3 — This is why we can run it well (~90 seconds)

"And just before I hand back for questions, three quick reasons why we think we're the right team to actually run this.

**The first** is that we wrote the strategy you've got in front of you. We know the brand, we know the audience segments, and we know what works in this space because we built that thinking. So there's no ramp-up cost in bringing us in. We start at week one rather than month three.

**The second** is that we're set up to find what works fast and double down on it, rather than guess. The short-form testing loop runs from Week 1 — three to five variants per platform per week, against a defined matrix of hook, length, format, and narrator-versus-text. By the end of Month 1 we know which combinations are winning per platform per geo. We lock the winners, drop the losers, and scale what's actually moving the audience. The headline number we report every month on top of that is geo-isolated velocity — US web sessions read against US retail velocity. If that line moves, we know the content is shifting copies, not just generating views.

**The third** is that we've baked the constraints in from day one. Hachette's AI policy is respected throughout — no AI voice, no AI imagery, no AI in published creative. AI sits in the backend only, for things like transcription, scheduling, and analytics. And Bear's bandwidth is protected — no new filming, pre-recorded material only, with three calendar-anchor posts a year on his personal channels written by the team."

1. Lead Video Editor — YouTube long-form

Brief · P1 · this week
Output: 2 episodes/week (104/year) Retainer: £2.0–2.5k/mo (scope-revised from earlier £1.5–2k) Start: within 2 weeks of contract signature

Scope

  • Episode A — "Reading": 8–12 min. Audiobook chapter audio laid over ambient, photographic-style visual treatment (lit interiors, landscape b-roll, scripture overlays, slow text reveals). Designed to feel devotional, not documentary.
  • Episode B — "Unpacking": 8–12 min. Single theologian on camera, intercut with referenced scripture, maps, historical imagery. Filmed in 3-day batch shoots every 6 months — editor cuts the whole batch into ~26 episodes per refresh.
  • Both formats hold ≥8:30 to clear YouTube mid-roll monetisation.
  • Locks intro/outro template + thumbnail system in week 1.

Deliverables in first 30 days

  • Episode 1 (Reading) — final cut, signed off by Olly + Jesse
  • Episode 2 (Unpacking — uses placeholder theologian footage from existing Bear interviews if first batch shoot not yet locked)
  • Visual treatment guide (LUTs, type, lower-thirds, scripture overlay style)
  • Trailer (60–90s)

Selection criteria

  • Show reel includes faith / documentary / long-form audio-led work
  • Comfortable cutting to audio first (most editors cut to picture)
  • Can manage their own assistants — we don't want to micro-staff
Felix-ask: "Who's the BGV YouTube long-form editor you'd trust with a 104-ep/year retainer? Faith-tone, audio-led. Need 3 names by Wed."

Decisions still on Olly

Three calls that unlock everything else.

1. Editorial sign-off chain. Hodder's deck implies Hodder's editorial team passes on Substack essays. Does that extend to YouTube scripts? Short-form captions? If yes, we need to build a lightweight review-and-approve workflow into the production line before week 1 — otherwise the publishing rhythm dies in a Friday inbox.
2. Rights & IP — who owns the YouTube channel and the Substack list? The deck doesn't name it. If Hodder owns the channel, every brief above shifts to "build it for handover." If Bear Witness owns it under a service contract, the briefs stay as-is. Worth surfacing before the contract is drafted, not after.
3. US launch date — soft or hard? The deck names "Months 1–12" for the US gap close. If month 1 is a public US launch (PR, partner activation with Lusko / Gifford / Roumie), the team needs to ramp 30 days earlier than a soft start would require. The hires above are sized for the soft model.

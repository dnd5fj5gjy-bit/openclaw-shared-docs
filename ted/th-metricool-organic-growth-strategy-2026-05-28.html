The Approach

Where to play, and why

Ted's Health sells a considered decision — getting tested, talking to a doctor. Organic social doesn't close that decision; it builds the trust and awareness that make it. The job is to be the calm, credible men's-health voice that men ~30–55 already follow when the moment comes.

Primary

Instagram

Where the 30–55 male health audience already is, and where saves + shares compound reach. Reels for discovery, carousels for education, Stories for trust and CTA.

Secondary

TikTok

Best organic reach for cold discovery right now. Talking-head myth-busters and Dr Andrews clips travel well. Repurpose IG Reels here — no separate shoot.

Secondary

YouTube Shorts

Long shelf-life and strong search intent for "low testosterone symptoms" type queries. Same vertical clips, reposted. Pure upside for the effort.

One engine, three outputs. Build for Instagram first. Every Reel you make gets reposted to TikTok and YouTube Shorts through Metricool the same week. Don't run three strategies — run one and distribute it.

Content Pillars

Five things we post about

Every post belongs to one of five pillars. Rotating them keeps the feed varied and stops it becoming one long advert. Tie the £40 test CTA in where it fits naturally — not on every post.

Pillar 01

Myth-busting

Correct the things men get wrong about testosterone, TRT and men's health. High-share, high-save, defines the brand as the straight-talking voice.

TH example"'TRT means you're on it for life.' — Not true." Carousel that explains it's a managed, reviewable treatment. CTA: soft — decide on facts, not fear.
Pillar 02

Symptom education

Help men recognise and understand signals — low energy, low mood, poor sleep, ED, brain fog. Frames problems without diagnosing, and points to getting tested.

TH example"ED is a symptom, not a verdict." Why ED can be an early flag for hormones, cardiovascular or sleep. CTA: a conversation + a blood test.
Pillar 03

Proof & credibility

The reasons to trust TH: doctor-led, CQC-registered, real process. Ambassador and patient voices where consented. Builds confidence without overclaiming.

TH example"What actually happens when you book a test" — the calm, no-judgement process walkthrough. Ambassador stories (e.g. via the ambassador roster) where they've consented.
Pillar 04

Dr Andrews authority

Dr Jonathan Andrews to camera — the warm, plain-English clinical voice. Answers the questions men are too awkward to ask. This is the brand's biggest asset.

TH example"The question I'm asked most in clinic…" 30-second clip answering one real patient question per week. Reuse across IG/TikTok/Shorts.
Pillar 05

Actionable tips

Genuinely useful, evidence-respecting habits for energy, sleep, training, recovery. Saveable, shareable, and positions TH as helpful before it's commercial.

TH example"3 things that move testosterone you actually control" — sleep, resistance training, body composition. No supplement hype, no promises.
Mix

Rough weekly split

Across 5 posts: 1 myth-bust, 1 symptom education, 1 Dr Andrews clip, 1 tip, 1 proof/credibility. Adjust to what the numbers reward — lean into whatever pillar drives saves and profile taps.

Medical-advertising guardrail — read before posting. Educate, never promise. No "cure," "fix," or "guaranteed" results. No before/after imagery tied to treatment. Don't claim individual outcomes. Frame benefits as general and evidence-respecting ("low testosterone is linked to…"), and let the doctor-led process do the credibility work. When in doubt, soften the claim.

Don't chase likes. Likes are vanity at this stage. For a considered-purchase health brand, saves and profile taps are the leading indicators of growth that converts. If a post gets modest likes but high saves, it's a winner — make more like it.

Hooks, Captions & Hashtags

Formulas you can reuse

The first line stops the scroll; the caption earns the save. Keep the Dr Andrews voice throughout — warm, plain-English, evidence-led, no bro-science, no hype.

Hook formulas
The myth flip
"[Common belief]." — Not true.
e.g. "'TRT means you're on it for life.' — Not true."
The reframe
"[Symptom] is a [X], not a [Y]."
e.g. "ED is a symptom, not a verdict."
The clinic line
"The question I'm asked most in clinic…"
Dr Andrews to camera — instant authority + curiosity gap.
The surprising number
"One blood test. A surprising amount of answers."
Concrete, specific, low-hype. Pairs with symptom education.
The "you're not broken"
"You don't need to feel broken to get [tested / a baseline]."
Removes the barrier — broadens the audience beyond men in crisis.
Caption shape
  1. Open by restating the hook or the real-world version of it ("This is the line I hear most in clinic…").
  2. Give the actual answer — plain, evidence-respecting, no overclaiming. This is the value.
  3. One human, de-stigmatising line — "No awkwardness, no judgement. Just straight answers from a doctor."
  4. Soft CTA where it fits — "A blood test is from £40, and a doctor reads your results in context." Link in bio / 📍 tedshealth.com.
Hashtag approach

Use 10–15 relevant tags, mixing reach levels so you can rank somewhere. Keep a saved set in Metricool and tweak per post. Avoid anything that reads spammy or makes a medical claim.

The 30-Day Starter Plan

Week by week — start today

Concrete enough to begin immediately. Each week builds a small batch of content, schedules it in Metricool, and reviews the numbers. By Day 30 you'll have a full content engine running and your first read on what works.

Week 1 · Set up & go live

Foundations + first posts

  • Connect IG, TikTok, YouTube in Metricool; set the best-time slots and build the Mon–Fri skeleton.
  • Batch 5 posts from existing material — you already have the TRT myth-buster, the ED education post, and the blood-panel post drafted. Turn those into 1 Reel + 2 carousels to start.
  • Film 1 Dr Andrews clip ("the question I'm asked most…") — one 30-sec take is enough.
  • Schedule the week, cross-post Reels to TikTok + Shorts, and post light Stories daily.
  • Goal: be live and consistent, not perfect.
Week 2 · Find the rhythm

Full pillar rotation

  • Run the full 5-pillar week: 1 myth-bust, 1 symptom ed., 1 Dr Andrews, 1 tip, 1 proof.
  • Batch-film 2–3 Dr Andrews clips in one session to bank a few weeks of authority content.
  • Add a Story poll ("What's stopped you getting tested?") — mine the answers for Week 3 topics.
  • First Monday review: note your highest-save and highest-reach post from Week 1.
Week 3 · Lean into what works

Double down + first CTA push

  • Make 2 posts in the style of your Week 1–2 winner (whichever pillar drove saves/profile taps).
  • Run the proof/process Reel with the clearest £40-test CTA of the month, plus a Story link-sticker push.
  • Use the Week 2 poll answers to write a symptom-education carousel that answers a real objection.
  • Review: is reach trending up? Are link clicks appearing? Adjust the mix.
Week 4 · Consolidate & plan ahead

Lock the engine

  • Run a clean 5-post week on the format that's working best.
  • Batch-schedule Week 5 fully in advance — prove the "always a week ahead" habit.
  • 30-day review: compare Week 1 vs Week 4 on the five metrics. Write 3 lines — what to keep, what to drop, what to test next month.
  • Decide the repeatable monthly shape from the evidence — this becomes the standing operating rhythm.
After 30 days

You'll have a running engine and real data

A connected, scheduled, three-platform presence; a batch-and-review habit that takes a few hours a week; and a first read on which pillar and format actually grows Ted's Health. From there it's iteration — more of what earns saves and profile taps, less of what doesn't.

The one rule that matters most: consistency beats perfection. Five solid posts a week, every week, scheduled ahead in Metricool, will out-perform occasional brilliant posts every time.

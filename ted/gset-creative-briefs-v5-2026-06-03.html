Brief 16 · Podcast guest tour · borrowed audio reach

The Christian Podcaster Playbook

Olly hosts a structured 12-month guest tour on existing Christian-faith podcasts. The book is the hook; the host is the asset. No new Bear footage required — Olly speaks as the publisher-side custodian of the engine, with theologian guests stitched in where the host wants depth.

Host slotOlly primary · theologian co-guest where the host invites depth
OwnerPartnerships lead (v4 B13) + Olly · supports: short-form editor

Why this surface, why now

Christian-faith podcasting is the single highest-trust discovery channel for a Bible-adjacent book. A host endorsement converts more strongly than any paid creative we can buy. The cost is time, not money. Olly is the right voice — he can speak to the publisher case, the engine, the read-along community and the testimony arc without ever needing to be Bear. Where a host wants the theological angle, one of the theologian roster (per v2 B01 / v3 B02) joins as a second guest.

The 200-podcast tracked list — how it's built

  • Tier A · Anchor hosts (15 shows): the biggest Christian-faith conversational shows in UK + US. These get a tailored personal pitch from Olly, not a templated one.
  • Tier B · Pastor + leader podcasts (60 shows): denominational and parachurch podcasts where pastors and small-group leaders listen. Templated pitch, hand-personalised opener.
  • Tier C · Apologetics + theology (40 shows): shows that book theologian guests as a matter of course. Pitch fronted with the theologian co-guest hook.
  • Tier D · Lay-Christian conversation (45 shows): creator-led shows for the cohort audience (read-along, testimony, lived faith).
  • Tier E · Crossover faith-curious (40 shows): mainstream shows that occasionally book faith guests (general-interest, cultural commentary, author interviews).

Brief 17 · YouVersion + Bible-app plans · platform reach

The YouVersion Plan Pipeline

YouVersion is the largest installed Bible-reader base in the world (~600m installs, plan completion runs into the hundreds of millions). A plan featuring GSET, branded to Hodder Faith, is a once-per-anchor distribution win that no paid budget can match. We submit four plans in Year 1, one per major anchor.

Volume4 YouVersion plans/year (Lent · Easter · Advent · Pentecost) + 1 Bible.com partner
OwnerSubstack editor + partnerships lead (v4 B13) + editorial lead
SubmissionYouVersion partnerships review · typical 6–8 wk cycle
Asset reuseCohort companion essays + Reading transcript scripture — repurposed, not new

Why this is a one-shot reach lever

YouVersion plans surface inside the daily-use UI of the most-used Bible app in the world. A featured plan placement (or even a non-featured plan with a partner-driven push) can route tens of thousands of plan completers into a discoverable book title. The plans cost almost nothing to make — they are 7-day or 21-day devotional sequences, ~250 words per day, with a scripture anchor. We have all the raw material already: the cohort companion essays from v3 B04 / v4 B12 are exactly the right shape.

The four Y1 plans — exactly what each one is

  • Lent · "40 days through the Gospels" — 40-day plan, one passage per day from across the four Gospels, with the GSET book quoted as the daily reflection. Targets Lent anchor (v4 B11). Submitted by Month 2 for late-Feb / early-Mar publish.
  • Easter · "7 days in the Passion" — 7-day plan covering Palm Sunday → Easter Sunday, with the four Gospel accounts cross-referenced. Daily reflection drawn from GSET's Passion narrative. Submitted by Month 3.
  • Pentecost · "10 days in Acts 1–2" — 10-day plan covering the ascension to Pentecost, tied to the v4 B12 Acts cohort. Submitted by Month 4.
  • Advent · "24 days of the Nativity story" — 24-day plan covering Matthew's and Luke's Nativity accounts. Daily reflection from GSET. Submitted by Month 6.

What each plan delivers

  • ~250 words of devotional reflection per day, ending with a scripture passage and one "now what" line.
  • End-of-plan card with the book cover, a one-line "if this changed something for you, read the full book" prompt, and a partnership-managed link out to the geo-routed retail tree (v2 B06).
  • Sharable image cards from the brand pack (v2 B07) — YouVersion permits one branded close card per plan.
  • A "next plan" pointer — Lent ends pointing at Easter, Easter pointing at the Acts plan, etc.

Second-tier Bible-app partnerships (one in Y1)

  • Dwell · Bible audio app — natural fit for the audiobook-derived narration. Pitch: a 7-day Dwell plan using the Reading audio tracks as the daily listen.
  • Lectio 365 / 24-7 Prayer — UK-native daily-prayer app with strong cohort overlap. Pitch: a Holy Week supplement.
  • Glorify / Hallow (selectively) — broader-reach prayer apps. Hallow is Catholic-skewed; Glorify is interdenominational. Pitch shape: a feast-day supplement.
  • Pick one for Y1. Don't chase three at once — partnerships lead has one slot of bandwidth here.

The submission discipline

  • Each plan goes through Hodder editorial sign-off before YouVersion submission — these are devotional reflections framed alongside scripture, not marketing copy.
  • No AI in any text per Hachette policy. Plans are written by the Substack editor + editorial lead.
  • YouVersion has its own theological-content review — leave 6–8 weeks for it; never submit late.
  • Every plan ships with the Brief 15 attribution surface — completers click → tagged → measurable retail action.
Production pipeline: Substack editor adapts 1 cohort companion essay slate into 1 YouVersion plan in ~6 working days. Editorial lead reviews. Hodder POC signs off. Partnerships lead submits, manages the YouVersion comms thread. On publish — short-form lane runs a 3-day "we're on YouVersion this week" push. Quarterly: Brief 15 reports plan-attributed retail conversions back into the monthly deck.
Sign-off: Each plan text approved by Hodder editorial. Branded close card approved by brand owner (v2 B07). End-of-plan retail link approved by Hodder POC.

The Apologetics & Faith-Creator Co-Marketing Engine

Twelve named Christian creators — apologists, theologians, faith YouTubers — whose audience overlaps almost exactly with the GSET target reader. We run a structured co-marketing programme: shared shorts, referenced episodes, joint Substack pieces. Not paid. Reciprocal.

Volume12 named partners · 1 active co-piece/mo across the slate

CurrencyReciprocal mentions · gifted books · co-published Substack pieces · clip rights

Why a reciprocal model, not a paid one

Paid endorsements in the Christian-creator space degrade trust quickly — listeners can tell, and the host's audience punishes it. A reciprocal "we cite each other's good work" model is slower but compounds. It also keeps us inside the Hachette guardrail: no paid creator deals, no influencer payments, just visible mutual respect between people working the same field.

What "co-marketing" actually means in practice

  • Cited clip: we cite their work in an Unpacking episode (v2 B01), they cite our episode in a clip of theirs. Each side gets a tagged short-form piece.
  • Substack co-piece: a joint or guest-authored Substack post. Their audience reads it on our list; our audience reads it on theirs. ~1/quarter.
  • Gifted books: a quarterly outbound of physical copies + cohort kit + a personal note from Olly. No expectation, no ask.
  • Clip permissions: a standing reciprocal-clip licence — they may use up to 60s of our episodes with credit; we may do the same with theirs.
  • Conference / event: if any creator is speaking at an event Brief 13 partnerships lead is attending, in-person warmth is the highest-ROI step.

The cadence

  • Month 1 — partnerships lead opens conversation with all 12; sets up the reciprocal-clip licence as the entry agreement.
  • Months 2–12 — one active co-piece per month across the slate (12/year). Track in v4 B13 CRM under a separate "creator" pipeline.
  • Quarterly review — which partners are reciprocating vs not. Quietly de-prioritise non-reciprocators after two quarters; do not chase.
  • Year-end — public "people whose work we drew on" credit roll on the Substack. Free, valuable to them, reinforces trust.

What we will not do

  • No paid placements. No influencer fee. No "sponsored mention" on any of their channels.
  • No exclusivity asks — they keep working with whoever they want, we keep working with whoever we want.
  • No back-channel pressure. If a creator declines or goes quiet, we move on without comment.
  • No public-callout of creators who don't reciprocate — it's their right, and the engine's reputation matters more than any single placement.
Production pipeline: Month 1 — partnerships lead opens warm intros via Hodder + BGV existing networks. Quarterly Substack co-piece is calendared a quarter in advance.
Sign-off: Reciprocal-clip licence drafted by Hodder legal once, then reused. Each co-piece (Substack, clip) approved by Hodder POC + the creator on each side. Gifted-books outbound is operational.
Brief 19 · Reader-generated content · the UGC engine

The Reader-Generated Content Engine

Reader testimony (Brief 05 / v3 B05) is one slice of a bigger asset class. Readers send us photos with the book, quote cards they've made, snippets of where they're reading it. The engine doesn't just capture testimony — it captures, curates, asks-permission-from and re-publishes a steady stream of reader-made artefacts.

Volume~40 reader pieces collected/mo · 8 republished/mo · 4 featured
OwnerCommunity lead · supports: short-form editor + Substack editor
SurfacesIG main feed · IG stories · Substack monthly round-up · cohort weekly thread
ConsentDM-template + email consent flow per v3 B05 · extended to photo + quote rights

Why UGC compounds and why we run it as a system, not a moment

Reader-made artefacts do three things paid media cannot. They demonstrate the book is being read, not just bought. They give the next reader a sense of who else is in the room. And they feed our short-form lane with the highest-converting hook type we have — a real person, in their kitchen, holding the book. The bottleneck is not supply. The bottleneck is curation, permission and the discipline to publish on a calendar instead of when it strikes us.

The four reader-asset types we systematically collect

  • Reader photos with the book — kitchen, commute, church pew, bedside. Goal: one tag per day visible across IG + Substack mentions.
  • Reader quote cards — passages they've highlighted and made their own visual of. Reposted with credit; sometimes redrawn into our brand pack for amplification.
  • Reader testimonies / stories — the v3 B05 flow. Continues as-is. UGC engine consumes this stream and routes the very best into the monthly featured slot.
  • Reader voice notes — short audio of readers reading their favourite passage aloud. Lives on Substack as a monthly "the book in their voices" round-up.

The capture flow (where readers send things to)

  • One inbox: readers@thegreateststoryevertoldbook.com. Linked on the website, on every Substack, in every IG bio.
  • One IG tag: #greatestStoryRead. Surfaced by IG-monitor lane (Brief 03 / v3 B06 owner).
  • One DM flow: standing template — community lead replies to every tagged post within 24hrs, thanks, asks consent.
  • One cohort post each week: "what passage caught you this week?" prompt in the cohort weekly thread (Brief 12 / v3 B04).

The consent + rights flow (do this right, do it once)

  • One DM-template consent ask per asset type — extends the v3 B05 testimony consent flow to cover photos, quote cards and voice clips.
  • Reader signs a one-page consent form (Hodder template) granting non-exclusive perpetual rights for marketing use — no payment, with-credit, attribution as they prefer (full name / first name / anon).
  • Consent stored in the v4 B13 CRM under a "reader-assets" pipeline. Audit-clean.
  • Anyone under 18 — full guardian consent flow, escalated to Hodder POC. No exceptions.

The republishing calendar

  • IG main feed (Brief 03): 8 reader-asset posts/mo blended into the steady-state 20 posts/mo. Two per week. Always credited, always tagged.
  • IG stories: ~1 reader-tag re-share per day. Cheap, friendly, builds the "we see you" signal.
  • Substack monthly round-up (Brief 04 / v2 B04): "From the readers · [Month]" post — four featured pieces with the reader's own words, sent to the full Substack list.
  • Cohort weekly thread (Brief 12): closing thread of each cohort showcases that cohort's best reader assets.
  • Brief 10 paid: top-performing reader testimony / quote card is permission-extended to paid use, runs as a bottom-of-funnel creative.

What this surface produces back into the engine

  • The week's highest-converting short-form hook (in our data so far, by 2x over editor-cut equivalents).
  • The Substack list's highest single open-rate post of the month, every month.
  • A continuous credibility flywheel for church partners (v4 B13) and bookstore partners (Brief 20) — "look at the reader response".
  • Trust signal for new readers landing cold on the website — visible community, not just marketing copy.
Production pipeline: Daily — community lead works the inbox + tag + DM queue (~30 mins/day). Weekly — Mon morning, community lead curates the week's incoming, runs consent asks, hands eight republish-ready assets to the Substack editor and short-form editor. Monthly — round-up post ships first Friday. Quarterly — UGC programme review feeds the Brief 15 monthly deck.
Sign-off: Consent flow + rights form approved by Hodder legal in Month 1, then operational. Republishing of any asset that references a third party (church, brand, family member) routed through Hodder POC. Featured reader of the month celebrated, never coerced — no quid-pro-quo.

Brief 21 · Schools · chaplaincy · RE-teacher pack · institutional youth reach

The Schools, Chaplaincy & RE-Teacher Pack

A free, curriculum-aligned classroom pack for UK + US RE teachers, chaplains and youth pastors. Not a sales pitch — a genuinely useful teaching aid that puts the book in front of the 14–18 demographic via the people they trust. Slowest of the v5 surfaces; biggest long-term compounding upside.

Volume1 free pack/year · 4 webinar sessions/year · ~500 teachers on the list by end Y1
OwnerPartnerships lead (v4 B13) + editorial lead · curriculum advisor (paid consultant, ad-hoc)
FormatPDF lesson pack + slide deck + 4 Reading episode clips · all free + ungated
DistributionRE-teacher associations (NATRE, RE Today) · CofE Education Office · chaplaincy networks · US Christian-schools associations

Why the schools surface — and why we land it last

14-to-18 is the most-deferred Bible-engagement demographic and the one with the highest lifetime value to a publisher building a 10-year list. RE teachers, school chaplains and youth pastors are the gatekeepers, and they are starved of well-designed free resources. The trade-off is patience — institutional adoption runs on terms (autumn / spring / summer), and the trust takes a year to build. We do not over-promise this surface; we set it up properly in Year 1 and let it compound.

What the free pack contains

  • One PDF lesson pack (40–60 pages): six lesson plans aligned to UK GCSE RE specifications + US Christian-schools curricula, drawn from across the four Gospels. Each lesson — learning objective, key passage, classroom discussion prompts, writing exercise, optional clip.
  • One slide deck (Google Slides + PowerPoint): editable, branded, ready to drop into a classroom session.
  • Four Reading episode clips (3–5 mins each): classroom-cleared (no inappropriate language, no controversial sermon-led overlays), with discussion-prompt cards.
  • One teacher's-introduction guide (~6 pages): the context for the book, the publisher case, suggested ways to use the pack across one term.
  • One classroom-display poster (A2): sharable image, no overt selling, just a quote.
  • Ungated + free — single landing page, no email-wall, just download. Optional teacher mailing list signup as a soft prompt.

The four annual webinar sessions

  • Autumn term · "Using the Gospels in the RE classroom" — 45-min webinar for RE teachers, hosted by editorial lead + a guest theologian.
  • Spring term · "Chaplaincy & the Gospel narrative" — for school chaplains specifically; how to use the book in chaplaincy programmes.
  • Easter term · "The Passion narrative across four Gospels" — Easter-anchored teaching session, also useful as a Holy Week preparation for chaplains.
  • Summer term · "Year-end reflection" — short Q&A with teachers who used the pack; soft launch of next year's refreshed version.

The institutional distribution paths

  • UK · NATRE (National Association of Teachers of Religious Education) — pitch a free-resource feature in their members' newsletter + REtoday magazine.
  • UK · CofE Education Office — for Church of England schools network; ~4,700 schools in scope.
  • UK · Catholic Education Service — for Catholic schools; selective fit (some Gospel-narrative framing may need a denominational sign-off).
  • UK · Scripture Union — youth-ministry network with strong school chaplaincy reach.
  • US · Association of Christian Schools International (ACSI) — ~25,000 member schools globally.
  • US · Christian School Educators & Christian Educators Association International — direct teacher reach.

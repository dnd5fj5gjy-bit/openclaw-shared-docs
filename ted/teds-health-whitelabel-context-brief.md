## 2. What "White-Label" Actually Means In Our Stack

A partner platform on our white-label gets:

- **Their own brand front** — domain, logo, colours, copy. The patient sees the partner's brand, not Ted's Health.
- **The TH clinical engine underneath** — same prescribers, same pharmacy fulfilment, same blood testing partners, same regulatory wrap. The partner doesn't have to register a clinic, hire prescribers, or build a pharmacy chain.
- **The TH patient pathway** — consultation booking, blood test ordering, prescription approval, repeat-script logic, medication delivery, ongoing review.
- **A partner dashboard** — visibility of patients on their platform, revenue share, content/marketing assets to use.

In other words, a partner brings the **audience and brand**; we bring the **clinic, compliance, fulfilment, and tech**. The partner runs what looks and feels like their own telehealth business, without any of the regulatory or operational lift.

## 3. Why This Opens Up The Business

A few things shift the moment the WL platform is shipping reliably:

1. **Distribution leverage.** Every ambassador who used to drive a referral code can now run their own branded platform. The same audience converts at a much higher rate when they're buying from "their guy's" platform, not from a third-party referral link.
2. **Recurring B2B2C revenue.** Each WL partner is monthly platform fee + revenue share on every patient. That's a stickier, more predictable line than affiliate-only deals.
3. **Cross-portfolio reuse.** The same WL chassis can be re-skinned for women's health, weight loss, sleep, performance — anywhere BGV has audience. We don't rebuild; we re-skin.
4. **Defensibility.** Once a partner is running their patients on our stack, the switching cost is real (clinical records, prescriber relationships, pharmacy logistics). Affiliate links are commodity; platform infra is not.
5. **Ambassador upgrade path.** People like Ollie who've already proven they can convert a TH audience now have an obvious next step: stop being a referrer, start being an operator.

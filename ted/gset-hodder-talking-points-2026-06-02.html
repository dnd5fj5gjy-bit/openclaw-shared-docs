How this works

The new shape of your bit

  • They've read the deck. Don't recap it. Don't read slides. Refer to it when useful — "as you'll have seen in Section X..."
  • Your job is the why and the first steps. The deck shows the what; you give the reasoning and the first weeks.
  • Six beats, ~60–90 seconds each. Open · Why this shape · Priorities · First steps · The number · Guardrails · Close.
  • Hold detail for Q+A. Tight beats invite the right questions.
Open · ~40 seconds, after Jesse

Who you are, then what you're going to do

Lead with your role — that's the first thing they want anchored. Then frame the next few minutes.

Part 1 · Role (~20s) "Thanks Jesse. Quick context on me for those I haven't met — I'm Olly, Market and Strategy Manager at BGV. I run the operational side across our brand portfolio. For Bear Witness specifically, I'm leading the day-to-day on the GSET engine — strategy across the six channels, the calendar, the content slate, the measurement loop all sit with me. If Jesse is the founder of the engine, I'm the one running it week to week."
Part 2 · Frame the next few minutes (~20s) "Since you've all had the deck ahead of time, I won't walk through it slide by slide. What I'll do is talk through the broader strategy and the reasoning behind it, and then the actionable next steps — what we'd be tackling in the first weeks. Six or seven minutes, then plenty of room for Q+A."

Beat 1 · ~90 seconds

Why this shape — one engine, six channels, one team

The reasoning behind the model. Why a single retainer beats the alternatives.

Natural"The reason it's shaped this way — one engine, six channels, one team — is that the book has already done the audience-finding job. 300,000 UK copies in 8 months, number one on the Sunday Times, 4.8 stars across 1,400+ reviews. That's a base most marketing engines never get to start from. The question isn't 'how do we find an audience' — it's 'how do we keep the one we have moving and open the one we don't.'"
Why six channels under one roof"The alternative is either Hodder building it in-house, or piecing it together across agencies. The first costs you a year of hiring; the second loses the through-line — short-form, Substack, YouTube and Facebook each end up pulling in slightly different directions. Under one team they reinforce each other. Reader testimony from Facebook becomes Substack copy. A short-form winner becomes a YouTube hook. The web rebuild is where it all converts. One team, one slate every week."
2
Beat 2 · ~75 seconds

The three priorities — and why that order

Order matters. Explain it so they understand the year's success criterion.

Natural"There are three priorities, and the order is deliberate.

One — close the US gap. That's the biggest commercial opportunity on the table this year and it's time-sensitive — the further we get from the launch beat, the harder it is to claim that ground. US-coded YouTube cadence, geo-locked short-form, US faith voices, and US retail velocity read against web sessions every month.

Two — compound the UK. The UK is the base. We don't want to lose what's already there, so we sustain it through the calendar — Lent, Easter, Pentecost, the June anniversary, the autumn church push, Advent.

Three — set up international. Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese — but this year that's foundations only. Rails, partner shortlist, calendar slot. Scale lands in year two."

Beat 3 · ~2 minutes — your longest beat

What we tackle first — Month 1

This is the part they care about most. Walk it as five concrete first steps.

Lead in"Month 1 is build and test, not full launch cadence — we'll stand the engine up while we test what shape of short-form actually moves the audience. Five workstreams running in parallel."
1
Start the YouTube long-form recordingLock the theologian roster. Book the studio. Schedule the 3-day batch shoot. Audiobook stems go into post-production. First Reading + Unpacking pair edited by end of month.
2
Run the short-form testing loop3–5 clips per platform per week — TikTok, Reels, Shorts. Never cross-posted. Geo-locked variants for UK vs US. Test hook · length · format · narrator-vs-text. By end of month we know what's winning.
3
Begin the website rebuildGeo-routed retail links, cover swaps for UK vs US, attribution tracker layer scaffolded. Build phase in M1, live by end of M2.
4
Stand up SubstackStandalone GSET account, separate from Bear's personal list. First 4 essays drafted from existing interview transcripts. Andy's editorial pass built into the workflow.
5
Stand up Facebook + Instagram + audit assetsCommunity group up. First cohort designed by eyewitness perspective. Instagram cadence on. Asset bank audited and locked — audiobook master, BG interviews, ChosenCon, licensed visuals.
Land it"By end of Month 1 the engine is built and tested. Month 2 it's live — website live, YouTube at two episodes a week, Substack weekly, Facebook cohort one opens. That's the visible step-change."

Beat 4 · ~45 seconds

The number we'll report on

Show them the measurement loop — this is the difference between a marketing agency and an operator.

Natural"On measurement — the headline number we'll report monthly is what we're calling geo-isolated velocity. US web sessions read against US retail velocity, month by month. That correlation tells us the content is shifting copies, not just generating views. Everything else — saves, shares, watch-time, cohort engagement — feeds the format decisions underneath. It depends on monthly retail velocity reports from your side and the attribution tracker on the rebuilt site."

Beat 5 · ~40 seconds

Two guardrails baked in

Pre-empt the two questions they'll be sitting with. Land cleanly and move on.

Natural"Two things worth flagging because I know they'll be on people's minds.

Hachette's AI policy. Respected from day one. No AI voice, no AI imagery, no AI in covers or marketing creative. AI sits in the backend only — transcription, scheduling, analytics. Every editor signs the policy before they commission.

Bear's bandwidth. No new Bear filming or recording. Pre-recorded only — audiobook, existing interviews, archive. His personal channels carry three anchor posts a year — Easter, Christmas, the anniversary — written by the team, approved by him, posted by him. That's the deliberate structural choice that lets the engine run year-round."
Close · ~20 seconds

Hand to Q+A

Natural"So to summarise — one engine, six channels, one team. Three priorities, in order. Month 1 builds and tests, Month 2 the engine is live, Month 3 we're reading the velocity loop monthly. The deck has the rest of the detail — volumes, phasing, SLAs, the commercial. Happy to take questions now."

How this works

The new shape of your bit

  • They've read the deck. Don't recap it. Don't read slides. Refer when useful — "as you'll have seen..."
  • Three-act structure. Plan → Start → Why us. Each act lands cleanly, then onto the next.
  • ~6 minutes total. Tight enough to land confidently, short enough that they want more in Q+A.
  • Hold detail for Q+A. Plenty to say if asked. Don't pre-empt.

Act 1 · ~2 minutes

This is our plan

The strategy, in plain terms. Two halves — the shape and the priorities.

The shape · why this engine"The plan is one engine, six channels, one team. The book's already done the audience-finding job — 300,000 UK copies in 8 months, number one Sunday Times, 4.8 stars. So the question for us isn't 'how do we promote this book' anymore — it's 'how do we operate the brand of the biggest Christian title Hodder Faith has published in a generation, across two geographies and the start of an international rollout, while staying true to the heart of what the book is.' One team, one slate every week."
The strategic gap · where the US sits"The strategic gap is the United States. The US Christian book market is roughly fifty times the size of the UK's, up 40% since COVID, with appetite at a generational high. Bear is universally known in America — but as an explorer, not yet as a person of faith the way he is in the UK. That work has been done here. It hasn't been done there. That's the year-one job."
What each part of the engine actually does"To make it concrete — YouTube long-form runs two formats every week: Reading episodes that take audiobook passages and layer them with editor-added soundscape, ambient audio, atmospherics — the door creaks open, the storm rises, the crowd builds; and Unpacking episodes where a theologian sits with the same passage — Hebrew roots, historical setting, the scholarship that fact-checked the book. Short-form is the audiobook stems cut into immersive vignettes, plus Bear's existing interviews and licensed visuals — three to five clips a platform per week, geo-locked, never cross-posted. Substack is the only owned email list in the stack — algorithms can take TikTok and Instagram away tomorrow; they can't take Substack away. Theologian transcripts edited into essays, reader letters, seasonal series, gated discussion guides for cohort members. Facebook is the cohort engine — five-week read-alongs structured by eyewitness perspective, Matthew first, then Mark, Luke, John, Acts. Instagram is the daily heartbeat between peaks — quote cards, audiograms, theologian-of-the-week. The website is the destination and the attribution layer — geo-routed retail tree, UK vs US cover swap, every outbound click tagged by source and geography. Each part feeds the next. Reader testimony from Facebook becomes Substack. A short-form winner becomes a YouTube hook. Everything converts on the site."
The priorities"Three priorities, in order. One — close the US gap. Biggest opportunity, time-sensitive — the further we get from launch, the harder it is to claim that ground. We've already mapped the US faith voices that open it for us — Levi Lusko, Kathie Lee Gifford, Jonathan Roumie when the Hodder title lands, The Shack on its autumn relaunch. Two — compound the UK. Don't lose the base. Sustain through the calendar — Lent, Easter, Pentecost, the anniversary, autumn church push, Advent. Three — set up international. Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese — foundations this year, scale next."

· Calendar anchors as content arcs, not posts. Lent, Holy Week, Pentecost, the June anniversary, the autumn UK church push, Advent. Each gets a planned content arc, two weeks prep, raised volume across the fortnight."

Act 3 · ~90 seconds

This is why we can execute it well

The credibility close. Three reasons we're the right team to actually run it.

Lead in"And the last piece — why we're the right team to actually run this. Three things."
1 · We already wrote the strategy"We wrote the strategy you've got in front of you. We know the brand, we know the audience, we know what works in this space because we built that thinking. No ramp-up cost to bringing us in — we start at week one, not month three."
2 · We're set up to find what works fast and double down"Operationally, we're set up to find what works quickly and double down on it — not guess. The short-form testing loop in Month 1 isn't theoretical. From Week 1 we're running three to five variants per platform per week, geo-locked, against a defined matrix — hook, length, format, narrator-versus-text. By end of Month 1 we know which combinations are winning per platform, per geo. Anything that clears the median earns its place in the steady-state mix. Anything that doesn't, we retire and replace. We lock the winners, drop the losers, and scale up the formats that actually move the audience. The headline number on top of all that is geo-isolated velocity — US web sessions read against US retail velocity, month by month. That tells you the content is shifting copies, not just generating views."
3 · The constraints are baked in already"We've built the constraints in from day one. Hachette's AI policy — no AI voice, no AI imagery, no AI in published creative; AI sits in the backend only. And Bear's bandwidth is protected — no new filming, pre-recorded only, three anchor posts a year on his personal channels. We didn't have to learn those mid-engagement. They're in the model already."

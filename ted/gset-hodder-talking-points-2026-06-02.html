Act 1 · ~2 minutes

This is our plan

  • One engine. Six channels. One team.YouTube, short-form, Instagram, Substack, Facebook, and the website. One retainer, one accountable team, one weekly slate.
  • The book has done the audience-finding work.300k UK copies in 8 months. #1 Sunday Times debut. 4.8★ across 1,400+ reviews. The question isn't "how do we promote this book" — it's how we operate the brand for the long haul.
  • The strategic gap is the US.US Christian book market is ~50× the size of the UK's, up 40% since COVID, appetite at a generational high. Bear's known in America as an explorer, not yet as a person of faith. That's the year-one job.
  • Three priorities, in order.1. Close the US gap. 2. Compound the UK through the calendar. 3. Set up international rails (Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese) — foundations this year, scale year two.
Act 2 · ~3 minutes — your longest

This is how we start Month 1 first steps + how the year unfolds

Five workstreams in Month 1, in parallel:
  • Start YouTube long-form recording.Two formats every week. Reading episodes — audiobook passages layered with soundscape, ambient audio, atmospherics. Unpacking episodes — theologian gives the passage context, Hebrew roots, historical setting. Theologians filmed in a 3-day batch shoot, twice a year. 52 eps per batch.
  • Run the short-form testing loop.3–5 clips/platform/week — TikTok, Reels, Shorts. Never cross-posted. Geo-locked UK vs US. Source bank: audiobook stems cut into immersive moments, Bear's existing interviews (Eldredge, Brierley, Metaxas, Cain), licensed visuals where clearable. By end of M1 we know what's winning.
  • Begin the website rebuild.Geo-routed retail tree (Amazon UK/US, Waterstones, B&N, Bookshop.org, St Andrews), UK/US cover swap, attribution tracker. Build in M1, live end of M2.
  • Stand up Substack.Standalone GSET account — separate from Bear's. Theologian transcripts edited into essays, reader letters, seasonal series, gated discussion guides. First 4 essays drafted in M1. The only owned email list in the stack — algorithms can't take it away.
  • Stand up Facebook + Instagram + asset audit.Facebook community group up, structured 5-week cohort read-along #1 by eyewitness perspective (Matthew first, then Mark, Luke, John, Acts). Instagram daily — quote cards, audiograms, theologian-of-the-week. Save/share ratio over likes. Asset bank audited and locked.
How the year unfolds:
Month 1Onboarding
Build phase. Roster locked, studio booked, asset audit done, Substack drafted, FB+IG up. Engine built and tested by end of month.
Month 2Ramp
Theologian batch shoot in the can (52 eps). Website live. YouTube publishes first 4 eps. Short-form turns on at full cadence. Substack weekly. First cohort opens. The visible step-change.
Month 3+Steady state
Full deliverable volume — 8 long-forms, 36 short-form, 20 IG, weekly Substack. Monthly reporting locked. First measurable signal on US traction.
Month 6+International
Spanish + Portuguese architecture stood up. Brazil-targeted Substack pilot active. US book tour support if confirmed. International growth visible in the dashboard.
Act 3 · ~90 seconds

This is why we can run it well

  • We wrote the strategy you're holding.We know the brand, the audience segments, what works in this space. No ramp-up cost — we start at week one, not month three.
  • We're set up to find what works fast and double down.The short-form testing loop runs from Week 1 — 3–5 variants per platform per week against a defined matrix. By end of M1 we know the winners per platform per geo. We lock them, drop the losers, scale what moves the audience. The headline number we report monthly is geo-isolated velocity — US web sessions vs US retail velocity. That tells you the content is shifting copies, not just generating views.
  • The constraints are built in from day one.Hachette's AI policy respected throughout — no AI voice, no AI imagery, no AI in published creative. AI backend only (transcription, scheduling, analytics). Bear's bandwidth protected — pre-recorded only, no new filming, three anchor posts a year on his channels (Easter, Christmas, anniversary), written by the team.

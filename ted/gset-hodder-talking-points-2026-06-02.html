How this works

The new shape of your bit

  • They've read the deck. Don't recap it. Don't read slides. Refer when useful — "as you'll have seen..."
  • Three-act structure. Plan → Start → Why us. Each act lands cleanly, then onto the next.
  • ~6 minutes total. Tight enough to land confidently, short enough that they want more in Q+A.
  • Hold detail for Q+A. Plenty to say if asked. Don't pre-empt.
Open · ~40 seconds, after Jesse

Who you are, then what you're going to do

Lead with your role — that's the first thing they want anchored. Then frame the next few minutes.

Part 1 · Role (~20s) "Thanks Jesse. Quick context on me for those I haven't met — I'm Olly, Market and Strategy Manager at BGV. I run the operational side across our brand portfolio. For Bear Witness specifically, I'm leading the day-to-day on the GSET engine — strategy across the six channels, the calendar, the content slate, the measurement loop all sit with me. If Jesse is the founder of the engine, I'm the one running it week to week."
Part 2 · Frame the next few minutes (~20s) "Since you've all had the deck ahead of time, I won't walk through it slide by slide. What I'll do is talk through the broader strategy and the reasoning behind it, and then the actionable next steps — what we'd be tackling in the first weeks. Six or seven minutes, then plenty of room for Q+A."

Act 1 · ~2 minutes

This is our plan

The strategy, in plain terms. Two halves — the shape and the priorities.

The shape"The plan is one engine, six channels, one team. The book's already done the audience-finding job — 300,000 UK copies in 8 months, number one Sunday Times, 4.8 stars across 1,400+ reviews. So the question isn't 'how do we find an audience' — it's 'how do we keep the one we have moving and open the one we don't.' YouTube, short-form, Instagram, Substack, Facebook and the website. Each feeds the next. One team, one slate every week."
The priorities"Three priorities, in order. One — close the US gap. Biggest opportunity, time-sensitive — the further we get from launch, the harder it is to claim that ground. Two — compound the UK. Don't lose the base. Sustain through the calendar — Lent, Easter, Pentecost, the anniversary, autumn church push, Advent. Three — set up international. Brazil, Spanish, Portuguese — foundations this year, scale next."

Act 3 · ~90 seconds

This is why we can execute it well

The credibility close. Three reasons we're the right team to actually run it.

Lead in"And the last piece — why we're the right team to actually run this. Three things."
1 · We already wrote the strategy"We wrote the strategy you've got in front of you. We know the brand, we know the audience, we know what works in this space because we built that thinking. No ramp-up cost to bringing us in — we start at week one, not month three."
2 · We're operationally set up for this"Operationally, we're set up to run a multi-channel engine. One accountable team, one weekly slate, one monthly summary. The headline number we'll report is geo-isolated velocity — US web sessions read against US retail velocity, month by month. That tells you the content is shifting copies, not just generating views. Everything else feeds the format decisions underneath."
3 · The constraints are baked in already"We've built the constraints in from day one. Hachette's AI policy — no AI voice, no AI imagery, no AI in published creative; AI sits in the backend only. And Bear's bandwidth is protected — no new filming, pre-recorded only, three anchor posts a year on his personal channels. We didn't have to learn those mid-engagement. They're in the model already."

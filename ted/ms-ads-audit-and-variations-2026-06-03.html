The 10-ad Bear Witness brief is the right backbone, but as a delivered set it's leaning hard on one register: VO over stock footage, kitchen / shelf imagery, supplement-shelf hook, same end-card. Jago's first cuts have leaned into that same shoot footage, so the set looks like one ad in ten outfits.

This doc is two halves. First, an honest audit of what's been delivered vs what's still outstanding from the original brief — flagging where the set has gone repetitive. Second, eight new image-led variation briefs built around still photography of Bear (existing assets only, no new filming) with on-image copy. Distinctly different visual register from the shoot-footage ads, and broader in hook, format and audience.

Section 3 · Where the set has gone repetitive

It reads as one ad in different outfits.

Olly's instinct is right — the delivered cuts share too much visual and structural DNA. Five specific repeats worth naming:

Repeat · Hook
"Shelf clutter" is doing too much.

Ads 01, 05 and (implicitly) 10 all open on the same shelf-of-bottles image as the scroll-stopper. The teardown beat lands once. By the third ad the viewer recognises the move.

Repeat · Footage
Same shoot footage cut three ways.

Same kitchen, same morning light, same scoop-into-glass beat appearing across 01, 02, 03, 05. The product cutaway is doing its job, but the b-roll is. Looks like the same edit re-titled.

Repeat · VO register
VO-led, no on-screen Bear.

By design (brief: "no face-to-camera, VO + stock + product"). Right call for some ads, but means we never anchor a viewer on Bear's face — losing the strongest unfair advantage we have.

Repeat · End-card
Identical CTA frame.

Same product still + URL closer on every cut. Means a feed-scroller seeing two MS ads in a row gets identical last frames — kills frequency efficiency.

Repeat · Format
Eight of ten are 30-40s video reels.

Only one carousel (Ad 09) and one in-feed-leaning piece in the brief. Image posts and statics are completely absent — yet they're the cheapest, fastest things to ship and the most easily varied.

Section 4 · Fresh variations

Eight image-led variations off existing BG photography.

Each of these uses an existing photograph of Bear — from the archive, not a new shoot — with on-image copy as the hook. The format is a still image post (or a 1-2 second still-with-subtle-motion reel cover), not a shoot-footage edit. Distinct hooks, distinct framings, distinct audiences. All copy is plain professional — no metaphors. Each variation includes a one-line "why it's different" pointer so the contrast against the existing set is explicit.

Variation 01 · Founders

Father & son · "We didn't licence this. We built it."

Hook (on-image copy)"This isn't a partnership. It's our family's standard."
Visual conceptExisting portrait of Bear and Jesse together — outdoors, candid, eye-level. Deep navy or black duotone treatment, gold serif overlay text top-left, small Modern Savage wordmark bottom-right. No product in the image.
Caption (3-line)I've put my name on supplements before. This one I built. Me and Jesse, from scratch, for our family first. Modern Savage launches July.
FunnelMOF · covers the still-outstanding Ad 06 "Family Mission" lane without a video edit.
Why it's different — no kitchen, no shelf, no scoop. Founder credibility is anchored on Bear's face for the first time in the set.
Variation 02 · The number

One scoop · "Six organs. One bag. No tablets."

Hook (on-image copy)"6 organs · 28 servings · 0 synthetics"
Visual conceptBG portrait — head-and-shoulders, looking off-camera, warm natural light from an existing field shoot. Large serif numbers stacked top-right, each with a one-word descriptor underneath in small caps. Bear sits in the bottom-left third — face, no product.
Caption (3-line)Everything in here comes from real food. Six grass-fed organs, real protein, gut-health stack. One scoop replaces a counter full of bottles. Launches July.
FunnelMOF · the "what's in it" pitch as a 3-second readable still.
Why it's different — replaces the "shelf of bottles" hook with a clean numerical proof point. No supplement-shelf imagery anywhere on screen.
Variation 03 · The line

Field portrait · "Eating this way for thirty years."

Hook (on-image copy)"30 years eating this way. One bag is the closest I've found."
Visual conceptExisting wilderness / expedition photograph of Bear — mid-action, in environment. Type set in italic serif overlaid as a quote, attributed "— Bear Grylls" in small caps underneath. Bottom-right corner: Modern Savage wordmark.
Caption (3-line)In the field, you learn fast what your body actually needs. Organs, protein, real food. Modern Savage is that in a scoop. No theatre.
FunnelBOF · stand-in for Ad 08 "Bear's Word" while shoot-footage cut is finalised.
Why it's different — the only ad in the set that uses outdoor / expedition imagery. Repositions Bear as field practitioner, not kitchen presenter.
Variation 04 · Carousel

Read the ingredients · static 6-slide carousel.

Hook (slide 1)"What's actually in a scoop. Read it."
Visual conceptSlide 1: portrait of Bear holding the bag (existing studio shot or composited if needed), title overlay. Slides 2–6: one ingredient per slide — Primal Protein, Organ Blend, Pre/Pro stack, Superfood Greens, Antioxidant blend. Each slide: clean cream background, one big serif word (the ingredient), one short paragraph, one source line.
Caption (3-line)Real-food nutrition with nothing to hide. Swipe to read every ingredient and where it comes from. Modern Savage launches July, UK + US.
FunnelBOF · covers Ad 09 "What's in the Scoop" — was outstanding, doesn't need video.
Why it's different — the only static-design piece. Built for trust and dwell-time, not scroll-stopping. Cheapest to ship, highest informational density.
Variation 05 · For mums

The Kids Blend ad we still don't have.

Hook (on-image copy)"Same standard as the adult bag. Smaller scoop."
Visual conceptExisting photograph of Bear with one of his sons — candid, warm light, outdoor or kitchen. Type overlay positioned to one side, not centred. Kids Blend wordmark + allergen note ("Contains milk · colostrum") in small caps at bottom — making the transparency part of the design.
Caption (3-line)Same real-food philosophy as the adult bag, dosed for smaller bodies. Half organ load, added colostrum for gut and immune. Honest on the label — contains milk.
FunnelMOF · the still-outstanding Ad 07 "For the Kids" lane.
Why it's different — the only ad that speaks directly to parents with a child visible. Allergen transparency is part of the creative, not a footnote.
Variation 06 · Compare

Side-by-side · the shelf vs the bag.

Hook (on-image copy)"Before. After."
Visual conceptSplit layout — left half: a real photographed cluttered supplement counter (existing stills, not the brief's video shelf). Right half: portrait of Bear holding a single bag of Modern Savage. Big serif "Before / After" labels. Numbers underneath: 8 products → 1; £100/mo → £62; synthetic forms → whole-food forms.
Caption (3-line)One product instead of eight. Real food instead of synthetic. Same job, done properly. Modern Savage launches July.
FunnelTOF / MOF · stand-in conversion for Ad 10 "The Switch" until a user voice is ready.
Why it's different — the only ad that visualises the swap as a single still. Numerical proof points instead of VO storytelling.
Variation 07 · Quiet authority

Iron · a quiet women's-health post.

Hook (on-image copy)"The form of iron your body was built to absorb."
Visual conceptExisting portrait of Bear in a more reflective register — softer light, indoor, looking down or off. No bag in shot. Overlay copy small and elegant, not loud. Sub-line underneath the hook: "Heme iron. From grass-fed liver. Not synthetic tablets." Modern Savage wordmark small bottom-right.
Caption (3-line)Iron deficiency is one of the most common shortfalls, especially in women. Most synthetic tablets are hard on the gut. Heme iron from real liver is what the body was built to use — and it's in every scoop.
FunnelTOF · educational. Covers script 53/70 "Iron" without filming.
Why it's different — the only ad addressing women specifically. Quiet, authoritative tone — opposite of the louder shelf-clutter hooks.
Variation 08 · The standard

One sentence · brand statement.

Hook (on-image copy)"Everything our family needs. In one scoop."
Visual conceptA single existing photograph of Bear — chosen to be the brand's "if you only see one image, see this one" image. Deep navy background bleed or dark gradient. Type set big, centred, in serif. No product. No URL. Just the line and a small Modern Savage wordmark at the bottom. Built to be the pinned post, the launch announcement, the cover frame.
Caption (3-line)Launching July. One brand, the whole family. Real food. No synthetics. Built by us, for ours and yours.
FunnelBrand · the anchor post the rest of the feed sits underneath.
Why it's different — not selling anything. The post that defines what the brand stands for in one frame. None of the existing ten do this job.
Section 5 · Recommended next step

If you have ten minutes, here's the call.

The set isn't broken, it's narrow. Two practical moves get it to launch-ready without commissioning new Bear filming.

Pick the variations you want, lock the photo pool, brief Jago.

This doc has eight variations. Realistically the set needs three to five new pieces, not all eight. The ones I'd push hardest: Variation 01 (founder still), Variation 04 (carousel — fastest unlock, closes Ad 09), and Variation 05 (Kids Blend — closes the most exposed gap in the funnel).

  • You pick which variations to ship — anywhere between three and all eight. Reply with numbers.
  • Pull the photo pool — confirm which existing Bear photographs we have rights to. If the archive is thin, that's the constraint to flag now, not later.
  • Brief Jago on stills + overlay, not shoot footage. Different skill, but the work is mostly typography and crop — not video editing. Day's work per variation, not a week's.
  • Hold Ad 10 ("The Switch") until an early seeder voice is available. Variation 06 covers the lane in the meantime.
  • Re-brief Jago on end-card variation — five different end frames, not one. Cheap fix to the frequency repetition problem.

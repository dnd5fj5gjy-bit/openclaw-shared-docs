01 · Context

Where this fits

This document is a companion to the Publisher Promotion Framework shared earlier this year. Everything else in the framework (audiobook clips, IG, Substack, calendar anchors, Give One Away) still stands behind it.

04 · The episode

What an episode actually looks like

Every episode is built on one chapter of the audiobook. The audio is the spine. The visual layer has two components, intercut:

The reader

60–70%

A single professional performer, sitting and standing in a neutral studio set, silently performing the chapter while the audiobook plays beneath. The same reader appears across all 20 episodes — the recognisable human anchor of the series.

Atmospheric B-roll

30–40%

Cinematic cutaways matched to the chapter's setting and emotional beats — landscape, hands, candlelight, scripture text, ambient texture. No dialogue, no on-screen titles, no graphics overlays during the read.

The result is something between a long-form audiobook take and a contemplative short film. It clears YouTube's 8-minute threshold for mid-roll ads, suits the platform's algorithm preference for sequenced playlists, and gives the audiobook's existing performance the cinematic frame it deserves.

05 · Production

How it gets made

A single batch shoot captures all 20 chapters in 2–3 consecutive days at one London studio. Lighting is set once, the reader stays in character across all chapters, and the entire 40-week run becomes an editing exercise — no scheduling fragility, no re-shoots, no scene-matching between episodes.

06 · Why this serves the book

Five reasons this works for Hodder

  • The audiobook is the asset. This format puts Hodder's existing recording in front of millions of new viewers on the platform they already use. The book is the destination, not a footnote.
  • One year of compounding discovery. Forty weeks of consistent, high-quality output sustains the conversation around the book long past the launch news cycle — every episode is an evergreen entry point.
  • Calendar anchors are baked in. The run is sequenced to put significant chapters at Advent, Christmas, Lent and Easter 2027, so the algorithm and the church calendar reinforce each other.
  • YouTube-native, not a podcast spillover. Purpose-built for the platform where book discovery is increasingly happening — complementing, not competing with, the audiobook's existing distribution.
  • Bear's name carries it; Bear's bandwidth doesn't have to. The patron framing means the series can run for a full year without depending on new Bear content, which has historically been the production bottleneck.
07 · The ask

What we'd like to confirm with Hodder

  1. Audiobook audio use — permission to layer the existing audiobook recording underneath newly-filmed picture, across all 20 episodes.
  2. Editorial cuts — rights to cut and re-sequence the audiobook into 8-minute episode lengths suitable for YouTube.
  3. YouTube monetisation — confirmation that ad revenue on the episodes is in scope.
  4. Short-form clips — use of 30–60 second audiobook excerpts for promotional shorts across YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok in the run-up to and during launch.
  5. Master delivery — agreed path and timeline for clean audiobook audio masters to reach the editor by Monday 9 June 2026.

Anything Hodder would want baked into the brief — attribution lines, end-card requirements, monetisation splits, approval points along the run — we're happy to lock in now so the series ships clean from day one.

08 · Quality assurance

How we're de-risking it

  • Reader is signed with a paid screen-test step before final commitment — on-camera presence is tested, not assumed.
  • Editor has 60-day notice in the retainer, with a backup briefed lightly in case of mid-run departure.
  • BGV operates the channel end-to-end; Olly Westmacott is the named owner for delivery, reporting to Jesse Grylls. Monthly KPI report shared with Hodder if helpful.
  • No paid amplification at launch — the audience is grown organically on the back of clip-led discovery, so the channel proves itself before any spend is added.
